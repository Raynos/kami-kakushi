神隠し UI-v2 prototype gallery

Seven answers to one question: what is the screen? Standalone living mocks — real copy, real numbers, no game engine. Each loads mid-game and ticks on its own; use the dev strip (bottom-right of each) to play the fight, the rung-up ceremony, the seasonal judge, a reveal, and to jump between minute-one / hour-three / hour-ten states.

The minute-one state is each frame's cold open — one verb against the dark — and shows how the UI writes itself into existence.

  1. Shusse Sugoroku出世双六 The screen is a promotion-board game print. Your marker hops rungs; the board spirals outward — no goal square, the world only widens. open →
  2. Nishiki-e in Progress摺 The screen is a woodblock print still being printed. Unlocks arrive as colour plates; locked things are blind-embossed, uninked paper. open →
  3. Yashiki Cutaway屋敷断面 The screen is a carpenter's cross-section of the living house. Rooms are the menus; repairs are carpentry; the house is the progress bar. open →
  4. Daifukuchō大福帳 The screen is the steward's desk. Stamps are verbs, soroban beads are the numbers, the seasonal judge is an audit ruled in red. open →
  5. The Household Stage舞台 The screen is a staged sumi-e theatre with the cast on stage. At the ceremony, the granter walks into frame and the line hangs vertical in the sky. open →
  6. The Filling Kura蔵 The screen is the storehouse your wealth fills. Coins string and swing; at one hundred the string is knotted and hung on a peg. open →
  7. Kage-e影絵 The screen is the guard-post shoji wall; the world appears only as shadows on paper. The bestiary is your eye learning to read silhouettes. open →
