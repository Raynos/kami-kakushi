### Top strengths (the spine is right)
1. **Sequential two-phase per-tier** (§7.1.4/§3.0.1) — same build skeleton every tier; the "half-rungs-maxed-deeds" exploit dies by construction; deeds-only-in-Phase-2 is a *derivable* state (rung==capstone, no stored flag) the §6.6 verifier machine-checks.
2. **Three cleanly-separated combat tracks** (kill→level / deed→Arms / curated→rung-meter) — the structural fix for v1's fused `CombatDeedsPool`, restated identically across §1.6.4/§2.8.1/§4.0.1/§6.4.1, locked by a no-leakage verifier; lets the auto-battler ship unit-tested at M2a before the economy exists.
3. **M0 front-loads the expensive-to-retrofit foundations** (pure-core ESLint boundary as *build failures*, per-named-stream seeded RNG, multi-backend save, content-verifier+gen:docs) with *falsifiable* acceptance checks (a planted `Math.random` fails the build; a fixed seed → byte-identical replay).
4. **Pacing is a machine-checkable FLOOR** — §4.8.1 T0 table is full-resolution and arithmetic-tied-out (270 min, ~21.5K koku, ≤2× ratios), enforced by an undershoot-only CI gate; rung-meter ≡ time-floor off one constant (`RUNG_FLOOR_MIN=30`).
5. **Textbook minute-one hook** — cold open, one verb + a log line (<5s first action, <30s first reveal), feature-unlock-as-plot-beat with escalating named granters; the signature "UI grows as you rise" delivered per-beat, wired as CI-tested headline beats.
6. **Determinism discipline is correct** — one RNG as per-named-stream cursors, stateless day-keyed weather, integer-pow-only (banning non-correctly-rounded IEEE-754 ops), active-only enforced structurally. The keystone the whole headless-QA/pacing-gate plan rests on.
7. **Strong Edo authenticity foundation** + a real anti-slop woodblock UI bible; **high fidelity** — all 79 decisions carried with Q-anchors, superseded locks annotated-not-deleted, highest-risk reshapes turned into build-failing §6.6.1 invariants.

### Blocking defects (confirmed; each = a human design pick → see questions)
- **B1 — Name pillar T2 tie-out is broken; the pillar auto-clears the v1 end-gate with ZERO deeds.** `JUDGE_K[Name][T2]=42,000` is computed off a stale `TIER_REF_NAME=140K`, not Name's own 28K good band — so the seasonal stream *alone* (42K) is 150% of the good band (= its GREAT band), while §4.2.1/§4.8.1b itemize **zero** Name deeds. The basis clamps `f_Name→1.0` once the other three pillars merely reach good, so "good in Name" is **vacuous** at the climactic gate. Falsifies three locked statements (seasonal=30%-of-good; deeds-70/seasonal-30; breadth-floor/deeds-dominate) and the §6.6.1 verifier can't pass for Name without an unstated exemption. Also §4.2.2 "Name gates no v1 tier" flatly contradicts §4.1. → **Q6**.
- **B2 — Estate builds E1/E2/E3 are gated on pillar-Influence floors that are structurally unreachable where the builds sit.** E1@R4 needs Estate≥0.3K and E2@R6 needs Estate≥0.6K+Arms≥0.3K — but those are T0 Phase-1 rungs where pillar Influence is pinned at 0 (Phase-2-only accrual, FU7). E3@G4 needs Estate≥40K+Arms≥15K but at G4 pillars are frozen at end-T1 (≤15K/≤10K) — impossible by ~2.7×. The §4.7.5 affordability tie-out checks only the koku *cost* and skips the Influence floors. → **Q7**.
- **B3 — Save import-over can be silently discarded by a higher-counter autosave.** An imported file (old self-export / QA hand-off / friend's save) carries its own typically-*lower* `saveCounter`; the spec never says import rebases the counter above local-max nor rewrites all backends, so on next load the still-present autosave (higher counter) is elected and the player-confirmed import is **silently lost** — and the multi-backend redundancy *propagates* the loss. Related: additive optional-fields *without* a `schemaVersion` bump (§6.8.2) lets a stale-CDN build silently truncate a newer same-version save. Violates the save layer's "no silent loss" headline. → see Q (save) in master list.

## MASTER human-question list (ranked, deduped — grows each round)

> These are the decisions **only the human can make**. Resolving them rewrites the entangled numbers/prose.

**Critical**
- **Q1 — Pace ceiling / optimizer loop.** Is the ≥30-min rung floor meant to also cap the *ceiling* within a rung (max & min efficiency clear in the same ~30 min, no fast-track even with mastery/gear)? §4.1.1 back-solves the meter so efficiency doesn't shorten the rung — is that intended, or should mastery let you out-pace the floor?

**High**
- **Q2 — Budget headline (~60h vs 28.5h).** §4.8.4 itself shows Phase-1 floor ~28.5h + Phase-2 adds ~+32h ≈ **~60h** realized v1 — ~2× the repeated "28.5h" headline and the stated ~32h target. Accept ~60h (restate the headline), or compress Phase 2?
- **Q3 — Phase-2 novelty.** Phase 2 is the *larger* half of each tier (T2 ~15.5h) yet by FU7 every reveal/weapon/area/tab unlocks in Phase 1 — so Phase 2 injects ~zero new content (T2 = only cross-pillar combos + the E3 sink), flatlining reveal cadence across the back half. Inject Phase-2 reveals, or accept a deliberately quieter "consolidation" half?
- **Q4 — Floor enforcement mechanism.** The ≥30-min floor (a locked acceptance criterion) has no *buildable* enforcement — §4.1.1's `threshold=30·rate` is a tautological back-solve and the ~14–19 thresholds don't reconcile with the specified tick model. How is the floor actually enforced/measured at runtime?
- **Q5 — Hybrid-gate breadth wall.** "Good in ALL revealed pillars, no overflow/substitution" is checked at the *end* of a 12–15h Phase 2 — a player who specialises (combat is the fun part) can be 200% over on Arms and still hard-blocked on a lagging pillar. Keep the hard breadth wall, soften with partial substitution, or surface the shortfall far earlier?

**Medium**
- **Q6 — Name pillar resolution (resolves B1).** Is Name a *required gate* pillar at T2 → then set `JUDGE_K[Name][T2]=0.30·28K=8,400`, author a ~19.6K Name deed inventory, fix the cap column to `0.04·28K=1.12K`; **or** is Name a *derived display bar* in v1 (not gated) → then fix §4.1 instead and keep "gates no v1 tier"?
- **Q7 — Estate-build gating (resolves B2).** Re-gate E1/E2/E3 on the Phase-1 *holdings* basis (koku-scaled index that grows in Phase 1) and re-derive the floors + §4.7.5 tie-out; **or** move the three builds into each tier's Phase 2; **or** drop the Influence floors (koku-cost only)?
- **Q-save — Import-over (resolves B3).** Make import rebase `saveCounter` above local-max + rewrite all backends (and bump `schemaVersion` on additive fields)? Confirm the intended import semantics.
- **Q8 — M3 scope.** Pre-split M3 at the R6→R7 (Phase-1→Phase-2) seam the way combat was split M2a/M2b? M3 currently fuses the whole four-pillar Phase-2 engine + hybrid gate + House screen + two nav tabs + combat content.
- **Q9 — Player-facing legibility.** Ship a single always-reachable "how the house rises" codex (character level vs the two rung-meters vs four pillars vs House Influence vs the banzuke) + a per-resolution combat readout?
- **Q10 — First-session shape.** R0→R2 is ~60–65 min of single-texture labour before combat (the hook) appears at R3, and 2 of 7 core fun loops never fire in the first 2h. Front-load a taste of combat/variety, or accept the slow burn?
- **Q11 — Active-only vs background throttling.** The ~60h grind's palatability rests on "leave it running, check back" (FU23), but browsers aggressively throttle/freeze background tabs (timer clamp ≥1/min). Does the active-only model survive real background throttling, or is a catch-up-on-focus reconciliation needed?

**Low**
- **Q12 — Authenticity name collision.** Lord = "Munenori", drillmaster = "Jūbei" → exactly Yagyū Munenori + Yagyū Jūbei, the two most iconic names in samurai sword culture, on the lord and the sword teacher. Intentional homage or rename to avoid the on-the-nose collision?
