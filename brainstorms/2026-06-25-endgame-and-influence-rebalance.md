## 1. Rebalance summary

> *RECOMMENDED REBALANCE — one design, all §1.11/macro notes applied.*

House Influence (家威 ka-i) stops being a single trade-leaning score (PRD §1.6's seven conversion levers, of
which trade/meibutsu was the dominant path and the literal Edo climax). It becomes the **umbrella name** for
**FOUR parallel, achievement-driven pillars** the player grows in lockstep:

- **ARMS (武威 bu-i, MARTIAL)** — retainers/men-at-arms fielded, victories & clears, road/estate security held,
  rival houses' forces bested.
- **ESTATE & WEALTH (家産 kasan, ECONOMIC)** — lands/koku, treasury & creditworthiness, AND trade. Trade/meibutsu
  is now **ONE of three sub-engines INSIDE this one pillar** (the others: LAND reclamation, TREASURY/finance),
  so the famous product — formerly the whole endgame — is one optional capstone in one sub-engine of one of four
  pillars.
- **STANDING & OFFICE (政威 sei-i, POLITICAL/TERRITORIAL)** — villages→region→castle-town under the house's sway,
  domain offices & administration held, alliances sealed, rivals eclipsed.
- **NAME & HONOUR (家格 kakaku, PRESTIGE/CULTURAL)** — formal kakaku rank, reputation, patronage, notable deeds,
  lineage restored, recognition recorded in official ink.

**Tiers** (T0 Estate → T4 Edo, adopting the prompt's numbering; = PRD's T1→T5 offset by one — see decisions)
are gated by a **four-value per-pillar threshold with a balanced-development FLOOR**: you must clear ALL FOUR
minimums to advance, but a **capped overflow (≤25%)** lets a strong pillar partly carry a lagging one, so
specialists are rewarded without ignoring a pillar. This **structurally forbids a pure-trade or pure-combat
tier-skip** — the single most important de-trade-weighting mechanism. Early tiers weight **Estate+Arms** (survive,
earn keep, get strong); upper tiers weight **Office+Name** (the takeover and the national ranking are won socially).

Each pillar maps to a distinct **protagonist domain** so the SPINE (a mediocre man growing only by grind across
COMBAT and LABOUR/JOBS/SKILLS/TRADES) stays load-bearing: **Arms ← combat/weapon-skills/men-at-arms leadership**
(where D-007 "combat earns standing" lives); **Estate ← labour/jobs/skills/trades/crafting**; **Office ←
jobs-as-offices/administration/quests**; **Name ← the recognition layer** reflecting the other three plus
deeds/patronage/lineage. Accrual stays **strictly achievement + seasonal-milestone** (never a time-trickle, never
a flat per-action increment): every +pillar event names a diegetic deed, and the periodic award is a **JUDGED
RESULT** of accumulated state appraised once per season, not a per-action drip.

The **T3 castle-town takeover** becomes a multi-route "become the leading house" conquest. The **T4 Edo climax**
becomes a national multi-pillar **mitate-banzuke** (period-authentic parody ranking broadsheet) on which the HOUSE
climbs all four columns — celebrating overall strength, not the product. **D-010's honest ceiling is preserved by
ranking the HOUSE, not the man**; the protagonist stays its architect/right-hand.

**Lean discipline (workflow's original recommendations — see human overrides above):** side factions remain
**MULTIPLIERS** into the four pillars (never new pillars); upper tiers stay **abstract holdings/correspondence/ranking
BOARDS** (not walkable maps); the full banzuke is a **T3+ endgame layer** (T0–T2 keep a simpler standing display).
The workflow framed this explicitly as a **T3–T4 roadmap capstone, not v1 scope** (workflow's v1 = T0–T1).

## 2. The four pillars

| Pillar | Kanji | Protagonist domain it maps to |
|---|---|---|
| **ARMS — MARTIAL** | 武威 (bu-i) | combat / weapon-skills / men-at-arms leadership (D-007 home) |
| **ESTATE & WEALTH — ECONOMIC** | 家産 (kasan) | labour / jobs / skills / trades / crafting |
| **STANDING & OFFICE — POLITICAL/TERRITORIAL** | 政威 (sei-i) | jobs-as-offices / administration / quests |
| **NAME & HONOUR — PRESTIGE/CULTURAL** | 家格 (kakaku) | the recognition layer (reflects the other three + deeds/patronage/lineage) |

> **Authenticity flag:** 政威 *sei-i* is a **coined compound**; an attested alternative is **領 *ryō* / "territorial
> sway"** — to confirm at the §4/§5 authenticity-harden.

### 2a. ARMS — 武威 (bu-i) — MARTIAL

- **What it is.** The house's martial authority: the strength, reliability and reputation of its men-at-arms and the
  protagonist's own combat record, plus the security it provides. **This is where the combat pillar EARNS standing
  (the D-007 premise)** — turning the already-specced first-class combat systems (weapon-skill lines, mob clears,
  loot/found+crafted gear, the men-at-arms leadership rungs) into house standing rather than a side activity.
- **How it grows.** Achievement JUMPS on recognised martial deeds + seasonal RESULTS appraising standing
  force/security — **never per-kill trickle**. The existing Combat-Deeds discipline (per-event caps, ~0.1-level/kill)
  carries over so no single fight spikes the pillar. *Jumps:* a bandit nest cleared and the road "declared safe" in
  the books; a rival's enforcer arm (Hanzaki / the racket muscle) broken; rival retainers bested in a sanctioned
  dispute/duel; a security crisis quelled. *Seasonal results:* "the men-at-arms held the quarter through the lean
  season with no losses"; a standing patrol kept.
- **Feeders (multipliers):** the **Matagi** hunters and drill-yard reputation multiply martial accrual.
- **Example milestones per tier:**
  - **T0:** survive the humbling first fight; clear the first pest/animal nest; stand a held night-watch.
  - **T1:** a valley road declared safe; 2–3 men-at-arms on a rota; the grain store defended.
  - **T2:** a bandit nest cleared; the high-pass route secured; a standing patrol.
  - **T3:** the house is the seat's de-facto road-security; a rival's enforcer arm broken; the security-crisis
    set-piece won (the assertive route to a domain office).
  - **T4:** a domain-defence/security commendation travels up to Edo; the **Hanzaki duel finally WON** (survived all
    game, beatable on labour-built endurance); a frontier held through a famine-winter.

### 2b. ESTATE & WEALTH — 家産 (kasan) — ECONOMIC

- **What it is.** The house's material base: lands held & reclaimed (koku), treasury/creditworthiness, AND trade.
  Critically, **trade is demoted to ONE of THREE economic sub-engines** —
  (a) **LAND** (shinden reclamation, the classic goshi-advancement lever),
  (b) **TREASURY/FINANCE** (debt→solvency→creditworthiness, goyōkin),
  (c) **TRADE** (routes, broker standing, the optional meibutsu).
  The famous product that used to *be* the endgame is now one capstone option in sub-engine (c) of this single pillar.
- **How it grows.** Achievement JUMPS + seasonal harvest/audit RESULTS. *Jumps:* the first shinden plot's yield
  recorded; the kura declared solvent; a usurious loan restructured into creditworthiness; the meibutsu graded; the
  **Kuzuhara embankment** securing yield. *Seasonal:* "this week's/season's harvest exceeded quota"; "the autumn audit
  records the surplus." A **judged result of accumulated state** — flooding a good or grinding posts nothing until the
  season's books acknowledge it.
- **Feeders (multipliers):** **Origin trade-ties** multiply the trade sub-engine; **Scholars** improve audit/finance
  efficiency.
- **HARD CAP:** trade is tuned to **at most ~1/3 of this one pillar**, so a pure-trade player maxes ~1/3 of one of four
  pillars and is hard-stopped at every tier gate by the other three.
- **Example milestones per tier:**
  - **T0:** first shinden plot's yield recorded; kura solvent; first season's quota met.
  - **T1:** cash-crop/proto-industry online; the mon on village contracts; a tradeable surplus.
  - **T2:** branch holdings; the Kuzuhara embankment; broker/warehouse standing.
  - **T3:** the seat's indispensable creditor/supplier; the domain's strangling debt restructured via **goyōkin** so the
    daimyo's finance depends on the house (the economic route to the **kanjō-kata** office).
  - **T4:** the meibutsu graded and celebrated in Edo markets (the OLD climax, now ONE column); restructured debt becomes
    national creditworthiness; origin-father **Kuranosuke's** debt bought back, crowning the trade sub-engine.

### 2c. STANDING & OFFICE — 政威 (sei-i) — POLITICAL / TERRITORIAL

- **What it is.** Governing authority: villages→region→castle-town under the house's sway, the domain OFFICES &
  administration it holds, alliances sealed, and rival houses politically eclipsed. This is the **win-condition substrate
  of the T3 castle-town takeover**, fed by **JOBS-AS-OFFICES** and quests. *(政威 sei-i is coined; attested alternative
  領 ryō / "territorial sway" — flag for the §4/§5 harden.)*
- **How it grows.** Achievement JUMPS on offices granted, territory secured, alliances sealed, rivals subordinated —
  **never trickle**. *Jumps:* named on the household roster; granted the home-fields **bailiff (jitō-dai)** duty; the
  headman cedes the granary's administration; a village dispute arbitrated; an appointment posted by the **daikan**; a
  neighbouring valley allied; a rival's office reassigned to the house. **The protagonist holds a deputy/right-hand post
  UNDER offices held in the house's/head's name** (preserving D-010 per-office).
- **Feeders (multipliers):** **Scholars** (administration), the **Neighbouring-Valleys bloc**, allied retainers.
- **Example milestones per tier:**
  - **T0:** named on the household roster; granted the home-fields bailiff (jitō-dai) duty.
  - **T1:** the headman's regard won; the house arbitrates a village dispute; first errand-authority beyond the gate.
  - **T2:** 2+ valleys allied/anchored; a district standing roll-up.
  - **T3:** domain offices held (daikan's favour → kanjō-kata recording → a magistracy-adjacent post); the 2+ rival
    houses eclipsed; the house tops the DOMAIN banzuke.
  - **T4:** a documented merit-appointment flows DOWN through the lord; the house leads the domain's retainer houses on
    the national chart's political column.

### 2d. NAME & HONOUR — 家格 (kakaku) — PRESTIGE / CULTURAL

- **What it is.** The house's rank, reputation and recognition: formal kakaku standing, patronage given/received,
  notable deeds publicly recorded, lineage restored & surpassed, the daimyo's favour, and recognition recorded in
  official ink. **It reflects the other three pillars PLUS authored deeds** — the pillar that turns substance into
  reckoned fame, and the **soft path** to the apex offices and the banzuke's upper ranks.
- **How it grows.** Achievement JUMPS on recognised honour-events + seasonal reputation appraisal. *Jumps:* the lord's
  explicit recognition; the house off the foreclosure list; a sponsored festival/shrine repair; the touring-inspector's
  report names the house; a marriage/adoption alliance; a kakaku merit-elevation (**karō-kaku**); a recognised treatise
  on the house's water-works; the house first printed on the national banzuke.
- **Feeders (multipliers):** **Pilgrimage/temple** and **Scholars** multiply prestige; the daimyo's confidence is its
  own head-to-head meter vs each rival.
- **Ceiling guardrail (per §1.13):** karō-kaku / "adoption into the house name" stays **aspirational narration**; the
  protagonist's plausible personal top is **chief steward / the lord's right hand**.
- **Example milestones per tier:**
  - **T0:** the lord's explicit recognition; the house off the foreclosure list.
  - **T1:** a sponsored festival/shrine repair; the valley calls it "the Kurosawa works."
  - **T2:** the embankment recognised as distinguished service; a head-temple's vouch.
  - **T3:** a marriage/adoption alliance; a kakaku merit-elevation; the daimyo elevates the Kurosawa over the hereditary
    house.
  - **T4:** the touring-inspector's report mints the top prestige step; the house named and climbing on the national
    mitate-banzuke; a celebrated patronage/cultural deed recorded in Edo.

## 3. Tier-gating — the balanced-development floor, overflow rule & trade hard-cap

The tier-up condition replaces "crossing a single Influence band" with a **four-value gate**:

1. **Balanced-development FLOOR.** You must clear **ALL FOUR per-pillar minimums** to advance. This structurally
   forbids a **pure-trade or pure-combat tier-skip** — the most important de-trade-weighting mechanism.
2. **Capped overflow-substitution (≤25%).** A strong pillar can **partly carry** a lagging one — but only up to **25%**
   of the missing pillar's minimum. Specialists are rewarded; ignoring a pillar is impossible.
3. **Per-tier weighting drift.** Early tiers weight **Estate + Arms** (survive, earn keep, get strong); upper tiers
   weight **Office + Name** (the takeover and national ranking are won socially). *(Both dials — floor values and
   overflow % — exposed as data.)*
4. **Trade HARD-CAP.** Trade is tuned to **at most ~1/3 of the Estate pillar**. With four pillars, a pure-trade run
   maxes **~1/3 of one of four pillars** and is hard-stopped at every gate by the other three.
5. **Roll-up shape.** The overall 家威 roll-up is closer to a **min / geometric-mean than a sum** (the weakest pillar
   drags hardest), with Estate capped at **~1/4 of roll-up weight** and trade at **~1/3 of Estate** — to be **generated
   into `docs/`** so it is auditable, and **headlessly regression-tested** for pure-combat / pure-trade / balanced play.

**Rejected alternatives:** a *pure weighted sum* (lets one giant pillar carry the rest → re-enables the pure-trade run
the human rejected) and *four hard ANDs* (forces tedious perfectly-even play). Floor+overflow is the chosen middle.

## 5. The T3 castle-town takeover — "peacefully or otherwise," honest within the bakufu

**What "takeover" authentically means** (the plausibility wall made into the design): under the bakuhan order a goshi
house **cannot militarily storm a castle-town** — open force = rebellion = bakufu attainder = the house destroyed. So
"take over" = becoming the **DOMINANT / LEADING HOUSE** of the domain: holding the commanding administrative OFFICES,
owning the daimyo's confidence, and reducing the 2+ rival houses to subordinate/allied/ruined status. The castle stays
the daimyo's; the Kurosawa come to **RUN it for him**. Historical anchors license this exactly: **Yoshida Tōyō of Tosa**
(district magistrate → karō on the daimyo's favour + reform competence, effectively directing the domain, displacing the
hereditary faction); **O-Ie Sōdō (御家騒動, "house strife")** is the period's own name for factional struggle over
offices/succession; and with the daimyo absent on **sankin-kōtai**, the **jōdai-karō and bugyō offices WERE the de-facto
government** — capturing them IS capturing the domain.

### 5a. The prize — the office ladder seated in the castle-town

Each office is tagged to the pillar(s) that win it and the rival who holds it:

| Office | Pillar(s) | Notes / current holder |
|---|---|---|
| **DAIKAN / gun-bugyō** (district admin & tax) | Office + Estate | the canon **Daikan / Tedai-Kuroiwa cluster** sits here; the **first office the house holds** |
| **KANJŌ-KATA** (domain finance) | Estate | held by **Tomita's faction**; won by rescuing the DOMAIN's debt |
| **MACHI-BUGYō-sho** (magistracy, law, security; the **osso** petition hall) | Arms + Office | — |
| **senior KARō / jōdai-karō seat** (the apex prize) | Name + Office | held by the OLD hereditary house **by birth** — the Yoshida-Tōyō target |
| **YōNIN** (attendant-advisor; proximity to the daimyo's ear) | Name | the **soft path** to all the others |

### 5b. The rival power-struggle — O-Ie Sōdō at goshi scale (two archetypes)

So the effort>talent thesis lands twice:

- **HOUSE TOMITA (canon)** — the **MODERN** rival: capital, connections, ruthlessness; holds finance/commercial offices
  and the daikan's favour. **Beaten by OUT-COMPETING** (substance vs bought advantage).
- **NEW — HOUSE OKABE** — the **OLD hereditary-conservative** house: birth-proud, complacent; holds the senior karō seat
  by lineage not merit. **Beaten by OUT-PERFORMING** (merit vs inherited entitlement).

Each rival is a contested set of meters (office-holds, daimyo-favour, allied valleys/brokers) flipped pillar by pillar.
**NON-LETHAL resolutions per house:** **DÉTENTE** (negotiated power-share); **ALLIANCE** (marriage/adoption absorbs their
strength); **CLEAN DEFEAT** (out-competed/out-performed until the daimyo reassigns their office to the Kurosawa); or
**DISGRACE** (only via the optional racket-evidence thread — exposed and stripped of office, honouring partial justice).
**The player never assassinates:** inverting the historical Tōyō (assassinated by extremists he beat), the Kurosawa are the
clean reformers who SURVIVE by daylight; a desperate rival's resort to violence/coercion is what DISCREDITS them before
the off-stage **metsuke** (the rebellion guardrail dramatised).

### 5c. Multiple routes — all valid, combinable, each leaning a pillar

**PEACEFUL:**
- **(A) ADMINISTRATIVE / REFORMER (Office, the Yoshida-Tōyō path)** — distinguished service (Kuzuhara flood-control,
  famine-relief, a clean audit) earns appointment daikan → kanjō-kata → toward karō; install Kurosawa allies in vacated
  posts.
- **(B) ECONOMIC LEVERAGE (Estate)** — rescue the DOMAIN's strangling debt via **goyōkin** + honest rice-finance until it
  cannot function without the house.
- **(C) MARRIAGE / ADOPTION ALLIANCE (Name + Office)** — a sanctioned tie leaps a rung or absorbs a rival by merger.
- **(D) DAIMYO'S FAVOUR (Name)** — the yōnin path: win the lord's personal confidence so he ELEVATES the Kurosawa over the
  hereditary house.

**ASSERTIVE / "OR OTHERWISE"** (pressure within bounds, never rebellion):
- **(E) MARTIAL / SECURITY LEVERAGE (Arms)** — the house's retainers + the protagonist's combat become the seat's
  de-facto security (put down a bandit surge/riot the rival house FUMBLES, guard the kaidō, defeat a rival's retainers in
  sanctioned bouts), making the house the one the daimyo relies on for order.
- **(F) COERCION / LEVERAGE within bounds** — call in a rival's debts, expose their racket complicity, exploit a crisis
  where they fail and the Kurosawa deliver.

### 5d. Win condition & victory shapes

A **DOMAIN STANDING roll-up** (the T3 face of the four-pillar model): the Kurosawa hold a **majority/plurality of the
offices OR specifically the karō seat**, the daimyo's confidence **exceeds the leading rival's**, and rivals are
subordinated; completion fires when the house **TOPS THE DOMAIN-LEVEL BANZUKE** (a T3 precursor to the national one).
Because offices map to different pillars, a pillar-heavy player still reaches "leading" by a different office-door. Three
named victory shapes give replay flavour:

- **THE STEWARD-DOMAIN** — won on administrative reform + offices.
- **THE BANKER-DOMAIN** — won on finance indispensability.
- **THE SHIELD-DOMAIN** — won on security/martial trust.

### 5e. Set-pieces (each draws a different pillar)

1. **THE OFFICE APPOINTMENT** — the daimyo names the Kurosawa to an office over the rivals' objection in the castle
   audience hall (the first time the house sits where a rival sat).
2. **THE OSSO / PETITION SHOWDOWN** — the machi-bugyō-sho case where the racket/rival is challenged on documented proof.
3. **THE SECURITY CRISIS** — a bandit surge/flood/riot the rival fumbles and the Kurosawa's retainers + the protagonist
   resolve (the assertive route's headline; the rare dangerous fight combat earns).
4. **THE GOYōKIN / DEBT-RESCUE** — the domain's finances fail; the Kurosawa post the loan no rival can.
5. **THE ALLIANCE / O-IE-SōDō RESOLUTION** — a marriage absorbs a rival, OR the daimyo arbitrates and reassigns offices,
   OR a rival over-reaches and discredits itself.
6. **THE METSUKE / INSPECTION as legitimacy test** — the touring-inspector judges WHICH house best runs the domain,
   bridging **T3 → T4**.

**Authenticity guardrails (must stay guardrails, not footnotes):** **leading house, NEVER the lord; offices not the
castle; pressure not rebellion; rivals subordinated/allied/disgraced, never killed by the player.**

## 6. The T4 national Edo climax — a multi-pillar mitate-banzuke

*(Replaces "the meibutsu celebrated in Edo.")* The climax is no longer the famous product reaching Edo's markets (the
trade-weighting the human wants gone). It is the **HOUSE climbing a NATIONAL, Edo-level RANKING CHART** — a
**mitate-banzuke**, the period-authentic **parody-ranking broadsheet** genre (cf. the **chōjya-banzuke** "ranking of the
rich"; charts ranked rich men, restaurants, even disasters — so a "banzuke of provincial houses" reads as a real woodblock
print, **NOT a game-y leaderboard and NOT an official bakufu register**, which is what keeps the D-010 ceiling intact).

- **FORM** *(workflow's recommendation — note the human override toward per-tier rank ladders, §0)*: a woodblock
  broadsheet UI — top-centre title/date, **EAST and WEST** columns, ranks descending **Yokozuna → Ōzeki → Sekiwake →
  Komusubi → Maegashira-1..N** with calligraphy shrinking down the chart, compiler's-name margin. It has **FOUR PILLAR
