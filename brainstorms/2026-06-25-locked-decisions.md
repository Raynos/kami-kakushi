## A. Core identity (grounded constraints — still binding)
- **Grounded / no magic.** Nothing supernatural ever happens; every belief/omen resolves to a human or
  natural cause. **No** player magic, ever.
- **Folklore = believed-atmosphere & LIGHT FLAVOUR**, not the spine. Delivered via the village **inn
  rumours board**; each rumour-quest is **optional**, resolves to a human/natural cause, and **unlocks
  organically** (conditions per tier; more unlock as the estate & village grow — never an all-at-once
  dump). Residual unresolved ambiguity **capped at ≤1**, off-screen, mundane-readable.
- **Mediocre start.** Weak & slow (NOT a bedridden invalid); no hidden edge / bloodline / secret training.
  Grows **only** through perseverance, repetition, training, gear. The porter's-knot habit and the
  recurring dream are identity flavour with **ZERO mechanical bonus** (memory grants *access*, not power).
  The first real fight is **humbling/near-fatal**.
- **Protagonist:** authored, **fixed male** (no name/appearance/gender creator, no rename), age **~18–20**.
  Village-name **"Tama"**; true name **Tahei** revealed as a **late, de-emphasised side beat** (identity
  is a side thread). Fixed-male protects the legend's gender-drift clue.
- **Title:** *Kamikakushi* (神隠し). **Setting:** mid-Edo, **stable & commercial (~18th c.)**, kept
  **fictional** — no real place/daimyo names.

## B. Spine, tiers & tier-transition gates
- **Spine:** an estate-restoration incremental RPG — rise the **Kurosawa** lower-samurai (*goshi*) house's
  ranks and grow **House Influence** to restore **and surpass** the house, climaxing in **indirect/mediated
  recognition at Edo** (the **house** is recognised; the MC stays its architect — **no** personal
  hatamoto/shogun ascension).
- **NO reset of any kind** — tiers replace prestige; everything persists.
- **Tiers (renumbered, 0-indexed):** **T0 Estate** (tutorial) → **T1 Village** → **T2 Region** (incl. the
  origin family/friends @ Sawatari-juku + Kuzuhara) → **T3 Domain/Castle-town** → **T4 Edo** (national).
- **Per-tier transition STORY GATES:**
  - **T0→T1:** do enough estate work + complete **basic repairs** → the estate sends you out into the village.
  - **T1→T2:** **"clean your room"** (estate healthy, village happy, immediate fires out) → the lord first
    believes impact beyond the estate is possible → quest to grow **regional influence**; the Region
    introduces **rival samurai houses** with more sway you must surpass.
  - **T2→T3:** **win the region** (rival houses no longer the leaders) → the castle-town rulers **confer
    regional leadership** on your house and **invite** it to the castle-town.
  - **T3→T4:** a **"taste of Edo"** — your house is **forced to build & fund an Edo estate** → "conquer the
    castle-town" / grow influence → the **national** tier.
- **Castle-town takeover = MULTI-ROUTE** (peaceful: office/economy/marriage/out-maneuvering rivals; AND
  assertive: martial-security leverage). "Take over" = become the **dominant house holding key domain
  offices** — NOT open rebellion against the bakufu.

## C. Factions
- **Three starter factions (T0–T2):**
  - **ESTATE (main)** — the Kurosawa household. **A fresh RANK LADDER PER TIER** (per-tier service/standing
    hierarchies); rungs **interleave labour & combat**; combat **earns standing**. The estate **CAST &
    BUILDINGS GROW per tier** — the player builds structures and recruits members, framed as **flavour /
    light systems, NOT a people-management sim**. Upper ranks (5/6/7) must be fleshed out around the MC's
    own gameplay.
  - **VILLAGE of Asagiri (side)** — a reputation **web** (chief **Yagoemon**, shops, craftsmen/artisans,
    the inn & rumours board); the kamikakushi "are you Tama?" legend lives here. **Cast mostly STATIC.**
    Never gates the spine.
  - **ORIGIN (side, memory-gated SUPPORT track)** — Tahei's **living** family/friends in post-town
    **Sawatari-juku**: mother **Oyuki**, **father Jinpachi** (re-added; renamed from the colliding
    "Kuranosuke"), sister **Okimi**, employer **Denbei**,
    friend **Kanta**, sweetheart **Ohana**, the porter guild. **Opens at T2 Region**; the **dream
    foreshadows from early game**; they **support** his estate achievements (pride/allies/trade-ties);
    access-only, ZERO mechanical gift.
- **Allegiance (Tama ↔ farmhand):** a **continuous, re-swingable** leaning; shifts standing-gain **rates &
  flavour, never availability**; neutral is valid. **Faction tension = light/flavour** (no mechanical
  penalty).
- **More factions/zones per tier** beyond the three starters (see world-expansion doc), each **itself
  incremental**; side content never blocks the spine.

## D. House Influence (家威) — the macro-resource
- **FOUR achievement-driven pillars:** **Arms (武威)** · **Estate & Wealth (家産)** · **Standing & Office**
  (plain-English label; **exact kanji deferred to the §5 authenticity pass** — no coined compound committed) ·
  **Name & Honour (家格)**.
- **Trade is demoted** to **1-of-3 sub-engines** inside Estate & Wealth (land / treasury / trade),
  **hard-capped to ~⅓ of that one pillar** — so trade can never dominate. The signature **meibutsu = SILK /
  sericulture** (LOCKED) — the famous product within the trade sub-engine (still capped); not an
  "optional/undecided" candidate.
- **Accrual = achievement JUMPS + seasonal JUDGED RESULTS** — the seasonal appraisal fires **PER SEASON
  (4×/in-game year; the autumn harvest is the headline)** — **never a passive time-trickle, never a flat
  per-action increment.**
- **Up-only + rare, scripted, recoverable dents** (scandal/called debt) — **MINOR & quickly recoverable**
  (a small dip clawed back within a season or two); **never a wipe**.
- **Tier gating = PER-TIER REQUIRED PILLARS via SIMPLE THRESHOLDS** (early tiers weight Arms+Estate
  "survive & get strong"; upper tiers weight Office+Name "win it socially"). **NOT** a balanced-development
  floor / ≤25%-overflow formula — that author-invented mechanic is **REJECTED** (Round A). Side factions
  are **multipliers** into the pillars, never new pillars.

## E. Combat (a first-class core pillar)
- **Introduced EARLY** — combat is a core pillar from the FIRST tier (T0), NOT a mid-ladder reveal; combat
  rungs/threats weave throughout the per-tier ladders, and the humbling first real fight is an early beat.
- **Style:** **idle auto-resolve + active setup** (prepare gear/stance/area; deterministic seeded fight;
  intervene with stance/ability/item/retreat). Low-APM, strategic.
- **Systems:** character **combat level** + weapon/martial **skills** + **mobs/bestiary** + **loot** +
  **equipment FIND and CRAFT** + **gear progression**.
- **Combat EARNS House Influence** via the **Arms** pillar / martial security (clear bandits, secure roads,
  defend the estate) — *supersedes the old "combat is never a source of standing" framing.*
- **Mediocre-start preserved on the combat track** (start weak; humbling first fight; earned via training;
  no hidden edge; no labour→combat training-rate cross-feed).
- **Failure = soft setback** (lose HP/time, maybe drop carried loot / take an injury to rest off) — **never
  lose levels/gear/permanent progress.**
- **Mobs are grounded** — boars, wolves, monkeys, bandits, ronin, smugglers, etc. **NO belief-creatures in
  grindable spawn tables**; any "yokai" is a misread human/animal surfaced through the optional rumour quests.
- **Martial scale hard-capped** — a small retinue, never a standing army.

## F. Antagonists, story & endgame
- **ONE ANTAGONIST PER TIER** (Estate / Village / Region / Castle-town / Edo), escalating, grounded, each
  **revealed incrementally**. (Design pass repurposes the world-expansion cast — Magobei / Hanzaki /
  Kuroiwa / Rival House **Tomita** / an Edo factor — across the tiers; **at most a light optional
  connective thread**; decide the racket's residual role.) **Estate decline = a simpler debt/misfortune**,
  NOT conspiracy-linked.
- **Two rival samurai houses** contest the region (LOCKED lineages): **Rival House Tomita** (head **Sōzaemon**,
  heir **Kageyuki**, agent **Yasubei**; contests on money) + **Rival House Akagi** (head **Gennai**; contests on
  honour/precedence). These names are now **confirmed** (formerly flagged for sign-off).
- **Generational succession beat:** aging **Lord Munenori**'s decline → heir **Naoyuki** comes into his own
  (MC as right-hand); secure the house's future across a generation. The **Naoyuki rival→brotherhood** in-house
  arc is now **LOCKED** (formerly author-elaborated / flagged).
- **Endgame STORY centers on the house's POWER** (office / debt-rescue / takeover / the national
  *banzuke* ranking) — **trade is OUT of the finale spine** (a supporting thread only).
- **Edo finale = a national multi-pillar *banzuke* ranking of the HOUSE** on all four pillars (D-010 ceiling
  kept). **LOCKED presentation:** a popular ***mitate*/parody broadsheet** (not an official register) using
  **sumo-rank vocabulary** (Maegashira/Komusubi for the attainable band; Ōzeki/Yokozuna for the sealed top) —
  now **confirmed** (formerly flagged for sign-off).
- **One authored ending** (house restored & ranked) + **post-game free-play** (no reset); branches are in
  *how* you got there (allegiance / takeover route), not separate endings.
- **Justice = partial:** the *osso* over-the-head petition (historically deadly) — an **ally / *gimin*-
  martyr NPC bears the risk**; reachable culprits answer; the truly powerful largely escape.
- **Personal side-mysteries (lost-child + origin/family) RESOLVE at the Region tier (T2)**; T3/T4 carry the
  house's power rise. **Lost-child (Tama)** truth = a **girl who ran** from a debt-sale (the villagers'
  mistaken-identity legend); the MC is **not** her. The grown lost-child is named **Otsuru** (LOCKED — name now
  **confirmed**; the substance "a girl who ran" unchanged). **Amnesia** = a mundane mountain accident (flood/
  rockfall hit Tahei's porter caravan on the pass; head trauma + near-drowning; found at the weir).
- **Relationships = narrative-only** (Sayo, Ohana, found-family); no dating-sim.
- **Tone:** grounded, warm, bittersweet; dark beats handled **off-screen**.

## G. Core loop, systems & content discipline
- **Core loop = the MC's OWN actions:** combat, skills, jobs, crafting (classic RPG). Meta (Influence/
  tiers/ranking) sits above, fed by his grind. **No people-management sim** (building/recruiting/teaching
  are flavour/light, not a minigame).
- **Quests:** open-ended, **NON-hand-holdy** (a suggestion + a story you find out in the world — never an
  A→B→C waypoint list); **fewer checklists**; the dominant minute-to-minute behaviour is the incremental
  grind ("the hero gets better at what he does"). **Per-region/tier side-quest lists** (lean).
- **FRACTAL INCREMENTALITY:** every new zone/area/faction/skill/rumour is itself incremental — arrive
  minimal, slowly learn it, never overload; **everything unlocks.**
- **LEAN / high-impact:** no fluff, no half-assed features; grinding/slow growth is core, not filler. When
  scope balloons, **split into "immediate" vs a parked "later"** bucket (e.g. §1.7 → §1.7 + §1.7.1) —
  park, don't delete.
- **Currencies:** **koku (rice)** base + **coin (mon)** trade + the **4 Influence pillars**; other resources
  (wood, fish, materials) feed crafting.
- **Auto-producers:** **limited / late-game only** (early game is active grind; idle helpers are a later
  convenience). Consistent with **active-only, no offline progress**.
- **Energy/stamina:** a **soft** stamina/satiety meter (slows, never hard-blocks; rest & eat to refill;
  paces the day) — no hard timer-wall.
- **Stats:** a **rich attribute system** (STR/AGI/INT/SPD/luck-style, deep interactions) + per-skill levels
  + gear.
- **Crafting:** **hybrid** — simple recipes early; the **component/quality** system unlocks later.
- **Skills/jobs:** start **lean** (farming, foraging, woodcutting, fishing, smithing, cooking; 2–3 weapon
  lines + conditioning); **more unlock as you climb tiers/regions.**
- **Marriage / adoption-into-higher-status:** a **REAL, lean late-game (T3/T4) alliance/status lever** that
  lifts Standing & Office / Name & Honour (and is a castle-town takeover route) — **NOT** a people-
  management / relationship sim.

## H. Time, presentation & tech
- **Time:** an **abstract clock advanced by active play** (days/seasons drive harvest/weather/festivals +
  the seasonal Influence results). **ACTIVE-ONLY — NO offline progress.**
- **Art:** **text + emoji + CSS** (woodblock palette; kanji season tags, colour-coded rarities, flourishes).
- **Numbers:** large values use **abbreviated K/M/B** formatting (not scientific, not myriad units);
  magnitudes kept human-scaled.
- **Romanization:** **macrons (proper Hepburn) project-wide (LOCKED)** — e.g. Tōkichi, Yagōemon, Jūbei,
  *kyō-masu*; no plain-ASCII slips.
- **UI shell:** **multi-screen navigation with screens/nav PROGRESSIVELY REVEALED** (appears single-screen
  early; more screens unlock as you progress). **Responsive (desktop + mobile), NOT hover-dependent.**
- **Cold open:** wake in the **kura** — one verb + persistent event log, short recovery, then first labour;
  panels reveal as earned.
- **Accessibility:** solid basics (scalable text, colourblind-safe cues, keyboard + touch, pause).
- **Audio:** light ambient beds + UI/event SFX + mute toggle.
- **Tech:** **Vite + TypeScript + Vitest**; **pure-core** (no DOM) + thin DOM renderer; **one seeded RNG**;
  static build for **itch.io**.
- **Save:** **IndexedDB** (robust, durable, static-friendly — no backend) + base64 **export/import** to
  file; **single autosave**; versioned **minimal-state** (recompute derived on load); seeded RNG persisted.
- **Difficulty:** one carefully-tuned difficulty (no modes). **Launch:** free / pay-what-you-want on itch.io.
