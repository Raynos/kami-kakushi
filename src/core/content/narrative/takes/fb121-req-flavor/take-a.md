## prose req-flavor

### rake-the-spill

{elder} says nothing of the spilled rice, only that the man raking it does not stop.

### take-to-the-fields

The paddy women no longer point out which rows are his; he has learned them.

### a-porters-back

At the gate the porters shift a strap for him without being asked, as they do among their own.

### learn-the-woodlot

{smith} hears the axe-fall from the smithy and no longer comes out to correct it.

### walk-the-hills

The kitchen stops picking through his basket before cooking from it.

### keep-the-stores-moving

{elder}'s tally sticks move faster on the days the new man carries.

### face-the-wolf

He is pulled from the grain-store bloodied, and the talk in the house that night is that he stood.

### clear-the-kura-rats

{steward} finds no droppings in the kura and asks {elder}, once, who saw to it.

### drive-off-the-thieves

The monkeys take their screaming elsewhere, and the paddy edge goes quiet again.

### keep-the-rows

The rows come in even though he wears a blade to work them now, and the old hands note it.

### timber-for-the-palisade

Split stakes pile by the gate faster than {drillmaster} can set them.

### the-stores-know-you

New porters are told to watch how he stacks; nobody remembers deciding that.

### mend-your-own-blade

{smith} turns the mended edge to the light and hands it back without a word of correction.

### a-hundred-mon-held

The house learns he keeps coin of his own, strung and counted, spent on nothing foolish.

### past-the-danger-line

He comes back from past the charcoal-burners' marker so often the kitchen stops waiting up.

### the-paddies-still-first

Whatever else he is now, the paddies get his mornings first, and {elder} sees that they do.

### a-made-corner

There is bedding in the north corner, bought with his own coin, and the maids sweep around it now.

### the-ledgers-agree

The count in the book and the count on the kura floor agree, and it is his carrying between them.

### three-hundred-mon-steady

His coin-string thickens and stays; {steward} hears of it and is not surprised.

### coin-into-the-works

His own coin goes into the kura-works, and the house understands it was never leaving.

### the-fields-run-on-you

The planting order goes out under his eye now, and {elder} lets it.

### the-woodlot-runs-on-you

{smith} banks the smithy fire against wood he no longer has to ask for.

### a-granary-against-winter

Rice against winter sits banked in the granary, and {lord} is told whose hands filled it.
