## prose req-flavor

### rake-the-spill

By dawn the spilled rice lies in even rows across the courtyard, and the rake's teeth are worn bright.

### take-to-the-fields

The paddy mud has learned the shape of his feet; the rows run straighter where he has passed.

### a-porters-back

A track shows in the forecourt stones now, cart to gate, the width of one man's stride.

### learn-the-woodlot

The axe haft darkens where his grip has settled, and the stumps at the woodlot edge stand cut clean.

### walk-the-hills

The near hills give up bracken and mushrooms without a search; the basket comes home heavy before noon.

### keep-the-stores-moving

Bales pass the gate and none sit long; the storehouse floor shows bare wood where sacks once rotted.

### face-the-wolf

There is blood on the grain-store threshold, most of it his, and the rice behind the door untouched.

### clear-the-kura-rats

The kura goes quiet at night; only shed gray fur in the corners says the rats were ever there.

### drive-off-the-thieves

The paddy edges keep their ears of rice again, and nothing chatters from the trees at dusk.

### keep-the-rows

A spear leans in the mud at the field's edge, and still the rows come up green and even.

### timber-for-the-palisade

Fresh-cut stakes stand seasoning in the yard, enough to close the gaps where the fence had rotted.

### the-stores-know-you

Every bale in the kura sits where a hand can find it in the dark; dust lies only where nothing moves.

### mend-your-own-blade

The blade carries a new-hammered edge over its old nicks, oiled and wrapped by the hand that dulled it.

### a-hundred-mon-held

Under a loose board waits a full string of copper, tied off, heavier than the month before.

### past-the-danger-line

His footprints go on past the shrine rope where the other baskets turn back, and they come home full.

### the-paddies-still-first

The deep hills can wait; the paddies are weeded first, and the water sits level from bund to bund.

### a-made-corner

New-stuffed bedding lies folded in a corner behind the kitchen, the first thing under this roof that is his.

### the-ledgers-agree

The tallies on the kura door and the bales inside come to the same count, season after season.

### three-hundred-mon-steady

The strings of copper in his box no longer fit one hand; they sit undisturbed, gathering weight.

### coin-into-the-works

New timber and fresh plaster stand in the kura works, paid from a labourer's own string of coin.

### the-fields-run-on-you

The paddies flood and drain to his reckoning now; the water follows the ditches he keeps.

### the-woodlot-runs-on-you

The woodpile stands stacked past the eaves before first frost, split to the size the stoves like.

### a-granary-against-winter

Rice sits banked to the joists in the granary, sealed against whatever snow the year intends.
