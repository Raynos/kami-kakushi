## scene-def nengu-autumn-frame
trigger: season-exit autumn
once: true
voice: narrator

> Autumn thins to its last week, and the board is lit at an hour that is not for
> hands. The household stands along the walls. You are sent for, to carry a lamp
> and hold it steady — light to read a reckoning by, and no more part in it than
> that.

> The day-book lies open. Genemon reads what the land owes against what the store
> holds, and the two do not meet. He does not say by how much. In this house the
> figure is never spoken; it is only stood in front of, the way the room is
> standing in front of it now.

> Above the book, where the lamp does not quite reach, Chiyo does the other
> arithmetic — the one that is never entered.

Chiyo: "The painted screen from the far room. It goes to the town, quietly, and
not through the valley. The room keeps its door shut after. No one is shown a
bare wall."

Chiyo: "If the screen sells well, the gate is painted before the New Year. If it
sells poorly, the gate is painted still, and the far room gives up its second
screen for it."

> No one asks the hired man what a screen is worth, or by what road such things
> leave a house, or how a year's due is paid out of the walls of one's own quiet
> rooms. You hold the lamp. You are learning the house's true size by what it
> will let itself be seen without.

Chiyo: "Enter it met. What we cannot pay in rice we will pay in screens, and no
one at the gate will know the difference."

> Genemon dips his brush and enters one line — the reckoning met, though you
> never hear with what. The book closes. The lamps are taken up. Autumn is let go
> with no word raised in the whole house: the silence the house has just paid for.
