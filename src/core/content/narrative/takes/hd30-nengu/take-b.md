## scene-def nengu-autumn-frame
trigger: season-exit autumn
once: true
voice: narrator

<!--#the-board-the-year-s-turn-->
> The board, the year's turn. Genemon foots the last column of the season by
> lamplight and reads it down aloud — item, count, condition — the whole house
> standing to hear its own account. You stand at the edge of it. Nobody set you
> there. Nobody sends you off.

<!--#autumn-rice-in-weighed-under-the-->
Genemon: "Autumn rice: in, weighed, under the store roof. Woodlot: cut and
stacked for winter, dry. Weir screens: three mended, one owed downstream against
the spring. The reclaimed rows bore — entered to the house, not to any wage."

<!--#behind-the-board-a-paler-square-->
> Behind the board a paler square marks the wall where a screen used to hang.
> Nobody looks at it. Nobody names what met the difference. The house does not
> discuss its arithmetic; it settles it in rooms you are not shown.

<!--#and-the-nengu-the-year-s-->
Genemon: "And the nengu. The year's tax against the year's yield. Paid. The store
stands level after it — level, not over. That closes the season."

<!--#he-names-the-tax-and-does-->
> He names the tax and does not count it — the one line at this board he never
> reads as a figure. The house carries it wordless, and every man standing feels
> the size of it anyway.

<!--#the-book-is-the-better-for-->
Genemon: "The book is the better for a hand on it this year. The store, the
screens, the rows — held or gained, each entered. The hand that did it carries no
name yet. The account holds a mark as well as it holds a name."

<!--#he-dips-the-brush-enters-the-->
> He dips the brush, enters the season closed, and squares the day-book with the
> lamp. The year is reckoned. You are in its book — the season's wages set against
> a mark where a name has not been written — and you are still at its edge.
