## prose cold-open

### lede

The river does not ask. It takes your coat, your breath, the order of
things, and it is still not done. Far above, thinned by the water, there
is light.

### cta

Reach for it

## scene dream
voice: narrator

> Water, all of it, and the cold has a weight and the weight has a
> direction. Things come loose and go past you — a knot pulled tight in bad
> light, a road going soft in grey rain — near enough to touch and already
> leaving. Above, thinned by the water, a voice is calling something. Two
> beats of sound that could be a name, could be men on a bank. The current
> takes it before it lands.

### decide · You have one reach left. Where does it go?

#### dream-dwell · After the voice
say: "Say it again."

> You turn down toward the sound and the surface goes farther off. Nothing
> lands. But the reaching stays in you, the way a knot stays in a pocket
> for the fingers to work.

stat: +int -spd
perk: The Inward Turn — A mind that deepens by dwelling, at the price of a
  slower body.

#### dream-shake · For the light
say: "Up. Up is enough."

> You let the voice go and the water gets simple: cold, weight, one pale
> seam above. From now on, the first thing you find in any room is the
> door.

stat: +spd -int
perk: The Clear Room — Senses sharpened to the way out — quick where thought is
  thin.

#### dream-hands · For anything solid
say: "Give me something to hold."

> Something rakes your palm — wood, slick, real — and the hand closes
> without asking you. It is a grip learned hauling loads you no longer
> remember, and it holds.

stat: +str -luck
perk: The Porter's Hands — Hands that remember the work before the head does.
