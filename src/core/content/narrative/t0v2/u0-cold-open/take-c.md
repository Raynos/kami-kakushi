## prose cold-open

### wake

Cold, and the sound of it, and then hands. They take him by the collar
and the belt and haul him over the weir frame the way men haul a snagged
log — by the heavy end, swearing at the weight. Somewhere above him a
voice says the screen is torn under him. Nobody asks his name. There is
a screen to mend.

### grounding

The bank, face up. The men who pulled him out have gone back into the
water; he can hear them working, and the river working against them. On
the far bank an old man counts the torn screens with a stick — once,
again — and goes back up his path without crossing. Where the bank
turns, a stone jizō stands no taller than a child, millet at its feet
still dry. One of the hands looks over at him, touches the charm at his
own neck, and says, to nobody, "Given back." It is not said to him.

### carried

They carry him up when the screens are mended — not before — because a
man with a medicine box, passing on his rounds, put two fingers to his
throat and said he would keep. Up through a gate that needs paint,
across a court too big for the quiet in it, to a lean-to that smells of
vinegar and smoke. He is asleep before they set him down.

### dayBook

One man, name unknown. Court raked; water hauled, six loads. Fed twice.

## scene soan
speaker: soan

> The lean-to surgery off the outer court: a mat, a brazier, a shelf of
> stoppered jars, a ledger on a low desk. Three days have gone
> somewhere. Sōan turns the man's hands palm-up in the light and looks
> at them longer than a doctor needs to.

Sōan: "Awake. Can you follow my finger? Good. Then drink what is in the
black bowl. All of it."

> He writes in the ledger — three lines, unhurried. When the door
> slides, he closes it. The ledger, not the door.

### ask r0-where · "Where is this?"

Sōan: "The Kurosawa house — what is kept of it. The weir you were
caught in is theirs to keep and another man's to own; that is the kind
of house it is. Does your head ache in the light? Then stay out of the
light."

### ask r0-pulled · "Who pulled me out?"

Sōan: "Hands sent down to mend the screens. You were tearing a leased
screen with your back — that is what got you noticed. They finished the
mending before they carried you up. Can you sit unaided? Then sit when
you eat."

### ask r0-body · "What happened to me?"

Sōan: "The river happened; what came before it, you would know better
than the water. A split scalp, a shoulder I took gravel out of, a fever
that has burned off. Your hands —"

> He stops there, the way a man steps around a hole he has already
> measured.

Sōan: "Do they hurt, the hands? No? Then work them, a little, every
day."

### ask r0-kami · "One of the men said the river gave me back."

Sōan: "They will say a kami hid you away and tired of you; by winter
there will be a story with your face in it. Let them have it. Most of
them have someone the river kept. Did you swallow much of the water?
Then honey in the morning, until I say stop."

### decide · One question is left. It costs more than the stitches did.

#### r0-name · "What is my name?"

> Sōan sets down the brush.

Sōan: "Nothing came out of the river with you but you. No pack, no
papers — and in three days nobody has come down either bank asking
after a man. Do you remember it wrong, or not at all?"

> Not at all. He knows it the way he knows the ceiling is above him:
> without being able to say how.

Sōan: "Then until someone asks after you, you are a man without one.
The house will write you down as it finds you; the house writes
everything down. Sleep now. Sleep is the whole of today's work."

> At the door O-Hisa has the broth, and has stood there too long to
> pretend she did not hear. She sets the bowl by the mat.

O-Hisa: "If it stays down — well."

> She goes. What she heard goes with her, to the kitchen, where
> everything goes.

Genemon: "You are standing. Good. The court wants raking before the
gate opens; the trough wants six loads from the far well — the near one
