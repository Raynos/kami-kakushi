# U0 cold open (R0) — verdict

Spec: the weir rescue → Sōan's examination → the FORCED name-question
beat (speaker flip `You:` → `Nameless:`, witnessed) → first verbs
(rake, haul water) → the day-book line *"one man, name unknown."*
Sōan answers the name question (04-cast: "the label flip is his
scene").

## Take A — "the ledger" (maximal restraint)

**What works.** The most disciplined take and the best-staged. The flip
and the day-book entry are fused into one beat — Genemon writes and
reads the entry aloud, single-sourced by `@`-reuse ("the spoken entry
IS the written entry"), so the spec's two central artifacts cause each
other. Sōan's main-scene rhythm is exactly his cast shape ("Do you
know the year?" → "Drink this" — a question, then a prescription); the
callus press and the ledger closed on his thumb carry the closed-ledger
seed without a word; "An'ei nine" is period-correct (1780). Genemon is
item-count-condition throughout ("Name, age, where from. In that
order.") and answers surprise by writing — cast-true. The MC's question
visibly costs him (the long silence before "What name did I give?"),
and post-flip lines render under `Nameless:` in-unit, so the player
actually sees the new label speak. Kernel #7 lands in prose, not
narration-about-it ("The kitchen does not thank the water. It uses
it."). Rule 7 holds: knot/work/count is an identity fork, not a
dutiful→selfish dial, and the only warmth (+1 sōan) rides the porter
clue, not virtue. The jizō rice, the carrier's hitch Sōan won't write
down while watched, "Wage: meals" — every seed is spec'd canon.

**Violations found.**

- **Law 3 (rewards are things — "never a mind/body chiasmus"):** the
  r0-knot perk description — *"a knot the hands kept when the head kept
  nothing"* — is the forbidden pattern by name. Redline (below).
- **Borderline, not a violation:** *"The Well Yoke — shoulders that
  answer before they are asked"* leans poetic-filler; the object still
  carries it.
- **Continuity wobble:** wake says "three days," Sōan says "Three days
  I have had nothing to call you," the day-book says "Fed, four days."
  Day-4 arithmetic can work; make it deliberate or align.

## Take B — "the held breath" (warmth priced)

**What works.** The most human take. O-Hisa's shape is perfect twice
over — *"If there's folk somewhere waiting on him—"* is her
never-finish-a-hope construction, and "The bowl back. Washed." is the
best single answer to "what do I owe" in any take. The porter seeding
is elegant (the yoke's "groove that is already there"), the
closed-ledger beat ("It was open before you stirred") is sly, and
deferring Genemon to a window glimpse keeps R1's board meeting fresh.

**Violations found.**

- **Law 4 (the dream cadence — "the first dream is T0-R7 (canon)"):**
  B stages an actual `### dream` key at R0 — *"Grey rain. Behind you
  somebody calls a name, once, and you wake before you can turn."*
  That is a dream, keyed `dream`, seven rungs early. Hard canon breach;
  cutting it costs B its best origin seed.
- **Law 1 + §0.5.2 (epigram saturation — the audit's slop pattern):**
  B runs ornament-hot for an unlicensed cast. Sōan: *"They have their
  reasons; most of them are graves"* — a maxim from a non-holder (the
  license is Munemasa/Kihei only, and it is fully spent). Narrator:
  *"it is not pay; it is trust with a number on it"*; *"You are an
  entry now. It is more than you were this morning."* Each is good
  alone; together the take reads wittier than the house style allows.
- **Scope:** `coinReveal` is R6 material staged inside a R0 unit
  (acknowledged in-file, but it pads the unit past its spec).
- **Wiring debt:** O-Hisa speaks (the flip's witness!) but is not an
  NpcId — the take's central staging choice depends on registry work
  it can only annotate.

## Take C — "the weather" (kernel #7 forward)

**What works.** The best weir sequence written: the rescuers finish
mending the screen before they carry him up, Matsuzō counts damage
from his own bank and leaves, *"Nobody asks his name. There is a
screen to mend."* Kernel #7 as staging, not statement. Sōan's
question→prescription rhythm is executed with total fidelity — all
four asks end in it ("Does your head ache in the light? Then stay out
of the light.") — the strongest voice work on any single character in
the wave. O-Hisa's *"If it stays down — well."* is her exact owned
construction.

**Violations found.**

- **Law 2 (shape ownership — "no verbal gesture belongs to more than
  one character"):** Genemon's orders — *"The court wants raking
  before the gate opens; the trough wants six loads"* — are
  Denshichi's assigned signature (orders as facts about the work:
  *"the wall wants restacking before noon"*), used twice in one line.
  Genemon speaks in item, count, condition; this line hands his mouth
  another man's shape.
- **Kernel #6 / taste 9 (a rung-up motivates each unlock; the player
  performs it):** the first verbs are NARRATED, not played — *"He
  rakes. … The trough takes its six loads."* The earning happens in a
  cutscene; the player's first verbs are pre-performed by prose. A and
  B both fire per-action prose off the player's own act; C would need
  structural rework, not a line edit.
- **Register break:** C narrates in third person ("he," "him") around
  a `You:`/`Nameless:` speaker-label system built on second-person
  address. Deliberate as stance, but incoherent on the actual screen —
  and the flip has no on-screen payoff: the MC never speaks again
  in-unit, so no `Nameless:` line is ever seen in R0.
- **Mild ventriloquism-adjacent:** Sōan's *"the house writes
  everything down"* states the game's thesis a touch too squarely.

## THE PICK — Take A

A is the recommended default. It is the only take with full spec
coverage and zero structural violations: the flip is witnessed by two
named men and pays off on screen (post-flip `Nameless:` lines inside
the unit), the day-book line is dramatized as the flip's direct
consequence, the first verbs fire as player actions with their own
prose, and the restraint law is kept without going bloodless. B's
warmth is real but it breaks locked dream canon and runs the epigram
budget hot; C's weir opening and Sōan rhythm are the wave's best craft,
but it mis-assigns an owned sentence shape, narrates the player's
first verbs out from under them, and fights the label system its own
beat depends on.

**Salvage worth stealing into A at the wiring pass (optional, small):**
C's question→prescription discipline for A's three ask-responses (at
least r0-where), and B's "The bowl back. Washed." register if an
O-Hisa touch ever enters this unit.

## Redlines on Take A before wiring

1. **REQUIRED — law 3:** rewrite the r0-knot perk description. *"a
   knot the hands kept when the head kept nothing"* is a mind/body
   chiasmus, forbidden by name. Name the thing plainly, e.g. *"The
   Carrier's Hitch — fast, one-handed, holds wet."*
2. **REQUIRED — continuity:** reconcile the day count (wake "three
   days" / Sōan "three days" / day-book "Fed, four days"). If the
   scene is day four, seed it; otherwise write "Fed, three days."
3. **Suggested:** tighten *"The Well Yoke — shoulders that answer
   before they are asked"* toward the concrete object (borderline
   poetic filler under law 3).
4. **Flag for the human, not a defect:** the day-book's *"Taken at the
   weir"* double-reads against kami-kakushi's "taken." As plain
   steward's register it is period-true and Genemon-true (he never
   reaches for metaphor); if the echo is unwanted, "Pulled out at the
   weir" kills it.
