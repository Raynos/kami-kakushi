## rung R1 · rung-r1
speaker: genemon
voice: steward
motivates: room-gate-forecourt, room-home-paddies, verb-farm, verb-haul,
  readout-clock, readout-stamina, panel-rung-ladder

> Rain since midday. At the board the day-book lies open with a cloth
> under it against the damp. Two places at the kitchen threshold have no
> bowls tonight.

> Genemon does not look up until the page is finished.

Genemon: "Two hands went down to the lowlands on the third day. Winter
wages are coin there. Their meals came off the book this morning."

Genemon: "That leaves the yard short and you fed. The house will keep the
second thing as long as you mend the first."

Genemon: "Terms. Work from first light to last. Two meals at the
threshold, morning and dusk. The mat in the woodshed is yours. No coin —
the house's coin has older claims until autumn."

> He turns the book so the written page faces the kitchen.

Genemon: "O-Hisa. You heard the terms."

O-Hisa: "I heard them."

Shinnosuke: "Is he staying? Does he get a name if he stays?"

> Nobody answers him. That is the house's answer to most of his
> questions.

### ask r1-lowlands · "The two who left — why?"

Genemon: "Coin. A winter down there is coin in the hand by New Year.
Here it is meals, and a roof that owes its own repairs. I entered no
fault against their names."

### ask r1-coin · "And coin, here?"
after: r1-lowlands

Genemon: "Autumn's question. The house answers its older ones first.
You'll see the shape of it by then without my drawing it."

### decide · The steward waits. The terms sit on the board between you.

#### r1-hands · "Hold out your hands: 'They can earn the meals.'"

Genemon: "Meals are by the day here. Bring the day; I'll enter it."

memory: genemon +1 (plain-dealer)
flags: r1-offered-hands

#### r1-count · "Ask it plain: 'Two left. What did two do?'"

Genemon: "The mowing, the hauling, the gate at dusk. More than one man's
day — do what fits in one, and I will write what doesn't."

memory: genemon +1 (asked-the-count)
flags: r1-asked-count

#### r1-silence · "Say nothing. The terms were not a question."

Genemon: "I'll write that you asked nothing."

flags: r1-kept-silent

> Genemon enters the terms in the hand he uses for entries that may be
> read back someday, and closes the cloth over the book.

Genemon: "Day-hand, name unknown. The work goes in the same column
either way."

> At the threshold there is a bowl already filled, and it was filled
> before the terms were done.

O-Hisa: "If the rice holds— well. There's tonight."

> Above the court, the veranda. Someone has been standing there long
> enough that no one saw him arrive.

Naoyuki: "Steward. The house is grateful for—"

> He does not finish it.

Naoyuki: "Two gone, one kept. Enter it plainly, and don't call it luck."

> He is gone before the steward can answer. Genemon reads the entry back
> to himself, finds it already plain, and lets it stand.

> In the day-book, under the day: day-hand, one. Name unknown.
