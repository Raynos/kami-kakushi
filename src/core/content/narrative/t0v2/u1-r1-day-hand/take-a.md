# U1 · R1 — the day-hand · take A ("the ledger")

## rung R1 · rung-r1
speaker: genemon
voice: steward
motivates: room-gate-forecourt, room-home-paddies, verb-farm, verb-haul,
  readout-clock, readout-stamina, panel-rung-ladder

> Before the morning meal you are called to the board. The steward's papers
> share the kitchen's end of it; the day-book lies open at today. Genemon
> does not look up until the page is ready.

Genemon: "Two hands quit for the lowlands inside the week. Their rows do not
know it yet. Were they still here, you would not be. They are not."

Genemon: "So. The gate and the forecourt at first light. The home paddies —
sown, weeded, hauled in. Meals, the woodshed, rice at the season if the work
stands. Kept by the day, counted by the day."

Genemon: "Work to the end of your strength and stop there. A hand laid up in
the sickroom is a cost, not a hand."

Genemon: "What you were before the weir is not entered here. What you do
from the gate out is."

Genemon: "A kept hand is entered before a witness. Stand there."

> Kihei, crossing from watch-change, is stopped at the door and told the
> terms in one line. He stands for it. When the brush lifts he looks at you
> once, the way he looks at the gate's hinges, and goes.

> In the day-book, under the weir line, the new entry: one hand, kept by the
> day. It is the whole of what the house knows of you. This week, it is
> enough.

> On the veranda above the board, the second son has stopped mid-step, a
> paper in one hand, listening without seeming to.

Naoyuki (steward): "Genemon. The house is glad of—"

> He stops. He leaves the rest where it stands.

Naoyuki (steward): "Two men left us. Do not be a third."

### ask r1-lowlands · "What took the two hands?"

Genemon: "Wages. The lowland terraces pay coin by the week. No mystery in
it; men follow coin. The house enters the loss and goes on."

### ask r1-veranda · "The man on the veranda."

Genemon: "Naoyuki-sama. The second son. His terms are the house's business,
not a day-hand's."

### decide · The terms are stated. Answer them.

#### r1-dutiful · "Show me where the two left off."

Genemon: "The near paddy, half-weeded. The gate at first light. By evening
the day-book will know what it kept."

memory: genemon +1 (dutiful)
flags: r1-dutiful

#### r1-practical · "Meals and a mat now. What at season's end?"

Genemon: "Rice, measured, at the season's close — if the work stands. A man
who asks his terms tends to keep them. Entered."

memory: genemon +0
flags: r1-practical

#### r1-ambitious · "And past a day-hand?"

Naoyuki (steward): "Whatever the house can pay for. Some years, less than
that."
