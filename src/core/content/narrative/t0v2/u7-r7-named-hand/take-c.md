## rung R7 · rung-r7
speaker: genemon
voice: steward
motivates: house-standing

> Evening, board-side, the lamp trimmed low. The household's sounds are
> elsewhere — dishes, the gate bar going down. Genemon has the day-book
> open to a fresh page and a brush wet, and he does not look up.

Genemon: "A year, come the tax. Three hundred days and some of wages,
entered against 'one man, name unknown.' The book will not carry that
into a second year."

Genemon: "You give me no name. The house keeps one for such cases."

> He writes it the way he enters charcoal: date, item, hand. Gonbei.

Genemon: "The house's name for you, then. Earn a better."

### ask r7-whose · "Whose name was it?"

Genemon: "Every hired man the house did not know better. Four in my
time. Two served out their seasons and went. One died in the west
field, a fever year. One ran, with a hand-axe and a sack of seed rice."

Genemon: "The book has all four under the one name. What each was worth
is in the entries."

### ask r7-better · "What earns a better?"

> Genemon looks at him a moment longer than usual. Then he writes
> something small in the margin, away from the entry, and does not say
> what.

Genemon: "That is the first thing you have asked me for since the weir."

Genemon: "Work the book can carry. Repairs that hold. A granary past
the winter's need. When enough of it is written, the house will know
what to call you."

### decide · The brush is still wet on the page.

#### r7-answer-name · "Gonbei, then."

Genemon: "Entered."

> He is back in his columns before the word is cold. The page dries.

memory: genemon +1 (square)

#### r7-had-name · "I had a name."

Genemon: "Then it is written somewhere, in someone's register. When you
find it, bring it to the board. An entry can be amended."

> He says it the way he says the price of oil. The page dries.

#### r7-say-nothing · "(Say nothing.)"

Genemon: "No objection, then."

> He adds a small line in the margin: given the house name; offered

## scene first-dream · THE FIRST DREAM
speaker: genemon
voice: steward
native: a once-only VN scene fired at the R7 sleep — the game's first
  dream (dream-cadence canon, 01-laws §0.5.4: recurrences from T1
  escalate, never verbatim). Unlabeled narrator voice throughout — no
  speaker prefix, no asks, no decide, no state changes; reduced-motion
  and typewriter contracts as per story scope; ends by waking into the
  `Gonbei:` label.

> A road, climbing under wet trees. There is weight across his
> shoulders, and his legs know the pitch of the slope without being
> told. It has been raining a long time — on the road, in the dream,
> both.

> Someone behind him, keeping pace, says a name. Not Gonbei. A short
> name, worn smooth with use, his the way the weight is his.

> He turns to answer it, and the road is not there. The whole slope is
> going down at once, unhurried, taking the road with it.

> Then water. The water is not against him. It is only going somewhere,
> and now he is going there too.

> The name follows him a little way over the water, getting smaller. He
> cannot keep hold of the sound of it any more than of the reeds.

> He wakes in the woodshed with the dark still on. Somewhere in the
> yard, early, a voice wants a hand for the water barrels and calls the
> name the book gave him.

> It is the only name that comes when called. He gets up.
