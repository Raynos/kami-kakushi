## rung R7 · rung-r7
speaker: genemon
motivates: house-standing

> The last of the tax carts went at first light. By evening the board is
> clear of the year, and the day-book is open at a new page, the old one
> ruled off behind it. Genemon does not look up until the ink is mixed.

Genemon: "Sit. The year: four seasons worked. Boarded at the woodshed. Kept
in meals to the second season, waged from the fifth month. One purse errand,
counted back to the mon."

> He turns the page sideways, to the column where a name goes.

Genemon: "A hand carried on the year's book is entered by name. The house
keeps one for the purpose. Every man hired to this yard has carried it."

> He writes it before he says it.

<!-- native: speaker-label flip — `Nameless:` → `Gonbei:` from this line

Genemon: "Gonbei. The house's name for you, then. Earn a better."

### ask r7-whose · "Whose was it?"

Genemon: "Nobody's. That is its use. Four men carried it in my time. Two
served their terms out. One ran. One we buried."

### ask r7-better · "A better name."

Genemon: "Not mine to write. This book keeps wages and stores. It has never
yet made a man, and I have kept it forty years."

### decide · The brush is still wet.

#### r7-answer · "Gonbei, then."

Genemon: "Answer to it. That is all it wants."

## dream R7 · the first dream

native: a rung-tail dream slot — the engine plays this after the R7 beat

  §0.5.4) this is the game's FIRST dream, the memory's metronome; the

  verbatim. No speaker labels inside the dream — narration only.

> The page, before the book shuts: the year ruled off; wages entered; one
> hand, written in by name.

> The woodshed. The mat, the bowl, the coat on its nail. He sleeps.

> A road, going up. He is under a load, and his legs know the road — where
> it narrows, where the water crosses it — the way legs know what a man has
> forgotten.

> Rain comes on. Somewhere behind him on the road a man calls a name, and
> he turns to answer, because it is his.

> The road is not under him. The water is loud. The name goes with the
> road.

> He wakes with his mouth already shaped around it. Nothing. The mat, the
> bowl, the coat on its nail.

> Down the yard, in the day-book, the house keeps a name for him.
