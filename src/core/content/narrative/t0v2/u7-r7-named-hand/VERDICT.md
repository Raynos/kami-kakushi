# VERDICT — U7 · R7 the named hand

The unit's four jobs, judged against: (1) the naming lands as bookkeeping,
the Nanashi-no-Gonbei idiom never spoken; (2) the R7 tier-up
(house-standing) gets a fictional cause — kernel #6, no grant-list; (3) THE
FIRST DREAM sets the metronome baseline — road/river, a slipping name,
vague-but-parseable, escalation room left; (4) the `Nameless:` → `Gonbei:`
flip is witnessed on screen. All three takes keep the idiom unspoken and
carry the spec line verbatim. None spends the ambiguity budget. None
morality-dials the decide.

## Take A — the ledger

What works: the tersest register of the three, and the opening audit of the
year ("Sit. The year: four seasons worked…") is Genemon's item-count-
condition shape done straight. The decide is rule-7-clean by design — the
blunt wage question is the one Genemon rates, and the warm answer carries
nothing. The morning-after log line ("Nobody in the yard remarks on it.")
is kernel #7 in one stroke, and the only rule-6 log texture in the wave.
The R0 echo is routed through `@`-reuse correctly.

Violations and misses:

- **Unlicensed epigrams — the take's own stance broken twice.** Genemon
  holds no epigram license (04-cast: Munemasa + Kihei only, fully spent),
  and his shape says he "has never in his life reached for a metaphor" —
  yet the take's two most quotable lines are maxim-shaped and his:
  *"This book keeps wages and stores. It has never yet made a man, and I
  have kept it forty years."* and *"A name is not a raise."* Both would
  survive in Munemasa's mouth; in Genemon's they are §0.5.2 leaks.
- *"Answer to it. That is all it wants."* — mild personification from the
  never-a-metaphor man. Small, but the shape law is one-voice-one-shape.
- **The tier-up is undramatized.** `motivates: house-standing` rides the
  metadata with zero scene grounding — the one take where the R7 unlock
  arrives as a spawn, not a need (kernel #6 application). The dream is
  spec-compliant but the tersest ("The road is not under him. The water is
  loud.") — parseable, but the thinnest baseline of the three.

## Take B — the held breath

What works: the strongest structural craft in the wave. The tier-up is
dramatized as Genemon's own NEED, in his own register — *"I have written
this house's losses for thirty-one years. I mean to write something else
before I am done"* — kernel #6 done exactly as the kernel's application
note asks, with the T5 succession root left unspoken underneath. The page
of four struck Gonbei lines quietly teaches "a closed life is a ruled
line," which is what makes T1's never-struck register land — seeding done
with furniture, not speeches. The R0 read-back before the name is retired
is the echo craft rule used with intent. The dream is the best of the
three at its actual job: every image has an authored answer (enumerated),
the WHAT is fully parseable (road, load, slope down, water, a voice
calling), and the staging note deliberately holds imagery back to leave
the metronome escalation room. And the flip is cashed on screen at the
loneliest possible moment — `Gonbei: "There was a road."` — a short
declarative, the MC's plainest-voice shape, said to nobody.

Violations:

- **The shape performed as a tic.** *"Item: your name, on the page. Count:
  a day-wage… Condition: watched…"* — the cast sheet gives Genemon
  item-count-condition as a REGISTER; speaking the literal column labels
  turns the palette into a performed quirk (the exact template-slop
  pattern the audit flagged — a sentence shape worn as a costume).
- **Ornament density.** Four figures in one unit — *"fine as sieved
  ash"*, *"the way a wall is there in a dark room"*, *"as if a chair had
  spoken"*, *"worn smooth, thrown easy"*. Three live in the dream, where a
  heightened register is defensible, but rule 1 prices ornament by rarity
  and this is the wave's densest take.
- *"Better is earned at the season's close, like everything on this
  book."* — maxim-adjacent; survives only because it is a ledger
  condition, not a general truth. Watch it at wiring.

## Take C — the weather

What works: the cleanest dialogue-side law compliance of the three — no
unlicensed epigram anywhere, and the best single cast-fidelity moment in
the wave (Genemon answering surprise by writing something down, twice, per
04-cast). *"Then it is written somewhere, in someone's register. When you
find it, bring it to the board. An entry can be amended."* is the wave's
best line — it pays T3's unstruck register invisibly, priced like oil. The
O-Yae beat (the name travels faster than he answers to it) is kernel #7 as
lived weather. The dream close — *"The water is not against him. It is
only going somewhere, and now he is going there too."* — is the
kernel-#1-softening (nobody's sin, a plain accident) rendered exactly.

Violations and misses:

- **The flip never lands on screen.** The staging keeps `Nameless:`
  through sleep and flips "at the first waking line after the dream" — but
  the unit contains NO MC speech line after waking. The witnessed flip
  (04-cast: witnessed, on screen — the unit's own payoff) is deferred out
  of the unit entirely.
- **Metadata contradicts the fiction.** The sleep and dream scenes carry
  `speaker: genemon / voice: steward` while the native note says
  "unlabeled narrator voice throughout" — compiled naively, the first
  dream attributes to Genemon. A wiring hazard, not prose, but real.
- The R0 echo (*"one man, name unknown"*) is quoted with no `@`-reuse
  routing note — the craft rule says deliberate repetition goes through
  the mechanism; A and B both flag it, C doesn't.
- The tier-up grounding is the thinnest that still counts: the standing
  criteria surface only inside an optional ask's answer, as a conditional
  ("when enough of it is written…"), never as anyone's need.
- Minor: *"The page dries."* is duplicated across two exclusive decide
  branches — the player never sees both, but it is accidental
  near-duplication by the craft rule's letter.

## THE PICK — take B

Take B is the only take that does all four of the unit's jobs, and its
faults are line-level and fixable, where A's and C's are structural. A is
the most restrained but breaks its own stance — two maxim-shaped lines in
an unlicensed mouth — and leaves the R7 unlock as a bare metadata grant,
which is the kernel #6 failure the wave exists to prevent. C's dialogue is
the cleanest prose in the wave, but the label flip — the rung's own
on-screen payoff — never fires inside the unit, and the dream carries
Genemon's speaker metadata: shipping C as-is ships a mis-attributed first
dream and an unwitnessed naming. B dramatizes the tier-up as a person's
need, seeds T1's register with furniture instead of speeches, ends the
dream with the MC's first line under his new name, and leaves the
metronome deliberate room to escalate. That is the take a canonical
version has to be.

### Required redlines before wiring (canon-blocking)

1. **Recast the spoken column labels.** *"Item: your name, on the page.
   Count: a day-wage, as before… Condition: watched…"* → plain Genemon
   speech carrying the same content in the same order without the labels,
   e.g. *"Your name, on the page. The wage as it stands, and the house's
   purse when it is sent out with you. And you are watched, as every name
   on this book is watched."* The shape is a register, never a costume.
2. **Cut one dream simile.** Drop *"fine as sieved ash"* (the most
   decorative); keep the wall-in-a-dark-room, which is doing parse work.
   Rule 1: ornament by rarity — three figures in one unit is the ceiling,
   and this take is over it.
3. **Steal C's line.** Fold *"An entry can be amended"* (or its exact
   sense) into B's `r7-mine` react — it is the wave's best T3 seed and it
   belongs in canon; B's guarded-look react can carry it without breaking
   the misread comment already attached there.

### Recommended (non-blocking)

4. Add A's morning-after log flavor line (*"The hands' column carries a
   new name this morning. Nobody in the yard remarks on it."*) as the
   unit's rule-6 texture — B is the only take without a log line, and
   kernel #7 wants the yard's indifference on record.
5. At wiring, hold *"Better is earned at the season's close, like
   everything on this book"* against the epigram line — if it ever reads
   as a maxim out of context, cut the first clause to *"At the season's
   close, like everything on this book."*
6. Confirm the `@`-reuse route for the R0 read-back binds against the U0
   cold-open key at migration (the comment is present; the binding is the
   craft rule's teeth).
