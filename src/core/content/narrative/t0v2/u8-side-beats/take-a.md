## side-beat sb-grove · shinnosuke-grove

speaker: shinnosuke
native: side-beat scene kind — fires on a bamboo-grove patrol between R4
  and R6, grove node open. The chosen flag routes one later kitchen line
  (O-Hisa) and one drill-yard line (Kihei).

> The yell comes from up the slope, past the cut-bamboo waymark. The
> heir's son stands with his back to it, and between the boy and the
> path sits the troop's big male, not moving, the way a thing sits when
> it has decided the ground is its own.

> A pole on stone, twice, and a shout. The male goes up into the green
> without hurry. The green closes.

Shinnosuke: "Did you see the size of it — is that the one that's been
  at the millet? Why did it move for you and not for me?"

Nameless: "It didn't move for either of us. Walk in front."

> He walks the boy down as far as the vegetable rows. Neither of them
> says the word grove.

### decide · The house did not see. What now?

#### sb-grove-report · "The house hears it from me tonight."

Kihei: "You saw, you said. That is a watch."

O-Hisa: "He asks you things because you're not the house. He won't
  now."

memory: kihei +1 (watchful)
flags: t0-grove-reported

#### sb-grove-cover · "I saw monkeys. Nothing else."

Kihei: "…Small prints by the waymark, going up. Mind what you don't
  see next time."

O-Hisa: "His sleeve was torn at the elbow. If the scare keeps him out
  of the green — well. It's mended."

memory: shinnosuke +1 (kept)
flags: t0-grove-covered

#### sb-grove-teach · "Next time you go, you go with me."

Kihei: "The heir's boy, schooled by a day-hand. Mind the order of
  things."

O-Hisa: "Someone is telling him true things. About time it was
  somebody."

memory: shinnosuke +1 (taught)
flags: t0-grove-taught

> The day-book, that evening: bamboo, two bundles. Nothing else.

## side-beat sb-bon · the-first-bon

speaker: ohisa
native: fires at the first Bon season the MC is on the books
  (six-season calendar); Iori's gate-lodging window open. The final
  sandal line is a next-morning tail (short scene or log line, the
  morning after the observance).

> Loads cross the forecourt all morning: trays, lamp-oil, cut flowers,
> a bundle of incense sticks O-Yae carries two-handed like a caught
> bird. Everyone is given something.

> The third load goes past before he asks it.

Nameless: "What do I carry?"

O-Hisa: "Not this. If a year from now — well. Not this."

> The house files toward the alcove corridor. The forecourt is his all
> day. For once, nobody crosses it.

> At dusk, from the weir path: O-Ume sets her lantern on the water and
> talks it out past the reeds, the way you'd see a guest to the road.

> At the gate, the monk Iori has his lodging and his one small fire.

Iori: "Sit. Two bowls, one mouth. I'll be gone before you learn my
  face."

> Through the corridor screen, past shoulders: a small tray set out for
> somebody, and straw sandals beside it, toes toward the gate.

> Morning. The trays are cleared, the lanterns down, the monk gone
> north. The sandals are new.

## side-beat sb-lease · the-lease-day

speaker: genemon
native: fires on the weir-lease due date in the house day-book; the MC
  present as labour. Matsuzō needs a NAMES entry.

> Matsuzō comes up from the water at mid-morning and stands in the
> forecourt like a man who would rather be standing in the river.

Genemon: "Weir lease, due today: one koku two to. Screens, forty, two
  seasons of wear, rats in the third bay. On the board is one koku."

> Nobody in the room moves. The one koku sits where anyone can count
> it.

Genemon: "The house asks a season's grace on the balance. Two to at
  harvest, and a to over for the waiting."

> Then the steward of the Kurosawa bows to the weir-keeper. It goes
> lower than a steward's bow has any business going, and it stays
> down.

Matsuzō: "The screens held all spring. Harvest, then."

> He looks at the river the whole time he says it, which is a
> kindness, because it means he is not looking at the bow.

> The rice goes down to the water on two backs, his one of them. In
> the day-book that evening: weir lease, one koku paid, two to owing.
> The bow is not entered.

## side-beat sb-dog · the-dog-that-stays

speaker: kihei
native: fires late in the orchard reclamation chain, den stages
  cleared. Fed branch: registers the yard dog + a recurring dog-rice
  cost drawn from the MC's own measure, and enables sb-dog-coda on the
  next new-moon night round. Driven branch routes one later paddy-edge
  flavor line: the dog dens at O-Ume's field margin and rice is left
  out for it; she would deny it.

> The pack has been gone two days. In the cleared rows one old dog
> stays — grey at the jaw, one ear, denned under a downed plum. It
> does not beg and it does not bare its teeth. It watches the work.

Kihei: "Pack ran. That one stayed. Your ground, your call."

### decide · The old dog will not leave.

#### sb-dog-drive · "It goes like the rest."

Kihei: "It'll stop at the boundary stones or it won't. Not the
  house's account."

flags: t0-dog-driven

#### sb-dog-feed · "It can have from my measure."

Genemon: "A dog, one. Fed from your rice, not the house's. Entered."

Kihei: "If it barks, it earns the rice."

flags: t0-dog-fed

#### sb-dog-kihei · "Drillmaster. Make it quick."

Kihei: "Buried at the far wall, then. Say nothing to the boy."

flags: t0-dog-ended

## side-beat sb-dog-coda · the-new-moon-bark

speaker: kihei
native: fires on the first new-moon night round carrying flag
  t0-dog-fed; binds to the hooded-lantern crossing already seeded in
  the Night rounds (answer authored: Toku, the packet, named at R5).

> New moon, and the round goes out into it. Rats in the store:
> cleared. The store settles. Then, at the yard's far edge, a lantern
> crosses with its light hooded, going upstream, unhurried, sure of
> the path.

> The rats said nothing. The marten, the time it came, said nothing.
> The dog barks once — once — at the lantern, and then is quiet.

> The lantern does not stop. Kihei does not turn his head. The dog
> watches it the whole way upstream, and nobody enters a bark in any
> book.

## side-beat sb-crest · the-crest-question

speaker: shinnosuke
native: fires at the kura after R3 (the grain-watch makes the
  seal-plate familiar). No answer surfaces here by design — the answer
  is authored (the old seat's crest; pays into T2's reveal).

> Sacks, up. The boy is on the kura steps, head back, looking at the
> seal-plate over the door the way he looks at the board.

Shinnosuke: "Why does the kura's crest have six petals when the
  gate's has five? Did we buy the kura off somebody — who sells a
  kura?"

Nameless: "I don't know."

Shinnosuke: "Nobody knows. Genemon wrote something in his book and
  didn't say. Grandmother told me the year the plate was last cleaned
  and nothing else. Kihei sent me up the wall for standing about."

> The sixth petal keeps its place over the door. Sacks, up.

Shinnosuke: "They don't tell you things either. Do you mind it? Would
  they tell you if you asked?"

Nameless: "I mind it less than you do."

> The boy waits for more. There isn't any. That, at least, he is used
> to.
