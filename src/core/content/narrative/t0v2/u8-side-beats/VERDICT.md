# U8 verdict — the five side-beats

## Take A — "the ledger" (maximal restraint)

The most law-compliant prose of the three. Genemon's voice is flawless
("Weir lease, due today: one koku two to. Screens, forty, two seasons
of wear, rats in the third bay." — item, count, condition, nothing
else). The declared ledger-silence motif (the day-book does NOT hold:
the boy / the bow / the bark) is real echo-craft — varied per
recurrence, never verbatim — and gives the unit a spine the others
lack. O-Hisa's Bon line — *"Not this. If a year from now — well. Not
this."* — is the best single line in the unit: her exact if-shape,
pricing the exclusion as temporary-if-earned (kernel #3) without ever
promising. The dog coda is the only take that dramatizes the spec's
"the ONLY one who barks": *"The rats said nothing. The marten, the
time it came, said nothing. The dog barks once."*

Violations:

- **POV drift (systemic).** A narrates the MC in third person —
  *"He walks the boy down as far as the vegetable rows"*, *"The
  forecourt is his all day"*, *"on two backs, his one of them"* —
  against the canon second-person narration (rung-beats.md: "your
  doing", "You cleared the stores"). Mechanical to fix, but pervasive.
- **The scare doesn't scare** (spec: "the boy, the monkeys, a scare").
  The danger is over in two narration lines (*"A pole on stone,
  twice, and a shout."*) and Shinnosuke shows zero fear — straight to
  stacked questions. In-voice, but the beat's premise is underplayed.
- **Invents an absolute petal count** — *"six petals when the gate's
  has five"*. Canon keeps it relative everywhere; a shipped number
  becomes canon T2's reveal must carry.
- **Reaction-menu symmetry.** Each grove branch resolves as one Kihei
  line + one O-Hisa line, immediately, in the same grid — reads as a
  reaction menu; kernel #7's world-keeps-living is better served by
  the delayed, staggered reads (take C's shape).
- **Adds canon, lightly:** *"Kihei does not turn his head"* at the
  hooded lantern implies Kihei knows and practices not-seeing.
  Defensible (from R3 the rounds are his and the MC's alone; R5's
  "nobody asks what it's for" is the same house habit) — kept in the
  pick, flagged for the human.

Rule 7: clean. Every grove branch gains someone different (report →
kihei, cover/teach → shinnosuke); the dog decide grades nothing at all.

## Take B — "the held breath" (grief close under the surface)

The most writerly take, with the unit's most beautiful single image
(O-Hisa's extra lantern lifted an inch, and *"whatever the wind was
going to be allowed to mean stays with her"*). Also the least
shippable.

Violations:

- **Rule 7 broken at the systems layer.** The grove decide's memory
  deltas grade one axis: report = `shinnosuke -1 (told-on)` is the
  ONLY negative in the unit while cover and teach both pay `+1`. The
  dutiful choice is the uniquely punished one — exactly the
  crackable dial rule 7 forbids. The narrative reads DO cross-cut
  (O-Hisa endorses the report, Kihei shrugs it off) — but they live
  in comments (next point), so the shipped mechanics are the dial.
- **Half the spec payload is HTML comments, not authored blocks.**
  ALL of the grove's O-Hisa/Kihei reads — the spec's core requirement
  ("each read differently by O-Hisa and Kihei") — and the ENTIRE
  fed-dog new-moon bark (the unit's key mystery link) are `<!-- -->`
  descriptions for migration. A comment describing prose is not
  prose; per this wave's rules the take ships what it authored.
- **Speaker label `You:`** — the label ladder says `Nameless:` from
  the forced beat until R7; all five beats sit pre-R7.
- **Says more than it knows** (law 1), repeatedly: *"That is what it
  costs."* · *"which is perhaps his way of saying he has seen the
  house's current move too, or perhaps is only an old man talking
  about water"* (glossing the subtext it just built) · *"It is the
  plain truth, plainly short, and he has never once been given it
  before."* — and the narrator quotes the cast sheet at the player:
  *"in item, count, and condition"*.
- **Epigrams handed to Genemon**, the no-metaphor man, no license:
  *"the kura keeps grain, not answers"* (reported) and *"the house
  does not keep what the house did not take on"*. Holders are
  Munemasa and Kihei, and the license is spent.
- **The "the way …" simile formula ~7 times** (door left unlocked /
  other boys read clouds / you once lay down in a woodshed / the way
  the lease day comes / …) — ornament not earned by rarity.
- **Muddled address:** *"Your grandfather's people paid early"* —
  said to the steward, whose grandfather is nobody in this story.
- **Wrong coin lane:** dog rice *"against your wage"* — the wage is
  the mon lane (R5+); dog rice is a kind-lane sink from the MC's
  measure.
- **Crest lands warm**, off-spec: *"You'd tell me if you knew. That's
  not the same as nobody."* flatters the MC; the spec's point is
  "nobody answers HIM either" — the mirror is shared silence, not
  consolation.

Salvage worth banking (not shipped): the lifted-lantern Bon image;
the kihei-end dog branch (*"He sends you out of sight first. That is
the whole of his opinion on the matter"*); *"Why does everyone here
know the same nothing?"*.

## Take C — "the weather" (kernel #7 forward)

The strongest dramatist of the three, and the best rule-7 design in
the unit. The dog decide is the exemplar: drive → `kihei +1
(thorough)`, feed → `genemon +1 (accountable)`, end → `kihei +1
(clear-eyed)` — the warm choice rewarded by the ledger-man for
accountability, the cold ones by the soldier, no branch purely
punished, every consequence from an unexpected quarter. The grove's
cover-branch cost — *"Your count was short." … From that day he
checks your counts.* — is rule 7 played on the tier's real currency
(trust in numbers). O-Hisa's reads arrive through MENDING in all
three branches (an empty doorstep, then smaller stitches; a boiled
egg nobody claims) — a behavior, not a tic, exactly what the cast
sheet asks of her. The lease day's dignity payment is the best
rendering: the bow *"measured now, got right the first time because
he will not let it be seen twice"* — and the grace converted into
the MC's mending evenings makes the weir-reeds grind story-caused
(TST3) and lands kernel #3/#7 in two sentences: *"It is the first
time you have been worth a number. The number is small."* The crest
beat is the only one that dramatizes the mirror as a transaction:
the MC pays a question at the board and receives the boy's answer
"in a different hand" (*"The kura wants its hinges oiled before the
rains. Take the small ladder."*), then the question comes back
priced at Yohei's stall — the village line crossed, kernel #7 as
weather.

Violations:

- **Spends a banked line.** Matsuzō, at the gate: *"You're the one
  the river left. It was quiet that night. Quietest all year."* —
  t0.md banks "the river was quiet that night" for the origin tier
  (T3's first honest clue). Speaking it in T0 cashes the bank two
  tiers early. MUST fix (redline 2).
- **The fed dog's price drifts off the player.** The MC says *"I'll
  feed it from my share"*; Genemon enters *"a handful, mornings, from
  the sweepings — entered against rats"* — the house absorbs what the
  spec prices to the player ("warmth priced in rice"; the economics
  sheet lists dog rice under the MC's recurring sinks). MUST fix
  (redline 3).
- **Invents an absolute petal count** — *"Eight on the gate. Nine on
  the storehouse."* Same defect as A; keep it relative (redline 4).
- **The bark doesn't establish only-ness** (spec: "the ONLY one who
  barks at the new-moon lantern") — C's log line has the bark but
  nothing marking that this dog barks at nothing else. Fixed by the
  pick (A's coda grafted, redline 5).
- Minor: Iori's *"hold the thought of me lightly"* is ornate for the
  man who speaks no maxims (borderline; superseded by the pick —
  beat 2 ships from A). · The narration says *"O-Hisa does not finish
  it"* over a line she visibly finished (*"— well. Better taught."*).
  · *"the one place on the estate where you were ever on a list"* —
  opaque referent (the weir? the day-book entry?); vague-but-parseable
  strains (superseded by the pick).

## THE PICK

**Take C is the canonical text, with two grafts from take A** — a
mixed pick, per the 2026-07-08 ruling, because both grafts are
clearly better at the spec's own named requirements:

- **Beat 1 · the grove — TAKE C** (scene + decide + aftermath log
  lines). Best scare, best rule-7 rewards, the mending-borne O-Hisa
  reads, the checked-counts cost.
- **Beat 2 · the first Bon — TAKE A** (`sb-bon` whole). Purest
  exclusion-as-friction (the forecourt his all day, nobody crosses
  it); the unit's best line (*"Not this. If a year from now — well.
  Not this."*); Iori's goodbye-inside-greeting exact to the cast
  sheet. Clearly better than C's Bon, whose O-Ume sit-down and
  ornate Iori line dilute the friction the spec names.
- **Beat 3 · the lease day — TAKE C**, with redline 2 (the banked
  line). The measured bow + the grace-into-evenings mechanic tie-in.
- **Beat 4 · the dog — TAKE C** (scene + decide + drive/ended
  aftermaths), with redline 3 (the price) — **and the fed-branch
  new-moon payoff is TAKE A's `sb-dog-coda` scene**, replacing C's
  `(new-moon night round, later)` log line. A's coda is the only
  rendering of "the ONLY one who barks" (the rats' and marten's
  silence framing the single bark), it keeps Kihei on the round
  (canon: the rounds are his and the MC's alone from R3), and its
  "nobody enters a bark in any book" close is the one piece of A's
  ledger-silence motif worth carrying into the mix.
- **Beat 5 · the crest — TAKE C**, with redline 4 (the count). The
  paid-question mirror and the Yohei aftermath.

### Required redlines (blocking, at migration)

1. **POV-convert the A-sourced blocks to second person** (canon
   narration voice): `sb-bon` — *"The third load goes past before he
   asks it"* → *before you ask it*; *"The forecourt is his all day"*
   → *yours all day*; `sb-dog-coda` — no MC pronouns present, verify
   on migration. Speaker label `Nameless:` throughout (already
   correct in both source takes).
2. **Cut the banked line from C's lease day.** Matsuzō's gate exit
   keeps the look and the making-nothing (*"looks at you the way he
   looked at the paint"* … *"He has been making nothing of it for a
   year."*) and DROPS the spoken *"It was quiet that night. Quietest
   all year."* — the sentence is T3's clue and stays banked. If the
   human prefers to seed it early, that is a human call, not a
   default.
3. **Re-price the fed dog onto the MC.** Genemon's entry must agree
   with the MC's own line ("from my share"): the rice comes from the
   MC's measure, entered as a recurring kind-lane sink — e.g. *"A
   dog. Old, one ear. Rice: a handful, mornings, from your measure —
   entered. If it earns, it stays entered."* The house pays nothing.
4. **Keep the petal count relative** in C's crest beat: Shinnosuke
   may count aloud, but the text fixes only "one more" (e.g. *"Count
   them. The storehouse has one more than the gate."*) — no absolute
   number enters canon before T2's reveal needs one.
5. **Graft mechanics for the coda:** A's `sb-dog-coda` native block
   (fires on first new-moon night round with the fed flag; binds to
   the seeded hooded-lantern crossing) replaces C's log-line native
   for that branch. C's other two aftermath log lines (driven /
   ended) ship as authored.

### Flags for the human (non-blocking)

- *"Kihei does not turn his head"* (A's coda, shipped): implies Kihei
  has long seen the new-moon lantern and practices the house's
  not-looking. Consistent with R5's "nobody asks what it's for," but
  it is new canon about what Kihei knows — confirm or trim.
- Take B's lifted-lantern Bon image and its kihei-end dog branch are
  strong material superseded by this pick; worth banking if a later
  pass revisits either beat.
