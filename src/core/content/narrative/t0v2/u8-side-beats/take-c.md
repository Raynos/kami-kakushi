## side-beat sb-grove · Shinnosuke's grove
speaker: shinnosuke
native: a triggerable side-beat VN scene outside the rung ladder —
  fires once, on a bamboo-grove patrol between R2 and R4. The
  aftermath block is two flag-keyed log lines landed over the
  following days.

> The grove, past noon. Yesterday's cut stalks lie where the work
> stopped. The troop is loud up the slope — and under the troop,
> smaller, a voice that has no business being here.

> Shinnosuke stands with his back to green bamboo, holding a stripped
> branch out in front of him the way nobody taught him. The big male
> is down on the ground. It is closer to the boy than monkeys come
> when things are going well.

Shinnosuke: "You're the new one. Do they bite? They bite, don't
  they — don't tell anyone I'm here."

> You come up the slow way, on the uphill side, and stand where the
> male can weigh you. It goes when going is its own idea. It takes a
> seed-pouch with it.

Shinnosuke: "I had a plan. Did you see it take the pouch? That
  wasn't my fault."

### decide · The boy is looking at you.

#### sb-grove-report · "The house will hear it from me. Walk home ahead."

Shinnosuke: "Fine. Tell them. See if I mind."

memory: kihei +1 (post-kept)
flags: sb-grove-reported

#### sb-grove-cover · "I count one pouch lost. That's all I count."

Shinnosuke: "You won't say? Why won't you say — you're not so bad.
  Don't expect anything."

memory: shinnosuke +1 (kept-quiet)
flags: sb-grove-covered

#### sb-grove-teach · "Stand uphill of them. Watch."

Shinnosuke: "Like this? Why uphill — so they can't get above you,
  or so you look bigger?"

memory: shinnosuke +1 (taught)
flags: sb-grove-taught

### aftermath · the two reads
native: flag-keyed log lines, one pair per branch, landed over the
  following days.

- **sb-grove-reported** —
  > At watch-change, Kihei hears it out and hands back a round, not a
  > word of thanks: "The grove goes on your circuit. The boy's name
  > comes off your mouth; it's done."
  > The woodshed doorstep stays empty that week. The week after, the
  > mending is back, and the stitches are smaller.

- **sb-grove-covered** —
  > Kihei, at the gate, not looking up from a strap: "Two sets of
  > prints in the cut rows. One of them is a boy's. Your count was
  > short." He lets it stand. From that day he checks your counts.
  > There is a boiled egg wrapped in with the mending. Nobody claims
  > it.

- **sb-grove-taught** —
  > At the drill-yard wall the boy takes the uphill corner without
  > thinking about it. Kihei marks it, and marks where it came from.
  > "…So. The grove goes on your round — both of you, then."
  > From the kitchen, not to you: "If he's to go there anyway — well.
  > Better taught." O-Hisa does not finish it.

## side-beat sb-first-bon · The first Bon
speaker: genemon
native: seasonal side-beat — fires once, the first Bon after R2 (the
  calendar is unlocked). Spans one evening and the next morning.

> Bon comes to the estate the way work does: as a list.

> Genemon reads it at the board. Lanterns to the gate — Rokusuke. The
> alcove's things — the lady's own hands. Rice for the tray, water
> for the flowers, the mule cart for the temple road. A name for
> every item, and the list ends, and your name was not in it.

Genemon: "That is the list. What is not on the list does not move
  today."

> Nobody says you may not watch. Nobody says anything to you at all.
> Every pair of hands on the estate carries something tonight; nobody
> minds your empty ones. That is the whole of your part in it.

> The monk is at the gate, settling into the corner room like a man
> folding a coat he will shortly unfold.

Iori: "A good Bon to this house while I'm in it. I'm away after the
  fires — hold the thought of me lightly."

> Passing the bent corridor you see it once, and briefly: a small
> altar where the corridor turns, lamps lit, a tray set for someone,
> and a pair of straw sandals placed toe-out, facing away from the
> house. The household files past you toward it. From inside, the
> dowager's voice, to no one who answers:

Toku: "He was eleven the year we hung the big lantern. Mind the
  wick."

> The door slides.

> You end the evening at the paddy's edge, because the river is the
> one place on the estate where you were ever on a list. O-Ume is on
> the bank with two lanterns, setting one into the current and
> holding the other back a moment, saying something to it first.

O-Ume: "The river gave one back this year. He'll have seen that,
  where he is. Sit — you're good weather tonight."

> You sit. The lanterns go downstream not caring whose they are.

> The next morning the corridor door stands open to air. The sandals
> are new straw, toes out, facing away. Nobody carried the old pair
> past you, and nobody will say where old sandals go.

## side-beat sb-lease-day · The lease day
speaker: matsuzo
native: side-beat scene — fires once at the season's lease day, after
  R3. `matsuzo` needs a NAMES/voices entry. May set `motivates:
  zone-weir-reeds` (the screen-mending loop) if the engine wants the
  unlock hung here.

> Matsuzō comes up from the river at the pace of a man nothing has
> hurried in sixty years. Genemon meets him in the forecourt with the
> day-book already open — which is how you learn the house knew it
> was short before the knock.

Genemon: "The weir lease. Due today: rice, one koku. In the store,
  against it: seven to. The house is short a third, and I will not
  count it smaller than it is."

Matsuzō: "Screens took a beating in the spring water. Rats after.
  Hard year on everything downstream."

> He does not say: on you too. He looks at the gate's paint, and at
> you, and back toward the river, the way he looks at everything —
> weather that will pass over his weir eventually.

Genemon: "Three to owing. I ask the season's grace on it. The house
  asks."

> Genemon bows. Not deeply — the exact depth a Kurosawa steward has
> never before had to measure for a weir-keeper, measured now, got
> right the first time because he will not let it be seen twice.

Matsuzō: "The river can wait a season. The screens can't — rats are
  in them now. Send your man down while the year turns and we'll
  call it even weight by winter."

> Nobody asks you. A line in the day-book, a nod toward the water,
> and a season of your evenings is settled between two old men. Three
> to of rice. It is the first time you have been worth a number. The
> number is small.

> At the gate the old man stops, level with you, and looks at you the
> way he looked at the paint.

Matsuzō: "You're the one the river left. It was quiet that night.
  Quietest all year."

> He goes back down the way he came, having made nothing of it. He
> has been making nothing of it for a year.

## side-beat sb-dog · The dog that stays
speaker: kihei
native: final stage of the Overgrown-orchard chain. The fed branch
  requires: a yard-dog estate state, a dog-rice recurring sink (kind
  lane), and a flag-keyed log line that lands on a later new-moon
  night round. All aftermath blocks are flag-keyed log lines.

> The last of the pack went over the wall two days ago. This one
> stayed. An old dog, grey to the jaw, one ear ragged from winters,
> lying in the dead leaves under the far fruit trees like the orchard
> is a post it was never relieved from.

> It does not run and it does not beg. When you work, it watches.
> When you eat, it watches the food, then you — and then it looks
> away first.

> Kihei crosses the cleared ground on his round and stops beside you,
> reading the dog the way he reads a blade for rust.

Kihei: "The old one. Too slow for the pack, too proud for the
  village middens. Drive it, feed it, or bring it to me. Don't leave
  it half-decided."

### decide · The dog watches you decide.

#### sb-dog-drive · "The orchard's cleared. All of it."

Kihei: "Cleared is cleared. Stop the gap in the wall behind it."

memory: kihei +1 (thorough)
flags: sb-dog-driven

#### sb-dog-feed · "It stays. I'll feed it from my share."

Genemon: "A dog. Old, one ear. Rice: a handful, mornings, from the
  sweepings — entered against rats. If it earns, it stays entered."

memory: genemon +1 (accountable)
flags: sb-dog-fed

#### sb-dog-kihei · "Take it to Kihei."

Kihei: "Give me your belt-cord. Go rake the leaves. This isn't a
  lesson."

memory: kihei +1 (clear-eyed)
flags: sb-dog-ended

### aftermath · what the choice buys
native: flag-keyed log lines; the sb-dog-fed new-moon line waits for
  the next new-moon night round after the fact.

- **sb-dog-driven** —
  > It comes back twice, to the same tree. The third week it stops.
  > Nobody sees where old dogs go.

- **sb-dog-fed** —
  > By month's end it has moved from the orchard to the woodshed
  > doorstep, and sleeps there as if the arrangement were its idea.
  > The day-book carries it as *one dog, old, against rats*. It has
  > no name yet. Neither did you.
  >
  > *(new-moon night round, later)* At the yard's far edge the dog
  > stands and barks — once, at the dark going upstream. A hooded
  > lantern in the dark does not pause. Nobody wakes. You mark it;
  > the dog marks it; the house sleeps on.

- **sb-dog-ended** —
  > The orchard is quiet after. Kihei buries it inside the wall, not
  > outside, and does not explain the difference.

## side-beat sb-crest · The crest question
speaker: shinnosuke
native: fires once, after sb-grove, at the kura exterior. The
  aftermath is one flag-keyed market-day line at Yohei's stall. The
  thread pays into T2's reveal; every line here has an authored
  answer behind it (the one-ambiguity budget is spent elsewhere).

> Loading at the kura. Shinnosuke is up on the stacked bales, where
> he is not allowed, counting something over your head.

Shinnosuke: "Eight on the gate. Nine on the storehouse. Why does the
  storehouse get nine petals and the gate eight? Who changes a
  crest — you can't just change a crest, can you?"

> You look. Above the seal-plate the mon is cut deep and old: the
> same flower as the gate's, and not the same flower. You count
> twice, because counting is the one kind of reading you trust.

Nameless: "Ask the steward."

Shinnosuke: "I asked everyone. Grandmother said mind the lamp.
  Genemon wrote in his book. Mother said if I've time for petals
  I've time for copywork. Nobody answers me. You ask. You work
  here — they have to answer you."

> Asking costs you something every time. You pay it at the board
> that evening.

Nameless: "The kura's crest. It has one petal more than the gate's."

> It is not even a question, the way you say it. Genemon looks at you
> the way he looked at the river-wet stranger the first morning — a
> thing to be entered under the correct heading. Then he opens the
> day-book, writes — not long — and closes it.

Genemon: "The kura wants its hinges oiled before the rains. Take the
  small ladder."

> That is the answer. It is the same answer the boy got, in a
> different hand.

> Shinnosuke is waiting on the woodpile to hear what you learned. You
> tell him: hinges. He nods like a man twice his age confirming a
> debt gone bad.

Shinnosuke: "See. You're the other one they don't tell. What did he
  write — did you see what he wrote?"

> You didn't. The boy climbs down and goes in to his copywork, and
> you oil the hinges under nine petals, and the kura keeps whatever
> it keeps.

### aftermath · the question comes back priced
native: flag-keyed (sb-crest asked) log line, next market day.

- > By market day the question has crossed the village line and come
  > back wearing your face.
  >
  > Yohei, over the stall: "Friend! The man with the eye for crests —
  > no shame in it, a man should know whose roof he hauls for.
  > Thread's two mon. For a man of taste: still two mon."
