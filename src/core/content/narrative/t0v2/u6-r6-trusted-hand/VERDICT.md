## Take A — "the ledger" (maximal restraint)

**What works.** The law-cleanest take. Genemon is pitch-perfect
item-count-condition throughout ("Thread, two hanks, the hemp. Nails,
thirty, the short kind — he will offer the long. Salt, if the count
holds.") — not one metaphor, surprise answered by writing, spec's
count-back image ("mon to mon, the change, each coin turned once")
delivered exactly. The arithmetic is genuinely load-bearing: at the
stranger's rate of 6 the purse comes out empty; only the re-price
brings 2 mon home — "salt, if the count holds" pays off silently.
Best wireability of the three: no native escape, identical arithmetic
across branches. The straw coat "nobody at this house has asked the
price of" seeds the economics canon (one coat per winter) with real
restraint.

**Violations / risks.** (1) *Epigram-adjacent patter clustering.*
Yohei carries an aphorism-shaped line in the ask AND in every decide
branch: "A face I'll not see twice pays for the road; a face that
comes back pays less" · "A face I'll see again is worth a mon this
morning" · "A stranger pays six; a house pays what it will pay again."
Each survives individually because it names a price (trade arithmetic,
not wisdom — his canon register), but two per playthrough is the
saturation pattern the audit flagged, at its rationing limit.
(2) *The decide is inert.* All three branches yield `yohei +1` and the
same re-price — no branch is a mark's behavior, so the "honest
arithmetic" is displayed, never tested; rule 7 is satisfied only
vacuously (no dial because no consequence). This is structural, not
redline-able without rewriting the decide. (3) No law-2, rule-7, or
canon violation found line by line. Passes the any-game test easily.

## Take B — "the held breath" (grief close under the surface)

**What works.** The richest character play. The decide is the best
rule-7 work in the unit: taking the wage is clean, *returning the
house's coin* deepens Genemon's fled-debt misread ("A man that careful
of other people's coin has usually had practice.") — the warmest
answer punished from an unexpected quarter — and the thread option
pays into O-Hisa's mending-basket thread. Kihei's wordless cameo
(eye finds the purse, weighs it, moves on) respects the "…"-react
ownership by not using it. Yohei's price-mid-sentence move ("Ten
years I've carried white incense for this house, and never once —
four mon, same as ever.") is textbook cast-sheet Yohei.

**Violations.** (1) *Unlicensed epigrams in Genemon's mouth* — the
worst law-2 breach in the unit. "Wages are cleaner than favors." and
"Argued down is earned, by my arithmetic." are maxim-shaped general
truths; the license is spent (Munemasa + Kihei, no one else), and
Genemon "has never in his life reached for a metaphor" or the
sententious. (2) *Shape borrow.* "This purse carried koku, once. Now
it carries this." is dated then/now elegy — Toku's register (everything
dated) leaking into Genemon, plus the chiasmus-template slop pattern;
the house performing its own decline is Chiyo's law broken by the
wrong mouth. (3) *Canon slip.* "Four rungs of wages against your name
and no shortfalls" — the man HAS no name until R7; that is the tier's
whole naming arc, and the precise bookkeeper is the last speaker who
would say it. (4) *Unsanctioned origin seeding.* The "Six on the
road" memory-surfacing plus Yohei reading porter's shoulders adds two
new origin-tells to a seeding roster t0.md places deliberately
(Sōan's ledger, the bundle, Matsuzō — banked). Vague-but-parseable is
honored, but a blind take expanding the mystery roster needs a human
ruling, not a default ship. (5) Speaker tag "Yohei (villager)" — he
is the pedlar at the estate's edge, not a villager. Fixing 1–3 guts
the take's stance; B is the least salvageable as-authored.

## Take C — "the weather" (kernel #7 forward)

**What works.** The strongest dramatization of what the rung actually
*earns*. The gateyard breathes without performing for him — the woman
pulling her basket an inch closer, the talk thinning "and it is not
about you either" — kernel #7 dramatized, not narrated. The trust is
arithmetic and need ("Two hands short since spring, and I cannot
leave the book on a market morning. So.") — exactly Genemon's
folded-him-in-by-arithmetic canon. The decide actually RUNS the
spec's honest arithmetic: counting Yohei flat to the steward's figure
earns the regular's standing (`+1 square`), while paying over
*kindly* leaves you "a mark still" (`+0`) — warmth priced correctly,
the warmest answer unrewarded (rule 7 at its best), and refusing the
thread lands from the unexpected quarter (O-Hisa "will not ask you at
all"). The day-book close — the same hand that wrote *one man, name
unknown* leaving the space where a name would go — is a declared
deliberate echo that escalates straight into R7's naming: the craft
rule used as designed. The count-back epilogue gives the spec's named
image (the finger, mon by mon) branch-reactive payoff: the finger
that "crosses the wood and finds nothing to count" is the best single
beat in the unit.

**Violations.** (1) *Speaker-label canon:* the MC's two lines are
labeled `You:` — the 04-cast label ladder reserves `You:` for the
cold open's first lines; at R6 he is `Nameless:`. Mechanical fix.
(2) *One unlicensed epigram:* Yohei's "Everything walks downhill but
the rain." is a proverb-shaped general truth — the license is spent;
cut or bend it back into priced news. (3) *The ending lingers a beat
past its law:* after "That is all of it," the final paragraph
restates the shown three times over ("The purse is in the drawer, the
entry has no name in it, and market day comes every sixth day") plus
a water simile — restraint performed instead of kept. Trim. (4)
*Wiring cost:* the post-decide `when:`-gated epilogue is a declared
`native:` escape (sanctioned by the plan, but real engine work), and
the R0 echo must ride the `@`-reuse mechanism at migration, not a
retyped line. (5) The spec's stray→regular turn is partly
retro-narrated in the ask ("First market, you were the road's… I
price the coming back") while the decide implies he's still testing —
readable (base rate regular, skim where he can), but the r6-flat
react should carry the turn unmistakably. It does ("I'd rather be the
stall you count at than the one you walk past"), so no edit required.

## THE PICK — Take C

C is the recommended default. Its flaws are all *enumerable and
mechanical* (a label swap, one cut line, one trimmed paragraph, a
declared wiring cost); its virtues are the unit's ceiling — the only
take where the re-pricing-by-honest-arithmetic is a system the player
operates rather than a speech they receive, the only decide whose
consequences all arrive per rule 7, and a day-book close that pays
the tier's naming arc forward. A is the safe runner-up: law-cleanest
and cheapest to wire, but its decide is cosmetic — the trust is shown,
never tested — and that weakness is structural where C's are surface.
B's best ideas (the misread-feeding return, the O-Hisa thread option)
are voice-deep in violation and cannot ship as authored; its decide
design deserves to be remembered, and C already carries an equivalent.

## Redlines on C before wiring

1. **`You:` → `Nameless:`** on both MC lines ("Forty." · "His.") —
   the speaker-label ladder, 04-cast.
2. **Cut "Everything walks downhill but the rain."** — unlicensed
   epigram; end the ask at "…half the carriers went down for them.
   Your house lost two hands to the same road, I hear."
3. **Trim the final paragraph** — end the scene at "In the gateyard
   the talk has closed over the morning — the rain, the salt, the
   dye-man at the ford." Delete the closing restatement sentence and
   the "like water" simile.
4. **Migration:** the *one man, name unknown* echo goes through the
   `@`-reuse mechanism (the comment already promises this — enforce
   it); the `native:` post-decide epilogue is real engine work the
   wiring plan must budget; confirm the grammar accepts `+0` memory
   annotations or convert `r6-over`/`r6-back` to flags only.
5. **NAMES registry:** pedlar Tokubei → Yohei before any line
   resolves (all three takes note this; it is a wave-level
   prerequisite, not C-specific).
