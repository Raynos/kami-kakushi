## rung R6 · rung-r6
speaker: genemon
voice: steward
motivates: house-purse, market-errand

> The board, before the morning tasks are called. A purse the size of a fist
> sits on the wood between you, its string already loosened.

Genemon: "Yohei's mat is at the gate till noon. Thread, two hanks, the hemp.
Nails, thirty, the short kind — he will offer the long. Salt, if the count
holds."

Genemon: "Twenty-four mon. It is counted."

> He pushes the purse across and turns back to the ledgers.

> The gate. Yohei's mat is squared to the posts, the stock laid out in rows:
> thread, needles, nails in paper, salt in straw twists, and a straw coat
> folded at the corner that nobody at this house has asked the price of.

Yohei (pedlar): "Rain took the low bridge and half the thread in the valley
with it — six a hank while it lasts — and the house sends the man who swept
this gate into being worth a stall."

> The long nails come out first, bright on their paper. You name the short
> kind. They go back without a word spent on it.

### ask r6-road · "You came by the low road."

Yohei (pedlar): "By the ford, since the bridge went — mud to the knee, and
everything on this mat carried through it, which is half the price when a man
asks why. The lowlands lost the early planting. Salt will be dear by Bon, and
rice after it."

### ask r6-dear · "Six is dear."

Yohei (pedlar): "A stranger's price. A face I'll not see twice pays for the
road; a face that comes back pays less, because I make it back over years, not
in one morning. Which you are, this morning decides."

### decide · The thread sits at six.

#### r6-square · "I'll count it onto the mat."

Yohei (pedlar): "Slow as a temple bell — count it, then. Five the hank, to a
man who counts another's coin like a debt he owes. A face I'll see again is
worth a mon this morning."

memory: yohei +1 (square)
flags: r6-square

#### r6-rate · "O-Yae pays five."

Yohei (pedlar): "She does, and haggles the morning away for it. Five to you,
and the morning saved. Tell the steward the house sends the better buyer."

memory: yohei +1 (sharp)
flags: r6-rate

#### r6-house · "The coin is the house's, not mine."

Yohei (pedlar): "And the house's coin walks back through this gate every
market day. Five, then. A stranger pays six; a house pays what it will pay
again."

memory: yohei +1 (steady)
flags: r6-house

> Twenty-two mon cross the mat, told out one at a time. The purse takes back
> two.

> The board again. Genemon lays the goods out left to right, then the coin.
> His finger moves — the thread, the nail-bundle told out in tens, the salt —
> and last, mon to mon, the change, each coin turned once.

Genemon: "Thread, two hanks, the hemp. Nails, thirty, the short kind. Salt.
And two mon home."

> He opens the day-book and writes. The entry is one line. Then a second.

Genemon: "Next market day, you carry it again."

> He is already at the next page.
