# VERDICT · U4 R4 — the pupil

Judged against: 01-laws §0.5 (whole) · 00-kernel · 04-cast (Kihei whole, the
"…"-react ownership, the epigram license) · tiers/t0.md (ladder row R4 +
mystery seeding + the drill-yard zone) · taste.md. Creed text verified
verbatim in all three takes; the "…"-react is Kihei-only in all three. All
three converge independently on the stalls-for-twenty line and an
oiled-gear device — spec-sourced, not copy-paste; the pick owns them.

## Take A — "the ledger"

The most restrained narration of the three, and the board scene is genuinely
good: Genemon's "Item: rice, two bales, fouled" is perfect
item/count/condition, and "He blots the line. The window stays open behind
you the whole way across the forecourt." is the house style working.
Violations and flags:

- **Canon (the hardest bend of the three).** R3 locks *"the rice
  untouched"*; A's confession is *"Two bales fouled. The inner door,
  split. I opened that door myself."* The header argues "untouched = the
  wolf ate nothing," but the plain sense of the locked ladder row — the
  house lost nothing but his blood — is rewritten into a material loss he
  caused. B and C both invent losses *compatible* with R3; A's fights it.
- **The BEG is missing.** Spec: shame drives him to *BEG*. A's ask is a
  bargain: *"I held the door. It cost the house two bales. Teach me to
  hold it for less."* — a negotiation, not a beg, and "hold it for less"
  is a crafted turn from the plainest voice in the game (§0.5.2: the MC,
  no figures of speech).
- **Rule 7 lean.** The decide is single-quarter (all three replies graded
  by Kihei) and compliance-shaped: the two agreeable answers pay +1, the
  haggling one +0 — a mild virtue dial, exactly the axis rule 7 bans.
- **Narrator ventriloquism.** *"the gear rack he has kept oiled, all these
  years, for nobody"* — the narration speaks Kihei's unspoken want (04-cast:
  "He will never say so"). The WHY is handed over instead of earned.
- **Epigram pressure in the decide replies:** *"The yard is not the
  board."* · *"no rice to spare for lessons that don't hold"* — two
  aphorism-flavored verdicts after the creed already spent the scene's
  maxim.
- **Structure:** no ask-topics at all — a bare decide (taste #11 wants
  ask → done-gate → decision for VN beats).
- Misses the R4 alcove glimpse (t0.md mystery seeding places "R4-glimpse →
  T1 the alcove's sandals" in this rung).

## Take B — "the held breath"

The best MC voice in the wave: *"The watch was mine."* · *"It isn't."* ·
*"Teach me." / "Will you?"* — the beg dramatized at cost while staying
plainest-of-all. The board beat is the deepest of the three: Genemon
refuses to enter the loss under his name (*"The account is even."*) and the
MC's flat *"It isn't."* plays kernel #7 — even his guilt is denied a
ledger. The oiled second staff is the need made a THING (the §0.5.3
instinct), handed over without a word of why, with an ask-topic that
defers the answer (*"Oil is cheap. Ask again when you can hold it
right."*). The decide is the wave's rule-7 exemplar: one option moves
GENEMON's regard, not Kihei's; the most personal option moves no ledger at
all. Violations and flags:

- **A repeated verbal template (the audit's chiasmus-class slop).** The
  same X-echoes-X device fires four times in one take: *"so you could
  walk, not so you could work"* · *"…You walked here on cracked ribs to
  ask for more of what cracked them."* · *"The rice is the steward's
  grief; mine is the sill it crossed"* (balanced antithesis) · *"carry
  it, if it carries you to this yard at dawn."* Each is fine alone;
  four is a template.
- **Kihei over-certifies his own cover story:** *"That is the whole of
  why this yard is open to you."* He is hiding the want behind the
  house-need — a man doing that does not certify the reason complete.
  (The preceding *"one man who can stand a watch, and he is grey"* is
  fine: vague-but-parseable, house-need dress, rule 5.)
- **"Oil is cheap"** rides the epigram line — gnomic in form. Passes as a
  deflection (its job is refusing sentiment about the staff), but it is
  the second crafted Kihei tag after the creed; note for the trim.
- **Canon wobble (minor):** the store watch standing empty three nights
  reads as negligence by the watch-keeper. It survives on B's own logic
  (one man cannot stand two posts), but nothing in the text pins that —
  one clause should.
- Misses the R4 alcove glimpse (same gap as A).
- Narrator-tells a cast-sheet tic: *"It is what he does with surprise"*
  — says what the gesture should carry alone. Minor.

## Take C — "the weather"

The most canon-complete take: the only one to land the spec-placed **R4
alcove glimpse** (sandals facing away, renewed this year — faithful to the
zone text, closed by a screen sliding shut from inside), and the only one
to deliver Kihei's need in the words canon assigns him — the mamushi line
(*"I have dug graves on this ground…"*), which 04-cast says IS how he says
"one student who outlives him." Kernel #7 is genuinely dramatized (the
kitchen's wolf grows at each telling; *nobody asks the man limping past*),
the confession invents nothing against R3, and the decide inverts the
warmth dial (dutiful pays least) with a commented deliberate echo
(*"underneath it when it chose to go"* — echo-as-choice, correctly
handled). Violations and flags:

- **Epigram saturation moved into the narration — the audit's #1 slop
  pattern, recurring.** *"They have the better wolf already."* · *"The
  tale does not consult the book."* · *"learns that the book pays out
  neither"* · *"A man who wants an answer from this yard can stand in it
  and want one."* · *"The question is left standing in the yard with the
  man who asked it."* Five aphoristic caps in one beat; §0.5.1 makes
  ornament earned by rarity, and licensing it to the narrator is the
  smuggling route.
- **Two extra Kihei near-maxims after the creed:** *"They always
  remember."* (gnomic tag) and *"That answer gets out of bed when it
  rains."* (a full aphorism). The scene's one-maxim budget is the creed;
  these push Kihei's "rare by temperament" license toward saturation.
- **Signature-line stacking:** mamushi line + creed delivered
  back-to-back (three consecutive Kihei blocks) — both of his charter
  lines spent in one breath, against the restraint law's pacing.
- **POV drift:** C narrates in third person ("he takes them") where A/B
  and the shipped register narrate in "you" — a wiring-pass
  harmonization cost.
- Weakest literal fit to "CONFESSES the granary loss": nothing of the
  grain is lost (bar, lamp, watch, half-work days). Defensible — the
  header flags and argues it — but the thinnest reading of the row.

## THE PICK — take B

**B is the recommended default.** The reasoning, ranked by what the laws
weigh heaviest:

1. **The spec's emotional spine lands hardest in B.** The confession is
   answered by the house *refusing* to count the loss his — and "It
   isn't." makes the shame self-owned, which is exactly what must drive
   the beg. The beg itself ("Teach me." / "Will you?") is the only one of
   the three that reads as begging while honoring the MC's
   plainest-voice shape.
2. **The rule-7 decide is the only fully compliant one** (cross-quarter
   consequence, one option deliberately unrewarded) — A leans into the
   banned dial; C inverts it but stays single-quarter.
3. **B's defects are line-level; C's are its texture.** Trimming B's four
   echo-templates and one over-certifying line leaves the take intact.
   Trimming C's narration aphorisms — the required fix — removes the
   stance that makes C distinct, and its POV needs rewriting throughout.
   A is disqualified on the R3 canon bend plus the missing beg.
4. The oiled staff is a durable object the fiction can keep its promise
   about (taste #10) — a seeded thread, not a grant.

C's alcove glimpse and its kitchen-tale kernel-#7 texture are the wave's
best *inventions* — the glimpse is spec-owed and must be added to B (it is
a placed seed, not C's property); the kitchen-tale device stays banked
with the DEV alternates.

## Redlines on B before wiring

1. **Add the R4 alcove glimpse** (t0.md mystery seeding) on the walk to
   the board — narration-only, no dialogue, the screen closed from
   inside. C's rendering is the reference; re-voice in B's register and
   B's second-person POV.
2. **Break the echo template — keep at most one.** Keep *"…You walked
   here on cracked ribs to ask for more of what cracked them."* (it is
   the "…"-react verdict and the best of the four). Rewrite: *"so you
   could walk, not so you could work"* → plain (e.g. "…strapped the ribs
   for walking. Not for work."); *"The rice is the steward's grief; mine
   is the sill it crossed"* → keep the division, drop the balance (e.g.
   "The rice is Genemon's grief. Mine is the sill."); *"carry it, if it
   carries you to this yard at dawn"* → cut the turn ("Then carry it to
   this yard at dawn.").
3. **Delete** *"That is the whole of why this yard is open to you."* The
   grey-watchman line before it already does the work, at the right
   vagueness.
4. **Pin the empty watch in one clause** so Kihei isn't negligent — e.g.
   Genemon's entry: "No watch stood — the gate cannot leave the gate." or
   equivalent; one man, two posts, made scene-logic (rule 5).
5. **Optional trim:** "It is what he does with surprise…" — let the pen
   stopping above the line carry it; the cast sheet doesn't need
   reciting.
