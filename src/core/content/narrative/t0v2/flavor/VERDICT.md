# Flavor verdict — F1 (nodes) + F2 (texture)

## F1 — the node sheet · verdict: SHIPPABLE, one blocking redline

Coverage is complete and exact: all 17 t0.md zones are present, every
wrong thing is carried at the strength its node places it, and no new
mystery debt enters. The discipline shows where it is hardest to keep —
the kura's petal count stays relative ("count the petals, and it
carries one more than the gate's" — no absolute number enters canon
before T2 needs one); the jizō line leans toward no candidate; the
sandals glimpse says the dead-man kind and the this-year straw and
nothing more; the weir-reeds seed stops exactly where t0.md stops it
("it is feeding them from above the weir" — no breach-pool hint); the
woodshed's no-wrong-thing is preserved with an explicit do-not-add
guard, which is the sheet protecting the spec from future editors.
Blurbs pass the any-game test line by line ("the market comes to the
house now" · "It is not much, and it is not anyone else's" · "Walk it
clean and the house sleeps. Fall, and you wake at Sōan's") — each names
this estate's facts, not a genre's. Action labels are period verbs, no
abstract game-words; staging notes are properly quarantined in
brackets. Narrator POV is second person throughout, per canon.

Violations:

- **BLOCKING — the new-moon line is a near-duplicate of u3's picked
  prose, on the same trigger.** F1 night-rounds wrong thing: *"New
  moon. At the yard's far edge a lantern crosses, hooded to a slit,
  going upstream. By the time you reach the wall there is nothing to
  catch."* u3 take-B (picked) `night-round-new-moon`: *"On the dark of
  the moon a lantern crosses the far edge of the yard — hooded low,
  held steady, going upstream. By the time the round brings you back
  the yard is empty…"* Same event, same gate (u3's native block fires
  "on new-moon rounds only" — F1's staging note says the same), same
  construction — and drifted detail ("hooded to a slit" vs "hooded
  low"). Two texts for one firing condition is the copy-pasted-gesture
  defect plus a TST1 breach (one home for everything): unmarked, it is
  accidental near-duplication, which the craft rule calls a defect.
  Redline 1 below.
- Minor — the kura wrong thing and u3's in-scene seal-plate line carry
  the same fact ("one more petal than the gate's", both relative —
  correct per the u8 ruling). Different surfaces (standing node line vs
  scene narration), so acceptable — but migration should keep them from
  rendering in the same beat.

Slop-pattern scan: no epigrams in narrator voice, no mechanics
ventriloquism (the blurbs describe things, never the interface), no
counted coin anywhere, no mind/body constructions. Clean.

## F2 — the texture sheet · verdict: SHIPPABLE, four blocking redlines

The strongest texture writing in the wave where it is world-breath: the
seasonal and weather pools are game-specific to the line ("The mule
stays in" — the one mule; "the woodshed roof holds, this year" — the
repairs; "Kindling fetches what firewood did in autumn" — a price
without a number). The economy shapes hold throughout: beasts drop
things, never coin (rat hides, seed-corn, the recovered hoard);
barn-filling is the house's economy ("the kitchen takes every one";
"The day-book opens a line it has not carried in years: orchard" — the
best reward line on the sheet); quest prose carries no arithmetic; the
only sums sit in the log, where §0.5.6 puts prices, not in speech.
Perks name the object or habit of their scene — whetstone, knot, post,
slats, stave, the second count — no chiasmus, no filler; the Second
Count is derived straight from u6-C's counted-back purse and reads as
that scene's residue, which is exactly what a perk should be. Attributed
speech is voice-true: Matsuzō answers about the water, Kihei's line is
imperative-into-verdict, Genemon's whole speech is *"Entered."*

Violations:

- **BLOCKING — `mob-otter` breaches the tier roster.** The otter is
  T1's designed enemy (t1.md: the kawauso raid the leased fish weirs;
  the folk-telling — the shapeshifter that is "wet, greedy" in
  daylight — and the dressed-stone-den wrong thing are T1's material).
  A casual T0 guide page — *"The river people tell stories about it;
  the basket is the whole story"* — pre-spends T1's kernel-#1 telling
  and dilutes its introduction: the same defect class as u8's banked
  Matsuzō line, cashed a tier early. It also invents catch-baskets, a
  loop T0 doesn't have. Redline 2.
- **BLOCKING — epigram leakage:** perk-whetstone opens its second
  sentence with a narrator maxim — *"An edge kept is work saved"* — a
  proverb shape in a voice with no license (the license is spent:
  Munemasa and Kihei, and the sheet's own header says none is spent
  here). The concrete half of the sentence ("the sickle bites all day
  on one honing") already carries the meaning. Redline 3.
- **BLOCKING — verbatim self-copy:** *"goes over the wall last, at his
  own pace"* appears word-for-word in both `quest-grove-male-complete`
  and `mob-monkey-male`, with a third rendering ("going last,
  unhurried") in `quest-grove-monkeys-complete` — three surfaces, one
  gesture, unmarked. Redline 4.
- **BLOCKING — the weir-rats strings triple-render one gesture and
  self-echo on completion.** F1's blurb: *"He counts the damage from
  his own bank, and does not raise his voice."* F2's complete:
  *"Matsuzō counts them from his own bank and finds nothing to say."*
  — near-verbatim across sheets. And within F2, the complete's *"the
  water runs through clean"* is immediately restated by the speech
  line *"Water's running clean."* — if both fire on one completion the
  player reads the same fact twice in two voices. Redline 5.
- Minor — the field-guide's closes lean aphoristic in several entries
  (*"Drive them all or you have driven none — they count you, too"* ·
  *"Break the pack, and what is left is only dogs"*). Field-craft, not
  life-maxims, so not license breaches — but ornament is earned by
  rarity, and two-plus snap-closes in eleven pages is a formula.
  Redline 6 rations them.
- Minor — perk-whetstone and perk-oak-stave open on the same
  construction ("Ten strokes on the stone each dawn" / "Ten motions,
  every dawn") — an unmarked near-duplicate in one surface; vary one,
  or mark the echo deliberate (drill discipline mirroring work
  discipline would earn it, but it must be chosen, not accidental).
- Minor — two missed-event lines use the same stale-food token
  (season-summer-2's red rice "already cool"; gossip-wedding-missed's
  sweets "a little stale"). Different pools, so tolerable — vary one if
  both can land in a single run.

Slop-pattern scan: no counted coin in speech (Genemon's sums live in
u6's scene, not here); no mechanics ventriloquism (the round-board is
kernel #6 done right — a fiction object that IS the visible state); no
mind/body chiasmus. The maxim and the copy-pastes above are the two
audit patterns that recurred; both are redlined.

## The waste-ground call — KEEP

F1's authored Display name — the map labels the ruined compound "The
waste ground" — is the sheet's best idea and should ship: t0.md's "not
even named honestly" is an instruction the label executes, and under
kernel #6 the map is a house document, so the household's dishonest
name belongs ON the label — the refusal to look, made into UI. The
blurb makes the lie legible in one stroke ("The house calls all of it
the waste ground, and no one's eyes stay on it while they say so") —
vague-but-parseable, TST4-clean: the player knows the label lies and
knows whose lie it is. The name propagates consistently (the grove
"close behind the waste ground"; the sett "toward the waste ground's
wall"), and no picked unit names the ruin at all, so nothing
contradicts. Two riders: keep `ruined-compound` as the stable id (the
sheet already does), and note for T1+ that the label RENAMING when the
house finally says what it was is a free story beat — bank it.

   his own pace"* in the field-guide page (the kept book is where a
   repeated observation lives honestly); vary
   `quest-grove-male-complete` so the two never render the same words.
5. **Vary the weir-rats completion:** drop the complete line's
   own-bank clause and its water-clean clause; let Matsuzō's speech
   (*"Water's running clean."*) carry the confirmation and F1's blurb
   carry the counting-from-his-bank. One gesture, one home each.
6. **Ration the field-guide's snap-closes to one.** Keep *"Break the
   pack, and what is left is only dogs"* (tactical content, the
   guide's single earned verdict); recast the monkey close as plain
