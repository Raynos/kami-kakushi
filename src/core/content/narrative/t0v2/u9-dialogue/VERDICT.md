# U9 dialogue — verdict

Global checks, all three takes: **Munemasa correctly absent** (a voice
through a wall has no node lines). **The MC speaks in none of them** —
plainest-voice rule moot, correctly. **The "…"-react**: A spends it once
(Kihei), B once (Kihei), C twice (both Kihei) — no take leaks it to
another mouth. **Epigram budget**: no take gives a maxim to Munemasa or
invents a third license-holder; leaks are of the *unlicensed-gnomic* kind,
quoted below.

## Take A — "the-ledger" (maximal restraint)

The most law-literal take: gate-free by design (line order = progression),
Genemon's fled-debt read kept private exactly as 04-cast.md marks it,
Naoyuki held to two touches. Its cost is temperature — several characters
sit at the same clipped register, and twice a character narrates their own
tic instead of doing it.

Per-character:

- **Genemon — CLEAN, best of the three.** *"The book takes either. It
  does not take short."* Pure item/count/condition; the private read stays
  private.
- **Kihei — clean.** One "…Good.", imperative→verdict throughout.
- **Sōan — borderline.** *"A chilled man heals at half pace and eats the
  same."* — a general truth from an unlicensed mouth; prescription-shaped
  enough to pass, but it is the take's closest thing to a leaked maxim.
- **O-Hisa — borderline.** *"A man is generally waited for somewhere. If
  yours — anyway."* — voices her PRIVATE woodshed motive (04-cast.md:
  she mends "because he is a man someone somewhere is surely waiting
  for") nearly verbatim. The hope should be guarded, not stated.
- **Shinnosuke — clean.** Every line exactly two stacked questions.
- **Naoyuki — clean, exact.** *"It is a yard. Keep it."* — the coldest
  restate in the unit; honors "never once warm" and the two-touch cap.
- **Toku — breaks shape by explaining it.** *"I keep the years by their
  weather."* — a character describing her own sentence shape is telling,
  not doing. Also *"the dark nights were the ones for walking"* has the
  packet-keeper hinting at her own secret — she is the one person who
  would not say this.
- **Yohei — clean but mechanical.** All three lines run the
  news→price→flattery template identically; the tic performed (the echo
  rule's near-duplication smell).
- **O-Yae — clean.** *"Water's all I carry."*
- **Matsuzō — clean.** *"I never made more of it than that."* — the
  unmade observation, correctly unmade.
- **Iori — clean; the unit's best Iori.** *"I made my goodbye when I
  bowed at the gate coming in. What we say now is over and above."* —
  goodbye-inside-greeting as craft, not formula; wants nothing.
- **O-Ume — clean.** *"he liked a small war, my husband."*
- **Rokusuke — BREAKS.** *"Nobody asks a hand for his tally — till the
  day they do."* — epigram-shaped from an unlicensed mouth, and
  portentous foreshadowing from the one man whose whole shape is
  refusing to be noticed. Double breach; worst line in the take.

## Take B — "the held breath" (grief close under the surface)

The strongest household writing in the unit — Kihei, Sōan, O-Hisa, and
Shinnosuke are all at their best here, and the gating (post-R3/R5/R7
flags) buys real progression. Its recurring defect is the same one as A's
Toku, at higher frequency: characters narrating their own tics. Plus one
mechanical speaker error.

Per-character:

- **Genemon — breaks the privacy line.** *"You work like a man working
  something off. I don't keep a column for what."* — 04-cast.md marks the
  defaulter's read as *private*; half-voicing it in ambient talk spends a
  misread that is load-bearing drama. (His R7 line — *"Two of them worked
  out."* — is excellent and confession-free.)
- **Kihei — clean; the unit's best Kihei.** *"You held the wall with
  cracked ribs and a rake. …Good. Tomorrow you learn what would have held
  it cheaper."* One "…"-spend, his. *"The quiet lies."* is a two-word
  maxim — but Kihei HOLDS the license, it's watch-ground, and it's the
  sheet's only spend; legal, count it.
- **Sōan — clean; the best dramatization of the closed ledger.** *"Where
  did your hands learn rope? — No. Forget I asked it."* — hoping-to-be-
  wrong, done in speech.
- **O-Hisa — clean and the unit's best.** *"A shirt lasts years if
  someone — it's done."* / *"Hold your bowl out. Both hands."* Verified
  against Naoyuki's break-off (the one-owner worry): his gesture opens
  formal and restates FLAT AND COLD; hers cuts a hope and replaces it
  with CARE. Distinct mechanisms; no collision.
- **Shinnosuke — content clean, MECHANICS BROKEN.** All four lines carry
  `voice: narrator` — the boy's spoken questions would render as
  narration (taste.md P3: voice is set at the source). Content-wise,
  *"Could someone come back from it after ten years, if there was?"* is
  the best mirror line in the unit.
- **Naoyuki — clean.** *"My brother used to stand where you — the veranda
  wants sweeping."*; the post-Count *"The house closes matters."* is a
  strong third touch (defensible: veranda + Count = the tier sheet's two
  scene-touches).
- **Toku — spends a private beat ambiently.** *"An old woman is allowed
  her arithmetic. Tell no one I still do sums."* — 04-cast.md gives her
  the trade-superstition *"once, privately"*; making it a repeatable node
  line cheapens a scene-worthy confession. Bank it.
- **Yohei — clean; the most alive.** *"…and half the valley says the
  river brought you, which for the two mon I never heard."* — flattery,
  price, and the rumor economy in one breath.
- **O-Yae — clean.** *"My mother says the kami take their own share. My
  mother says a lot of things."* — attribution even for skepticism; also
  the unit's only jizō-mystery seed.
- **Matsuzō — BREAKS twice.** *"Ask the water the right question and it
  generally answers."* — gnomic, unlicensed. And *"That's the whole of
  what I know, and it's about the water."* — narrating his own tic.
- **Iori — borderline.** *"Watching counts. I'd know."* — brushes the
  maxim line for a man the sheet explicitly denies maxims; also the
  wordiest Iori of the three.
- **O-Ume — content lovely, idiom forked.** oume-racks and oume-bon are
  narrator lines with quoted dialogue inside — no other line in the unit
  does this (TST1: one primitive per idiom). *"He never minded it cold
  either."* deserves a straight dialogue rendering.
- **Rokusuke — same leak as A, softer.** *"nobody asks a hand till they
  need him"* — the general-truth wink again, though the post-Count
  placement (*"Forty loads. I had you at forty."*) is better than A's
  foreshadowing.

## Take C — "the-weather" (kernel #7 forward)

The best estate's-edge writing and the best node discipline (defs split
per node, matching the spec's talk-at-node shape). Toku, O-Ume, Matsuzō,
O-Yae, and Rokusuke peak here. Its household lines overreach: two
characters say aloud what the cast sheet locks as unsaid.

Per-character:

- **Genemon — BREAKS restraint at the gate.** *"The book doesn't care
  which you turn out to be. I confess I do."* — Genemon performing
  feeling aloud, from the man who "answers surprise by writing something
  down" and hands over no apology. A fine line for someone else; not his.
  (gen-hours also half-voices the private read: *"a man with a sum
  against his name somewhere"*.) The narrator gen-surprise line, by
  contrast, is the portrait done exactly: *"what he writes is not about
  the grain."*
- **Kihei — borderline twice.** *"Whatever it was you didn't do, you did
  not do it here."* — comes within a breath of saying the word the
  portrait says he never says. And the second "…"-spend (*"…You know the
  sill."*) is not a reappraisal-before-verdict — it's off-pattern for the
  licensed gesture. (*"A yard that empties when it's watched was never a
  drill yard."* is a licensed drill-ground maxim; legal.)
- **Sōan — clean.** *"Did you sleep, or did you lie down?"* is the best
  opening question in the unit; the narrator ledger line (*"he does not
  explain it"*) is correct seeding.
- **O-Hisa — clean.** The narrator woodshed line (*"Nobody knocked."*) is
  a fine touch; her spoken lines are a shade below B's.
- **Shinnosuke — two flaws.** shin-quiet stacks only ONE question
  (*"They do for me."* — statement second, breaking the two-at-a-time
  shape), and *"is it the room they don't open, or is he the room they
  don't open?"* is a writer's metaphor-riddle in a twelve-year-old's
  mouth — the authorial voice showing (any-game test: it reads
  literary-clever, not boy-curious).
- **Naoyuki — dilution.** Four break-off lines mechanize the tic (echo
  rule: near-duplication is a defect — three ambient veranda lines all
  running "formal — . flat imperative" make the gesture a pattern to
  crack). nao-cold's double break is individually good.
- **Toku — CLEAN and the unit's best.** *"We salted plums the summer the
  old lord's father died. Forty jars, that year. There are three. Take
  none of them."* — dated, counted, the decline in four sentences, and
  guarded. *"The draught is younger than you'd think."*
- **Yohei — clean.** *"…six mon, and I'll do my weeping on the road."*
  (*"it's arithmetic"* states the portrait's theme-word a touch nakedly.)
- **O-Yae — clean; the best.** *"The miller's wife says the house can't
  pay past autumn. Cook says the miller's wife should mind her own
  stones. I only carry the buckets."* — the shape at full power. (*"I say
  you rake. Well — you do."* — her one owned claim is the maximally
  blameless one; ruled in-shape.)
- **Matsuzō — clean; the banked clue done right.** *"The river was quiet
  that night. I've said so before. Nobody asked twice."* — the T3 origin
  clue sitting ignored because nobody asked twice: kernel #7 in one line.
- **Iori — BREAKS.** *"Is there rice water going spare? No matter if
  not."* — the sheet's whole point is *he wants nothing*; even the
  humblest ask inverts the tier's one correctly-priced stranger-kindness.
- **O-Ume — clean; the best.** *"Not from me — the river owes you, and
  I'm only handing it across."* / *"He was never one to miss rice over a
  little water."* — grief-work with its own dark tenderness, never
  mocked.
- **Rokusuke — CLEAN; the only take that gets him right.** *"Sixteen
  loads by my count. But if the board says fourteen, fourteen. The
  board's the board."* — the tally habit SHOWN pre-Count with zero
  portent, and the agreement tic doing the work. The dog line ties
  side-beat 4.

## THE PICK

**A per-character mix — clearly better than any single take** (each take
wins 4–5 characters outright and each carries at least one hard breach; B
is the best single take if a mix were refused, but the mix loses none of
B's peaks and sheds all three takes' worst lines):

| Character | Take | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Genemon | **A** | purest ledger; the private read stays private |
| Kihei | **B** | kihei-wall; one clean "…"; the coward-he-likes done unsaid |
| Sōan | **B** | the take-back question; Q→prescription throughout |
| O-Hisa | **B** | hope cut and replaced with care; "Both hands." |
| Shinnosuke | **B** | shin-taken is the unit's best mirror line |

| Matsuzō | **C** | "Nobody asked twice." — the banked clue, correctly unmade |
| Iori | **A** | the bow line; wants nothing, breaches nothing |

| Rokusuke | **C** | the tally shown without the portentous wink |

### Required redlines (blocking, on the mixed sheet)

1. **B-Shinnosuke: fix the speaker.** All four lines `voice: narrator` →
   the shinnosuke voice key. His questions must render as HIS (taste.md
   P3; TST4).
2. **B-Sōan: add the closed-ledger narrator line.** The picked set drops
   the sickroom's wrong-thing. Port C's soan-ledger (*"…he does not hurry
   about it, and he does not explain it."*) — A's and C's versions
   converged; C's is tighter.
3. **C-O-Yae: add one jizō-rumor line.** The mixed sheet loses B's only
   weir-jizō seed (oyae-jizo). Re-author its substance in C's register
   ("they say someone leaves rice-cakes… my mother says…") — the R0
   mystery needs an ambient carrier.
4. **B-Kihei: "The quiet lies." stands as his ONE ambient maxim** —
   licensed, watch-ground, sheet-wide budget of one. No other line in the
   mixed sheet may reach for a general truth (verified: none does after
   redlines 1–3).
5. **Unify the gating scheme at migration.** The mix inherits three
   conventions (A gate-free · B `wolf-held/named/count-cleared/
   pedlar-square/bon` · C `r3-wolf/r5-cleared/r7-named/orchard-dog-fed`).
   One flag vocabulary before the rewrite's flag pass; A's gate-free
   Genemon/Naoyuki/Iori lines route as always-available. Mechanical.

### Non-blocking notes

- **Bank, don't ship:** B's toku-sums (the private superstition — save it
  for an authored scene) and B's nao-closed (a good optional post-Count
  third touch for A-Naoyuki if the Count node wants ambient coverage).
- Genemon's R7 ambient line stays absent by design — the canon rung beat
  (*"The house's name for you, then. Earn a better."*) owns that moment;
  duplicating it ambiently would violate the echo rule. If a post-R7
  Genemon ambient is later wanted, B's gen-gonbei (*"Two of them worked
  out."*) is the donor.
- All three takes converged on O-Yae's river line ("I don't say it. They
  say it.") — it ships once, in C's rendering.
