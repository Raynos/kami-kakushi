## night-watch · the-watch-is-set

<!-- native: pre-rung story beat — fires at the board when the first rat
  loss is entered in the day-book. This beat introduces the "begin the

> Genemon comes out of the kura with the day-book open on one arm. He has
> been in there since the rice went up.

Genemon: "The near bale. Opened at the seam, low, where it meets the floor.
Two shō gone. Say three."

> He writes it standing. Kihei has crossed the yard without being sent for
> and studies the gnawed seam the way other men study a wound.

Kihei: "Rats now. Whatever hunts rats, after. I'll chalk a round."

Kihei: "Lantern, stick, the route as I mark it — gate, kura, the far edge,
back by the woodshed. You walk it with me tonight."

> You look at the stick.

Kihei: "For the rats, it's enough."

> Genemon enters the watch into the book under the lost rice, oil and wick
> and all.

## night-round · round-first-rats

<!-- native: the FIRST night round — a quest. The gate-post action puts him

> The estate at night is the same estate with the doors shut. Kihei walks

> In the kura the lantern finds them at the near bale: eyes, then bodies.

## night-round · round-marten

<!-- native: escalation stage — fires on a later round once the store rats
  are cleared; the marten ESCAPES with the dried fish the first night (the
  loss entered against his watch — R4's confession fuel) and is taken on a
  later round. -->

> With the rats gone the kura goes quiet, then too quiet, then one night
> the quiet has a smell in it — musk over the rice-dust.

> It is out of the lantern light before the light knows it was there. What
> it wanted was the dried fish on the high shelf, and it has some, and the
> far edge takes it. You chase as far as the light goes and stop where
> Kihei taught you to stop.

Kihei: "Marten. You're thinking of the shelf. Walk the round — the shelf
is one shelf."

> In the morning Genemon weighs what is left of the fish and writes the
> difference. He does not look up while he writes it, which is worse.

> Three rounds on, the lantern finds it flat along the shelf-beam, and this
> time the stick is where Kihei said to keep it. The book gets one line:
> marten, one, shelf mended.

## night-round · round-wolf

<!-- native: the R3 climax round — authored, fires once, in the lean end of
  winter. Survived-not-won is the ONLY outcome: the fight ends staged (the
  wolf withdraws hurt); he cannot die here but CAN fall first — a fall
  wakes him in the sickroom and the round re-fires a later night.
  Completing it triggers the R3 rung beat at first light. -->

> Winter, the lean end of it. You know before you reach the kura, because
> the yard is wrong — no rat-noise, no owl, the mule pulling at its rope.

> It is at the sill when the lantern finds it, and it does not run from the
> light. It is bigger than the stories and thinner than the stories, and it
> looks at you the way nothing on this estate looks at you: with a decision
> to make.

> You do not remember most of it in order. The lantern goes first. There is
> the doorframe at your back, the stick, a weight that comes in low, and
> teeth — and the one thing you keep is the route in your feet: you do not
> leave the doorway. Whatever it gets, it gets through you.

> Something in your side goes. You hear it more than feel it.

> Then the weight is gone. It crosses the yard low and uneven, favoring a
> foreleg, and the dark past the far edge takes it.

> You sit down on the sill because it is that or fall on it. The blood on
> the boards is mostly yours. Behind you, in the dark, the rice is where
> it was.

## prose flavor

<!-- native: new v2 node keys — the kura node and the night-round post must

### kuraNode

The kura stands off the omoya's corner, whitewash gone grey — the working
store of a house that keeps less than it built for. Rice on the raised
floor, tools on the walls, dried fish on the high shelf. Over the door a
seal-plate carries the house crest with one more petal than the gate's.
Nobody reads it aloud.

### nightRoundPost

The round starts at the gate post: lantern, stick, Kihei's route chalked on
the board — gate, kura, the far edge, the woodshed. Begin it and the estate
goes to its night shape around you.

### nightRoundNewMoon

On the dark of the moon the yard's far edge has, once, a light in it: a
lantern hooded low, moving upstream, unhurried. At breakfast no one is
missing, and no one asks.

### nightRoundFall

You wake on the sickroom mat with the lantern smell still on you. Sōan asks
what the ground was doing when you met it, listens, and prescribes the mat
until sundown. What the night took, the morning count will say.
