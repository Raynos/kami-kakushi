# U3 · R3 the grain-watch — VERDICT

## Take A — "the ledger"

What works: the best fight prose of the three — *"You do not remember
most of it in order"*, *"bigger than the stories and thinner than the
stories"*, *"it looks at you the way nothing on this estate looks at
you: with a decision to make"* — survived-not-won rendered exactly (the
blood mostly yours, the rice behind you where it was). Genemon's
item/count/condition is pitch-perfect (*"The near bale. Opened at the
seam, low... Two shō gone. Say three."*), the R3-alone spec is
dramatized in one line (*"From tonight the round is yours and mine. No
third."*), and A is the only take that ships the requirements block.
The decide is rule-7 clean — an epistemics-of-the-entry choice, Kihei
docking the overclaim.

Violations:

- **Epigram budget broken twice, once unlicensed (§0.5.2).** The rung
  scene carries Kihei's *"A watch holds a door. It doesn't buy a
  pelt."* AND Genemon's *"The book does well on what a man knows, and
  poorly on the rest."* — two maxim-shaped lines in one scene (cap: one
  total), the second from the man who holds no license and "has never
  in his life reached for a metaphor." The header's own claim ("No
  maxims anywhere in this unit") is false twice.
- **The copied gesture recurs (the audit's slop pattern, cross-unit).**
  Req flavor: *"The rows don't know he keeps a watch," {elder} says* —
  the same rows-personification U1-A was dinged for (*"Their rows do
  not know it yet"*), in Genemon's mouth again. The exact defect the
  wave exists to retire, recurring under the same stance.
- **The R4 seam doesn't hold (canon).** The header claims the marten
  loss is "the granary loss R4's confession spends" — but the prose
  spends it: *"Genemon weighs what is left of the fish and writes the
  difference."* A loss already weighed and entered leaves R4's *"he
  limps to the board and CONFESSES the granary loss"* with nothing to
  confess. (And it is dried fish, not grain.)
- Minor: *"The work is short and without honor on either side"* —
  narration grading a rat-kill; epigram-lean for the ledger stance.

## Take B — "the held breath"

What works: the only take where the R3↔R4 seam actually functions —
the marten night uncovers the gnawed seed-bale, *"You make the count
alone, by lantern, twice... You bar the door on it and tell no one
yet"*, then Genemon's *"Grain — none lost"* lands as dramatic irony
(*"you do not correct him. Not yet."*) — the shame R4's confession
spends, seeded and held, flag `r3-bale-count` named in the engine
contract. The declared maxim is earned and this game's own (*"Stand
where the rice is long enough and you will meet everything in this
valley, in order of teeth."*). Voices: Kihei's *"…Alive. Good."* is his
ellipsis, and *"For a moment there is more in him than the verdict. He
checks the broken bar instead"* is restraint enacted; Genemon's tally
(*"Screen, one, torn. Bar, one, broken... Hand, one. Standing."*) is
his shape whole, and *"whatever he came to say, he writes something in
the day-book first"* is the cast-sheet behavior dramatized, not
quoted; O-Hisa's *"If the ribs knit as straight — well."* uses her
assigned unfinished-hope shape with new content (shape-true, not the
U1-B verbatim paste). The wolf flavor passes the any-game test cold:
*"the dark around them is the wrong shape for a dog. The bar is behind
you. So is the rice."* The decide is rule-7 clean — no axis, and the
boldest option is answered from the unexpected quarter (Sōan, not
Kihei). The engine-contract block makes wiring concrete.

Violations / weaknesses:

- **A stolen ellipsis-react (§0.5.2 one-owner).** Sōan: *"Breathe to
  the bottom of it. …No. Cracked, then, not broken."* — the held beat
  of reappraisal before a verdict is Kihei's ALONE (04-cast, assigned
  2026-07-07). Two-second fix; still a violation as written.
- **A second maxim-shape in the watch-set scene (§0.5.2 cap).**
  *"A watch that wanders is no watch."* closes watch-2 — quotable
  general truth in the same scene as the declared watch-3 maxim. The
  route order already does its work.
- **Wrong role tag (TST4 / canon).** `O-Hisa (steward):` — she is
  Katsuhide's wife at the kitchen; Genemon is the steward. Same defect
  class as U1-A's `Naoyuki (steward)`. The header even admits the
  missing NAMES entry — the placeholder must not say something false.
- **Accidental near-duplication (the echo craft rule).** Sōan closing
  the ledger "when you turn your head" appears near-verbatim twice —
  the `night-round-fall` flavor and the `r3-ribs` ask. One is a
  gesture; two is a template.

## Take C — "the weather"

What works: the best kernel-#7 texture of the wave. The O-Yae rumor
beat is gold — *"a stray draws strays, they say. Them, not me. I only
carry it."* — the world misreading him dramatized, voice exactly hers;
*"no one asks him what he sees at night anyway"* prices the new-moon
seed correctly; the Sōan coda (*"closes a ledger when he comes in —
without hurry, as if it were the next thing he had meant to do
anyway"*) is the closed-ledger thread at its most alive. The decide is
rule-3 exemplary — the player literally asks for things (a boar-spear,
an oak plank and an iron strap). The wolf's break-off (*"it will not
spend more on a door that fights... the way wild things decide"*) is
fine animal writing.

Violations:

- **Third-person narration (one primitive per idiom, TST1).** The
  narration runs *"he / the man who kept them"* throughout; the game's
  log narrates in second person around the `Nameless:` label (the
  built rung-beats, A and B, the speaker ladder). Shipping C forks the
  narration voice for one rung or forces a full-person rewrite —
  stance carried by the wrong lever.
- **Epigram saturation (§0.5.2 — the audit's #1 slop pattern).** The
  rung scene stacks *"That is what a watch buys it"*, *"Lean enough,
  there is no difference"*, and Genemon's *"Three days of a watchman
  costs less than a burial"* — three quotable generalizations in one
  scene (cap: one), one from a non-holder. The narration epigraphs too:
  *"nothing missing looks the same as nothing done"*, *"steps over it
  without looking, which is its own kind of looking"* (the mirrored
  template, recurring).
- **The count contradicts itself (canon/internal).** Watch-set:
  *"Three bales since the autumn count. Call it sixty mon, eaten in
  the dark."* Rung: *"Forty-one. Forty-one at the autumn count. None
  opened."* — the pre-watch loss vanishes from the book Genemon keeps.
  And nothing unconfessed remains for R4's granary-loss confession.
- **An unlosable authored fight (systems canon).** *"This night never
  falls through to the sickroom wake"* — t0.md's rounds canon is
  finish OR fall-to-sickroom, everywhere; A handles it correctly (a
  fall re-fires the night later). A prose take doesn't get to script
  invulnerability.
- **R2's move spent twice (echo craft).** *"The title arrives the way
  the yard-round did: it is simply not taken back."* — reusing the
  silent rung's signature line dilutes the one rung that owns it.
- Minor: Genemon's *"You are the cost of a lamp"* reaches toward
  figure from the no-metaphor character (cost-arithmetic idiom —
  borderline, flag not fatal). Writing *"One wolf, driven"* into the
  day-book overclaims the survived-not-won spec A's decide explicitly
  litigates.

## THE PICK — take B

B is the only take where the unit does its structural job: R3's
survived-not-won AND the held, unconfessed loss that makes R4's
confession a scene instead of a recap. Its law breaks are the
smallest and the most mechanical of the three — a mislabel, a stolen
ellipsis, one surplus maxim-shape, one duplicated gesture — every one
a line-edit, none load-bearing. A has the finest fight prose but
hands Genemon an unlicensed maxim, repeats the exact rows-personification
slop U1 already flagged, and its own R4 claim is contradicted by its
own prose; C has the best world-texture but forks the narration
person, saturates the rung scene with epigrams, and breaks its own
bale arithmetic. A false green on A ships the recurring slop pattern;
on C, a voice fork and a broken count. B ships the spec.
