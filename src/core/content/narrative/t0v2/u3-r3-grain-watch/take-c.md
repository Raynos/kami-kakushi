## native watch-set · the watch is set
native: the "begin the night round" action at its gate post; the FIRST round
  runs as a quest (on rails through the zones' night states — gate, kura,
  paddy racks, woodshed wall); repeatable after; a fall ends in Sōan's
  sickroom with days lost. Escalation across repeats: rats → marten → the
  authored wolf night below.

> Evening at the board. Genemon has the autumn tally open and a gnawed
> sack-corner on the table like evidence at a hearing.

Genemon: "Three bales since the autumn count. Call it sixty mon, eaten in
the dark."

Kihei: "Then it wants a watch."

> Neither of them looks at the hired hands' bench. Genemon looks at him.

Genemon: "The hands sleep in the village. You sleep at the woodshed. You are
the cost of a lamp."

> Kihei walks the round with him once, at dusk, naming it.

Kihei: "Gate. Kura. The racks. The woodshed wall. Once every wick-burn until
the grey. Take the oak stave — a blade in the dark cuts the wielder."

> The first rat dies by the seed shelf; the second gets the wall gap. In the
> morning nobody asks how the round went. The sacks are the answer, and the
> sacks say nothing yet.

## native wolf-night · the round's climax
native: the authored climax fight, once. Outcome fixed — the wolf breaks off
  wounded below a kill and escapes (it returns in T1 on the boundary-stone
  fields); the MC takes cracked ribs, an injury that gates heavy labour
  until healed. This night never falls through to the sickroom wake — he
  finishes the round standing.

> Deep winter, the far turn of the round. The mule will not settle. There
> are no rats tonight; there is nothing tonight, and the nothing has weight.

> The wolf is at the kura sill when he comes back around — a long grey shape
> working at the door-gap with its shoulder, winter-thin, past caution.

> It goes to the ground twice and comes back twice. What ends it is the
> doorway: it cannot get past him to the gap, and it will not spend more on
> a door that fights. It breaks off all at once, the way wild things decide,
> and goes off the way the ground slopes, not hurrying, leaving a dark line
> in the snow.

> There is blood on the sill. He sorts out whose by lamplight: most of it
> his. The door-gap held. The rice never knew.

> He does not follow the line into the dark. He sits against the door with
> the stave across his knees, breathing in halves, and keeps the watch to
> grey. Nothing else comes.

## prose flavor

### kura-post

The kura keeps its own weather: cold stone, straw, the sweet dust of rice
bran. Over the door a seal-plate carries the house crest — counted twice by
lamplight, it has one petal more than the gate's. Nobody has painted it out,
and nobody reads it aloud.

### night-round-rats

Two rats by the seed shelf, quick work with the stave-butt. He lays them on
the wall for the crows, who at least are pleased.

### night-round-marten

The rats have gone quiet, which is not the same as gone: something quicker
is killing them ahead of him and taking the dried ayu besides. It is out of
the lamplight before the light knows a marten was in it. Kihei's whole
comment, told of it: kill the quicker thief first.

### night-round-quiet

Nothing moves the whole round. The house burns its one lamp down and sleeps.
No entry gets made for a quiet watch; the proof it happened is that nothing
is missing, and nothing missing looks the same as nothing done.

### night-round-newmoon

On the dark of the moon, at the yard's far edge, a lantern crosses — hooded
to a coin of light, going upstream, at a walk that knows the ground. By the
time he reaches the wall there is only the river sound. It is not his to
ask, and no one asks him what he sees at night anyway.

### night-round-shared

Some nights now Kihei walks the first turn with him, saying nothing the
length of it, and peels off at the gate. No other hand is ever set to the
round. Whether that is trust or economy, nobody says.

### kura-sill-after

The sill is scrubbed. One patch of the wood stays whiter than the rest,
where the scrubbing had the most to do. The household steps over it without
looking, which is its own kind of looking.
