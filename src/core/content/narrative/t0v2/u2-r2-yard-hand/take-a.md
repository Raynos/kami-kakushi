## rung R2 · rung-r2
speaker: rokusuke
voice: villager
motivates: panel-calendar, verb-turn-season, verb-yard-round

> Morning, the forecourt. The day's tasks go out from the steward's window,
> one by one, to the hands standing in first light. The yard is not called.

> The brush keeps moving behind the window. He does not look up.

Rokusuke: "Yard wasn't called."

You: "No."

Rokusuke: "It won't be. Keep the rake where you can find it."

> The rake is where he left it, against the woodshed wall.

> The gate first, then the well-path, the stack, the forecourt stones. Raked,
> the court shows its size — swept edge to swept edge, ground laid out for a
> household five times this one.

> At the woodshed door he cuts a mark, low on the post where the wood is
> soft. One, for the day kept. He stands a moment. There is room on the post
> for a winter.

### ask r2-before · "Who had the yard?"

Rokusuke: "Three men, once. Two when I came. The last quit for the lowlands
at the thaw. Nobody called it his, either."

### ask r2-day · "What day is it?"

Rokusuke: "Two short of Yohei's stall. Count from that — the rest of us do."

> Evening, the kitchen door. O-Hisa holds out the measure — rice, not the
> bowl.

O-Hisa (steward): "The steward wrote it this morning. Rice, evenings, while
the yard is kept. Bring your bowl tomorrow."

### decide · The first measure is yours.
