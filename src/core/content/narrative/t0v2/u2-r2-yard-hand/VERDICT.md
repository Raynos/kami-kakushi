# VERDICT · u2-r2-yard-hand (R2, the silent rung)

## Take A — "the ledger" (maximal restraint)

The cleanest law sheet of the three, and the thinnest scene. What works:
the promotion-by-absence is staged exactly right — *"The brush keeps
moving behind the window. He does not look up."* — no summons, no
praise, the yard simply not called. The calendar unlock lands in one
restrained image (*"There is room on the post for a winter."*) and the
day-count is grounded in the economy the way t0.md wants (*"Two short of
Yohei's stall. Count from that — the rest of us do."* — day-of-week
load-bearing, the market as the clock). Kihei's one line (*"Bank it
before dark. Sparks sit in straw."*) is his imperative→verdict shape,
licensed and under the one-maxim cap. Rule 7 holds: the decide is
practical logistics, answered by three different quarters, no
warmth dial. Violations / defects: **(1)** `O-Hisa (steward):` — twice —
borrows the *steward* voice class for a woman who is not the steward;
under taste principle 3 (voice set at source, renderer colours by
category) this ships her in Genemon's colour. A wiring defect, but a
real one, and the take leans on it rather than flagging a new voice
properly. **(2)** Her line *"If the pot's on anyway — well."* is her
canon "if — well" shape, correctly hers — fine — but her OTHER line has
her relaying the steward's terms (*"The steward wrote it this
morning…"*), which hands the rung's ledger moment to the wrong
character; Genemon's shape (item, count, condition) is the natural
carrier of a pay-terms line and A never uses him. **(3)** Rokusuke is
slightly over-authoritative for a man whose whole register is agreeing
with his head down (*"It won't be. Keep the rake where you can find
it."* — directive, twice). Not a violation, a stretch. Craft: no slop
patterns; if anything the scene ends TWO beats early — the future the
spec asks for ("a man counts days again when he has a future") is
present but only just.

## Take B — "the held breath" (grief close under the surface)

The most affecting prose of the wave — *"Lower on the post, grey with
age, older rows. … Every row ends even."* is the best single image in
any take, and the native note correctly renders the calendar panel AS
the doorpost (kernel #6, exactly). *"He stands at the board the way a
man stands to be told, and is not told"* is the silent rung in one
sentence. Violations: **(1) shape ownership (§0.5.2) — the real one.**
O-Hisa twice performs the abandon-and-restate break-off: *"Bring it
back when — bring it back."* and *"You'll be at the threshold come
morning for the — no. The kitchen doesn't feed a man twice-paid."* That
construction — begins, breaks off at feeling, restates flat — is
**Naoyuki's** assigned shape ("his sentences break off when they come
too near feeling… restates flat and cold"). O-Hisa's canon guard is the
unfinished *if* (*"If the rice holds — well."*); B gives her the
neighbour's gesture, twice, and it is the emotional engine of the take —
not trimmable without gutting it. **(2)** The measure-arithmetic speech
(*"Three shō. Five gō the day and it comes out even at the market…"*)
is item-count-condition — Genemon's shape — in her mouth, AND it
hard-codes magnitudes in dialogue with no sim-owned flag (ADR-132
tension; take C flags the same problem, B doesn't). **(3)** Restraint:
*"It is a long time since he had a day worth counting."* narrates the
spec's theme aloud — the one on-the-nose line in an otherwise
restrained take. **(4)** Borderline: *"Nobody counts water."* is a
maxim-shaped flourish from a non-license-holder; small, but the license
is fully spent (Munemasa + Kihei, no one else). Craft: high; no
chiasmus, no ventriloquism — but the break-off gesture stamped twice in
one scene is exactly the copy-pasted-gesture pattern the audit flagged,
here at character-collision grade.

## Take C — "the weather" (kernel #7 forward)

The strongest canon fidelity and the strongest scene logic. Genemon is
pitch-perfect and load-bearing: *"Rice from the new moon. One measure
the day, dry, weighed at the kura door. Meals were terms for a man
staying a week."* — item, count, condition, no warmth, does not slow;
and the ask-at-the-window response — *"The book has had it so three
days now."* — is kernel #3 whole: the promotion existed in ink before
anyone spoke it. Kihei appears as behaviour only (*"His eye snags on
your grip on the broom, a beat too long for a broom."* — the
failed-fight misread, seeded without a word; his "…"-react correctly
NOT spent here). O-Yae is deployed exactly per the cast sheet —
everything "they say", the conduit working in both directions, and the
moved-wood decide response (*"They say the stranger rearranges the
yard. Then the rain came and the kindling was dry. They say that too —
quieter."*) is the best rule-7 consequence in the wave: read by an
unexpected quarter, arriving late, warmth not the reward. Rokusuke's
*"The yard shows from the window. The paddy doesn't. I do the paddy."*
is the yard's oldest lesson in his own register. The spec's core image —
*"You take the broom. Nobody takes it back."* — is the rung in eight
words. Violations — all in the NARRATOR, none in the cast: **(1)
restraint / ornament-by-rarity.** The take saturates on narrator
aphorism: *"A meal ends with the eating. Rice keeps. A man with rice
put by has a next month, whether he asked for one or not — and a next
month wants counting."* — the first two sentences are earned; the rest
is the theme explained aloud (the spec text ventriloquised into the
narration). Plus three polished flavor closers: *"Nothing else here
does."*, *"Most things will be."*, and O-Yae's *"I carry news. I don't
make it."* Individually fine; together this is the audit's
epigram-saturation pattern at low grade — four ornaments where the law
wants one, maybe two. **(2)** Minor: the moved-wood option credits
`memory: genemon +1` while O-Yae speaks — defensible (the yard shows
from the window; the book enters it) but opaque on first read.

## THE PICK — Take C

**C**, with redlines. The reasoning: A's flaws are wiring-and-thinness,
B's flaw is structural (a §0.5.2 shape collision fused to the take's
emotional core — fixing it removes what makes B worth picking), C's
flaws are four cuttable narrator lines sitting NEXT to the best
character work in the wave. C is the only take where the rung's meaning
arrives entirely through the cast and the ledger — which is what this
game is — and it covers the spec most completely (promotion-by-absence,
meals→rice with the steward as its correct voice, the calendar bundle
with the post-tally as the panel's fiction, four motivates ids, and two
free canon seeds: Kihei's misread and the river-trade rumor reaching
him through O-Yae, both t0.md-placed). Judged by the values when the
principles run out: TST3 (the fiction causes the mechanic — the book
had it three days before he asked) and kernel #7 (the world reads him
sideways and keeps moving) are C's spine.

**Salvage note:** B's doorpost image (*"older rows… Every row ends
even."*) is better than C's first-mark flavor and is narrator-voice,
not character-voice — it can migrate into C's `r2-first-mark` /
`calendar-post` lane without importing B's violation. Recommended, not
required.

## Redlines on C before wiring

1. **Cut the theme-essay.** End the rice narration at *"Rice keeps."*
   Delete *"A man with rice put by has a next month, whether he asked
   for one or not — and a next month wants counting."* — the notch
   flavor (`r2-first-mark`) already carries the counting; the law says
   say less.
2. **Thin the flavor aphorisms to one.** Keep `r2-yard-round`'s first
   sentence, cut *"Nothing else here does."*; in `r2-heard-late` cut
   *"Most things will be."* (the wedding being old news already says
   it). O-Yae's *"I carry news. I don't make it."* may stay — it is her
   one polish and it is characterization, not ornament — but it is then
   the scene's ONLY narrator-adjacent flourish.
3. **Registry gate (already flagged in the take):** O-Yae needs
   NAMES / voices.ts / NPC_NAME entries before compile — do not ship
   her under a borrowed voice class (the exact defect take A has with
   O-Hisa).
4. **Magnitude guard:** *"One measure the day"* is deliberately
   unit-vague — keep it that way; do not "improve" it to shō/gō at
   build (B's mistake). The sim owns the number (ADR-132).
5. **Optional, recommended:** replace/augment `calendar-post`-class
   flavor with B's salvaged older-rows image (per the salvage note),
   via the `@`-reuse mechanism if the wave adopts it — deliberate echo,
   credited.
