## rung R2 · rung-r2
speaker: rokusuke
voice: villager
motivates: panel-calendar, action-season-turn, verb-yard-round,
  row-rice-wage

> First light. The broom stands against the gatepost where you left it.
> The hand who kept the yard's round quit for the lowlands; nobody has
> said whose it is now.

> You take the broom. Nobody takes it back.

Rokusuke: "The yard shows from the window. The paddy doesn't. I do the
paddy."

> He does not say whose window.

> The round takes the morning: the gate swept, the forecourt raked, the
> water in, the wood up off the ground. At the kitchen door O-Yae counts
> the ladles back onto their hooks while you stand there. Not unkindly.
> She counts them every time.

O-Yae: "The mill house married a daughter, if nobody told you. Three
days back. They say it was a good wedding. They say other things too."

> Kihei crosses at watch-change. His eye snags on your grip on the
> broom, a beat too long for a broom. He says nothing and does not
> stop.

> Dusk round, then the kitchen threshold, the bowl. Genemon comes past
> with the day-book under his arm and does not slow.

Genemon: "Rice from the new moon. One measure the day, dry, weighed at
the kura door. Meals were terms for a man staying a week."

> He is through the door before you can set the bowl down.

> A meal ends with the eating. Rice keeps. A man with rice put by has a
> next month, whether he asked for one or not — and a next month wants
> counting.

### ask r2-whose · "Whose round was this?"

Rokusuke: "A man's who left for the lowlands. Called it light work."

> Rokusuke looks from the yard to the window, and from the window to the
> paddy, and goes to the paddy.

### ask r2-they-say · "What else do they say?"

O-Yae: "Nothing to me. To each other — a river gives a man back, people
ask what it was paid. I carry news. I don't make it."

### decide · Nobody gave you the round, and nobody takes it back. How do you hold it?

#### r2-kept-order · "Run it as it was run."

Rokusuke: "Good. Same corners, same hours. Nobody comes asking why."

memory: rokusuke +1 (steady)
flags: r2-kept-order

#### r2-moved-wood · "Move the wood-stack out of the rain."

O-Yae: "They say the stranger rearranges the yard. Then the rain came
and the kindling was dry. They say that too — quieter."

memory: genemon +1 (entered)
flags: r2-moved-wood

#### r2-asked-window · "Ask at the window whose round it is."

Genemon: "Yours while it is done. One round, morning and dusk, the gate
and the forecourt. The book has had it so three days now."

memory: genemon +1 (squared)
flags: r2-asked-window

## prose flavor

### r2-first-mark

That night you cut a mark into the woodshed post, low, where the grain
hides it. One day, kept.

### r2-yard-round

The round comes back the same size every day. Nothing else here does.

### r2-heard-late

The mill house wedding is old news by the time it reaches the yard.
Most things will be.
