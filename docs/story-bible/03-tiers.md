# Story Bible §2 — the tier ladder: seven circles

> Part of [the story bible](README.md). The structure is human-locked
> canon; the seven per-tier ladders below await one whole-ladder lock.

## The structure

**SEVEN tiers (T0–T6)** — a build/PRD-cutting change (tier enum 0..5 →
0..6; the ADR lands with Phase 4's re-plan). Time-skips at tier seams;
the game spans ~13 years (1780 → 1793+); Shinnosuke grows 12→~25 on
camera. The house ladder is ONE unbroken career built across all seven
tiers — each tier's ladder ends where the next one starts, and every
rung stays plausible for a nameless-born man ("steward" is a T5 word,
not a T0 word). The career in one line: *hand → named hand → registered
man → officer → expeditionary → under-steward → steward → the house
itself.*

**Rungs alternate.** Every other rung the MC is INSIDE the estate —
repairing, growing, strengthening — and every other rung he is OUTSIDE,
increasing the house's strength in the world. In **T2 and T3 the
alternation is a HARD LOCK**: an inside rung locks the world out (the
estate map only — forced estate actions, skills, levelling); an outside
rung locks the estate closed until the objective is done. **T4 drops
the gimmick** — free travel everywhere from then on. (Mechanics ADR at
build time.)

**Parallel reputation tracks.** The VILLAGE (from T2) and the ORIGIN
TOWN (T3) carry their own reputation tracks, completely distinct from
house rungs — they never convert 1:1. In **T6 the ladder's subject
changes: the rungs become the HOUSE'S Edo standing (H0→H7)**, not the
man's — kernel #4 completed in mechanics.

**The setting under all of it** (see [`02-house.md`](02-house.md) THE
RUIN, and [`05-world.md`](05-world.md) the estate anatomy): through
T0–T1 everything called "the main house" is the grand GUEST HOUSE of
the old jin'ya seat; the true compound is a ruin, revealed ~T2 and
restored tier by tier. The home base grows EVERY tier.

## The seven ladders *(⏳ awaiting the whole-ladder lock)*

### T0 · The Estate — the household (1780)

It starts with a body at the gate: fed once out of form, day-labor
bought by the day, the log calling him "the man from the weir." It ends
with the use-name written in the day-book by GENEMON — not the lord: T0
ends and he has never properly met Shigemasa, who is a figure on a
veranda, a voice through a wall. He earns a fixed sleeping place in the
yard buildings, the threshold of the board, and the house's tool-name.
The ruined true compound looms at the map's edge — visible, locked,
unexplained.

*Theme: from weather to tool — the house learns his hands, not his
face.* The tier keeps its variety: the wolf, Kihei's drill yard,
forage and crafting-lite, and the market COMES TO HIM (Tokubei's
pedlar at the gate; no town access). Beats carried: the cold-open
speaker flip (`You:` → `Nameless:`, witnessed) · the wolf
survived-not-won · the shame→begging arc + Kihei's creed · the
accusation night (the Count: the packet, the day-book clears him,
no apology) · the use-name.

**Rungs:** R0 **The man from the weir** (pulled out; raking; the
speaker flip) · R1 **The day-hand** (kept by arithmetic — two hands
quit that week) · R2 **The yard-hand** (the silent rung: a task simply
not taken back) · R3 **The grain-watch** (the wolf — survived, not
won; ribs cracked) · R4 **The pupil** (he begs for drills; the creed,
once; the drill yard opens) · R5 **The accused** (the Count night) ·
R6 **The trusted hand** (the first coin errand, counted back to the
mon) · R7 **The named hand** (Genemon writes the hand-me-down name;
sleep; the first dream).

**Zones:** the Weir & riverbank · the Gate & gateyard · the Forecourt ·
the Woodshed (his corner) · the Kitchen threshold & board-side · the
Kura exterior + grain-store · the Drill yard (the old stable court) ·
the Home paddy & vegetable rows · the Woodlot edge (kindling, forage —
wolf country) · the shrine-alcove corridor (glimpsed once) · Sōan's
sickroom. The ruined compound: visible scenery, locked.

### T1 · The Estate — the land & "the main house" (1781–82)

Starts: the named hand sent out of the yard with the paid crews — the
terraces, the woodlot proper, the works. The tier's arc is access: the
slow earning of the right to work, then walk, then be trusted inside
"the main house" — in truth the grand guest house's inner wings — a
third of it by tier's end. Cast members are met in the wings as they
reopen: O-Sato airing a room that was hers, Toku at the alcove,
Naoyuki where his brother's things are stored. Trust arrives as
objects and keys: a tool of his own, then the KURA KEY — and the
storehouse's secrets with it (the memorial alcove kept wrong; the old
ledgers' cut page-stubs).

It ends in the room he himself restored: the shoin, the first room
brought back — Shigemasa's only scene. The dying lord writes him into
the house register as his LAST ENTRY; three lines up, unstruck, no
death date: Katsuhide. The lord dies at the seam. *Theme: from tool to
man of the house — literally: the house lets him in one wing at a
time.* *Skip ~2 years.*

**Rungs:** R0 **The field-hand** (the crews) · R1 **The terrace-hand**
(the paddies his) · R2 **The woodcutter** (the lot; the charcoal
clamp) · R3 **The kura-warden** (the key; the alcove; the stubs) ·
R4 **The wing-hand** (first steps inside the wings) · R5 **The
tally-keeper** (the day-book tallies under Genemon) · R6 **The
wing-warden** (the east wing under his charge) · R7 **The registered
man** (the restored shoin; Shigemasa's last entry).

**Zones:** the Terraced paddies (upper & lower) · the full Woodlot +
the charcoal clamp · the Weir & flood-works (the OLD BREACH among
them) · the Kura interior · the Workshops (joiner's bench; the cold
forge, lit late-tier) · the Boundary stones & far fields (boar) · the
Family plot (Bon; the sandals) — and the guest house's inner reaches:
the East wing (cleared room by room) · the West wing (closed rooms —
Katsuhide's things) · the inner garden · the Shoin (restored last, for
the scene). The true compound stays locked scenery.

### T2 · The Valley — lordless (1784)

Starts: sent to speak WITH THE HOUSE'S NAME IN HIS MOUTH — a regency's
messenger to a village that watches a house with no lord at its head.
Early in the tier THE REVEAL lands (three signals, no lecture: village
rumor at the well · the compound's old plans found in the kura, the
"main house" a small box labeled *guest quarters* · one Toku
past-tense line) — and the map re-labels itself: what he earned his
way into was the guest house; the true restoration begins with the
ruin's outer domain and the gatehouse, the estate's new face to the
valley. The house and staff GROW: new hires under him, more buildings
waking, and the village rumors it — "that yard was a town once."

It ends at the yard-officer: daily operations his, the regency leaning
on him in fact but not yet in title. *Theme: carrying a diminished
name in public while the house grows bones again.* Main-quest work
runs in BOTH arenas — the village's troubles (the bandit camp made
playable) and the estate's expansion — under the hard-lock
alternation: inside rungs shut the valley out; outside rungs shut the
estate until the objective lands. The village reputation track opens
at zero, parallel: stranger's surcharge, watched hands.

**Rungs:** R0 **The house's messenger** *(outside)* · R1 **The
works-hand of the outer court** *(inside — the ruin's outer domain
cleared)* · R2 **The dues-carrier** *(outside — village + temple dues;
Genemon's silver, the payment road)* · R3 **The steward's shadow**
*(inside — the ledgers, deeper)* · R4 **The market-man** *(outside —
the stall dealings; the surcharge visible)* · R5 **The works-master**
*(inside — the gatehouse raised; the clearances; the mill share)* ·
R6 **The house's voice** *(outside — speaks for the house at the
village council, witnessed)* · R7 **The yard-officer** *(inside — the
staff and yards under him)*.

**Zones:** the Village (well & lanes · market square · shrine & temple
with the register of the vanished · the headman's house · the mill ·
the ferry & bridge) · the Valley woods (dark under-forest) · the Hill
shrines (high paths) · the River gorge · the Old quarry · the Bandit
camp (the valley's trouble — a real fight arc) · the Night roads ·
other houses' fields — and the estate's growth: the ruin's Outer
domain · the rebuilt Gatehouse · the New clearances.

### T3 · The Region — the expeditionary & the origin (1786)

Starts: the house's EXPEDITIONARY — sent out because he has earned
trust AND strength: road-guard to the house's goods, opener of routes,
the house's reach made flesh. The region answers force as often as
paper. The ORIGIN mainline runs here: the rumor-trail leads to the
origin town — the nearest LARGER town, a full content hub — and to his
own name and what his vanishing did. He KNOWS by tier's end; the ink
waits for the end of the game, his choice. The origin-town reputation
track opens at zero and colder: there he is a rumor about a dead man,
and house standing buys nothing.

It ends at the house's factor — a HOUSE office, the outward-facing
charge — while inside, the ruin's wings and inner domain open (the
steward's office migrates to the East hall; the sealed storerooms of
the inner domain come within reach: the BURIED TRUTH). *Theme: where
nobody knows the house, he IS the house.* Hard-lock alternation, as
T2.

**Rungs:** R0 **The road-guard** *(outside)* · R1 **The house's
quartermaster** *(inside — provisions and gear for the expeditions)* ·
R2 **The caravan-master** *(outside)* · R3 **The keeper of the
far-ledger** *(inside — the expedition books; the East hall office)* ·
R4 **The rumor-follower** *(outside — the origin trail)* · R5 **The
inner-wing warden** *(inside — the ruin's inner domain opens; the
sealed storerooms found)* · R6 **The expedition-leader** *(outside —
new routes; region alliances)* · R7 **The house's factor** *(inside)*.

**Zones:** the Mountains (the high passes, the ridge shrines — dead
white in winter) · the Deep forest (older and darker than the valley
woods) · the Grasslands (open road country) · the Riverfront (the
barge towns' edge) · the Post-towns (4–5 zones each) · **the Origin
town** (a full hub: market · register office · temple · inn quarter ·
the family holding outside it · the grave that carries his name) ·
bandit country proper · the Charcoal valleys — and the estate: the
East & West halls · the inner domain · the sealed storerooms.

### T4 · The Castle Town — the campaign (1788–89)

Starts: the house's ENVOY at the town gates — diplomatic, a real house
title, and doors open halfway. The tier has a FACE for an enemy: the
lord of the castle town — the office whose hands are in BOTH old
wounds (the famine-year arrears that priced Katsuhide's arithmetic;
the flood-works skim the buried truth names) — now moving to swallow
the region the house anchors. Mid-saga (≈R3–R4) Katsuhide is FOUND at
the temple quarter and RENOUNCES IN WRITING; the house, whole at last,
names its new heir. Then the campaign turns: open contest — and it
ends with the house TAKING the castle town, the enemy's machine
broken, the town's charge in the house's hands.

It ends at the UNDER-STEWARD: the campaign won and the internal ladder
landing at the stewardship's doorstep. *Theme: the house stops
petitioning power and contests it.* (Kernel #5 holds: the enemy-lord
answers; his patrons above him walk; the daimyō is never a scene.) The
hard-lock gimmick is DROPPED — free travel everywhere; side-quests and
hard encounters send the player back through region and valley zones.
*Skip; genpuku at the seam.*

**Rungs:** R0 **The house's envoy** *(house)* · R1 **The petitioner**
*(outside — the humiliation rung: paper power used against him, once,
so taking the town later means something)* · R2 **The keeper of the
town-house** *(house — the rented room becomes the house's town
compound)* · R3 **The finder** *(outside — Katsuhide, the temple
quarter)* · R4 **The witness** *(house — the renunciation; the heir
named)* · R5 **The challenger** *(outside — the weighing-yard, the
tribunal)* · R6 **The master of the town-house** *(house — a true
second seat)* · R7 **The under-steward** *(house — the town's charge
held)*.

**Zones:** THE CASTLE TOWN, the first LARGE zone — five sectors of ~4
zones each: the Official quarter (gates · the magistrate's office ·
the tribunal · the records office) · the Merchant quarter (warehouses ·
the weighing-yard · the exchange · the shop rows) · the Temple quarter
(Katsuhide's registry among the temples) · the Waterfront (port ·
docks · the fish market) · the Back streets (the poor quarter · the
night lanes). Plus the living region behind it: encounters and
side-quests with reasons to backtrack through T2/T3 zones.

### T5 · The Domain — the inherited debt (1790–92)

*(A DOMAIN — han — is a lord's castle town plus all its surrounding
lands.)* Starts: the reward that is a burden. The bakufu's men place
the broken domain under caretaker administration, and the house — the
region's anchor, the town's new charge-holder — carries it. You wanted
to right one town; you inherit a domain's ruin as your debt: multiple
towns, twenty-plus villages, silted works, broken roads, hungry
people. THE RUNG-UP INVERTS: in T0 you were summoned for promotion; in
T5 the domain's people are summoned to YOU — each rung an audience-day
in the restored AUDIENCE HALL, a wider circle of petitioners arriving
with a bigger share of the domain's problems.

Beneath it runs the stewardship's succession: Genemon failing, the
day-book passing, the silver-secret formally handed over — and
Shinnosuke, adopted, learning the house from the man who rebuilt it.
It ends at THE STEWARD: Genemon's chair, the highest an outside-born
man can rise inside a house. *Theme: from serving the house to
carrying it — healing at domain scale what once drowned Kuzuhara at
estate scale.*

**Rungs:** R0 **The surveyor** *(outside — ride the shambles;
vanishings at scale)* · R1 **The deputy-steward** *(house)* · R2 **The
bearer of rice** *(outside — famine relief; the first petitioners
follow the rice back)* · R3 **The keeper of the day-book** *(house —
Genemon hands him the book)* · R4 **The hearer of the near villages**
*(the audience-days begin)* · R5 **The right hand of the regency**
*(house)* · R6 **The hearer of the domain** *(the full circle of
petitioners)* · R7 **The steward** *(house — the chair; the secret
handed over)*.

**Zones:** the Castle town (home-adjacent now) · the Port town · the
Mountain town · the village clusters (20+ named; a handful visitable,
the rest reachable by cause — a petition, a summons, a repair) · the
Domain roads (the arteries the tier repairs) · the Silted great-works ·
the Domain granaries · the Provincial temple & records — and at home,
the compound's center restored: the AUDIENCE HALL, the tier's venue.

### T6 · Edo — the house's ladder (1793+; deliberately open)

The game changes: personal rungs retire — **the rungs are the HOUSE'S
Edo standing, H0→H7.** He arrives as steward of a provincial house
with a strong but SHORT reputation: twelve months of domain-healing
that mean nothing here; the house's name is unread. H0 is a name
nobody in Edo reads; H7 is the provincial house's honest ceiling —
bakufu ink, the banzuke's readable ranks, the shogun's clerks and
never the shogun (kernel #5 made literal). Shinnosuke is lord.
Everything else — plot turns, more time-skips, even the MC contesting
a lordship — is RESERVED: the Edo tier is a game-within-a-game, the
pseudo-prestige act (nothing resets, §0.9) for which T0–T5 is the
demo. Its zones are sketched only when its own design pass opens (the
Edo lodging → yashiki · Nihonbashi, the lone real place · the
wholesale docks · the waiting rooms of power — the ceiling as a
literal room).

Shigemasa's last entry) → knows his birth name (T3) → the renunciation
witnessed (T4) → the stewardship (T5) → what the register finally says
is his choice, at the end (T6).
