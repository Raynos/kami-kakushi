## The ladder (detail)

| Rung | Title | The scene / the earning |
|---|---|---|
| R0 | The field-hand | Sent out the gate at morning muster with the paid crews — the first time he works land beyond the yard, under a crew boss who wasn't there for any of it. A named hand, and nobody again for exactly one rung. Day-book: his wage now one line among many. |
| R1 | The terrace-hand | The upper paddies become HIS round — earned by a terrace wall he re-stacked unasked after the thaw slumped it. Genemon reassigns him at the board, three words, no ceremony: the house has learned it doesn't need to watch him. |
| R2 | The woodcutter | The full lot and the charcoal clamp. His charcoal is the first thing the house SELLS that his hands made — the kind lane feeding the mon lane, on-screen. The T0 woodlot solitude, revisited from the other side: now the quiet is his to spend. |
| R3 | The kura-warden | THE KEY — the first key he has ever held. Inside: the memorial alcove kept wrong (the sandals renewed, facing away), the old ledgers with their cut page-stubs. **Toku, at the alcove — the real meeting:** she doesn't explain, and he doesn't ask, and both notice both. |
| R4 | The works-hand | The weir and flood-works — the OLD BREACH under his hands, and upstream of it the silted pools where the rats have bred all along (the T0 thread paid). The works are also how the wings first open: sent into the east wing as a workman — shutters, floors — and finds **O-Sato airing a room that was hers**. The house lets him in tool-first. |
| R5 | The tally-keeper | The day-book's tallies pass to him under Genemon's eye — and **the DEBT PANEL unlocks: the number that has ruled every scene since R1 of T0, finally seen.** And a quieter knife: Toku's packet crosses his own brush now, entered as something it isn't — he tallies the lie without knowing it's one. |
| R6 | The foreman | The first hiring — HE states terms at the board, witnessed: T0-R1 mirrored, the man once kept by arithmetic now doing the keeping. The east wing passes under his charge; opening its closed rooms he finds **Naoyuki where his brother's things are stored — the real first meeting.** On the boundary-stone fields, **the wolf returns** — a player DECIDE: won, or spared (the T0 dog beat's mirror). |
| R7 | The registered man | The shoin — the first room of the main house brought back, by him, board by board. Shigemasa's only scene: the dying lord writes him into the house register as his LAST ENTRY; three lines up, unstruck, no death date: *Katsuhide*. The lord dies at the seam. *Skip ~2 years.* |

The rung-title merge is deliberate: the overview once named R4/R6 for
the access arc (wing-hand / wing-warden); the locked ladder keeps the
WORK titles and folds the access into them — the wings open to him as
a workman at R4 and pass under his charge at R6, because in this house
trust arrives as tools and keys, never as invitations.

## Cast at nodes (locked with the human, 2026-07-07)

**The wings meetings — why each person is there (the diegetic law):**

- **Toku · R3, the alcove** — the key reveals he is NOT the only
  keyholder. The rites (the sandals, renewed) were never a mystery to
  the household — they are Toku's, and the kura-warden now simply
  shares a building with her habit. Nothing is staged; the meeting is
  what holding the key means.
- **O-Sato · R4, the airing** — rooms are opened, emptied, and aired
  BEFORE a workman touches them. The works schedule sends her into the
  east wing ahead of his repairs, room by room — and one of those
  rooms was hers, from before the household compressed.
- **Naoyuki · R6, the brother's things** — clearing the east wing
  turns up crates that are Katsuhide's, and stored family things need
  a family member's say. Naoyuki comes to carry them to the west wing
  HIMSELF — and refuses help. The refusal is the meeting. (The west
  wing stays closed: the scene is the things CROSSING to it, never the
  MC entering it.)

**The new T1 faces (the cast grows by four):**

- **Denshichi** — the crew boss (R0–R1). Runs the paid seasonal crews
  from the village; wasn't there for any of T0 and treats the named
  hand as green — the status reset made human. The first village-side
  face met as a BOSS: the T2 village track previewed from the labour
  side, as O-Yae previews it from the gossip side.
- **Heikichi** — the master joiner (R4–R7). Hired for the works; the
  MC learns real carpentry under him; the shoin is their joint work —
  but the last boards Heikichi makes the MC set ALONE. His past PAYS:
  he apprenticed on the compound before the fall, and at the shoin
  restoration he reads the salvage marks in the timbers — the shoin's
  bones came from the compound — said as craft, not revelation ("Good
  wood. Came from over there."), dated never. The line turns legible
  only at T2's reveal: a SEED, not a fourth signal — the three-signal
  reveal design holds.
- **Ribei** — the creditor's clerk (R5–). The debt panel's number gets
  a face: he comes twice a year on interest days, polite as weather.
  T1's renegotiation war is fought across the board against that
  politeness. The lender himself stays OFFSTAGE, deliberately banked —
  whether he threads into T4's enemy web is the T4 design cell's call.
- **Tetsuji** — the smith, and R6's first hiring. The foreman's first
  decision as a hirer LIGHTS THE COLD FORGE — a dead building wakes
  because he chose a man. One hire is trust demonstrated; the WAVE of
  hiring stays T2's.

Crew hands and harvest women stay ambient until the story touches them
twice (kernel #2). Carryovers deepen at their cast-sheet T1 nodes:
Chiyo the inner guest house · Toku her room · O-Sato the east wing.
