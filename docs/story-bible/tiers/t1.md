## The zones — the land (locked with the human, 2026-07-07; the wings
## land at their own piece)

**The Terraced paddies (upper & lower)** — the estate's real farmland
beyond the home paddy; R1's round. *Actions:* field work at scale (the
deed engine's T1 heart), re-stack terrace walls, manage the sluice
water. *Who:* Denshichi's crews at season peaks. *Wrong thing:* the
terrace stones are NUMBERED — cut numerals running far past the
terraces that still exist; the hillside above the worked rows was let
go, terrace by terrace, and the numbers still count it.

**The Woodlot proper + the charcoal clamp** — T0 saw the edge; this is
the lot. *Actions:* fell timber (the works' material), coal the clamp
(the charcoal that feeds the mon lane), overnight clamp-tending — the
tier's peaceful solitude vigils. *Who:* nobody, by design. *Wrong
thing:* T0's undated char marks resolve into a BURN LINE WITH A
STRAIGHT EDGE — a firebreak someone cut and held. The lot survived a
fire the household has never once mentioned. *(Answer owed: the fall's
design — see the one-answer note below.)*

**The Weir & flood-works** — channels, sluices, the leased screens —
and the OLD BREACH. *Actions:* mend the works (repair-labour), and at
R4 close the breach's upstream pools — a repair that PERMANENTLY
drains the Weir-reeds rat pressure: fixing the land fixes the combat
zone, on-screen. *Who:* Matsuzō across the water; Sōan passing on
rounds. *Wrong thing:* the breach isn't storm damage — THE STONES WERE
TAKEN. Dressed stone doesn't wash away; it walks. The flood came
through a hole people made.

**The Kura interior** — R3's ground; the key's whole meaning.
*Actions:* warden the stores (the seasonal inventory up close), read
the old ledgers (ambient story), keep the alcove's corridor swept —
around Toku's rites, never touching them. *Who:* Toku at the alcove;
Genemon on audit days. *Wrong thing:* the CUT PAGE-STUBS — a run of
pages razored from the old ledgers, all from the same years. The
missing years are the ones nobody talks about.

**The Workshops** — Heikichi's joiner's bench; the cold forge until
R6. *Actions:* assist the joiner (craft learning), make and mend tools
(durables the mon lane doesn't have to buy), forge work once Tetsuji
lights it. *Who:* Heikichi; Tetsuji late-tier; Shinnosuke on the
wall — the mirror follows the work. *Wrong thing:* the tool racks hold
SOOT SHADOWS — outlines of tools long gone, racks sized for thirty
men's kit. The compression, written in absence.

**The Boundary stones & far fields** — the estate's legal edge, far
past the worked rows; the boar's ground, and the wolf's return at R6.
*Actions:* walk the bounds (the reclamation survey), clear far fields
(expansion labour), the boar chain. *Who:* nobody; crews only at
pushes. *Wrong thing:* one stone on the river side is NEWER than its
brothers, and stands a field short of where the terrace numbering says
it should. Someone moved the line inward; nobody official remembers
when. *(Banked for T2 — a village-track dispute waiting.)*

**The Family plot** — the Kurosawa dead; Bon #2's ground. *Actions:*
tend the graves (a kura-warden-adjacent duty), the Bon observance.
*Who:* Toku for the rites; the household at Bon. *Wrong thing:* one
plot SWEPT AND WEEDED WITH NO STONE IN IT — ground held for a burial
nobody will schedule. (The register's unstruck line, in earth.)

**The Upstream pools** *(combat zone — grindable, and it ENDS)* — the
silted pools behind the old breach; the rat source itself, finally
reachable. Rat swarms thick at the water; a marten den as the
mini-cap. **The R4 breach repair DRAINS it**: the zone converts to
quiet works-ground and the Weir-reeds pressure drops for good — a
combat zone the player retires by fixing the land. *Who:* nobody sane.
*Wrong thing:* the pools exist at all — water standing where the works
once ran it away.

**The Let-go terraces** *(combat zone — authored chain; T0's orchard,
escalated)* — the scrub hillside above the worked rows, where the
numbered stones keep counting. Boar setts; the monkey troop's fallback
ground. *Actions:* clear it stage by stage — each cleared stage
becomes a RECLAIMABLE TERRACE: combat converts to reclamation converts
to income, and the terrace numbers come back into use one by one.
*Who:* Denshichi's crews once a stage is safe. *Wrong thing:* the
numbering (shared with the paddies' row above).

**The Downstream shallows** *(combat zone — grindable)* — Matsuzō's
stretch of river, where KAWAUSO — otters — raid the leased fish weirs.
Kernel #1's next folk-loaded animal played plain: in the stories the
otter shapeshifts and drowns men; in daylight it is wet, greedy, and
mundane. Damage lands on HIS side of the lease — owed back in coin or
labour. *Who:* Matsuzō, counting losses. *Wrong thing:* the otters den
in STONEWORK — dressed stone, in a riverbank.

**One answer, three signs (locked):** the firebreak, the taken breach
stones, and the otters' dressed-stone den all route to ONE authored
answer at the fall's design (T2/T3) — three surface signs, one buried
story; no further mystery debt taken by this sheet.

## The zones — the wings (locked with the human, 2026-07-07)

**The East wing** — the tier's interior frontier, cleared room by
room. *Actions:* repair rooms (works-labour with Heikichi), carry and
clear, warden its keys from R6. *Who:* O-Sato airing ahead of the
work; Chiyo begins using the first restored room — her "inner guest
house" node made concrete. *Wrong thing:* the fittings are
MISMATCHED — good pieces from many rooms consolidated into few. The
house ate itself to keep face, and the furniture remembers.

**The West wing** — closed, all tier. Katsuhide's things are stored
there; at R6 the crates cross INTO it, at the threshold, from
Naoyuki's own hands. The MC never enters — the door is a fact, not a
lock to pick. *Wrong thing:* the household keeps the closed wing like
an open one — O-Sato sweeps its corridor as ordinary duty, and nobody
finds that strange. Not a mystery: a portrait of the refusal.

**The Inner garden** — between the wings; overgrown, then tended.
*Actions:* clear and tend (light labour; the tier's recovery-flavor
node — T0's woodshed warmth, promoted), gather for the kitchen and
Sōan. *Who:* Chiyo walks it once it's decent; Shinnosuke cuts through
it against instruction. *Wrong thing:* the stepping-stone path runs to
a gate in the wall — and the gate opens on the rope and the ruin. The
garden was designed to walk INTO the compound. *(Existing debt;
nothing new owed.)*

**The Shoin** — restored last, for the scene; Heikichi's and the MC's
joint work, the last boards set alone. *Wrong thing:* the salvage
marks in its timbers (Heikichi's locked beat). Then: the register, the
last entry, the unstruck line.

## Combat structure & enemies (locked)

T1's combat is still the estate defending its margins — but now the
margins MOVE OUTWARD as he reclaims, and every zone the land-work
fixes, the fighting map feels. **The no-human line holds through T1**
(locked): the first man he ever fights is a T2 bandit, staged as a
threshold beat.

The zones by shape: **grindable loops** — the Upstream pools (rat
swarms at the source; the marten den as mini-cap — RETIRED by the R4
breach repair), the Downstream shallows (kawauso vs the leased fish
weirs; worst in autumn when the fish run fat), and the carried T0
loops: the Weir reeds (pressure visibly drops once the pools drain),
the Field margins, the Bamboo grove; **authored chains** — the Let-go
terraces (boar setts and the monkey fallback ground, cleared stage by
stage into reclaimed farmland) and the boar chain at the boundary
stones, feeding the wolf's ground; **the set-piece** — the wolf at R6,
met in DAYLIGHT, IN THE OPEN FIELDS — the deliberate inversion of T0
(then, it came in the dark to the stores; now, face to face with
nothing between them); DECIDE: won, or spared; **the mini-dungeon** —
the Night rounds, T1: the route GROWS with the estate — every building
brought back adds a stretch to the walk, and the round stays
all-combat end to end. Rats fade from the route after R4 (the
world-change reads in the round itself); winter puts feral dogs back
on it; spring adds a cornered boar sow as the tier's night apex —
deliberately smaller than the wolf, whose set-piece stays the tier's
peak.

Progression around the fights: the Drill yard carries forward
(sparring with Kihei; Shinnosuke now drills too, badly); Tetsuji's
forge opens the equipment lane at R6 — durables from the mon lane,
mended and made on the estate. Seasons keep driving the enemies
(monkeys Summer/Bon, boar at the autumn crops and bold in winter, rats
at harvest storage until the pools drain). Systems canon carries
whole: defeat is never game-over — the sickroom, days lost, Sōan's
ledger grows; labour never costs HP.
