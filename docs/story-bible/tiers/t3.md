## The ORIGIN (relocked — accident, and a warm reunion waiting)

- **What happened (locked):** no flight, no guilt, no grave. The MC is
  **Tahei**, a kaidō porter — **a landslide took him into the river**
  (kernel #1's plain accident; the porter's-knot habit and the
  tally-hand competence are his old trade surfacing). His family
  **assumed him MISSING, never dead**: at the origin register office
  his entry was **kept open** — his family refused the strike. *The
  second unstruck line:* the MC has stood on BOTH sides of the same
  refusal — the Kurosawa house keeps Katsuhide's line open upstairs
  while he sleeps in its woodshed, himself a line someone else keeps
  open two valleys away.
- **The family (locked — ALL ALIVE):** **his father Jinpachi, his
  mother O-Nobu, and his YOUNGER SISTER Suzu** (named at the sweep),
  at the family holding outside the
  post-town. The sister was a child when the mountain took him — grown
  now, she barely remembers the brother she never stopped hearing
  about: the reunion's sharpest face. The reunion is WARM and it is
  WAITING — the tier's promise is not a wound; it is a door he doesn't
  know is still open.
- **The two payoff rails (locked, the PRD shape restored):**
  - **The TAMA TRUTH resolves on the SPINE** — guaranteed for every
    player, at the rumor-follower rungs: **Tama was a girl who RAN —
    Otsuru — found GROWN in the down-valley market town
    (Yanagi-watari, working name).** The MC was never her. Grounded
    and partial: she may not forgive the village its grief, and the
    village must give a vanished child back her real name (kernel #2).
  - **The TAHEI reunion track is OPTIONAL and missable** — the origin
    side-track at the post-town (**Sawatari-juku**, working name;
    the O-ladder, O0→O5 in the PRD's shape): opens mid-tier,
    doubly-earned (enough memory returned + the standing to travel
    the checkpointed kaidō); an old woman drops her basket —
    *"Tahei…?"* — and the reunions run through it (Jinpachi, O-Nobu,
    Suzu; **Kenta**, the porter friend, its first
    face). At its end he **reclaims his name** — earned, warm, and
    skippable; a player can finish the game never having gone home.
    Never gates the spine.
- **He KNOWS by tier's end** (spine-guaranteed via the Otsuru truth +
  the returned memory); **the ink still waits** — what the KUROSAWA
  register finally says is his choice at the end of the game (the ink
  thread, unchanged).
- **Matsuzō's payoff:** "the river was quiet that night" dates and
  places the arrival — the old weir-keeper's banked oddity becomes a
  working clue on the origin trail.
- **Sōan's ledger payoff:** what the doctor clocked and closed the
  book on — crush injuries, a porter's hands, calluses in the wrong
  places for a farmhand — reads true at last: he was right, and what
  he was right ABOUT was innocent. The reluctant witness gets to be
  relieved.

## The reunion staging (locked — the Tahei track's beats)

1. **The basket (the track opens).** A Sawatari-juku street; an old
   neighbor woman drops her basket — *"Tahei…?"* The name lands like a
   stone in still water. The track stays doubly-earned: enough memory
   returned + the standing to walk the checkpointed kaidō.
2. **The circling.** He doesn't knock. The inn quarter, the market,
   questions asked sideways — **the player chooses WHEN the door is
   knocked on**; the track never forces it. Here **Kenta** finds
   *him* — the porter friend, the face that aches to remember — and
   tells him the missing years: they searched the river both springs;
   the entry was never struck.
3. **The threshold.** He arrives at the holding on the house's
   business, dignity intact — met at the gate by **his sister, who
   doesn't know him** (she was a child). She fetches Jinpachi;
   Jinpachi knows him before he is through the gate. The mother is the
   scene's peak and its fewest words (the space is load-bearing).
4. **What they kept.** His **carrying-pole**, oiled and hung twelve
   years — rewards are things (prose law), and the reunion's object is
   a tool, the same verb the game opened with: haul.
5. **O5 — the name confirmed.** At the register office the kept-open
   entry is amended: not struck, not mourned — **confirmed**. The
   origin register gets its truth in ink; what the *Kurosawa* register
   says at the game's end stays his choice. The ink thread doubles:
   two registers, two names, one man.

## The Otsuru scenes (locked — the spine, guaranteed)

The rumor-trail he follows FOR THE HOUSE crosses the Tama story where
the trade roads do. At **Yanagi-watari** he finds her: grown, working,
a life of her own making. The truth is plain and it is hers: **Tama
RAN.** *Why she ran is an OPEN CELL for the T3 build — kernel #1
bounds it: mundane, and not required to wound.* What the MC gets to do
is kernel #2's whole job: **give the vanished child back her name** —
the village learns Otsuru lived; **Ekai amends the false taking-entry
on screen**; Sayo receives the truth and her grief-work ends in
daylight. Whether Otsuru ever visits, forgives, or writes is HERS —
grounded and partial, exactly as promised. (Guaranteed for every
player; the Tahei reunion above is the optional, missable sibling
rail.)

## The buried-truth quest (locked — the sealed storerooms, R5)

The Kuzuhara account is, of course, **a ledger**: the flood-works'
costs with the warnings entered as line items, the drowned hamlet's
dead listed with compensation entries — some marked paid, most not.
**Genemon's silver is those unpaid lines, still being paid.** And the
account **names the office that took the flood-works cut** — the skim
that made the works fatal: **the T4 enemy's first documentary
shadow.** His T1 tally-hand reads it for what it is; the columns
speak, no lecture needed.
