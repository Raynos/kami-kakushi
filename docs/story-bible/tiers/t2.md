## The reveal, staged (locked — early, three signals, no lecture)

- **Signal 1 · R0, the well** — village rumor, overheard plain: *"that
  yard was a town once."* Nobody explains it to him.
- **Signal 2 · R1, the kura** — clearing for the works turns up **the
  compound's old plans**: the whole map drawn true, and "the main
  house" a small box labeled *guest quarters*. He reads it alone.
- **Signal 3 · Toku, one line past-tense** — in passing, dated as
  always: *"When the main house still stood, we dried the screens in
  its court."* She doesn't correct herself. The third signal is
  DELIBERATELY hers: the household's own mouth closes the reveal, not
  a stranger's.
- **The re-label** — at the third signal's scene end, the map redraws
  its two labels: *Main house → Guest house; the ruin → the Main
  house.* One log line in the day-book's voice, no ceremony.
  Retroactively legible: Heikichi's salvage-marks line, the kura's
  one-more-petal crest, the garden gate onto the ruin. (The UI change
  IS the story beat — kernel #6's sibling; the B2 map re-label work.)

## The TAMA thread (RESTORED by the human, 2026-07-07 — the PRD design
## returns; this is the title's own thread)

- **The naming (R0):** **Sayo**, the headman's daughter, names him
  "Tama" on sight — a village child lost to a "taking" years ago,
  grieved by the whole valley. The village half-adopts him as its
  returned Tama before it has granted him so much as a fair price:
  the two reputation currents run crossed from the first scene
  (kernel #7 — the world misreads him, warmly this time).
- **Tama-vs-farmhand — REAL choices (locked):** the lean is played as
  **authored DECIDEs with real consequences**, not flavor: at the
  naming (accept the name · deny it · say nothing) · at the Bon rites
  (stand with the grieving as Tama, or stand apart) · at the well,
  when the questions come. Leaning Tama warms the village fast and
  opens its grief-coded scenes at friction with the estate's trust;
  leaning farmhand keeps the house's ground solid while the village
  cools. Consequences move BOTH tracks and change which scenes exist —
  the exact magnitudes are the build's (B2/sim), the choices are
  canon. **The guardrail (locked):** scenes differ, ARENAS STAY OPEN —
  neither the village arena nor the estate arena can ever hard-lock on
  the lean; completability survives under real choices.
- **The truth is banked, not spent:** T2 never resolves what happened
  to Tama. **Ekai's register of the vanished holds Tama's
  taking-entry** — beside Katsuhide's: TWO false entries in one book,
  and the MC reads both while the village is deciding he's one of
  them. The resolution belongs to T3's spine (Otsuru — see
  [`t3.md`](t3.md)), where the vanished child is given back her real
  name.

## The village track (locked; never converts to rungs)

Five stages, in the village's own voice: **the surcharge** (stranger
prices, served last) → **fair price** (money honest, face known) →
**the nod** (greeted at the well unprompted) → **named** (his own
name, not "the Kurosawa man") → **vouched-for** (the village speaks
for him to outsiders). Movement by deeds in the village arena only —
and it can FALL (a bad debt, a fight gone wrong): standing is theirs
to give and theirs to take.

## The main-quest threads (both arenas, sketch)

**Valley:** (1) the bandit pressure mainline — roads → raids → the R5
breach → the R6 campaign; (2) the ferry crossing trouble (Funakichi's
landing, the night roads); (3) **the register of the vanished** — he
reads it at Ekai's temple and finds BOTH false takings in official
ink: Katsuhide's, and Tama's — the name the village has just hung on
him. **Estate:** (1) the gatehouse → outer-domain clearance
stages; (2) the payment road (the silver thread, R2+); (3) the hiring
wave — the staff grows under him (T1's one hire becomes T2's roster).

## The threshold aftermath (locked — the first man DIES)

He dies under Sōan's hands, **in the same sickroom where the MC
himself was saved** — the room that gave the game its man takes its
first man back. Sōan works the whole night and loses him; his ledger
gains its darkest page. Kihei says nothing next morning — but the
drills change (men now, not beasts). Genemon's day-book line is his
coldest: *one man dead at the gatehouse works; costs entered.* The
body is carried down through the village, and the village watches:
the track feels it. At Bon #3 the motif escalates again: the MC
lights a lantern HE chose to light — for a man whose name he had to
ask. The scene refuses to be small; the ledger refuses to make it
big.

## Economics & seeding (half detail)

**Economics:** the surcharge decays by deeds, never by time; village
dues open their own day-book row; the camp campaign has real costs
(guards hired, works idle). The debt war continues on the renegotiated
schedule — background, not beat. **Tier-up:** what T2→T3 buys is
trust AND strength — the valley's watching becomes leaning, and the
expeditionary tier opens because the house can now be AWAY from home
without falling. **Seeding:** the silver rumor (R2, locked) · the
crest answer arrives legibly with the old plans (the one-more-petal
crest drawn on the compound's gate — Shinnosuke's T0 question,
answered by paper, not people) · the moved boundary stone becomes a
village-track dispute quest · the bandit accents → T4 · the register's
taking-entry → the T4 packet-trail · Matsuzō's quiet river stays
banked for T3. The ambiguity budget stays UNSPENT.
