## The zones (⏳ draft — node grammar: blurb · actions · who's there · the wrong thing)

**The Weir & riverbank** — where the river left him; the weir-jizō
stands here. *Actions:* mend the weir screen, haul stone, (hidden:
search the reeds — his washed-up bundle, a water-ruined paper).
*Who:* Sōan passes on his rounds. *Wrong thing:* offerings appear at
the jizō that nobody admits to leaving — a long-running MYSTERY (not
an ambiguity: it has an answer, authored and revealed in a later
tier).

**The Gate & gateyard** — the estate's face, kept barely. Yohei's
pedlar stall sets up here on market days (THE market — it comes to
him). *Actions:* gate-watch, sweep, receive/see-off, trade at the
stall. *Who:* Kihei crosses at watch-change; Yohei on his days.
*Wrong thing:* the gate's crest board is newer than the gate — the old
one is not discussed.

**The Forecourt** — the working heart of the guest house's outer
court. *Actions:* rake (the first verb), haul, stack, odd jobs.
*Who:* Genemon's window overlooks it; the day-book lives off it.
*Wrong thing:* the court is sized for a household five times this one.

**The Woodshed** — his corner: a mat, a chipped bowl, the comfort
floor. *Actions:* rest/recover, keep his few things. *Who:* O-Hisa
leaves mended things without knocking (later rungs). *Wrong thing:*
none — this node is the one warmth the tier allows, and it is earned.

**The Kitchen threshold & board-side** — meals at the threshold; the
board is where the household's shape is overheard. *Actions:* eat
(recovery), overhear (ambient story), later carry dishes in. *Who:*
O-Hisa rules it; Shinnosuke interrogates from it; Genemon states terms
at it. *Wrong thing:* the steward's papers share the kitchen — an
estate compressed into a guest house (the twist, hiding in plain
sight).

**The Kura exterior & grain-store** — the working storehouse; the
grain-watch's post. *Actions:* load/unload, the night watch (R3), the
wolf fight. *Who:* Kihei sets the watch. *Wrong thing:* the kura's
seal-plate carries a crest with ONE more petal than the gate's (the
old seat's crest — nobody reads it aloud).

**The Drill yard** — the old stable court, repurposed; Kihei's ground.
*Actions:* drills (combat skills), spar, the field-guide's first pages.
*Who:* Kihei, always; Shinnosuke watching from the wall. *Wrong
thing:* stable stalls for twenty horses; the house owns one mule.

**The Home paddy & vegetable rows** — the guest house's skirts; the
labour baseline. *Actions:* field work (the deed engine's heart),
seasonal planting/harvest. *Who:* Rokusuke-class hired hands come and
go; Otoku-class village women at harvest. *Wrong thing:* boundary
stones stand far beyond the worked rows — the fields were once four
times wider.

**The Woodlot edge** — kindling and forage country; the wolf's ground
before R3. *Actions:* gather kindling, forage, (hidden: the silted
sluice the field-work keeps hinting at). *Who:* nobody — that is the
point; the first solitude node. *Wrong thing:* char marks on old
stumps — a burn nobody dates.

**The shrine-alcove corridor** — a family altar in a CORRIDOR
(compressed worship — the twist again). Glimpsed once in T0, entered
in T1. *Wrong thing (glimpse only):* rites set out for a dead man,
and the straw sandals face AWAY from the house, renewed this year.

**Sōan's sickroom** — a lean-to surgery off the outer court.
*Actions:* treatment (injury recovery), the R0 examination, quiet
questions not asked. *Who:* Sōan. *Wrong thing:* he keeps notes on
the MC's healing in a ledger he closes when anyone enters.

**The ruined compound (scenery)** — beyond a rope and a warning: roofs
fallen, a great gate crumbled, walls the village quarried for stones.
LOCKED all tier; not even named honestly. *Wrong thing:* the whole
node — and the household's refusal to look at it.

**The Overgrown orchard** *(combat zone)* — the old compound's orchard
gone wild; feral dogs den in it, bold from lean winters. *Actions:*
drive the dogs (a staged combat chain), then RECLAIM the orchard —
clearing it is repair-labour that turns a threat into a food source.
*Who:* nobody goes there; Kihei calls it "the dogs' yard" without
irony. *Wrong thing:* fruit trees planted in courtyard rows — an
orchard laid out by someone who expected paths and lanterns.

**The Bamboo grove** *(combat zone)* — behind the compound; the monkey
troop raids the vegetable rows from it. *Actions:* patrol, drive the
raiders (recurring skirmishes; loot the pilfered goods back), cut
bamboo (craft material). *Who:* Shinnosuke sneaks here against all
instruction. *Wrong thing:* a cut-bamboo waymark renewed each season,
far too old a habit for anyone who'll claim it.

**The Night rounds** *(combat activity — a mini-dungeon, not a map
state)* — no day/night system: a **"begin the night round" action**
(its post at the gate). The FIRST round is a quest; after that it is
repeatable. Starting a round puts the MC on rails through several
zones in their night state — clear each of enemies to finish the
round, or fall and wake in Sōan's sickroom. The escalation across
rungs: rats in the store → a marten → the R3 WOLF as the arc's climax.
*Who:* Kihei designs the round; from R3 it is his and the MC's alone.
*Wrong thing:* on new-moon rounds, someone crosses the yard's far
edge, lantern hooded, going upstream (Toku — the packet, seeded
visually long before R5 names it).

**The Weir reeds** *(combat zone — grindable)* — river rats gnaw the
weir screens the house leases from Matsuzō; every screen lost is coin
owed. *Actions:* clear the rats (repeatable), mend the screens
(repair-labour), wade the shallows. *Who:* Matsuzō counts the damage
from his side of the water. *Wrong thing:* the rats always come from
UPSTREAM — something upstream feeds them (a T1 thread: the old breach
pools).

**The Field margins** *(combat zone — grindable)* — tanuki and badger
setts at the paddy's edge, raiding the drying racks and seed stores.
Folk-loaded animals played PLAIN (kernel #1: the game shows you, in
daylight, the mundane animal the stories are made of). *Actions:*
drive the raiders (repeatable, seasonal peaks at harvest), dig the
setts, set watch. *Who:* O-Ume's plot borders the worst of it; she
thanks the kami, not the MC. *Wrong thing:* an old sett runs UNDER the
ruined compound's wall — a way in that nobody official knows exists.

   O-Hisa and Kihei. The mirror's first use.

## Combat structure & enemies

T0's combat is the estate defending its margins — mundane, seasonal,
escalating; every fight has an economic reason the ledger could name.
**No human combat in T0 OR T1** (locked): the first man the MC ever
fights is a T2 bandit, and crossing that line is staged as a threshold
beat.

The zones by shape: **grindable loops** — the Bamboo grove (monkey
raids; the troop's big male as its mini-cap), the Weir reeds (river
rats vs the leased screens), the Field margins (tanuki/badger raids,
harvest peaks); **an authored chain** — the Overgrown orchard
(feral-dog stages → reclamation; combat converts to repair and
income); **a repeatable mini-dungeon** — the Night rounds (a
begin-the-round action: on-rails through zones' night states, finish
or fall to the sickroom; first round is a quest; rats → marten → the
R3 WOLF as climax). The Drill yard grows skill between fights (safe
sparring with Kihei, from R4).

**The wolf returns in T1** (locked): met again on the boundary-stone
fields when he is stronger — survived-not-won finally resolved (won,
or spared).

**Systems canon (game-wide; mechanics ADR at build):** defeat in T0 is
never game-over — you are carried to Sōan's sickroom and lose days
(wages, season time), and his closed ledger grows. **Two body
economies:** labour spends the WORK/body unit and NEVER costs HP;
combat risks HP; being at low HP impairs work capacity (one body, two
meters, coupled one way). Seasons drive the enemies: monkeys peak
Summer/Bon, dogs bolden in Winter, rats swarm at harvest storage.

## Economics (human-locked shape; all magnitudes sim-owned)

**Three nested ledgers.** (1) *The MC's:* paid in meals (R0–R1) → rice
(R2–R4) → a day-wage in mon (R5–R6) → trusted with the house's purse
(R6–R7); end-of-tier net worth: single-digit mon, a straw coat, a
whetstone, maybe a dog. (2) *The household's day-book:* income from
harvest, woodlot, and whatever the player reclaims; sinks in wages,
the lease day, repairs, interest — and Toku's packet, entered as
something it isn't. **Filling the barn is the HOUSE'S economy, not the
player's inventory** — barn-filling actions raise house stores and
flavor, never player loot. (3) *The debt:* the standing antagonist —
principal untouchable in T0; the war is interest + the tax without new
borrowing.

**The calendar bundle (locked).** Seasons UNLOCK at R2 (R0–R1 show
only the day of the week — a man counts days again when he has a
future). Season change is a manual action with a per-season VN
overlay. **The nengu is Autumn's exit event** — an authored scene at
the board (assessment read, the gap named plainly once, the MC as
furniture at his own house's most important scene; T5's audience-days
invert this). The seasonal judge rides every season exit. R7 gating on
the tax season means T0 contains at least one full year lived.

**The two coin lanes (DIRECTION, not locked — known open problems,
flagged by the human for build/balance time):** (a) kind overflow —
unbounded materials/rice will pile up and players will dump them at
any shop that takes them; the kind lane needs its own sinks or caps.
(b) the day-wage's collection moment is awkward against auto-actions
and auto-combat — when does a moving, autoing player "collect"?
Resolve both in the economy ADR with the sim; the shape below is the
working direction.
*The KIND lane* (unbounded): labour pays in rice and goods; combat
drops materials, never coin (beasts carry no mon — hides, recovered
produce, bamboo); consumables run on kind — food, resting, dog rice,
mending thread. *The MON lane* (bounded): the day-wage is FIXED PER
GAME-DAY worked, not per action — grinding fills the barn, not the
purse; the only other mon source is SELLING on Yohei's market days
(day-of-week load-bearing; his coin is finite per visit). Durables run
on mon: tools, equipment, upgrades, the stall's season-scarce stock
(it restocks per season — one straw coat this winter; scarcity plus a
pull to advance the wheel). Recurring sinks: dog rice, offerings,
thread, and his own sickroom bills once he draws a wage.

**The debt's three-stage introduction (locked).** (1) R1 — named
sideways in Genemon's terms; (2) all T0 — FELT in scenes (the lease
day; the nengu), never numbered; (3) T1's tally-keeper rung — the debt
panel UNLOCKS and the number that ruled everyone is finally seen: a
story beat. T1's war is the renegotiation.

**The tier-up (locked).** The house-standing grind opens at R7: the
same verbs start banking ESTATE-DEEDS into the house's standing —
reclamation bearing fruit, repairs holding, the granary past
this-winter's need: surplus and order, written down. Standing is the
house's own verdict on itself (T0 wins influence over nothing outside
its gate — the valley isn't watching yet). Graded at every season
exit, one plain line in the day-book's voice. Top grade unlocks the
manual, ceremonial ASCEND: the house commits to the LAND CAMPAIGN
(New-Year-themed if timed there). What T0→T1 buys: influence over its
own land — the right to try again.
