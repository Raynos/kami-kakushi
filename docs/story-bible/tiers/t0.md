## The zones (⏳ draft — node grammar: blurb · actions · who's there · the wrong thing)

**The Weir & riverbank** — where the river left him; the weir-jizō
stands here. *Actions:* mend the weir screen, haul stone, (hidden:
search the reeds — his washed-up bundle, a water-ruined paper).
*Who:* Sōan passes on his rounds. *Wrong thing:* offerings appear at
the jizō that nobody admits to leaving — a long-running MYSTERY (not
an ambiguity: it has an answer, authored and revealed in a later
tier).

**The Gate & gateyard** — the estate's face, kept barely. Tokubei's
pedlar stall sets up here on market days (THE market — it comes to
him). *Actions:* gate-watch, sweep, receive/see-off, trade at the
stall. *Who:* Kihei crosses at watch-change; Tokubei on his days.
*Wrong thing:* the gate's crest board is newer than the gate — the old
one is not discussed.

**The Forecourt** — the working heart of the guest house's outer
court. *Actions:* rake (the first verb), haul, stack, odd jobs.
*Who:* Genemon's window overlooks it; the day-book lives off it.
*Wrong thing:* the court is sized for a household five times this one.

**The Woodshed** — his corner: a mat, a chipped bowl, the comfort
floor. *Actions:* rest/recover, keep his few things. *Who:* O-Sato
leaves mended things without knocking (later rungs). *Wrong thing:*
none — this node is the one warmth the tier allows, and it is earned.

**The Kitchen threshold & board-side** — meals at the threshold; the
board is where the household's shape is overheard. *Actions:* eat
(recovery), overhear (ambient story), later carry dishes in. *Who:*
O-Sato rules it; Shinnosuke interrogates from it; Genemon states terms
at it. *Wrong thing:* the steward's papers share the kitchen — an
estate compressed into a guest house (the twist, hiding in plain
sight).

**The Kura exterior & grain-store** — the working storehouse; the
grain-watch's post. *Actions:* load/unload, the night watch (R3), the
wolf fight. *Who:* Kihei sets the watch. *Wrong thing:* the kura's
seal-plate carries a crest with ONE more petal than the gate's (the
old seat's crest — nobody reads it aloud).

**The Drill yard** — the old stable court, repurposed; Kihei's ground.
*Actions:* drills (combat skills), spar, the field-guide's first pages.
*Who:* Kihei, always; Shinnosuke watching from the wall. *Wrong
thing:* stable stalls for twenty horses; the house owns one mule.

**The Home paddy & vegetable rows** — the guest house's skirts; the
labour baseline. *Actions:* field work (the deed engine's heart),
seasonal planting/harvest. *Who:* Rokusuke-class hired hands come and
go; Otoku-class village women at harvest. *Wrong thing:* boundary
stones stand far beyond the worked rows — the fields were once four
times wider.

**The Woodlot edge** — kindling and forage country; the wolf's ground
before R3. *Actions:* gather kindling, forage, (hidden: the silted
sluice the field-work keeps hinting at). *Who:* nobody — that is the
point; the first solitude node. *Wrong thing:* char marks on old
stumps — a burn nobody dates.

**The shrine-alcove corridor** — a family altar in a CORRIDOR
(compressed worship — the twist again). Glimpsed once in T0, entered
in T1. *Wrong thing (glimpse only):* rites set out for a dead man,
and the straw sandals face AWAY from the house, renewed this year.

**Sōan's sickroom** — a lean-to surgery off the outer court.
*Actions:* treatment (injury recovery), the R0 examination, quiet
questions not asked. *Who:* Sōan. *Wrong thing:* he keeps notes on
the MC's healing in a ledger he closes when anyone enters.

**The ruined compound (scenery)** — beyond a rope and a warning: roofs
fallen, a great gate crumbled, walls the village quarried for stones.
LOCKED all tier; not even named honestly. *Wrong thing:* the whole
node — and the household's refusal to look at it.

**The Overgrown orchard** *(combat zone)* — the old compound's orchard
gone wild; feral dogs den in it, bold from lean winters. *Actions:*
drive the dogs (a staged combat chain), then RECLAIM the orchard —
clearing it is repair-labour that turns a threat into a food source.
*Who:* nobody goes there; Kihei calls it "the dogs' yard" without
irony. *Wrong thing:* fruit trees planted in courtyard rows — an
orchard laid out by someone who expected paths and lanterns.

**The Bamboo grove** *(combat zone)* — behind the compound; the monkey
troop raids the vegetable rows from it. *Actions:* patrol, drive the
raiders (recurring skirmishes; loot the pilfered goods back), cut
bamboo (craft material). *Who:* Shinnosuke sneaks here against all
instruction. *Wrong thing:* a cut-bamboo waymark renewed each season,
far too old a habit for anyone who'll claim it.

**The Night rounds** *(combat zone — the yard/grain-store/gate circuit
in its NIGHT state)* — the watch made playable: rats in the store,
then a marten, then worse; the escalation the R3 wolf CLIMAXES. Same
map, different world after dark. *Actions:* the watch circuit, night
combat, listening. *Who:* Kihei sets it; from R3 it is his and the
MC's alone. *Wrong thing:* someone crosses the yard's far edge at the
new moon, lantern hooded, going upstream (Toku — the packet, seeded
visually long before R5 names it).

## Combat structure & enemies (draft)

T0's combat is the estate defending its margins — mundane, seasonal,
escalating. Three combat zones + one climax: the **Bamboo grove**
(monkey troop — recurring raids, the training loop), the **Overgrown
orchard** (the feral-dog chain — clear it, reclaim it: combat that
converts to repair), and the **Night rounds** (the watch circuit's
night state: rats → marten → the escalation that makes R3's WOLF a
climax, not an ambush). The Drill yard is where skill grows between
fights; the Woodlot edge stays wolf-country dressing until R3.
Friction beyond fangs: the weather/seasons, hunger arithmetic, and the
accusation (social friction made mechanical). Full enemy/stat design:
with the economics pass.

## Mystery seeding (placed)

R0 the weir-jizō offerings (a long mystery — resolves in a later
tier) · the Night rounds' hooded lantern at the new moon (Toku,
unnamed) · R4-glimpse → T1 the alcove's sandals · R5 the packet + the
new-moon line · R7 the day-book's use-name page — and the register
waits in T1 with the unstruck line. The ruined compound looms all
tier, unexplained. Sōan's closed ledger is a SEEDED thread: he clocked
things about the man from the weir that "lost memory" doesn't explain,
and says nothing — a witness the origin tier (T3) can call on. The
kernel's one-ambiguity budget stays UNSPENT: every mystery T0 plants
has an authored answer. Genemon's silver is NOT seeded until T2's
payment road — T0 carries one secret per payer at most.
