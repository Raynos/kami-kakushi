## The enemy design cell (locked — the tier's rock)

**The office:** **the lord of the castle town — Lord TOMITA** (the
house named at the sweep; his personal name defers to the T4 build) —
a proper bushi house with retainers and a real martial seat (NOT an
administrator). The
tie that makes him THIS house's enemy: **his family sits in the
precedence the Kurosawa fell from.**

**The personal stake — scavenger in the old chair (locked):**
two–three generations ago the Kurosawa were the jin'ya-class seat of
the region (the ruined compound was their power). When they collapsed
under the flood and the famine, **this lord's house was the scavenger
that grew fat on it** — absorbing their lands, their standing, their
place at the domain's table. He holds the rank the Kurosawa
grandfather held. And it was not passive: his predecessors **helped
the fall along** — called in the famine arrears at the killing moment,
took the cut that forced the flood-works cheap — not as ledger
line-items but as a bushi house REMOVING a rival to rise.

**His hands in both wounds:**
- **Kuzuhara** stays the Kurosawa's OWN sin — the grandfather built
  fatal works; the buried shame is real and **the house is not
  innocent** (locked: full truth cuts both ways). The enemy's house
  only PROFITED from and accelerated it — took the contract cut,
  pocketed the rise.
- **Katsuhide's** famine arrears were terms the enemy's house
  **sharpened on purpose**, to break the seat they meant to replace.

**Why it is personal and desperate (not a mustache-twirl):** the
Kurosawa restoration doesn't only cost him a region — it threatens to
reveal that **his family's whole rank is scavenged from an engineered
ruin.** He fights for his bloodline's legitimacy. This is why T3's
"someone in the castle town has noticed the house": the ghost of the
seat he replaced is standing back up. **Kernel #5 holds:** the house
breaks his machine and takes the region, the truth comes fully out —
but he keeps his head, and the daimyō who ratified the old
land-transfer and shielded him is never a scene; his patrons walk.

## Katsuhide met (locked — the finder → the witness, R3–R4)

- **His exile identity:** a **temple registrar** in the temple
  quarter — the man who erased his own name has spent ten years
  writing everyone else's. Found because the MC, literate in the
  packet-trail AND in registers (the T1 tally-hand), recognizes a hand
  he half-knows in a temple ledger.
- **The scene:** no embrace. Katsuhide is calm — he made his peace a
  decade ago and does not want it disturbed. The MC brings the one
  thing he can't refuse: **his father is dying with the line still
  open** (T1's Munemasa capstone pays here — the unstruck line was a
  real cost; Katsuhide learns his father never once struck him).
- **The renunciation (authored — R4, the witness):** he does IN
  WRITING the thing Munemasa refused for ten years — **strikes his
  own line** so the named heir can hold the house cleanly — and **does
  not return.** The son completes the erasure the father wouldn't; the
  house is made whole by the heir choosing to stay gone.
- **The ink thread, tripled:** Munemasa who wouldn't strike the line ·
  Katsuhide who strikes his own · Tahei (the MC) whose family REFUSED
  to strike his. The register's whole argument, in one tier.
- **What the renunciation frees (locked 2026-07-07):** **Toku lives to
  see him found** — the packet's decade answered to her face, the
  grandmother learning the alms arrived all those years; she may die
  at the seam, at peace. And the cleared chair is offered to
  **Naoyuki, who DECLINES it** — his one proud scene; he holds the
  regency, **Shinnosuke is the named heir** (genpuku at the seam),
  and "never lord" becomes his choice. O-Hisa gets her ending —
  either one, as she wanted; the scene is the build's.
