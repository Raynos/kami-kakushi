## The audience-day system (locked — the inverse rung)

The whole tier is **T0 run backwards.** In T0 he was SUMMONED TO BE
JUDGED — the nengu scene, furniture at his own house's most important
moment. In T5 **the domain's people are summoned to HIM**: each rung
is an audience-day in the restored audience hall, a wider circle of
petitioners arriving with a bigger share of the domain's ruin. He sits
where the lord sat and does the judging now.

**The petition is the content.** Each audience-day generates
petitioners — a village's problem arrives as a PERSON WITH A FACE AND
A REQUEST: a taking to resolve (kernel #1 at scale — a missing man,
mundane cause, given back his name), a relief call (rice, a repair, a
road), a dispute to settle. He allocates from the domain's stores and
speaks verdicts. Every rung he ever climbed, inverted into a rung he
now grants.

**The inversion made personal (locked):** at R2 (bearer of rice — the
first petitioners follow the rice back), the first man to kneel before
him is **someone he once stood beside as an equal** — a hired hand
from the T0 crews, or a face from the road. The man who was carried
half-drowned to a sickroom now hears the domain from a chair, and the
game lets that land without a word about it.

## The handover (locked — the tier's heart)

Genemon fails across the whole tier. The **day-book passes at R3** (the
keeper of the day-book — Genemon hands it over); the **chair passes at
R7** (the steward). But the real weight is **the silver secret**, and
this is WHY the stewardship succession exists at all (02-house canon:
his secret cannot die with him).

**The confession (R7):** dying, Genemon tells the MC the whole
Kuzuhara truth — which the MC already half-holds from the T3
buried-truth ledger — but now it is not paper: it is the old man who
folded him in by cold arithmetic at T0-R1, who never once gave an
apology, confessing **the house's original sin** and the decades he
spent paying for it in silver on a dead man's standing instruction. He
hands over the chair, the book, AND THE BURDEN — the MC inherits the
duty to keep paying Kuzuhara's unpaid lines. Genemon dies having
handed the book to the man he hired.

**The rhyme, made the tier's spine:** Kuzuhara at estate scale — the
grandfather drowned a hamlet with fatal works — **↔ the domain's
silted great-works at domain scale**, now HIS to heal. He atones for
the house's original sin by doing the works RIGHT this time, for
everyone. The domain's ruin isn't just inherited debt; it is the same
sin, offered a second chance.

**Shinnosuke's formation:** adopted, near-ready, learning the house
FROM THE MAN WHO REBUILT IT — the mirror becomes the heir. A beat
where the MC teaches him the way Kihei taught the MC: **the creed
passing down a generation** (Kihei → the MC → Shinnosuke), the boy who
watched the drill yard now being formed into the lord he'll be by T6.
**And the truth passes here too (locked 2026-07-07): the MC tells him
about his father** — chose to leave, chooses not to return — the
mirror's payoff: the one other person the house never told things to
breaks the pattern, before the boy takes the house. The hardest
kindness in the game; the scene is the build's.

## Economics · reputation · tier-up (fifth detail)

The house no longer fights its own debt (retired at T3) — it
**administers a domain's ruin**: the resource is relief and stores,
the "debt" is the domain's, inherited as burden (the reward that is a
burden). Domain-scale standing isn't "is he trusted" but **"is the
domain healing"** — villages relieved, works repaired, roads opened.
Tier-up T5→T6: the domain saved, the house has climbed as far as a
provincial house can — and T6 (Edo, deliberately open) asks what comes
after.
