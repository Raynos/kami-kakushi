# Story Bible §3 — the cast sheet

> Part of [the story bible](README.md). **Portraits, not stat-blocks
> (human redline, 2026-07-07):** each entry is a short portrait meant
> to make a reader CARE — who this person is, what they carry, how
> they behave inside the bigger story. The old field template (want ·
> misreading · sentence shape) survives as a **palette, not a form**:
> some characters get a sharp, licensed way of talking; most get a
> plain register and a *behavior* — a thing they do, not a tic they
> perform. Prose law §0.5.2 still binds what remains: no sharp shape
> belongs to two characters, the epigram license caps at two holders,
> and the MC talks plainest of all. Tier sheets place people at nodes
> and link here — one home per character.

## The MC

- **The use-name: GONBEI (locked at the name sweep, 2026-07-07).** At
  T0's final rung the house writes him down under its **hand-me-down
  name** — the name every Kurosawa hired man has carried, handed down
  like a tool. *"The house's name for you, then. Earn a better."* The
  name is **Gonbei** — and the joke is period-true and never spoken:
  *Nanashi no Gonbei* is what Japanese folk speech calls a man with no
  name. The house's placeholder name IS the idiom for the nameless;
  his name is literally the House's property (kernel #4), and earning
  a better one is the game.
- **The speaker-label ladder.** The log/VN speaker label is itself a
  story surface (kernel #6): `You:` for the cold open's first lines →
  a small FORCED beat where he asks his own name and Sōan answers that
  he has none → the label flips to **`Nameless:`** on screen, witnessed
  → **`Gonbei:`** takes over at T0 R7 → he KNOWS his birth name at T3 →
  what the register finally says is his choice at the end (the ink
  thread, [`03-tiers.md`](03-tiers.md)).
- **Sentence shape:** plainest voice in the game — short declaratives,
  no figures of speech; a question visibly costs him.
- **The ORIGIN (relocked by the human, 2026-07-07 — the PRD design
  restored):** his birth name is **TAHEI** (working canon; §0.9
  recasting stays open at the name sweep). He fled NOTHING — a kaidō
  porter, **a landslide took him into the river** (kernel #1's plain
  accident). His family — **father Jinpachi, his mother, and his
  younger sister — are ALIVE** at the holding outside the post-town,
  and assumed him MISSING, never dead: his register entry was kept
  open (the second unstruck line). The reunion is warm, optional, and
  waiting — full design: [`tiers/t3.md`](tiers/t3.md). The old
  "what he fled / the grave with his name" origin is void.

## Motifs

- **Shinnosuke is the player's MIRROR** — the house's other outsider:
  both ask questions the household won't answer, both stand outside
  its silences. Reused deliberately across T0 and T1 (the grove beat,
  the crest question, onward). The boy watching the drill yard is
  watching what he'll one day have to become.

## The ground rules (voices sitting, 2026-07-07 + the clean pass)

- **The epigram license: Munemasa and Kihei, no one else** — the
  §0.5.2 cap (≤2 holders, at most one maxim per scene, total) is fully
  spent. Munemasa's maxims FAIL upward — he reaches for the
  sententious and can't land it, which is the house in one voice;
  Kihei's are drill-ground verdicts, the creed their charter, rare by
  temperament.
- **Misreadings are engines, not a template.** Only a few characters
  carry a real misreading of the MC, and each one is load-bearing
  drama: Genemon's fled-debt guess · Kihei's failed-fight guess ·
  Chiyo's purchased-loyalty read · Toku's private river-trade
  superstition · **Sayo's Tama certainty (a whole thread)** · the
  enemy lord's jumped-up-hand contempt. Everyone else simply meets a
  stranger and treats him like one — kernel #7 needs no machinery.
  Misreads are early-game weather: they dissolve as his standing
  becomes the thing people read instead. Naoyuki is the exception who
  reads TRUE from T0.
- **Sharp shapes are rationed.** A signature way of talking is
  reserved for the few it makes MORE real: Genemon (item, count,
  condition) · Kihei (imperative → verdict) · Toku (everything dated) ·
  Katsuhide (the completed tense) · Sayo (declared certainty) · the
  mother (a boy's present tense) · Munemasa (the failing maxims).
  Everyone else sounds like where they're from and what they do — a
  register, not a quirk. Where two registers neighbour (Chiyo and
  O-Hisa both lean on "if"), the difference is WHAT the word is doing:
  Chiyo's if carries a price; O-Hisa's protects a hope.

## The household

*(All household voices locked 2026-07-07 — reviewed and kept by the
human, batches 1–2; the clean pass re-rendered them as portraits
without changing substance.)*

- **Genemon** — the steward. He learned the house's worst secret as a
  young hand and has kept its books through the whole decline, and he
  believes — it is the nearest thing he has to faith — that the house
  can survive anything it can still count. What he wants is to see it
  outlive him *countably*, and to find someone fit to hand the silver
  instruction before he dies (the T5 succession's root). He folded the
  MC in at R1 by arithmetic, not kindness, and privately reads the
  man's silence as a defaulter's — a fled debt being worked off — and
  respects the working-off. He answers surprise by writing something
  down; he speaks in item, count, condition, and has never in his life
  reached for a metaphor. He runs the accusation night by the book and
  hands over no apology; he writes the use-name at R7; he pays the
  **Kuzuhara silver** on a dead man's standing instruction, decade
  after decade — anonymous compensation to the drowned hamlet's
  scattered survivors ([`02-house.md`](02-house.md) §The fall) — and
  gives only rumors if asked. *Nodes:* the Forecourt
  window; the board; the day-book; the steward's room from T1-R5.
- **Kihei** — the drillmaster and watch-keeper: the house's one armed
  retainer, keeping a soldier's habits in a house with nothing left to
  guard but grain. The mamushi/rat-snake line is what he says; what he
  means is **one student who outlives him**. He reads the MC's scars
  and flinch as a man who once failed to fight, and trains him the way
  you train a coward you like — without ever saying the word. He talks
  in orders and verdicts, gear-nouns between them, and holds one of
  the game's two epigram licenses, spent rarely and only on the drill
  ground; the creed is its charter: *"Talent is a story the lucky
  tell. You are not lucky. So you will work."* He sets the grain-watch
  and designs the night rounds. *Nodes:* the Drill yard; the Gate at
  watch-change; the Night rounds.
- **Sōan** — the physician: inside the house's walls, outside its
  silences. He pulled the half-drowned man's examination at the weir
  and answered the name question in the cold open — the label flip is
  his scene. He noticed at once what "lost memory" doesn't explain —
  calluses in the wrong places, a porter's hands — and wrote it in a
  ledger he closes when anyone enters, hoping to be wrong: he would
  rather have over-noticed than be the good witness to a man in
  trouble. His bedside rhythm never varies — a question, then a
  prescription. At T3 the ledger pays warm: he was right, and what he
  was right about was innocent. *Nodes:* the Sickroom; the Weir on his
  rounds.
- **Chiyo** — the lady; the arithmetic of the house's dignity. The
  house may fall — it will not *perform* falling, not while she
  chooses which screen is sold from which quiet room (the nengu
  reckoning pays by inches we SHOW, never discussed). She trusts
  arrangements, not affection, and reads the MC's loyalty as
  purchased — dependable exactly as far as wages reach; his staying
  past arithmetic is what will finally undo the read. Her sentences
  carry prices: *"If the screen sells, the gate stays painted."*
  *Nodes:* the board; the nengu reckoning; the inner garden + the
  first restored east-wing room (T1).
- **Toku** — the dowager; the house's memory, and the payer for the
  living (as Genemon pays for the dead). The packet must keep moving
  until the line can be read aloud — the grandson fed until the house
  can say his name again — and she cannot die while the lie still
  stands. Everything she says is dated — *"The year the weir went, we
  sent it at the new moon."* — a woman living at anchor in the past
  because the present is a held breath. Once, privately, she permits
  herself the superstition she publicly performs: perhaps the river
  gave the house a man back, in trade for the one the mountain took.
  **She lives to see him found (locked):** the packet's decade is
  answered to her face at T4 — the grandmother who fed a vanished
  grandson learns the alms arrived, all those years. *Nodes:* the
  shrine-alcove corridor; her room (T1); the new-moon walk.
- **O-Hisa** — Katsuhide's wife, the widow-who-isn't: ten years
  married to an unstruck line. What she wants is an ending — *either*
  one — some way to stop waiting that doesn't betray the waiting. She
  mends the MC's things and leaves them at the woodshed without
  knocking, because he is a man someone somewhere is surely waiting
  for, and someone should do it in that someone's place. She never
  lets a hope finish out loud — *"If the rice holds — well."* — she
  guards it by not naming it whole. *Nodes:* the Kitchen; the Woodshed
  doorstep; the East wing (T1), airing a room that was hers.
- **Shinnosuke** — the heir's son, ~12 at T0; the player's MIRROR. He
  wants his father to have been *taken* — taken is kinder than left —
  and, underneath, simply to be told the truth his own house keeps
  from him. He interrogates the board in questions stacked two at a
  time, never waiting for the first answer; watches the drill yard
  from the wall; sneaks the bamboo grove; asks the crest question
  nobody answers. He takes the MC for a natural ally — the one other
  person the house doesn't tell things to. Adopted heir T4–T5; lord by
  T6, formed at the last by the man who rebuilt the house — **and it
  is the MC who finally tells him the truth about his father (T5,
  locked): the mirror's payoff — the one other person the house never
  told things to breaks the pattern, before the boy takes the house.** *Nodes:*
  the board; the drill-yard wall; the Bamboo grove.
- **Naoyuki** — the second son, heir by subtraction: his whole life a
  provisional entry, and he knows it. What he wants is to be
  *enough* — for the house to survive on him without his ever being
  the son they wanted. He is the one person who reads the MC true from
  the first — *"you are hiding something"* is the truest sentence
  anyone says in T0 — and his sentences break off when they come too
  near feeling: begins formal, abandons it, restates flat and cold.
  Regent through the lordless years; the MC's ally to the end, and
  never once warm. **His ending (locked):** offered the cleared chair
  after the renunciation, he declines it — the refusal is his one
  proud scene, and "never lord" becomes his choice. *Nodes:* the veranda; the Count; the East wing
  threshold (T1), carrying his brother's things himself.
- **Munemasa** — the old lord, dying by inches; in T0 a voice through
  a wall. **The witness-heir (locked):** as a young man he watched his
  father build the fatal works, sign past the warnings, and swallow
  the fire and the quarrying in silence — his voice has its wound.
  For ten years his single act of fatherhood has been refusal: he will not strike his son's line, will not be the man who
  wrote his boy dead — hope kept alive as an unfinished entry. His
  sentences turn on themselves and end unsure, and his licensed maxims
  fail upward — he reaches for the sententious and can't land it,
  which is the house in one voice. His one scene is T1's capstone in
  the restored shoin: the register, his last entry, three lines
  unstruck above it; he dies at the seam. *Nodes:* through a wall
  (T0); the Shoin (T1).
- **Katsuhide** — the elder son, THE WOUND
  ([`02-house.md`](02-house.md)); self-vanished ~1770, alive as a
  **temple registrar** in the castle town — the man who erased his own
  name has spent ten years writing everyone else's. He made his peace
  a decade ago and wants only to stay filed away; what undoes him is
  the one fact he cannot refuse: his father is dying with the line
  still open — never once struck. He speaks in closed entries, the
  completed tense: *"That account is closed. This one I keep."* At
  T4-R4 he renounces in writing — strikes his own line, the erasure
  his father refused — and does not return. What ten years made of
  him: open for the T4 prose. *Nodes:* the temple quarter's registry
  (T4).

## The estate's edge

- **Yohei** — the pedlar *(renamed from old-canon "Tokubei" — the Toku
  collision)*. His stall at the gate on market days is T0's whole coin
  economy: a finite purse, stock that turns with the seasons, and a
  tradesman's warmth that arrives exactly as fast as the money is
  good. He prices the stray as a mark at first and re-prices him as a
  regular once the man deals square — the one relationship on the
  estate that warms by honest arithmetic. He talks the way he trades:
  news, flattery, and the price in one breath, the sale in the middle.
  *Nodes:* the Gate & gateyard, market days.
- **O-Yae** — the scullery day-girl: the only person who crosses the
  estate/village line every day, and the line's whole news service in
  both directions. Nothing she says is her own claim — it is always
  what *they* say — which keeps her blameless and keeps her listened
  to. The village track previewed two tiers early; rumors about the MC
  flow out through her as surely as rumors flow in. *Nodes:* the
  Kitchen by day; the estate/village road.
- **Matsuzō** — the old weir-keeper downstream, whom the house leases
  the weir from. He has outlived needing much: the lease paid, the
  river left alone. Ask him about people and he answers about the
  water — not a dodge so much as where his attention has lived for
  sixty years. He saw the night the MC arrived — *"the river was quiet
  that night"* — and has never made anything of it; at T3 that unmade
  observation becomes the origin trail's first honest clue. *Nodes:*
  the Weir & riverbank; the lease day.
- **Iori** — the traveling monk, lodging at the gate at New Year and
  Bon. The tier's one stranger-kindness, priced correctly: he wants
  nothing, gives what he has, and leaves. His goodbyes live inside his
  greetings — a man permanently passing through. Temple-network
  texture: the same network Toku's packet rides, possibly carried
  unknowing; a jizō red herring. He speaks no maxims — the license is
  spent, and he is the man who'd have earned it. *Nodes:* the Gate.
- **O-Ume** — the widow at the paddy's edge, who lost her husband to
  the river and speaks to him still — sets his place at Bon, thanks
  the kami for what living hands did. T0's on-screen believer: kernel
  #1's whole tenderness in one person, grief-work the game never
  mocks. She is kind to the MC the way you are kind to an omen — the
  river gave one back. **The jizō offerings are HERS (locked; revealed
  T3):** a decade of grief-offerings for her drowned husband, left at
  the spot where the river later gave a man back — the game's oldest
  mystery closes warm as the origin trail crests. *Nodes:* the paddy's
  edge; the Field margins; the river at Bon.
- **Rokusuke** — the named face of the hired hands, who has learned
  the yard's oldest lesson: the noticed hand is the blamed one. He
  agrees early and often, head down, and outlasts everyone who didn't.
  His load-tally is what clears the MC at the Count — the quietest man
  in the scene turns out to be holding the exculpatory number. *(Wider
  hands and harvest women stay ambient until touched twice — kernel
  #2.)* *Nodes:* the Home paddy; the board at the Count.

## The works & the crews — T1

- **Denshichi** — the crew boss, running the paid seasonal crews out
  of the village; his real ledger is men-fed-through-the-lean-season.
  He wasn't there for any of T0, so the named hand is just green
  labour until proven otherwise — the status reset made human, nothing
  systemic. His orders come out as facts about the work: *"the wall
  wants restacking before noon"* — and you'd better. The first
  village-side face met as a *boss*: the T2 village track previewed
  from the labour side. *Nodes:* the morning muster (T1 R0–R1); the
  terraces at season peaks.
- **Heikichi** — the master joiner, hired for the works; the MC's
  carpentry teacher. He apprenticed on the compound before the fall
  and remembers where the orchard lanterns hung; he dates nothing out
  loud. Praise and grief both come out of him as carpentry — he talks
  to the wood and lets people overhear. At the shoin restoration he
  reads the salvage marks in the timbers — *"Good wood. Came from over
  there."* — a seed that turns legible only at T2's reveal, never a
  fourth signal. The shoin is their joint work; the last boards he
  makes the MC set alone. *Nodes:* the Workshops (joiner's bench); the
  wings under repair; the Shoin.
- **Ribei** — the creditor's clerk: the debt's face, twice a year on
  interest days, polite as weather. There is no malice in him and no
  mercy either — he apologizes his way to a figure that is always
  exact and always last. T1's renegotiation war is fought across the
  board against that courtesy; the lender himself stays offstage
  (banked — the T4 design cell's call). *Nodes:* the board, interest
  days only.
- **Tetsuji** — the smith; R6's first hiring, the choice that lights
  the cold forge. A craftsman whose whole ambition is a fire that
  stays lit, and who spends words the way he spends charcoal — as few
  as the job takes. He knows the MC as the man who gave him the fire,
  which is simply true: the first person the MC ever hires knows him
  by reputation, not silence — the tier turning, on schedule. *Nodes:*
  the Workshops (the forge, late-T1).

## The village — T2 (see [`tiers/t2.md`](tiers/t2.md))

- **Mohei** — the headman *(renamed from the sitting's "Jinbei" —
  yields to Jinpachi)*. He keeps the village's grievances the way
  Genemon keeps the house's debts — entered, weighed, settled at the
  least possible cost — and he wants the valley quiet more than he
  wants it just. His daughter's certainty about Tama is one more thing
  he'd rather not stir. The R6 headman's-table scenes are his.
  *Nodes:* the headman's house; the well; the table.
- **Sayo** — the headman's daughter, THE NAMER: *"Tama? You're
  alive?"* She was a child when Tama vanished and the grief never
  closed; she needs the MC to be him, and declares it with a certainty
  that dares the village to differ — *"I know your walk."* The warmest
  and most insistent misreading in the game, the Tama thread's living
  heart, the engine the player leans into or against all tier. Her
  grief-work ends in daylight at T3, when the vanished child gets her
  real name back. *Nodes:* the well; the market; the Bon rites.
- **Ekai** — the temple keeper, and keeper of the **register of the
  vanished** — every taking the valley ever recorded, in ink, two of
  them false (Katsuhide's; Tama's). A parish archivist whose fidelity
  is to the entries: he names the missing by when they went, because
  that is what the register knows. He forwards anonymous alms without
  asking whose — the temple is a node on the packet's network; whether
  he suspects stays unwritten until the T4 design needs it. At T3 he
  amends Tama's entry on screen. *Nodes:* the temple; the register.
- **Kyūbei** — the miller: everyone's grain crosses his stones, so
  everyone owes him small and resents him small, and he grumbles about
  it into every sack he hands back — sour, never unkind. First
  fair-price ally once the surcharge cracks: money honest is the only
  argument he respects. *Nodes:* the mill.
- **Funakichi** — the ferry keeper; Matsuzō's downstream mirror, the
  river's other human. He knows the valley by its crossings — who went
  over, with what, saying how little — and reports it plainly when
  asked. The night-roads trouble lands on his crossing first. *Nodes:*
  the ferry; the ford.
- **Seiroku** — the bandit chief the village calls **"the mountain
  dogs"** — kernel #1 played plain: hungry men, not monsters. He
  states hunger as fact, without apology or threat — *"we ate the seed
  rice in the snow month; men do."* Some of his men carry the castle
  town district's accent: hungry for a reason nobody in the valley
  made (the T4 enemy's first shadow, unexplained all tier). The R5
  threshold fight and the R6 campaign are his. *Nodes:* the bandit
  camp; the night roads.
- **The ordinary pool** — Kumazō (a tenant farmer; the village's
  loudest laugh) · O-Haru (the pickle stall; a young mother) · Ganzō
  (the well's old man; remembers everything exactly one year wrong) ·
  O-Mitsu (O-Yae's friend on the village side) · Bunta (a boy who
  follows the MC's cart the length of the village, every time, saying
  nothing). Plot-roleless BY DESIGN — faces for the scenes, ambient
  until the story touches one twice (kernel #2); deliberately no
  assigned voices — a voice is a promise the story will come back.

## The region — T3 (the origin, relocked warm — [`tiers/t3.md`](tiers/t3.md))

- **Jinpachi** — the father. Twelve years of a want he never once said
  out loud: his son home. He kept the register entry open — refused
  the strike — and kept the boy's carrying-pole oiled and hung. Met at
  the holding's gate, he knows his son before he's through it, and
  everything he cannot say comes out as chores: *"Your pole's where
  you left it."* *Nodes:* the family holding outside Sawatari-juku.
- **O-Nobu** — the mother *(named at the sweep: nobu, "faith" — she
  kept it)*. She never struck him from her
  own reckoning either, and when he stands in the doorway the twelve
  years collapse: she speaks to him in the present tense of the boy
  who left — old diminutives, old scoldings, time folded in her mouth.
  The reunion's peak and its fewest words. *Nodes:* the holding.
- **Suzu** — the younger sister *(named at the sweep: a bell — a
  small bright sound from his childhood)*. A child when the
  mountain took him, grown now; she barely remembers the brother she
  never stopped hearing about, and she has a life built in his absence
  to protect. Guarded, formal, watching — she says "brother" like a
  word in a language she's forgetting, and means it a little more each
  time. The reunion's sharpest face. *Nodes:* the holding.
- **Kenta** — the porter friend; the origin side-track's first face.
  He talks to the MC as if no time passed — easy, teasing, finishing
  the man's sentences the way he did on the road — and the ache is
  that the MC can't yet return it. He tells him the missing years:
  they searched the river both springs; the entry was never struck.
  *Nodes:* the post-town roads; Sawatari-juku.
- **Otsuru** — the grown woman who was "Tama." She ran (why is an open
  cell, kernel-bound), built a life at Yanagi-watari that is hers, and
  owes the valley nothing — least of all its grief. She answers
  exactly what is asked and nothing past it; whether she ever visits,
  forgives, or writes is her own. The Tama truth resolves on the spine
  through her — grounded and partial, exactly as promised. *Nodes:*
  Yanagi-watari.
- **Zenbei** — the toiya master *(named at the sweep)* — the
  post-town transport office, where the region's houses are a berth-order and he keeps it.
  He registers the Kurosawa third, behind the two that were always
  there, and says so without cruelty: rank is simply his trade. The
  export ramp to T4 runs through his book. *Nodes:* the toiya.

## The castle town & the domain — T4–T5 principals

- **Lord TOMITA** — the enemy *(house named at the sweep — the PRD's
  rival-house name, revived; his personal name is deferred to the T4
  build, and logs read "Lord Tomita"; To-/Toku is a documented
  class-exception — house name vs given name)* — the lord of the
  castle town: a martial house that rose on the Kurosawa's fall,
  absorbed their lands and their place at the domain's table, and
  helped the falling — the famine arrears called at the killing
  moment, the flood-works cut taken (the design cell:
  [`tiers/t4.md`](tiers/t4.md)). He speaks in inheritance and
  precedence — earned, held, owed by blood — because legitimacy is the
  one thing he defends; to him the MC is a jumped-up hand carrying a
  dead house's name, and the MC's whole life is the refutation (kernel
  #3). He answers partially, per kernel #5: the region lost, the truth
  out, his head kept; the daimyō above him never a scene. *Nodes:* the
  official quarter (T4).
- **Inspector HORI** — the bakufu's man *(named at the sweep)* — the shogunate's man
  who places the broken domain in the house's care and lodges in the
  guest house while it happens (T5). To him the ruin is a filing
  problem and the MC a competent provincial — useful, ranked, never a
  peer; he cites code and precedent because that is the world he is
  from and the weight he carries. The reward-that-is-a-burden arrives
  through him. *Nodes:* the audience hall; the guest house.
