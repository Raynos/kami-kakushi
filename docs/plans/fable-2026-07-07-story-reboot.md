## §4 · Phase 1 output — the kernel, redlined (locked point by point)

The canonical full statements, written for the first time in the Phase 1
step-1 redline (the names alone were the only durable form before this).
Each point below is HUMAN-LOCKED as it lands; this section graduates into
the pillars doc → `story-bible.md §0`.

1. **The lie of the vanishing** *(locked 2026-07-07 — teeth softened by
   the human: mundane truths MAY out-cruel the myth, not MUST).*
   *Kami-kakushi* — the spirit-abduction the title promises — is a lie the
   world tells itself to bear what it cannot face. Every vanishing has a
   human cause: debt, flight, shame, violence, cold arithmetic. The
   folk-belief is never mocked — believing is grief-work, and the game
   treats believers with kindness — but the truth, when the player finally
   holds it, is always mundane; it may cut deeper than the myth would have
   (the best ones do), but it is not required to. The game permits itself
   at most ONE moment of genuine ambiguity, ever. No supernatural cause is
   ever real, no yokai ever spawns, and the player is never teased with
   magic the fiction won't cash.

2. **Nobody twice unnamed** *(locked as drafted, 2026-07-07 — all three
   readings).* A person may enter the story nameless — a stranger washed
   up at the gate, a vanished child in a decade-old rumor, a face in a
   field. But no one the story touches twice stays that way. The MC
   arrives with no name, and the game is the earning of one. The vanished
   are recovered BY NAME — to solve a vanishing is to give someone back
   their name, and with it their story. And once the player has learned a
   name, the fiction never takes it back: nobody we've met is ever again
   "a farmhand," "a pedlar," a prop. Namelessness is where every thread
   starts; leaving someone unnamed twice is the one cruelty the game
   never commits. *(Readings: the arc — the ladder IS the earning of a
   name; the mystery — solving = re-naming, cashing #1's grief-work; the
   craft rule — no disposable extras on the second touch.)*

3. **Worth written by work** *(locked 2026-07-07 — anti-flattery clause
   softened by the human: praise scarce-and-earned, no literal
   ledger-trail required).* No one in this world is owed anything by
   blood, destiny, or gift. Every advantage on screen was bought, lucked
   into, or trained — never innate; talent is a story the lucky tell. The
   MC is mediocre by design: what raises him is labor, repeated, until it
   is recorded. And "written" is literal — this is a world of ledgers,
   deed-books, and rank-charts, where worth exists because someone wrote
   it down. Work becomes deeds, deeds become standing, standing becomes a
   name (#2). The game never grants what the player hasn't ground; praise
   is scarce and earned — the fiction never hands out warmth the work
   hasn't paid for, though it needn't produce the ledger every time.

4. **House before the man** *(locked 2026-07-07 — the OPEN version: the
   late game MAY stage the man-vs-House question; the resolution honors
   the House).* The unit of this story is not the hero but the household.
   The MC's rise is real only as the House's rise — his ladder is its
   ledger (#3), and what ascends from circle to circle is the House's
   standing in the world, with him inside it. Every named member serves
   the House first and their own heart second, and the gap between those
   two is where the drama lives: trust extended because the House needs
   hands, coldness kept because the House cannot afford another wound,
   praise withheld because the House's dignity is spending it. The enemy
   attacks the House, not the man — debt, disrepair, a name in decline.
   The House outlasts everyone in it: choices weigh what they cost the
   House after the chooser is gone. The late game may ask whether the man
   outgrows the House — the asking is drama, and permitted — but the
   answer honors the House.

5. **Partial justice** *(locked as drafted, 2026-07-07 — incl. the
   full-truth contract).* The world's wrongs are answered the way real
   wrongs are: incompletely. Some who deserve ruin answer for it; some
   walk; some are never even reached — glimpsed once, protected by
   distance, rank, or paper. Justice, where it lands, is mediated: it
   travels up through other hands, costs its seekers dearly — sometimes
   everything — and arrives smaller than the wrong. The MC's reach has a
   ceiling and the story honors it: no throne room, no audience with the
   untouchable, no final scene where power kneels for the camera. What
   the game promises instead is the other half of the deal: the TRUTH is
   recovered in full even where the justice isn't. Full truth, partial
   justice — that is the contract with the player, and it is never
   broken in either direction.

6. **Form is the fiction** *(locked 2026-07-07 — enforcement clause
   softened by the human to a strong NORM: exhaust redesign before
   shipping a story/mechanic disagreement, not an absolute).* The game's
   form — its panels, logs, charts, numbers, buttons — is not a window
   onto the world; it is made OF the world. Every surface the player
   touches is a thing that exists in the fiction: the log a house
   journal, the bestiary a field-guide someone actually keeps, the rank
   chart a banzuke, the balance a ledger in real koku and mon. No
   abstract game-word — XP, buff, level, quest — ever reaches the player;
   every mechanic wears its period name and earns its period shape.
   Surfaces appear because the story produced them: the reveal follows
   the beat, the beat dramatizes the need, and nobody in the fiction ever
   describes the interface (that is the ventriloquism this point
   forbids). When a mechanic and the fiction disagree, exhaust redesign
   before shipping the disagreement.

- **~~Woodblock restraint~~ — DEMOTED (human, 2026-07-07).** The name
   predated UI-v2 — the woodblock/washi lock retired with ADR-127/ADR-144
   (Andon Steel is the locked visual language, owned by ui-design.md).
   The redrawn register-wide statement ("Restraint is the house style" —
   prose says less than it knows; ornament earned by rarity; cut when in
   doubt; visuals delegated to the ui bible by pointer; the any-game
   distinctiveness test for fiction-voiced text) is PARKED as a Phase-1
   step-2 fiction-pillar candidate, not ground.

7. **The world is nobody's mirror** *(NEW — adopted at the redline's
   closing check, 2026-07-07, replacing the demoted slot; the
   anti-fairness-machine law as positive ground).* The world does not
   exist to assess the MC — and it assesses him wrong as often as life
   does. People misread him, suspect him, use him, forget him; some warm
   slowly, some never; the village keeps living when he isn't looking,
   and he hears of it late. Being misread is not an injustice the game
   corrects — it is the weather. The player's true standing lives in the
   ledgers (#3); hearts are slower, stranger, and never owed. A stranger
   who works hard is still a stranger.
