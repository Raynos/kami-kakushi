## §0 · The one geography (master truths)

There is ONE world. The T0 sheet is a **window** into it; the T1 sheet is
the same world seen wider and one-to-two years later. Every landmark keeps
its position across both sheets — T1 is *revealed*, never *rearranged*.
(Implementation: one master layout in shared world units; T0's viewBox is a
crop of T1's.)

- **G1 · The valley.** Hill country rises across the whole NORTH in two+
  tiers; the land steps down southward. The village (unseen, T2's ground)
  lies SOUTH down the valley; the temple-country (Toku's packet road) lies
  NORTH-WEST upstream; the next valley lies EAST over the wooded hill.
- **G2 · The river** enters at the NW from the hills, runs down the WEST
  side, exits SW. It flows **north → south**. In river order: upstream
  pools (T1) → the old breach (T1) → the flood-works channels → **the weir
  + weir-jizō** (T0) → the weir reeds just downstream (T0) → the
  downstream shallows, Matsuzō's stretch (T1). Matsuzō's hut sits on the
  **far (west) bank** — "his side of the water" — with a plank footbridge
  below the weir connecting it.
- **G3 · Water works.** A channel leaves the weir EASTWARD and branches
  into ditches feeding the paddies — the single most functional line on
  the sheet, and it must be DRAWN. In T0 exactly one channel runs and one
  old branch lies silted (broken, dotted — the hidden sluice the
  field-work hints at). In T1 the works are repaired: fresh-ink channels,
  the breach closed, the pools drained.
- **G4 · THE OLD PRECINCT — the sheet's buried truth.** A great
  jin'ya-class compound occupies the CENTRE-NORTH: wall lines mostly
  robbed to footings (the village quarried them), a crumbled great gate
  facing south, fallen roof masses, an overgrown inner garden. It is
  **5× the footprint of the lived-in house**. On BOTH sheets it is locked
  scenery — roped off, never honestly named (T0 calls it "ruined
  compound"; T1 drops even the seal but keeps the drawing).
- **G5 · The guest house sits IN the old precinct's SE corner.** The
  lived-in compound — "the main house," as everyone calls it — is a
  walled sub-compound tucked into the dead precinct's south-east corner,
  sharing its ground. The T0 sheet shows this WITHOUT saying it: the
  precinct's footing-lines continue past the guest house's own neat wall;
  the forecourt's swept ground is sized for a household five times this
  one; the orchard between ruin and guest house stands in courtyard rows.
  A cold reader should be able to say *"the little compound sits inside /
  at the corner of something much older and larger."*
- **G6 · The guest-house anatomy** (one winged residence + yards): south
  gate & gateyard (the only formal entrance; Yohei's stall ground) →
  oversized forecourt with the well → the winged main house centre-north
  (kitchen threshold on its WEST flank facing the paddies; the
  shrine-alcove corridor INSIDE, joining the wings) → the working kura at
  the NE corner (fire-separated) → woodshed near the SW corner → Sōan's
  sickroom lean-to off the outer court SW → **the drill yard = the OLD
  STABLE COURT on the EAST flank, inside the walls** — a stable range
  with stall divisions for twenty horses, holding one mule.
- **G7 · The fields.** Paddies lie WEST/SW of the compound on the flat
  between house and river — home paddy & vegetable rows nearest the
  kitchen side, field margins the fringe band beyond them. GHOST bunds —
  faint traces of former fields — extend the worked pattern to ~4× its
  area, out to the boundary stones. In T1 the terraced paddies climb the
  NW lower slopes (upper & lower runs), with the let-go terraces —
  numbered stones still counting — in the scrub above them.
- **G8 · The wilds.** The bamboo grove stands BEHIND the precinct
  (N/NE rear); the overgrown orchard fills the precinct ground between
  ruin and guest house (courtyard rows gone feral) — the monkeys' raid
  corridor runs grove → orchard → vegetable rows. The woodlot edge
  scatters conifers along the EAST, thickening into forest (T1: the
  woodlot proper + charcoal clamp deep E/NE; the char marks resolve into
  a straight-edged burn line). The family plot (T1) terraces the lower
  slope N/NE above the precinct — grave stones, one swept plot with no
  stone.
- **G9 · The bounds.** Boundary stones ring the estate FAR beyond every
  worked row — south, west along the river, east at the forest. On T0
  they are unnamed marks near the sheet's edges (the wrongness is
  visible, not explained); on T1 they are a named zone, and ONE stone on
  the river side stands fresher than its brothers, a field short of the
  old line.
- **G10 · The roads.** The main road runs from the south gate SOUTH off
  the sheet (period note: *to the village*). A hill track exits EAST
  through the woodlot (*over the hill*). The upstream path follows the
  river NW (*to the temple country*). Work paths radiate sensibly: gate →
  paddies → weir; compound → orchard/bamboo; compound → woodlot.
- **G11 · The night layer.** The night-rounds patrol rail loops
  gate → kura → forecourt → paddy edge → gate (drawn when the 夜 seal is
  selected). A separate faint red thread — the hooded new-moon lantern —
  leaves the yard's far edge and goes UPSTREAM, unexplained on the sheet.
- **G12 · Scale & witness.** A period scale bar (間/町), the north
  arrow, the title cartouche with the red 改 seal, and a 凡例 legend
  decoding the survey's own marks (戦 · 人 · 怪 · 新 · chip borders ·
  the reviser's red). The mystery marks (the jizō offerings, the lantern
  thread) stay deliberately OUTSIDE the legend.

## §2 · The T1 sheet — the land & "the main house" (1781–82)

Same world, full frame, drawn as the RE-SURVEY at the tier's end (R7):
the reviser's red records what the tier changed. The 13 carried T0 zones
keep their exact places. What T1 adds or transforms:

- **The land at true extent.** Terraced paddies (upper & lower) climb the
  NW slopes in numbered runs; the let-go terraces above them keep the
  numbering on stones running into scrub (tiny kanji numerals — the
  wrong thing made legible). The woodlot proper + charcoal clamp sit deep
  E/NE (the clamp: a mound + smoke wisp; the straight-edged burn line in
  the forest texture). Boundary stones & far fields: the S/SE bounds
  named; the river-side stone fresher and a field short of the ghost
  line. Downstream shallows: Matsuzō's fish weirs + the otters' den in
  DRESSED STONE (squared blocks in a wild bank). Upstream pools: drawn
  as drained beds (post-R4) with the red strike + 改 note; the old breach
  shown CLOSED in fresh stonework (new-ink), robbed-stone hollows still
  visible beside it.
- **The guest house opened.** The sub-compound grows a legible interior
  plan (large enough for its seals to sit on their rooms): East wing
  (fresh-ink whole roof — rebuilt), West wing (intact but drawn CLOSED —
  shutter marks, no ghost-ruin: the household keeps it like an open one),
  the Inner garden between the wings (stepping-stone path running to a
  small gate in the N wall that opens on the rope and the ruin), the
  Shoin at the N end (fresh-ink, the last boards), the Workshops along
  the outer court (joiner's bench; the forge LIT at R6 — a small smoke
  wisp), Kura interior as a second seal on the same kura.
- **The family plot.** N/NE lower slope above the precinct: a small
  walled grave terrace, stones in rows, ONE swept, weeded, stoneless
  plot; the Bon path up from the compound.
- **The build spine in red+fresh ink.** Everything the tier rebuilt reads
  as NEW WORK against the old survey: re-stacked terrace walls, the
  charcoal clamp back in rotation, the breach closed, the east wing +
  steward's room, the forge, the shoin. The reviser's red strikes what
  died (the pools) and stamps 新 on what this survey added. The ruin
  itself shows NO new work — it waits.
- **What T1 must NOT do:** move any landmark; name the precinct honestly
  (that is T2's reveal); put the wings anywhere but the guest house;
  show human enemies anywhere (the no-human line holds).

## §3 · The look — the AA bar

The sheet is an **aizuri-night survey**: a lamplit estate map drawn in
silver-ink and gold on deep indigo-steel, fully committed — atmosphere
earned by rendering, not by darkness. (The warm-washi alternative was
built A/B, reviewed live, and REJECTED by the human — night-indigo is the
locked substrate; ADR-149 as amended.) All colour stays on the
Andon Steel tokens (ui-design.md §2): steel grounds, ink ramp, silver =
drawn state, gold = built/worked structure, shu = the reviser's red only.

- **L1 · Substrate.** Layered ground washes (flat steel-band fills — no
  raster grain): a valley-floor tone, field tones, forest under-wash,
  water tone, swept-court tone. Edge-darkening vignette; a faint
  3×3 fold-crease line grid (stroke-based). Empty ground must read as
  PAPER-OF-THE-SHEET, never as unrendered void.
- **L2 · Stroke hierarchy, brush life.** Four weights: hairline detail
  (~0.8) · fine texture (~1.2) · working line (~1.8–2.2, paths/water/
  buildings) · structure (~3–4.5, precinct wall, main roofs, the weir).
  Structural strokes are TAPERED variable-width brush polygons with
  dry-brush breakup on long runs — never uniform CAD polylines. Contrast
  floor: every load-bearing line ≥ ~35% against its ground; recede by
  weight + warm-grey tone, never by opacity-to-invisible.
- **L3 · Buildings are ink pictograms.** Filled roof masses (steel-hi
  face + heavy ridge + shadow-side stroke + eave ticks), engawa lines,
  ground-plan fidelity: the winged house reads as ONE building with
  wings and a joining corridor. The kura wears the white-plaster body.
  The gate is an EVENT: gatehouse roof, flanking wall, worn threshold.
- **L4 · Ruin is depicted, not notated.** Broken/gapped wall strokes,
  robbed footings as dotted stone-lines, fallen roof planes as collapsed
  geometry, rubble stipple, grass tufts breaching floors, an ash-grey
  wash. Dashed-outline notation survives only as the seal-chip border.
- **L5 · Water is a treatment.** Double tapered bank strokes, current
  threads, wave-combs at pools and below the weir, shoal ticks; the weir
  as laid stones + stakes; channels/ditches as fine double lines with
  sluice-gate bars. Flow is readable N→S at a glance.
- **L6 · Vegetation is a system.** 3–5 pine variants (rotation/scale
  jitter) clustered into masses over an under-wash, culm-massed bamboo
  with leaf flicks at the silhouette, round-crown fruit trees on the
  orchard grid with feral tangle strokes, reed tufts, grass ticks.
  Never a stamped identical glyph grid.
- **L7 · Ground-use texture.** Paddy = pale wash + transplant-row dashes
  + bund lines; dry rows = furrow hatching; courts = clean swept tone
  with rake arcs; forest floor = sparse stipple; slope = fish-scale hill
  profiles + contour hachures (accent, not the only terrain mark).
- **L8 · Furniture.** Title cartouche (黒沢家領内絵図・改) with the red
  hanko; the 凡例 legend (G12); scale bar; north arrow; sheet border
  with corner ticks; period distance notes at the three exits. The
  mystery marks stay unexplained.
- **L9 · Labels.** Keep the seal-chip grammar (it works). Add knockout
  halos in the ground tone; collision-checked placement; the river name
  set along the river; English glosses one step smaller; interior-room
  seals sit ON their rooms (the T1 house is drawn big enough).
- **L10 · Zoom rewards.** Two LOD tiers: a `fine` layer (stall
  divisions, offering marks, stone numerals, rake arcs, shutter marks)
  fades in past ~2× zoom so the fit view stays composed and the close
  view pays craft.

## §4 · Code shape (the primitives contract)

No inline one-off drawing in sheet composition. The toolkit lives in
`src/ui/map-sheets/` (player-bound — ADR-151): `brush.ts` (seeded rng, scrawl, tapered
brushStroke, stipple, hatch, waveComb) · `terrain.ts` (hillRange,
hachureBand, groundWash) · `water.ts` (river, pool, weirBar, channel,
flowTicks, bridge) · `fields.ts` (paddyBlock, terraceRun, ghostBunds,
furrows) · `flora.ts` (pine, fruitTree, bambooClump, reedTuft, grassTuft,
treeMass) · `built.ts` (roofMass, wingedHouse, kura, leanTo, gatehouse,
wallRun, robbedFooting, ruinFootprint, stoneMarker, well, forge,
stableRange) · `furniture.ts` (cartouche, legend, scaleBar, northArrow,
border, foldCreases, distanceNote) · `layout.ts` (the ONE master
geography: every anchor in shared world units + the T0 window) ·
`t0-sheet.ts` / `t1-sheet.ts` (thin compositions: layout data → primitive
calls). Grown since the rebuild landed: `ground.ts` (the L1 wash bands +
the life scatter) · `sheet.ts` (the shared shell — pan/zoom/pinch,
roster, detail, night-rail, the LOD gate, the caption pass) · `nodes.ts`
(zone roster/detail data, bible-distilled, + the `RUNG_LADDER`
placeholder) · `reveal.ts` (the ADR-151 rung-reveal fog: unsurveyed
paper, 未 ghost chips, rumor notes). The look is FROZEN by the golden pin
(`golden.test.ts` hashes both grounds into `golden.hash.json`; an
intentional visual change regenerates it with `UPDATE_MAP_GOLDEN=1`,
committed WITH the change) — every refactor proves itself look-neutral
against the pin.

## §5 · The blind-reader rubric (the pass bar)

A fresh agent given ONLY a screenshot set must recover these. **M** =
must-say (fail the iteration if missed) · **S** = should-say.

**Both sheets**
- R1 **M** — a pre-modern Japanese rural estate survey; north up; a
  named family's holding.
- R2 **M** — a river on the west flowing north → south (pools/weir/reeds
  /shallows in the right downstream order on T1).
- R3 **M** — hills across the north; the land organized valley-wise.
- R4 **M** — paddies between river and house, fed by DRAWN channels
  from the weir.
- R5 **M** — ONE great ruined compound and a smaller lived-in walled
  house AT ITS CORNER / INSIDE ITS GROUND — the reader must express
  that the living compound sits within/against something older and
  larger, not merely "a ruin stands nearby."
- R6 **M** — the estate has visibly SHRUNK: worked fields are a
  fraction of the old traces; boundary stones stand far beyond
  everything worked.
- R7 **M** — roads lead somewhere: south to a village/world, east over
  the hill, upstream along the river.
- R8 **S** — the drill yard is an old stable court (stalls out of
  proportion to any horses present).
- R9 **S** — the orchard was planted as courtyards (rows aligned to the
  ruin, not the fields); the bamboo grove pressures the vegetable rows.
- R10 **S** — a legend exists and decodes the survey marks; scale is
  stated; the sheet reads as a commissioned, revised document.
- R11 **S** — quality read: "illustrated place," stroke variety, zoom
  reveals craft — NOT "labeled diagram," "floating labels," "empty."

**T1 additions**
- R12 **M** — same world as T0, wider: no landmark moved.
- R13 **M** — the lived house now shows wings: one wing fresh/rebuilt,
  one closed; a garden between; new work legible against old survey.
- R14 **M** — the water works were REPAIRED: breach closed, pools
  drained/struck in red, ditches extended.
- R15 **M** — terraces climb the NW slope; abandoned numbered terraces
  continue above the worked ones.
- R16 **S** — the ruin shows NO new work while everything around it
  does.
- R17 **S** — a grave terrace on the north slope; one plot swept but
  stoneless (or at minimum: a family plot outside the walls).
- R18 **S** — the charcoal clamp/woodlot economy east; the straight
  burn-line edge in the forest.

Iteration loop: capture (`src/scripts/map-audit-shots.mjs`) → one fresh
blind-describe agent per sheet → a judge agent scores THIS rubric →
fix the misses → repeat until all **M** pass and ≥ half of **S**.

The T2 sheet's own pass bar (V-lines) lives with its spec in **§6.4** —
it is not a T1-style addition to the estate rubric but a wider-frame
rubric of its own, because T2 changes the register (valley, not estate).

## §6 · The T2 sheet — the Valley, Asagiri (1784)

**Status: SPEC, pre-build — awaiting the human's HR read (map-styles §4
locked flow: spec → HR → build). No drawing until sign-off.**

The register widens. The T0/T1 sheets are the *estate survey* (mura-ezu
村絵図) — the holding drawn field-by-field. The T2 sheet is the SAME
idiom **pulled back to the valley** (mura-ezu, wide): the estate is now
one walled compound in a settled valley, and the VILLAGE of **Asagiri**
(朝霧 — "morning mist") joins the frame downstream. Whole compounds
become single pictograms; fields become pattern masses; the river runs
the length of the sheet as the valley's spine.

Derived from the story bible ([`tiers/t2.md`](../story-bible/tiers/t2.md)
· [`05-world.md`](../story-bible/05-world.md)); the bible wins every
disagreement. This is a **same-class extension** of the estate survey,
not a new scale class — so per map-styles §4.5 it needs **no ADR-075
diverge**; it EXTENDS the one master layout (T0 ⊂ T1 ⊂ T2 stays a
mathematical fact — see §6.5), it does not fork the look.

### §6.0 · The valley geography (master extension)

The one-geography law still holds: T2 does not rearrange the estate — it
**pulls the camera back** and adds the valley south of it. Everything on
the T1 sheet keeps its exact ground; the T2 frame simply shows more world
and draws the estate smaller.

- **VG1 · The frame grows SOUTH (and a shade wider).** The estate holds
  the NORTH (upstream) third of the sheet at the coordinates T1 already
  surveyed. Below it the valley floor runs south along the river to
  **Asagiri**, ~half a ri down (the T0/T1 "村へ 半里" note is now a place,
  not a promise). Wooded flanks wall the valley E and W; the hills close
  it to the north as before.
- **VG2 · The river is the spine.** It still flows **north → south**,
  entering from the NW hills, past the estate's weir (Matsuzō's stretch,
  upstream), on down to Asagiri where it drives the **mill** and is
  crossed at the **ferry**; a **gorge** pinches it between estate and
  village. The downstream (south) exit carries it on out of the valley.
- **VG3 · The demotion — the estate is now ONE compound pictogram.** The
  whole T0/T1 world collapses to a valley-scale glyph: the precinct wall
  ring, the RUIN's footprint (still the largest single built mass in the
  entire valley — bigger than anything in Asagiri), the guest house a
  small roofed pictogram in the ruin's SE corner, the new **gatehouse**
  in gold on the south face, the fields a washed pattern mass to the
  west, the terraces a texture on the NW slope. Its interior
  room-detail is RETIRED to the T0/T1 sheets (it does not draw at valley
  scale). The reward of the demotion (map-styles §2): the player has
  *walked* every ditch of that compound and knows how much world the one
  glyph hides.
- **VG4 · The anchors that must agree.** Relative geography is canon
  across the class and may not contradict T0/T1: estate NORTH/upstream ·
  Asagiri SOUTH/downstream · temple **country** still upstream NW (Toku's
  packet road, off toward the region) · the next valley EAST over the
  wooded hill · the road onward (to the region / castle town, T3+) leaves
  the valley past Asagiri south.
- **VG5 · What Asagiri IS (the counter-weight).** A real settlement so
  the estate's shrinkage reads: a street of houses along the river's east
  bank with its named public works — the **well**, the **market square**,
  the **shrine & temple** (Ekai's, holding the register of the vanished),
  the **headman's house** (Mohei's), the **mill** (Kyūbei's, on the
  river), the **ferry landing** (Funakichi's). At valley scale each is a
  small pictogram or a block of house-tone, never field-by-field.

### §6.2 · The reveal re-label (the honesty flip — `ruinRevealed`)

T2's signature beat is a **map event** (kernel #6; TST3 — the UI change
IS the story). Early in the tier, three signals converge (t2.md "the
reveal, staged"): the well rumor (R0), the old plans found clearing the
kura (R1), and Toku's one past-tense line. At the third signal's scene
end, **the sheet redraws its two labels**:

- **Before** (`ruinRevealed: false`) — the estate glyph is labelled the
  way T0/T1 name it: the lived compound is **"the main house" (母屋)**;
  the great footprint beside it is only **"ruined compound" (廃屋)**,
  never honestly named.
- **After** (`ruinRevealed: true`) — the two labels swap to the truth:
  the lived compound becomes **"guest house" (客殿)**; the ruin gains its
  real name, **"the Main house" (本邸)**. One log line in the day-book's
  voice, no ceremony. Retroactively legible: Heikichi's salvage marks,
  the kura crest, the garden gate that opens on the ruin.

This is `TIER_DELTA`'s `ruinRevealed` seam (already stubbed in
`ground.ts`) driving a label swap on the estate compound — NOT a
geometry change. Nothing moves; only the survey's honesty changes. The
crest answer (VG3's wrong thing) lands legibly WITH the reveal — the old
plans that name the ruin are the same paper that draws the one-more-petal
crest.

### §6.3 · Look deltas vs the estate-survey default

Everything in §3 (the aizuri-night AA bar) carries unchanged — same
substrate, same silver/gold/shu grammar, same brush-alive strokes, same
paper furniture, same LOD reward. The class is the same; only the
GRAMMAR of what a mark means at this zoom shifts (map-styles §3):

- **D1 · Whole compounds are single pictograms.** The estate that filled
  the T0/T1 frame is now one glyph; Asagiri's houses are house-tone
  blocks, not drawn room-by-room. The honesty rule of the wide mura-ezu:
  draw the compound's FOOTPRINT and roof truly, retire its interior.
- **D2 · Fields become pattern masses.** The paddy grid, terraces, and

  washed texture-masses at valley scale (their fine detail is `.ms-fine`,
  gated to close zoom, or simply the estate glyph's own texture).
- **D3 · The river gains its full course.** Pools/weir/reeds/shallows

- **D5 · The document fiction widens.** T0 is the family's own survey;

  re-label (§6.2) is this survey correcting its own oldest lie.

### §6.4 · The T2 blind-reader rubric (the pass bar)

A fresh agent given ONLY the T2 screenshot set must recover these. **M**
= must-say (fail the iteration if missed) · **S** = should-say. These are
the spec's teeth — a change that makes a V-line recoverable is progress;
one that doesn't is decoration.

- **V1 M** — a wider survey of a whole VALLEY (not just an estate): a
  settled valley with a village downstream and one large compound
  upstream, on one river, north up.
- **V2 M** — the river runs the length of the valley **north → south**;
  the compound sits UPSTREAM (north), the village DOWNSTREAM (south),
  with a crossing (ferry/bridge) at the village end.
- **V3 M** — the northern compound reads as ONE walled estate whose
  RUINED precinct is the LARGEST single built footprint in the entire
  valley — out-massing even the village's biggest buildings; the lived
  house sits small in its corner. (The T0/T1 twist, now readable AGAINST
  the village's scale.)
- **V4 M** — the village is a real settlement: a street/cluster of houses
  with recognizable public works among them — at minimum a well, a
  temple/shrine, a mill on the river, and a ferry or river crossing.
- **V5 M** — same world as T1, pulled back: no landmark that appeared on
  T1 has MOVED; the estate occupies the exact northern ground T1
  surveyed (a reader who saw T1 recognizes it demoted, not redrawn).
- **V6 M** — roads/river connect the valley to a wider world: the
  estate's south road reaches the village; the way continues out past the
  village (south/onward); the upstream path still leaves NW (temple
  country); a track still crosses EAST over the hill.
- **V7 S** — the survey names the ruin honestly in the revealed state:
  the label reads the RUIN as the main house and the lived compound as
  the guest house (the T2 re-label — recoverable only from a revealed
  capture).
- **V8 S** — a water-driven mill sits on the river at a choke; a ferry
  crossing exists where the river is too deep/gorged to ford.
- **V9 S** — the valley's edges carry the trouble: a bandit/mountain camp
  up a side draw OFF the settled floor; the night-roads legible as a
  danger layer.
- **V10 S** — an old quarry scars a valley flank; its dressed-stone cuts
  visibly match the ruin's robbed footings (the village took the main
  house's stones — the reader can connect the two).
- **V11 S** — the wrong things read, and stay OUTSIDE the legend: a crest
  on the estate's new gatehouse; a boundary stone standing a field into
  the village's land; something town-made in the camp.
- **V12 S** — quality/register: reads as ONE document with the estate
  sheets (same aizuri-night hand, whole compounds as pictograms, fields
  as pattern masses) — a wider mura-ezu, NOT a different game and NOT a
  labelled diagram.

Iteration loop as §5: capture → one fresh blind-describe agent → a judge
agent scores THESE V-lines → fix the misses → repeat until all **M** pass
and ≥ half of **S**. A full both-sheet pass (T0/T1 + T2) is required at
T2's acceptance (map-authoring §5 / the skill's non-negotiable 2).
