>
> **Roads.** A single diagonal road cuts across the lower-right portion of
> the map, unconnected to any visible waypoint until it reaches the
> ferry/night-roads junction near the village, then continues northeast in
> a long, lonely, unbroken line across open blank ground, off the top-right
> edge of the sheet, labeled only "over the hill · next valley" and (in
> fainter text running alongside) "to the castle town." That stretch has no
> buildings, no forest, nothing on either side of it for its whole visible
> length — an exposed, empty road to somewhere else, and it's the same
> stretch flagged near the village end as 夜 "night roads," a
> dotted-red-bordered zone laid right over the ferry crossing — travel
> through there specifically at night is called out as its own
> hazard/encounter zone, which reads ominous: the one road out of the
> valley toward "civilization" is also the one place explicitly marked
> dangerous after dark.
>
> **Old vs. new vs. abandoned.** Nothing on the sheet is explicitly labeled
> "ruined" or "abandoned," but several things read as functionally cold or
> marginal: the old quarry (石) sits walled off in the forest with no road
> connecting it to anything, just adjacent to the mountain-dogs' camp —
> reads as disused industrial ground, now squatted by something feral. The
> "moved boundary stone" reads as a small act of past tampering nobody has
> corrected. The two red "revised" tags on the estate buildings imply the
> original drawing is now out of date there — something changed inside the
> estate (a guest hall rebuilt, a main residence redesignated) after this
> map was first drawn, and the redraw is patching it rather than replacing
> it outright.
>
> **What strikes me as wrong, oversized, or deliberately mysterious.**
> - The scatter of small unlabeled marks filling the main house's yard —
>   never keyed, dense, oddly specific for a "garden."
> - The river's second, thinner fork peeling off the top of the frame with
>   no destination, name, or explanation.
> - The dark hatched triangular notch beside the river gorge — reads like
>   a cave or cut, but nothing names it.
> - The "moved boundary stone" — a boundary marker that has been *moved*
>   is a strange thing to note formally on a survey unless it's a plot
>   hook (a land dispute, a crime, a supernatural displacement).
> - The unlabeled 祠 "hill shrines" glyph sitting inside the east forest
>   with no node frame around it, unlike literally every other feature —
>   it's present but visually withheld, as if the map itself hasn't fully
>   revealed it yet.
> - The asymmetry of scale: the estate compound (three buildings) occupies
>   far more paper than the entire village (six+ named features crammed
>   into a fraction of the space) — an odd allocation for a "survey," more
>   expressive than technical.
> - The lone empty road running off toward "the castle town" with nothing
>   on it at all — for a document this careful about naming wells, mills,
>   and gatehouses, a multi-mile stretch of totally blank road stands out
>   as a deliberate void.
