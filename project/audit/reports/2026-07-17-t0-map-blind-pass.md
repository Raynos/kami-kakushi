> WHAT STRIKES AS WRONG / OVERSIZED / MYSTERIOUS
> - The "stalls for twenty" and "old ring's corner" imply a former
>   martial/estate grandeur wildly out of proportion to the shrunken
>   living compound — deliberately poignant, not an error.
> - ¾ of the fields explicitly abandoned while the household still
>   keeps a drill yard: the estate is contracting but still
>   play-acting its old status.
> - The RED dashed 夜 "Night rounds" node is the deliberate mystery —
>   a night-watch/haunting/combat event pinned to the SE gate corner,
>   the only red thing on an otherwise cold monochrome sheet; it
>   signals "something happens here after dark."
> - "The house keeps the old ring's corner" is cryptic — a former
>   enclosure ("ring") of which only one corner is still occupied.
> - The mountains, weir, and river are drawn far larger/heavier than
>   the tiny living compound, so the eye reads a big empty derelict
>   landscape with one small clinging household in it — which is
>   presumably the intended mood.
> - Minor: the upstream note "the temple lands" and the eastbound
>   "pass road" both point off-sheet to places this map only gestures
>   at (temple, village, pass), framing the estate as an isolated
>   pocket between temple, mountains, and village.
