### FB-123 · double border under scroll in the reader — ✅
**Verbatim:** _"There's a werid border box with a line here as you scroll, it
feels like theres multiple borders here, one broken and one working"_
**Reading:** the `.frame::after` inner key-line (absolute-positioned) scrolled

the inset box-shadow frame stayed — two borders, one broken.
**Doc-update plan:** none new — this is the existing "chrome pinned, panes
scroll internally" rule applied.
**Distilled rule:** never make a `.frame` element its own scroll container —
scroll an inner pane; the key-lines belong to the fixed chrome.
**Fixed in:** (this drain's commit) — title + pills pinned, content pane
scrolls.

### FB-124 · identical Tokubei lines read as a broken swap — ✅
**Verbatim:** _"The lines for TOkubei are the same here in all three variants.
that feels like a bug to me in narrative diverge."_
**Reading:** not a code bug (substitution replaces the whole scene) — the DEMO

across takes is ILLEGIBLE (can't tell a working swap from a broken one).
**Doc-update plan:** takes/README rule below.
**Distilled rule:** in a take-compare surface, a line byte-identical to canon
must READ as "shared with canon" (dimmed + labelled), so deliberate sharing
never looks like a failed substitution (TST4).
**Fixed in:** (this drain's commit) — demo takes now vary Tokubei per
register; the galley reader dims canon-identical lines.

### FB-126 · cold-open restore line reads as an ugly button — ✅
**Verbatim:** _"omg its such an ugly button lol, wtf. Let's have a really small
subtle link anchored at the bottom, almost like a footer."_
**Reading:** the HD-24 "Returning? Restore a saved game" affordance (shipped
s94) sat right under the wake verb at `fs-small` with an underline — it read as
a second button competing with "Open your eyes". The human wants it demoted to a
tiny, subtle footer anchored at the bottom of the field.
**Placement:** viewport-bottom footer (human's pick via AskUserQuestion, over a
card-bottom variant) — detached from the title card entirely.
**Doc-update plan:** none new — reinforces the existing TST3/§4.8 hierarchy rule
(a secondary affordance must not compete with the primary CTA).
**Distilled rule:** a secondary/opt-in affordance next to a primary CTA belongs
demoted and spatially separated (a footer), not stacked directly under it where
it apes the CTA's weight.
**Fixed in:** (this drain's commit) — `.coldopen-restore` moved out of the card
to a `position:absolute` bottom-anchored footer, `fs-micro`, dimmed
(`color-mix … 55%`), underline held for hover/focus so at rest it reads as a
faint footer link, not a button.
