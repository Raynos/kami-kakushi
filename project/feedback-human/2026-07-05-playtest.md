## Feedback log

### F118 · Intro typewriter — beat after a click-to-complete is too long — ✅

**Verbatim:** _"The delay before the GBA type writer starts after clicking to go
to end of line is too long."_

**Reading:** In the cold-open VN intro, clicking completes the currently-typing
line — but the NEXT line then waited the full **2 s** atmospheric auto-advance
hold (`INTRO_LINE_ADVANCE_MS`) before starting. When you're actively clicking
through, that beat should be snappy; the slow hold is for a hands-off reader.

**Reproduced:** cold open, R0/kura (the intro plays); `introAdvance()` on a
mid-type line called `introLineComplete()`, which scheduled the 2 s hold.

**Fix:** a click-to-complete now schedules a short **`INTRO_CLICK_ADVANCE_MS`
(450 ms)** beat instead of the 2 s hold; the hands-off auto-advance keeps its 2 s
atmospheric hold. (Tunable.) `render.ts` — `introLineComplete(advanceMs)` +
`scheduleIntroAutoAdvance(ms)`.

**Fixed in:** <this drain commit>.

### F119 · DEV panel too narrow — the Scenarios tab is clipped — ✅

**Verbatim:** _"If your going to show me scenarios then the dev menu has to be
wider because scenarios is cut off"_

**Reading:** The F6 **Scenarios** tab's fixture names are wider than the DEV
panel's `max-width: 16rem`, so they clip.

**Reproduced:** opened the DEV panel; its computed `max-width` was 16rem.

**Fix:** widened the panel `max-width` **16rem → 24rem** (`width: fit-content`
still shrinks it when collapsed). `dev.ts`. Verified live: panel now 24rem.

**Fixed in:** <this drain commit>.

### F120 · Rung-meter popover vanishes the instant you leave — ✅

**Verbatim:** _"This hover box vanishes the moment you hover off it, so it should
have a small delay."_

**Reading:** The rung-meter detail card (`.rung-head-card`, the "Day-labourer ·
日雇 / Estate service 476/1100 / Next: …" popover) is a pure CSS `:hover` reveal,
so it disappears the instant the cursor leaves — a twitchy flicker if you drift
off by a pixel.

**Reproduced:** hovered the top-right rung meter; the card showed on hover and
vanished immediately on mouse-out.

**Fix:** the card's **hide** now has a ~**0.4 s linger** (delayed opacity +
visibility transition); **show stays instant** (the `:hover` rule zeroes the
delay). `styles.css` — `.rung-head-card`.

**Fixed in:** <this drain commit>.

### F121 · Rung progress: raw points meter → requirements checklist — 💬 design proposal

**Verbatim:** _"Ok so for the progress on the rung the day-labourer / Its
rendered as a percentage bar based on the actual numeric info 476/1100 / I think
this is showing too much raw info, like it's too in your face, there's a random
ass number and it has to go up and when its high enough you rung up. / I think we
want to show progress out of 100% broken out into internal rung requirements. /
For example for R0 there could be 5 rung requirements: Rake 100 / 200 / 300 /
400 / 500 spilled rice / So as you rake spilled rice, the meter will jump to
20%, 40%, 60%, 80%, 100%. At 100% the rung up stuff progresses as normal. / I
want to move away from a really dumb 'x points to rung up' and 'all activities
give N points' and move towards / Rung up has a finite list of specific
requirements that can be completed in arbitrary order and as you do the bar moves
up."_

**Reading:** A **progression-model redesign**: replace the raw points meter
(476/1100 — "too in your face, a random ass number") with a bar broken into a
finite list of **named rung requirements**, completed in ANY order, each filling
a chunk of the bar (R0 = rake 100/200/300/400/500 rice → 20/40/60/80/100%). Move
away from the current "every activity yields N points; X points → rung up" model
toward a **checklist of concrete goals** per rung.

**Triage: DESIGN FORK (what the game IS) → surface, don't build (R4).** This
changes the core progression model — today a rung is a single points meter
(`rungThreshold` / `RUNG_POINTS_PER_ACT` in `balance.ts`, rendered as the
rung-head bar). Requirements-based progression needs a design pass (the
per-rung requirement lists, how they interact with the balance sim / pacing
bands, whether they replace or wrap the points model) + the human's scope call.
Captured durably; NOT auto-implemented.

**Doc-update plan:** proposal captured in
[`project/brainstorms/2026-07-05-requirements-based-rung-progression.md`](../brainstorms/2026-07-05-requirements-based-rung-progression.md);
→ a plan (grill-me / diverge) once the human confirms the direction.
