# World expansion — factions, zones & NPC clusters beyond the 3 starters — *Kamikakushi*

> **Status:** raw discovery capture (archival). Generated by the world-expansion workflow
> (4 parallel facet drafts → curate/synthesize → adversarial constraint verification, verdict
> **FLAGGED — does not pass as-is**). This is **not yet canon** — it is the distilled record of design
> work already done, kept for provenance and as a quarry to cut a sane v1 from.
> Informs **PRD §5 (world / content)** and the **upper-tier gating in §1.6** (the 7 levers across 5 tiers).
> Read the verification & scope notes (§8) before treating any node as buildable: the verifier recommends
> cutting ~18 nodes down to ~6–8.

This designs the world **beyond Kamikakushi's 3 starter factions** — the Estate (gated rank ladder, spine),
the Village of Asagiri (reputation web, side), and the Origin / Sawatari-juku (memory-gated support track,
side) — primarily the **upper influence tiers** (region **T3** / domain-castle-town **T4** / Edo **T5**)
plus **cross-cutting networks**, each itself incremental.

**Constraints in force throughout** (locked elsewhere; this doc must honour, not re-decide them):
grounded / **no-magic**; **fractal incrementality** (each node arrives minimal, then unlocks);
**spine-vs-side** (side content never blocks the spine); **House Influence (家威)** as the macro-resource;
**no reset**.

## 1. Summary / governing shape

The four facet drafts deliberately overlapped (the cross-cutting facet re-covered the porter guild,
broker/finance, racket, pilgrimage and artisan lineage that the T3/T4/T5 facets also covered). The
curation **dedupes** by treating **six** of these as **cross-cutting networks** (picked up minimal at a
low tier, re-met larger at every higher tier) and the rest as **tier-local clusters** (bloom at one tier).
This collapses **~37 raw facet entries into 18 curated nodes** = **6 cross-cutting networks + 12 tier-local
clusters**, "losing nothing of value while removing duplication."

### The three locked rules everything obeys

1. **Fractal incrementality.** Every node arrives as **ONE contact / ONE place / ONE verb**, then teaches
   its people and systems as story beats — the whole-game UI-reveal motion repeated in miniature.
2. **Three distinct shapes** (preserved from the starters so the world never reads as "one Influence bar
   painted 18 colours"):
   - **Route / producer networks** — Porter Guild, charcoal, Kuzuhara works.
   - **Per-node patron WEBS** — Neighbouring Valleys, Merchant-Finance, Artisan Lineage, Nihonbashi wholesale.
   - **Discrete MILESTONE tracks** — Pilgrimage, Scholars, Marriage/Adoption, sekisho pass-tiers, the Edo conduits.
   - **ONE evidence/petition META-track** — the Racket (friction, not a producer).
3. **Spine vs side** is rigorously audited so a spine-only player has a **thin-but-complete path to T5**
   and **all depth is genuinely optional**.

### The spine through-line (what a minimal player touches, never more)

| Tier | Minimal spine path |
| --- | --- |
| **T3** | a tradeable surplus + a thin logistics+pass backbone to move it + a broker to convert it + the **Kuzuhara embankment** as the headline distinguished-service beat |
| **T4** | a castle-town **townhouse beachhead** + officialdom that **RECORDS** achievements + debt **restructured** into creditworthiness + the **meibutsu** graded toward Osaka |
| **T5** | the lord's **sankin-kotai** outfitting + the domain **Edo yashiki** correspondence + the **meibutsu** reaching Edo markets + the **touring-inspector** set-piece + a **mediated merit commendation** flowing DOWN through the lord |

Everything else — courting valleys, the pilgrimage circuit, the matagi, the bandit investigation, the
artisan masters, the scholars, the marriage brokerage, the entertainment district's deeper layers, the full
racket prosecution — is an **optional accelerant / colour**, tuned inside the locked **~halves-time-to-tier**
budget.

### Dedupe / merge decisions (load-bearing)

- **(a)** Porter Guild, Merchant-Finance, Racket, Pilgrimage, Artisan Lineage, Scholars/Medicine are **ONE
  cross-cutting entry each**, NOT separate T3/T4/T5 copies — the T3 "broker network", T4 Marutaya +
  Kuramae-quarter + wholesale-row, and T5 Osaka-Edo-finance-chain + Nihonbashi + banzuke all fold into the
  appropriate network's higher rungs.
- **(b)** The **Sekisho / Tegata pass system** and the **Bandit / Ronin menace** MERGE into the Porter
  Guild as its friction layer — they only exist to make logistics felt, and the bandit menace **is** the
  racket's enforcer arm.
- **(c)** Two duplicate **"Onui"** NPCs are split-named: the Hibara weaver stays **Onui**; the merchant heir
  is renamed **Osato**.
- **(d)** The "regional shrine circuit" and "pilgrimage order" are **one Pilgrimage network**.
- **(e)** The **Townhouse** and **Entertainment-District** (T4) merge into one hub;
  **Samurai-Society** and **Marriage/Adoption-Brokerage** merge into one T4 faction;
  **Nihonbashi-wholesale** and the **banzuke-fame** milieu merge into one T5 node (commercial + reputational
  faces of the one meibutsu outcome).
- **Grounded / no-magic** is held everywhere: every belief/omen resolves to a human/natural cause; the
  **≤1 residual-ambiguity cap** stays at the **Asagiri boundary-stone**; recovery aids / porter's-knot /
  dream confer **ZERO mechanical edge** (access/efficiency only).

### 4.1 The Rice-Quota & Smuggling Racket — the one load-bearing antagonist

## 5. How it all layers incrementally / the reveal cadence

The cadence is governed by ONE rule protecting fractal incrementality: **never surface more than one or two
NEW first-contacts per Influence sub-step**, gate each behind a distinct sub-threshold + story flag, and open
the **SPINE node of a tier FIRST** so the rest drip as optional accelerants. The 18 nodes resolve into roughly
**14 first-contact gates** across the climb (the 6 cross-cutting networks each have ONE first-contact, then
re-reveal as deepening RUNGS rather than new clusters — the key anti-overload move).

**SEEDS (T1–T2, already in the starter region — foreshadowing, not new factions).** Six thread-shapes learned
in miniature inside the cozy starter loop, each one node, before any expansion — none reads as a "faction" yet:

| Seed | Network | Tier |
| --- | --- | --- |
| the porter's-knot beat (zero bonus) | Porter Guild | T1 |
| Ranpo + Obaa Sato | Scholars | T1/T2 |
| Gonta/Risuke/Onatsu/Tokuemon | Artisan | T2 |
| the Asagiri rice-broker | Finance | T2 |
| Priest Ryoa's shrine + register | Pilgrimage | T2 |
| Magobei's skim → Yagoemon's ledgers | Racket | T2 |

**T3 opening order (region band, hard-staggered).** (1) The trade BACKBONE first — Porter Guild proper
(Kanta's favour run) + the first sekisho pass + the first lowball broker sale (Heikichi) — surplus + a way to
move it + a way to convert it is the minimum spine (three close together, each one contact/one verb). (2) Then
the headline SPINE beat — **Kuzuhara** (survey the broken seki) — once memory + conditioning + standing align.
(3) THEN optional accelerants drip one at a time behind rising district-standing thresholds: Wider Post-Town
(toiya registration), Neighbouring Valleys (Hibara → Toge-mura), charcoal-burners (Tasuke), matagi (Shozo),
regional Pilgrimage circuit, Scholars' han-school. (4) Cross-cutting PRESSURE threads (Racket escalation;
Tomita's first underbid) are not "opened" on a gate — they INTRUDE as story beats triggered by expansion
(the first raid loss, the first contested deal), so they feel like consequences of growth.

**T4 opening order (gated on distinguished service rendered).** (1) The Townhouse beachhead first (the hub —
one bare room; nothing else in T4 is reachable until the introductions hub exists, mirroring the T1 kura
cold-open). (2) Then officialdom (the Daikan's summons — the recognition-recording engine). (3) Then, dripping
as the player has business: Marutaya finance (debt restructuring), the entertainment district's introductions,
the artisan kabu-charter + wholesale grading, samurai society + the academy. (4) PRESSURE: Kuroiwa-at-the-
office + the racket-evidence track sharpen; Tomita contests broker terms and society standing. (5) DEEP/
OPTIONAL/LATE: the marriage-adoption brokerage matures only once samurai-society standing is non-trivial.

**T5 opening order (gated on domain sponsorship + presentable standing, carried via the lord).** (1) The
sankin-kotai outfitting (seeded late T4) + the first Edo yashiki dispatch (Konoe's letter) — the two conduits
— arrive first. (2) Then the meibutsu reaches Nihonbashi (one trial order) + the Osaka→Edo finance chain
returns its first rice-bill. (3) Then the banzuke milieu (the chart that omits you) + bakufu-permits-via-rusui.
(4) The touring-inspector survey is the CAPSTONE set-piece, announced as a distant date, climaxing the tier
and producing the merit record that flows DOWN through the lord. Throughout T5 multiple slow async loops
(finance/wholesale/fame/attendance/correspondence) run in PARALLEL so something always resolves and no loop
goes fully cold.

**The felt experience:** the player is never handed a new continent of content. They meet a thread as one
face, grow comfortable, watch THAT thread enlarge as they climb, and only occasionally open a genuinely new
tier-local frontier — and even then it is one valley, one office, one teahouse, one letter at a time.

## 6. Integration with the 3 starters (build outward, don't redesign)

The expansion is built as concentric growth **OUTWARD** from the three starters — none is redesigned; each is
the literal seed and shape-template for what blooms above it.

**From the Estate** (gated rank ladder, main spine). The rank ladder is untouched (Stray → chief steward at
the local tier); above Rank 7 the climb stops adding personal ranks and tracks House Influence outward (per
§1.14). Existing gatekeepers graduate without new roles invented:
- **Genemon** hands off the sankin-kotai outfitting (T5).
- **Tanomo** runs the debt-restructuring interface (Finance).
- **Lady Chiyo** becomes the alliance-strategist (Marriage brokerage).
- **Dowager Toku**'s flood-venture memory motivates Kuzuhara + the Scholars' engineering.
- **Jubei**'s creed is restated at domain scale by academy-head **Rai Genpachi**.
- **Tokujiro** is seconded to staff the townhouse (re-homing the "teach others from zero" layer).
- **Naoyuki** turns from internal rival into the ally against external Tomita and the Edo-attendance reporter.

The protagonist's achievements always convert to the HOUSE's / the LORD's standing, never personal elevation —
the mediated-recognition ceiling is honoured by routing T4 recognition through the daikan and T5 through Konoe
+ Munenori.

**From Asagiri** (reputation web, side). The village's per-node web is the TEMPLATE replicated, slimmer, at
district and castle-town scale — the **Neighbouring Valleys are literally "Asagiri, fractally, ×N"** (each a
headman + shrine + craft + grievance); samurai society and the Nihonbashi houses reuse the same per-node
patron-meter shape. Cast extends without redesign: **Sokichi** → road-scale info broker + pilgrim-bridge (and
his Edo echo Sokichi-no-Edo-en); **Ryoa**'s single shrine + register → regional Pilgrimage circuit +
cross-valley evidence net; the village artisans → base of the Artisan Lineage that climbs to the meibutsu; the
rice-broker → base of the Finance network; **Magobei/Yagoemon** → the entry rungs of the Racket; the inn's
rumours-board motif survives as a single light T4 "castle-town ghost story" wink *(see §8 — flagged for
deletion)*.

**From Sawatari-juku / Origin** (memory-gated support track, side). The origin is the engine room of the
cross-cutting TRADE networks. **Denbei/Kanta/Zenroku** ARE the Porter Guild's first contacts + compromised
apex; **Okimi**'s trading family IS the Finance/Osaka-facing trade-tie multiplier; the porter-guild mates
become recruited idle carriers. The **Wider Post-Town** region is literally the OUTWARD commercial face of the
existing reunion hub — registration needs standing, not the reunion, so the commercial road stays available to
a spine-only player while the emotional content stays optional. **Kuzuhara** is the locked faction-3 fusion
node (Origin payoff + spine river-works), access-only and grind-built, with Oharu/the real Tama and the
cover-up resolving there.

The **§1.5.4 multiplier** intent is preserved exactly: village allies become idle producers seconded to
estate/region works; origin trade-ties become the supply chains that make region/Edo expansion affordable;
weaving both in roughly **halves time-to-tier** (felt, never a wall). The expansion never replaces the
starters' currencies (Estate Service XP / Village Reputation / Origin Ties); it adds region/domain/Edo BANDS
of the same House Influence macro-resource over the same three feeders.

## 7. v1 vs later scope

**V1 vertical slice** (per §1.14: Tiers 1–2 complete, T3 stubbed as the cliff-hanger). **NONE of the 18
expanded nodes ship as full factions in v1.** What v1 ships is the **six cross-cutting network SEEDS** already

first-contact** (Kanta's favour run + the first sekisho turn-back) because it (a) directly pays off the
protagonist's own porter past, (b) is the trade backbone everything else hangs from, and (c) demonstrates the

world" opens onto exactly this one teaser, then ends.

| Roadmap milestone | What it adds |
| --- | --- |
| **Early (completes T3)** | the T3 SPINE pair (Kuzuhara fusion + the regional broker runway) + the trade backbone (Porter Guild proper + pass-tiers + Wider Post-Town). throughput board) and **Edo** (mostly correspondence + a fame bar). This keeps each milestone affordable and
lets depth be added without bloating the first playable slice.

## 8. Verification & scope notes

> Adversarial constraint check. **Verdict: FLAGGED — does not pass as-is.** The expansion is thoughtful, mostly
> grounded, and preserves the locked tier/lever/ceiling architecture with real craft (the SHAPE taxonomy and
> spine-through-line are genuinely strong), but it trips several hard constraints. The v1 carve-out is sound;
> the problem is the world it commits the project to building afterward.

### Scope assessment (verbatim intent)

> **Bloated.** 18 multi-system nodes + ~50 NPCs + a 7-NPC antagonist web + a rival house + a 3-NPC capstone
> set-piece is a multi-year content mountain dressed as "the broader world." The dedupe from ~37 to 18 is
> genuine and the SHAPE taxonomy + spine-through-line are strong, but the deduped result is still far too much
> and several "one node" entries are entire economic/social simulators. The v1 carve-out is sound; the problem
> is the world it commits the project to building afterward. **For a sane v1 + early roadmap, cut to ~6–8
> nodes** that reuse the existing cast and pay off the spine directly. This matches the locked v1 scope (T3
> stubbed to a single Porter Guild first-contact) and the user's "start lean, add back deliberately" preference.

### Violations (hard constraints tripped)

1. **Fractal incrementality (the most-stressed rule) — violated by three cross-cutting networks.** They smuggle
   tier-sized structures into one "node": **Rice & Silver** enumerates SEVEN financial verbs (sell →
   forward-contract → store → rice-bills → credit → restructure → goyokin → bill-discount) plus market
   saturation, diversified-goods hedging, a creditworthiness meter, kakeya auctions, Edo fudasashi — an entire
   economic simulator behind one first-encounter. **Porter Guild** stacks routes + a 4-rung pass-tier ladder +
   roster management + route-risk + seasonal closures + sukego politics + horse-strings + a caravan-line
   holdings node. **Townhouse+Entertainment** fuses a multi-room upgradeable base + introductions + entertaining
   + an info-broker feed + patron-of-arts. Sharing one first contact does not make these "one node."
2. **Grounded / no-magic — folklore cap exceeded.** New believed-omens at nearly every node (one-eyed mountain
   god of the pass, fox-fire ridge kiln, matagi yamanba/tengu, bandit "mountain god", a Pilgrimage "miracle
   cure", per-valley rumours across unbounded N, and a **NEW "T4 castle-town ghost-story wink" the PRD never
   authorised**). Sanctioning a brand-new T4 ambiguity beat is by construction a **second residual token**;
   canon fixes the single token at the boundary-stone. The volume of debunk-beats also courts the Scooby-Doo
   failure canon warns against.
3. **Do-not-redesign / build outward from canon NPCs — contradicted.** NPCs are labelled "Existing" that are
   NOT in PRD §1.8. **Genza** is tagged "Existing region NPC" with a load-bearing matagi-bridge role, but Genza
   appears only in the **explicitly SUPERSEDED** grounded-story-spine doc (0 hits in `docs/living/prd.md`) — reviving
   superseded canon as current is a direct contradiction. The expansion also silently RENAMES a canon-adjacent
   NPC (**"Onui" → "Osato"**) and hands many starter NPCs sweeping cross-tier portfolios with no arc-map.
4. **Lock dependencies before authoring — the meibutsu is still an OPEN question.** Five nodes (Artisan Lineage
   endgame, Finance payload, Porter payload, Nihonbashi tonya category, banzuke chart + Edo tastemaker) are
   authored as if the canonical meibutsu is settled, while canon explicitly leaves WHICH product is the meibutsu
   unresolved. The most load-bearing T5 content (the visible win condition, the chart-rank) is therefore not yet
   specifiable — designing on sand.
5. **Scope / v1 slice + number-range guard.** 18 multi-system nodes (~37 deduped) with ~50 NPCs, a 7-NPC
   antagonist web, a rival house, a 3-NPC capstone, six parallel async loops at T5 — a multi-year content
   mountain that directly triggers canon's own scope-explosion guardrail. (The v1 carve-out itself is correct.)

### Weaknesses (softer, named)

- **Spine-vs-side muddied** by a new in-between label ("SPINE-ADJACENT"/"SPINE-ENABLING"/"SPINE-CRITICAL") on
  the Porter Guild, Townhouse hub, Daikan's office, Finance network. The canon binary is spine
  thin-but-complete + ALL side optional/non-blocking; declaring the Porter Guild and Townhouse de-facto
  mandatory quietly converts side infrastructure into soft gates (defensible only if each "thin form" is
  provably trivial — asserted, not demonstrated).
- **Racket optional-status erosion.** Called "STRUCTURALLY woven into the spine" / "the one rotten root" whose
  removal "feeds the spine," but canon makes the lost-child/racket mystery an OPTIONAL side-thread; the
  friction-removal framing risks making racket progress feel necessary for efficient play.
- **The "halves time-to-tier" multiplier is a LOCKED single budget for the two side factions combined**, yet
  the expansion has SIX cross-cutting networks plus many tier-local clusters all claiming to be "optional
  accelerants." Stacking 6+ accelerants against a ~2× budget is a real balance contradiction (deferred to an
  open question rather than solved).
- **Reveal-fatigue at T3 named but not solved** — after the spine pair, T3 still introduces six optional
  accelerants plus two intruding pressure threads.
- **Neighbouring Valleys is unbounded** (no cap on N) — a recipe for samey content sprawl.
- **Combat surface quietly grows** (Hanzaki fight, famine-band mercy-vs-force, matagi "terrain that kills",
  high-route bandit security) — aggregate weight should be checked against the "peaceful labour is the daily
  texture" pillar.
- **Macro-tier spatiality** (abstract boards vs walkable maps) is presented closer to decided than it is — still
  an open question in canon.
