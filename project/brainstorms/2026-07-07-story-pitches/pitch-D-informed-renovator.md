# The Ledger and the Legend

*A renovator's pitch for the story reboot of kami-kakushi.*

## 1. The premise, one page

In 1780, in a valley that has a word for every loss, a half-drowned man with no name is pulled from a weir. The valley's word is *kami-kakushi* — spirited away — and it is a lie, and the game is sixty hours of replacing that lie with names, one book at a time, without ever snatching the lie from the people who need it to sleep.

This game's story is the contest between two kinds of record. The **legend** is what a community tells: the kappa in the ford, the tanuki in the rice, the kami who take children. The **ledger** is what someone wrote down: wage-tallies, temple registers, deed-books, a *banzuke*. Every mystery in the game is the gap between the two, and every reveal is earned the same way — by climbing high enough that the next book opens to you. Worth is written by work (kernel #3); so, in this game, is truth. The rung ladder isn't a metaphor next to the mystery; it is the mystery's key-ring.

The title is earned twice over. First: every vanishing the player touches resolves to a human cause — debt, flight, shame, neglected flood-works — recovered *by name*. Second, and this is the renovation's frame: **the protagonist is himself a kami-kakushi story, running in reverse.** He is the one the river gave back. The estate doesn't care (to a house at foreclosure's edge, he is a mouth, then a hand); the village, when it meets him, will make him proof that the legend is kind — if the water returned him, it can return Tama. For sixty hours the player raises a house from a leaning gate to a line of bakufu ink, and in doing so gives three vanished parties their names back: Otsuru, who the valley calls Tama; the drowned of Kuzuhara, who the house calls a debt; and — only if the player chooses to dig — himself. The last of these is missable by design. He arrives with no name and the game is the earning of one; whether he also *recovers* one is his to decide.

**What I keep and what I fix.** I keep the entire load-bearing architecture, knowingly: the title-is-a-lie premise and the belief→cause discipline; the MC-is-not-Tama reveal and Otsuru's unforgiving freedom; compound interest as the T0/T1 antagonist; partial justice honored to the terminus (Sōbei glimpsed, never reached); the researched Edo texture doing plot work; the cashed promises (the straw mat, the chipped bowl); Shigemasa's R7 scene nearly verbatim — the two best lines in the repo stay. I keep the proper nouns — Kurosawa, Asagiri, the cast — because the human already ruled on Kurosawa (D17) and because continuity is cheap where the rot is elsewhere. What I fix is the rot the audit proved: the three canon breaks (one wolf outcome — survived, not won; the shame→begging arc restored; Kihei's creed on-screen and his flattery deleted); and, structurally, the **fairness machine** — T0's world of accurate assessors and dawn performance-reviews is rebuilt so that trust is extended out of *need*, promotions are sometimes small defeats for the promoter, suspicion is staged rather than narrated, and the decide menus stop being a moral thermometer and become trades with orthogonal consequences. The world stops being the protagonist's mirror and becomes weather.

## 2. The mystery spine

Three vanishings, one lie, one contract.

**The valley's wound — Tama (T2 ignition, T3 spine payoff).** A decade ago a child vanished; the valley chose the kami over the truth. What actually happened: Tama was a girl — Otsuru — fleeing a violent stepfather and a near-sale into service to clear a family rice debt; Sokichi the pedlar helped her run; she has been alive in Yanagi-watari the whole decade, too ashamed to return. **How the player earns it:** the legend's gender-drift is the planted fair clue (grandmothers say "the child," Sayo says "he," Ryōa's register holds a girl's name); the register's back pages open only to a man who has *worked* the shrine — logging a vanished traveller for Ryōa is the errand that earns them; Sokichi talks only on the far side of a *sekisho*, away from valley ears, so his testimony costs the standing that opens the roads; the near-sale's paper trail surfaces inside Yagōemon's old ledgers when the Magobei skim is cracked (one investigation exposes the other — the village's small lie and its great one share a bookkeeper). At G6 the threads triangulate down-valley: guaranteed, on the spine, for every player. The payoff honors the kernel: full truth, and only the justice Otsuru herself chooses. She may not forgive. She is freed to decide, which is more than the legend ever gave her.

**The house's wound — the Kuzuhara drowned (T0 seed, T1 root, T3 atonement).** The debt that is strangling the house is not only money. Renovation: in T0, the kura repairs literally excavate the storehouse — under the third roof-beam, a child's sandal and half a page of wage-tallies, the last names struck through in old ink; in the oldest ledgers (readable only at R6), payments upstream to a temple, every Bon, in Dowager Toku's hand. Nobody will answer a season-hand's questions. At T1, Toku parts with the root: Sadamune's flood-venture failed and the failure had people under it. At T3 the player reaches the broken embankment, rebuilds it, resettles the hamlet, and — the payoff — **names the drowned** into a temple register, read aloud. No justice is available: Sadamune is dead. The house answers with masonry and ink, which is what atonement looks like when the culprit is your own grandfather.

**The player's wound — the nameless man (T0 cadence, T3 optional payoff).** The dream escalates per recurrence, never repeats: the porter's knot; then a bell on a strap; then mud the wrong color for this valley; then a voice with the name in it, gone by waking. What actually happened: Tahei, a porter, his caravan dead in a mountain pass, his body swept down the river system into Asagiri. The Origin track (O0→O5) stays optional and missable; the name-reclaim is its capstone, never a spine beat. The renovation sharpens the choice's meaning: by T3 he *has* a name — the one the house's books wrote, entry by entry. Digging up the old one is not completion; it is a second life claiming its due, and the game lets a player decide the earned name is enough.

**The full-truth contract (kernel #5) pays out on the rigged box.** The doctored *kyō-masu* motif climbs the whole arc: Magobei's skim (T2 — he answers; Yagōemon mostly walks), Kuroiwa's weighing-yard (T4 — beaten by evidence; Iemasa walks; the *osso* petition's lethal risk falls on a *gimin* martyr, not the MC), Echizen-ya Sōbei (T5 — the apex glimpsed once, named, untouchable). Justice arrives smaller than the wrong at every rung. But the truth arrives whole: the renovation gives the contract a physical payout — the house's annals, the journal the player has been reading all game, close the arc with **the full account written and lodged**: every name, every rung of the machine, including the ones no magistrate will read. The player holds in ink exactly what power will not act on. That page — not a throne room — is the mystery's ending.

One ambiguity, ever: the offering left at the weir-jizō, where he was found, by a hand never identified. Kept exactly as is.

## 3. The core cast

Nine, each with a want that isn't the protagonist, a misread of him (kernel #7), and a sentence shape (prose law #2). Epigram license: **Shigemasa and Sōan only**; Kihei carries exactly one *borrowed* maxim — the creed, quoted from the man who trained him. The MC talks plainest of all: one clause, rarely two.

- **Genemon**, chief steward. *Wants:* to hand over a house in order before his back gives out. *To the House:* its working spine. *Misreads the MC as* an absconder — a man with no name is running from something, and Genemon counts the spoons for a season. *Shape:* ledger clauses — item, count, condition; never metaphor. "Rice, a corner, my eye on you. Those are the terms."
- **Kihei**, drillmaster. *Wants:* to stop burying men who couldn't tell a mamushi from a rat-snake; beneath it, a soldier of a dead house who wants this one not to die. *To the House:* its walls. *Misreads the MC as* a soldier hiding — the renovation flips the audit's broken flattery into suspicion: a farmer who doesn't run has stood a line before, and men who've stood lines have reasons to be nameless. He trains him partly to keep him where he can see him. *Shape:* imperative first, gear-nouns, verdict last; no ornament of his own.
- **Sōan**, physician. *Wants:* the valley to stop dressing drownings as spirits — he has lost patients to remedies-by-rite. *To the House:* its rational conscience, half-welcome. *Misreads the MC's* fragments as a wound best left closed; he is wrong that the buried name is worthless. *Shape:* a question, then a prescription — always the two beats, in that order.
- **Lady Chiyo**. *Wants:* the ledgers balanced before Shigemasa dies, and a Naoyuki who can survive inheriting them. *To the House:* its arithmetic of dignity. *Misreads the MC as* loyal-to-nothing and therefore easy to read — wrong: the dream owns him some nights, and she never sees it. *Shape:* conditionals; every sentence carries an if or a price.
- **Naoyuki**, heir. *Wants:* to matter before the house is saved — if a stranger saves it, what was the heir for? This is the engine of his hostility, and it never fully cools: ally, never warm. *Misreads the MC as* a grifter working an old man's sentiment; later, as the man who stole his redemption. *Shape:* abandoned sentences — begins formal, breaks off, restates flat and cold.
- **Lord Shigemasa**. *Wants:* to know, before he dies, whether "house before the man" was wisdom or only habit. *To the House:* its weight. *Misreads the MC as* the answer arrived — the kindest misread in the game, and burden-shaped. *Shape:* the licensed maxim; long sentences that turn on themselves and end unsure.
- **Dowager Toku**. *Wants:* the water paid off — the Bon packet upstream every year, the dead to be *somewhere*. The house's one believer, treated with kindness per kernel #1: her belief is grief-work, never mocked. *Misreads the MC as* sent — the river's repayment on the house's oldest debt. *Shape:* past-anchored; every utterance dated. "The year the weir went, we sent it at the new moon."
- **Rokusuke**, fellow kept-hand. *Wants:* an easier life than his father had, and a friend who doesn't audit him. *Misreads the MC as* a fellow bottom-rung shirker; his warmth curdles slowly as the MC rises past him — the cost of the climb the fairness machine never charged. *Shape:* comma-spliced run-ons, gossip's momentum.
- **Sayo**, the headman's daughter (T2). *Wants:* Tama back — wants the legend true, and kind. *Misreads the MC as* Tama: the grand misread the whole valley joins. *Shape:* questions that presume their answer. "You came back, so she can too, can't she?"

*(Otsuru, Tokubei, Tōzō, Tanomo and the wider PRD cast persist as written; the nine above are where the drama concentrates.)*

## 4. The arc in circles

Six circles, each named by what the House is to it; each ascension crosses a social line; each tier owns one movement of the mystery.

**T0 — the household.** Standing: foreclosure's edge; a leaning gate and a red ledger. The line crossed: a mouth becomes *a name in the house's books* — the first act of the game's central magic, performed on the player. Mystery owned: the seed — the struck names in the rafters, the Bon packet, a dream that will not say its word. (Rung detail below.)

**T1 — the land.** Standing: solvent by inches; the estate mends where the player has bled. The line crossed: the house stops being prey — a palisade, paid men, a ledger no longer only red. Mystery owned: the root — Toku parts with Sadamune's failure, and the wage-book that stops mid-season twenty years back tells the player the debt has people in it. The debt is restructured, not erased; the antagonist remains compound interest, personified by a tired pawnbroker who is not cruel about it.

**T2 — the valley.** Standing: from customer to anchor; the house's writ crosses its own gate for the first time. The line crossed: a *gōshi* house's man holds office in village affairs — commoners' business, samurai paper. Mystery owned: ignition — Sayo names him Tama on sight; the belief→cause machine runs in the open (the kappa, the tanuki, the fox-fire seed); the register of the vanished becomes a surface the player works; Magobei's rigged *masu* gives the game its first human culprit and its first partial verdict — the foreman answers, the headman mostly walks.

**T3 — the region.** Standing: from upstart to leading house; two older names — Tomita's money, Akagi's honour — cede, dethroned by supply and precedence-restoration, never by blood. The line crossed: houses that would not seat the Kurosawa now sign beside them. Mystery owned: **the payoff tier** — Otsuru found (spine, guaranteed), the Kuzuhara drowned named (the house's atonement), and the Origin track open for those who walk it, Tahei's name waiting at its end. The deck is cleared so the last circles carry the house alone.

**T4 — power.** Standing: recorded — yields, contracts, conduct entering officialdom's books at domain scale. The line crossed: from petitioner to office-holder; the takeover is social, never rebellion. Mystery owned: the chain runs upward — the gracious man at the gate *is* the rot; Kuroiwa falls to cross-referenced ledgers, Mosuke's flinch, evidence over violence; Iemasa walks; the martyr's petition prices justice honestly. The game as shipped closes on this tier's first two beats — the sealed summons, the bare townhouse mirroring the kura — a cold open at a new frontier.

**T5 — the realm.** Standing: a name in bakufu ink, mediated down through Mukai and the lord; the MC caps at *yōnin* and never sees the shogun. The line crossed: the house stops petitioning for regard and becomes how the domain is seen. Mystery owned: the terminus — Sōbei glimpsed once; the full account written into the house's annals; the register closed with every recoverable name in it. One ending: the house restored and ranked, the rot's apex still out of reach, the truth entire.

**T0 at rung level (R0→R7).** The ladder's titles stay; the scenes are rebuilt. The standing renovation rules: no two promotions staged alike; at least one lands in the MC's absence; one is contested on the record; trust is extended out of need as often as merit; and each rung's deeper access — rooms, keys, books — is also deeper access to the house's paper memory.

- **R0 · Day-labourer.** The cold open, kept nearly as built — it is the tightest prose in the game. One fix: Sōan no longer glosses the game's title to a fellow Japanese speaker; he says the villagers' word the way a man spits out a superstition, untranslated. The spilled rice; the pallet; the first dream-fragment, no stat attached.
- **R1 · Kept hand.** Renovated: Genemon keeps him not as praise but as arithmetic — two hands quit for the lowlands that week, and the house cannot be choosy. Naoyuki on-screen from here (D4): one look from the veranda, one abandoned sentence. Tokubei lays his mat at the tended gate. The keeping is a transaction and both men know it.
- **R2 · Trusted hand.** The woodlot, Rokusuke, the porter's knot. The wolf warning lands with its false note fixed: Kihei cannot stand two watches, and the gate is the one that cannot be left — so the grain-store goes to the man the house can spare. Rokusuke declines it first, on-screen, which tells the player exactly what the assignment is worth.
- **R3 · Gate-watch.** The canon repair, whole: the fight is survived, not won — the wolf bolts bleeding, the MC is thrashed, ribs cracked, alive by stubbornness. He limps to the omoya and *confesses*, and the shame drives him to beg Kihei for drills. Kihei's answer is the creed, on-screen at last, delivered as a quotation from his own dead master: *"Talent is a story the lucky tell. You are not lucky. So you will work."* And under it, the misread: farmers run; this one didn't; men who've stood lines have reasons to be nameless. The drill yard, the spear, the field-guide — all of it arrives as Kihei's need (he is done burying men who can't tell a mamushi from a rat-snake), not a grant list.
- **R4 · Kura-warden.** The suspicion beat — the tier's second wound and the fairness machine's execution. Toku's Bon packet goes missing; the nameless man is the first name said, and Naoyuki says it; Genemon defends him with nothing but the day-book, and the count clears him; the packet surfaces where Toku herself put it, and nobody explains what it was for, because to explain is to open the older ledger. No apology. Then the key, as arithmetic: the book cleared you; the book will keep clearing you, or it won't. Tōzō and the whetstone stay — the audit's one praised orthogonal consequence becomes the model for every decide menu.
- **R5 · House-servant.** The stance choice with Kihei, kept, but earned by a season of deep-woods work rather than staged as an exam. The world breathes without him (D11): he hears late of a wedding, a departure. And the rafters give up the sandal and the half-page of struck names — he asks, and learns that a house-servant's rank buys the finding but not the answer.
- **R6 · Steward's man.** Chiyo, her conditionals intact. The grant-list ventriloquism is rebuilt as need: the joiner will not take direction from a season-hand, so she gives him standing to stop the work stalling. Ledger access at last — the Bon payments in Toku's hand, decades deep; Chiyo closes the book: the older pages are not his errand. Naoyuki, unretracted, begins using the MC's tallies anyway, because they are right — grudging, never warm.
- **R7 · Trusted of the house.** The shoin scene, kept nearly verbatim — Shigemasa's self-doubt is the best writing in the repo and the renovation's proof that the foundation holds. One rebuild: the House-Influence reveal stops being "the measure takes shape before you" and becomes the old man's actual bookkeeping — four pillars, a lifetime's tallies, a hand too tired to carry them alone. Toku's beat lands here: he has earned the room where the flood story is told. Then Phase 2, the deeds, and the first ascension — big, once, as designed.

## 5. Fiction↔mechanics causation

The premise's frame — truth is rank-gated paper — makes the incremental structure and the mystery the *same system*. What the ladder mechanically gates (rooms, panels, verbs) the fiction re-describes as access to the house's memory: the key at R4, the ledgers at R6, the shoin at R7, the *sekisho* passes at T3, the *toiya* manifests, the banzuke. Nothing new is built for this; it is a reading of what exists, enforced in the scenes.

**My pitch REQUIRES (all within the constraints sheet, none touching core systems):**

1. **The T0 re-scening** — R1–R7 rebuilt as above: canon repairs (wolf, creed, shame arc), the R4 suspicion beat, need-motivated unlocks, no two promotions staged alike. Pure narrative-source work; the rung/meter/milestone machinery is untouched — indeed the suspicion beat *dramatizes* the AND-gate (the count clears you: the meter is the fiction).
2. **Decide menus rebuilt as trades, not thermometers.** Same data shape (intra-line choices, flags, memories); the renovation is that consequences go orthogonal — the r4-generous pattern (an unexpected quarter answers) becomes the norm, and no option is the "warm" one. The moral thermometer dies in authoring, not in code.
3. **The dream as escalating motif** — each recurrence adds one sensory element, never repeats verbatim; routed through the narrative grammar's reuse mechanism as deliberate echo.
4. **Voice-bible enforcement** — the §3 sentence shapes and the epigram license become the cast sheet the prose law demands; perk and reward copy names the object of its scene (the whetstone, the knot, the post).

**My pitch SUGGESTS (each earns its place separately):**

- **The register of the vanished** as a player-facing surface at T2+ — the mystery's bestiary, appearing when Ryōa's beat produces the need (kernel #6's application rule). T0 needs only log lines.
- **Without-you log lines**, 1–2 a season (already adopted, D11) — the weather of kernel #7.
- **The annals payout** at T5 — the full account as a readable in-fiction document, the contract's receipt. Cheap (it is generated from what the player did), late, and the ending's spine.
- **Struck-names excavation as kura-repair flavor** — the T0 seed rides the existing repair activity; no new node.

Progression feel is otherwise kept as shipped: two-phase tiers, deeds in Phase 2, the manual opt-in ascension, no prestige, nothing rewound. The renovation's conviction is that the machine was never the problem; the machine was always the fiction, and T0 just forgot to say so.

## 6. One written sample scene + 5 log lines

*R4 — The Count. Night, the omoya's outer room. The household assembled; the Bon packet missing from the shrine cupboard.*

They fetched him from the gate with his supper half-eaten, and nobody would say why on the walk over.

The household stood along the walls of the outer room. Genemon knelt at the low table with the day-book open. Naoyuki stood where the lamplight ended.

"The packet is gone from the shrine cupboard," Genemon said. "Silver, wrapped in oiled paper. It was there at the last cleaning."

"I know the cupboard," he said. "I've never opened it."

"There it is," Naoyuki said. "He knows the cupboard." He looked around the room as if the sentence had finished itself. "A man walks out of a river with nothing, and inside a season he knows which cupboard holds—" He stopped. He said, flat: "Count his corner."

"I was at the gate until dark. Rokusuke tallied my loads."

Genemon turned the day-book without answering. He read it the way he read everything, twice, with his finger under the line. "Gate log has him from the noon bell. Eleven loads in, marked by Rokusuke's hand. The cupboard was opened before the noon bell — the cleaning-girl swept that corridor after, and the dust was already off the latch." He closed the book. "The book clears him."

"The book," Naoyuki said. "The book didn't watch him at—" He left it. "Then the book can find the silver."

From the doorway, small, in her night-clothes, Toku: "It's in the temple box at Kuzu—" and then, "It's gone up already. I carried it at the new moon. The year the weir went, we sent it at the new moon. I keep to the new moon."

The room's silence changed shape. Chiyo looked at Genemon; Genemon looked at nothing. No one asked the dowager what the packet was for, and he understood, standing in the lamplight with his supper going cold at the gate, that the question had a rank, and he didn't hold it.

"To bed," Genemon said to the room. "The house has work at dawn."

The walls emptied. Naoyuki went last, and at the door he half-turned. "If it had been you—" He didn't finish it. "Count it twice, whoever keeps it."

Genemon waited until the boards stopped creaking. Then he took a key from his sleeve, iron, worn smooth by hands before his, and set it on the table between them.

"The kura key. Grain in, grain out, in the book by dusk. The book cleared you tonight. It will keep clearing you, or it won't — that part is yours."

He picked up the key. It was heavier than it looked.

"No one will say anything more about this," Genemon said, "and no one will say sorry either. Learn which of those matters."

- Genemon read the gate log twice before the household and said one word: cleared. No one apologized.

## 7. Kernel self-score

1. **The lie of the vanishing** — honored and extended: three vanishings, all mundane, believers (Toku, Sayo) treated as grievers; the one ambiguity stays the weir-jizō offering; the MC as the legend-in-reverse deepens the lie without adding magic.
2. **Nobody twice unnamed** — the frame makes it mechanical: the struck names are recovered by rung-climb, the drowned by register, Otsuru by spine; the strain is the MC's own optional name, resolved by ruling the *earned* name canonical and the reclaim a choice.
3. **Worth written by work** — the creed restored on-screen; truth itself made ledger-gated, so even reveals are ground for, never gifted.
4. **House before the man** — trust extended as need (R1, R4), the packet's silence outranking the man's vindication, Naoyuki's wound being the house's arithmetic; the late-game outgrowing question is asked at T5 and answered in the house's ink.
5. **Partial justice** — kept whole at every rung (Yagōemon, Iemasa, Sōbei walk); the contract's other half paid in a physical artifact — the full account, written and lodged.
6. **Form is the fiction** — the pitch's spine: ledger, register, annals, banzuke; unlocks rebuilt as needs (Kihei's field-guide, Chiyo's stalled joiner); zero grant-lists survive the renovation.
7. **The world is nobody's mirror** — the fairness machine is demolished: nine distinct misreads, a staged suspicion beat that ends without apology, promotions that are someone else's defeat, a village that marries and buries while he's in the deep woods. Being misread is the weather, and T0 finally rains.
