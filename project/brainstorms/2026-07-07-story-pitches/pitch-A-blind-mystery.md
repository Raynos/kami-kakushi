# The Hollow Book
*A pitch for kami-kakushi — built from kernel #1: the mystery is the spine.*

## 1. The premise

In 1780, in the Sasakage valley, a man with no name is pulled half-drowned from
the weir below the village of Utsuho. The first person to reach him is an old
woman who looks at his face and calls him by a dead girl's name. That mistake is
the whole game in miniature: this is a valley that has been losing people for
sixty years and telling itself the mountain took them.

*Kami-kakushi* — spirited away — is what Utsuho says when someone is gone and
the reason cannot be spoken. A child in the fog year. A miller on the ridge
path. A wife between one market day and the next. The stories are tended like
graves, and they are kinder than graves, because a person the gods have hidden
might still be alive. The title is earned because the game is about that phrase:
who says it, what it covers for, what it costs the sayers, and what it takes to
put a true name back where the phrase has stood for twenty years.

The player is that nameless man. He is taken on as day-labor by House Amagiri —
once the headman's house of the valley, now a mortgaged farm run by a stiff old
man and his widowed daughter — and for sixty hours he does what the poor have
always done: works. Hauls, mends, copies, carries, fights off dogs on the
charcoal paths. The work is written down, because this is a world of ledgers,
and written work becomes deeds, and deeds become standing, and standing —
rung by rung, tier by tier — becomes a name. That is the incremental game.

The mystery is why the game exists. The House's decline and the valley's
vanishings share a root: one autumn in 1761, three children disappeared in a
single fog season, and House Amagiri fell within the year. The player does not
investigate instead of working; the investigation *is inside the work* —
found in the day-books he is set to recopy, the registers he is trusted to
carry, the paths he is paid to walk, the old man he mends nets beside. The
deeper he is written into the House, the closer he stands to what the House
wrote down and hid. And the oldest thread — the one the game holds longest —
is that one of the three children taken in 1761 has already come back, nameless,
through the weir.

All proper nouns above are mine and recastable: *Sasakage* (a shaded valley),
*Utsuho* (hollow — chosen so the valley's name, the hidden ledger, and the lie
all rhyme), *Amagiri* (rain-mist: a house whose name is already halfway to
vanishing). Rename freely; keep the rhyme of hollowness if possible.

## 2. The mystery spine

**What actually happened.** In 1761 the harvest failed and the intendant's
office pressed the valley for arrears it could not pay. The headman —
Amagiri Sōbei, father of the current head — settled it the way such things
were settled: quietly, with a broker. Three children were sold east under
cover of the autumn fog, chosen by cold arithmetic — the ones whose absence
cost the village least. Koma, a widow's daughter. Shin, the bankrupt miller's
boy. Sutekichi, a foundling raised at the temple, owned by no one. The
proceeds covered the shortfall; the district clerk stamped the receipts and
took his cut; the story of the mountain was allowed to grow because it was
more bearable than the truth and safer than it. Sōbei wrote it all in a
private ledger — this world writes everything down — and walled the book
into his storehouse. The miller hanged himself two years on; the valley said
the mountain called him too. House Amagiri never recovered: Sōbei stopped
eating at his own table, let the headship pass to House Tokita — who took it
one year after the takings, and took it *knowing* — and died leaving his son
Sōetsu the debts, the silence, and the shape of the thing but not its pages.

Koma died of fever in a post-town within two years. Shin lived — sold on to a
shipping house, now a grown clerk far east who will answer a letter but never
come home. Sutekichi was sold to a packhorse contractor, ran or was traded on,
and remembers nothing before the road. He is the man from the weir.

**How truth surfaces — and what the player does to earn it.** The spine is
built on one mechanical conviction: every reveal is *inside labor the House
actually needs*. Nothing is told; everything is found mid-task.

- **T0:** Set to recopy the House's rotting day-books for the tax season —
  hours of real copying-work, banked as deeds — the player finds a struck
  entry: Hōreki 11, three lines inked out, one margin word: *settled.* Old
  Denzō, across a winter of shared mending, twice calls the player by a name
  that belongs to no one living. Earned by: copying labor, and repetition —
  the same bench, enough times.
- **T1:** The priest, needing a copyist he can't pay, sets the player to
  duplicate the temple's death-register — and its margin-kin, the missing.
  This work produces the game's investigation surface (the Missing-book) and
  its training arc: a dozen older vanishings run down one by one, each truth
  small and human — a debt-flight, an elopement, a drowning covered by a
  brother's shame. The player learns the game's honest grammar: *the mountain
  is never the answer.* Earned by: register labor, travel, and the discovery
  latch — asking again at a node until rumor consolidates into fact.
- **T2:** Standing in village politics puts the player across a market table
  from Gonsuke, the broker's grandson, who wants his family's trade buried
  for the same reason Sōetsu does. His papers name the buyer's road east.
  Earned by: leverage, not friendship — a market favor the House's new
  standing makes possible, traded for a box of receipts.
- **T3:** The estate rebuild reaches the old storehouse. Tearing out the north
  wall — a funded construction stage the player has ground toward for a full
  tier — drops Sōbei's hollow book at the player's feet: sums, the broker's
  mark, the district stamp, and the children entered as *prices, not names.*
  The mechanism now known in full; the names still owed. Sōetsu must be faced
  — and the House, at the very hour of its restoration, must decide what a
  restoration is *for.*
- **T4:** The names. Letters down the post-road, the contractor's ledgers,
  paid passage to the towns the road passes through. Koma found — a fever
  register, a temple grave, twenty years old. Shin found — alive, and
  unwilling; his letter declines to return, and encloses the one thing only
  the third child would know: the counting rhyme the three of them shared.
  The player learns whose name was the third struck line. Earned by: the
  longest travel arc in the game, coin, and combat on roads where the
  vanished actually walked.
- **T5:** The payout of the full-truth contract (kernel #5). The stamped
  receipts lead up — and stop. The clerk who took the cut died with honors;
  the intendant's office is a wall of passive voice; there is no throne room
  and no scene where power kneels. What the player *can* reach: House
  Tokita's nineteen-year complicity, answered through the village's own
  process, at real cost to the Amagiri. And the rite: three names restored
  to the temple register and read aloud — Koma, Shin, Sutekichi — the game's
  whole promise landing as three lines of ink. Full truth. Partial justice.
  The gap between them is the ending's weight, not its flaw.

**The one ambiguity** (kernel #1's single permitted moment): O-Tose, dying in
T4, says her daughter came to her in the night and sat where the player is
sitting. The game never adjudicates it. Everywhere else, the mundane truth is
delivered in full — and twice it cuts deeper than the myth: Koma's fever bed,
and Shin's refusal, which no folk-tale ever imagined — that the hidden might
be found, and *decline to be brought back.*

## 3. The core cast

- **The player** — no name; entered in the day-book as **Yodo**, after the
  weir-pool he was pulled from. Wants, at first, only to eat; then to be
  written down; then — too late to stop — to know why Denzō's slip lands in
  his chest like a stone. *Shape:* plainest voice in the game; short
  sentences, no figures of speech, questions of one clause.
- **Amagiri Sōetsu** — House head, sixty, keeper of what ledgers survive.
  Wants the House's standing restored before he dies. Knows the *shape* of
  1761, not its pages; his silence is inherited, and defended as duty.
  Misreads Yodo as hands the House needs, later as a man digging for
  leverage. *Shape:* declaratives that end on the practical consequence;
  never asks — turns every question into an order ("You will tell me.").
- **O-Riku** — Sōetsu's widowed daughter; runs the kitchen and, in truth, the
  day-book. Wants her son to inherit a House worth having. Misreads Yodo as
  the drifter who robs them the day the granary's full; warms slowest, and
  her warming is the game's most expensive prize. *Shape:* everything a
  bargain — "if… then," even in kindness ("If you're staying, the gate wants
  a hinge.").
- **Denzō** — the last field-hand who never left; was there in 1761. Wants to
  die employed. Has spent nineteen years unseeing one night. His "misread"
  is the game's cruelest irony: he alone reads Yodo *right* — calls him
  Sutekichi — and everyone, player included, files it as senility. *Shape:*
  starts mid-thought, as if resuming a talk from years ago, and trails off
  into work-instructions.
- **Kaishun** — the temple priest; keeps the death-register and its missing
  margin. Wants grief and truth kept from injuring each other; believes a
  name should wait for the ear that can bear it. Misreads Yodo's digging as
  self-absolution. *Shape:* paired clauses; the game's only licensed
  maxim-holder, one per scene at most.
- **O-Tose** — Koma's mother, at the valley's edge; the vanishing-faith's
  kindest believer. Wants the story to be true, because spirits return and
  sold children don't. Misreads Yodo — at the weir, and forever after a
  little — as something the mountain sent back. *Shape:* addresses the
  absent mid-sentence; the dead share her room.
- **Gonsuke** — heir to the pedlar's stall and the broker's blood. Wants
  respectability; would pay to have his grandfather's trade stay buried.
  Misreads Yodo as blackmail growing legs. *Shape:* hides feeling in
  inventories — lists, sums, counts, then the true sentence last, if at all.
- **Ihei** — the district office's clerk, twice a season, the visible face of
  the ceiling. Wants quiet books. Misreads Yodo as nobody, then as a
  paperwork problem. *Shape:* passive voice; nothing has an actor ("It was
  decided. The entry was made.").
- **Kihachi** — young charcoal-burner; ranges the paths the vanished last
  walked; Yodo's first almost-friend. Wants to leave the valley — a
  vanishing-in-waiting the player watches with new eyes. Misreads Yodo as a
  man fleeing a crime, and likes him for it. *Shape:* stacks two questions
  and answers his own first one.

## 4. The arc in circles

**T0 — Day-labor.** The House: a mortgaged farm nobody visits. The ascension
crosses the line between *vagrant* and *written man*: at the spring census
Yodo is entered in the register under the Amagiri roof — taxable, musterable,
askable — a body becomes a line of ink. Mystery beat owned: the struck entry
of Hōreki 11, found in the recopying, and Denzō's first slip. (Rung detail
below.)

**T1 — Retainer.** The House: solvent; a tenant field recovered; Yodo stands
surety at market with the House's chit — an outsider's word now carries
Amagiri weight, and half the village resents the arithmetic. Mystery beat:
the temple copying produces the Missing-book, and the training vanishings —
a dozen old cases closed small and human, each a name restored, the player
taught that the mountain is never the answer and that O-Tose's year is the
one case nobody will speak into the register.

**T2 — A voice.** The House: back at the village council table after
seventeen years; the line crossed is an *outsider speaking in the assembly*
with the valley's old families glaring. Mystery beat: Gonsuke — the broker
thread. The player's new standing is the lever that opens the family papers,
and the vanishing stops being weather and becomes *commerce*: there was a
road, a buyer, a price.

**T3 — The compound.** The House: rebuilding — walls, weir, mill — and
contesting the headship House Tokita has held since 1762. The line crossed:
the House stops asking and starts *appointing*. Mystery beat: the hollow book,
walled in the storehouse the rebuild tears open. The full mechanism, in
Sōbei's own hand — and the tier's drama is kernel #4 at full weight: Sōetsu
on his knees in the sawdust, and the question of whether a House restored on
this silence is restored at all.

**T4 — The headship.** The House: keeper of the valley's registry — the man
written in as a servant now writes others in, and finds, pages back, his own
struck line. The social line crossed is exactly that recursion. Mystery
beat: the road east. Koma's grave. Shin's letter. The counting rhyme. The
player learns his own first name from a man who won't come home — and
O-Tose's deathbed carries the game's one unadjudicated moment.

**T5 — The valley's voice.** The House: speaks for Sasakage to the district
office — the highest reach the story permits, and it is not high enough. The
line crossed: Amagiri's man sits across from the state — and learns that
sitting across is all he will ever do. Mystery beat: the contract pays out.
The stamped receipts climb until they stop at a dead man's honors; Tokita
answers through village process at bitter cost; the rite restores three
names to the register aloud. Final DECIDE: under which name is *he* written,
now that he holds the brush — Sutekichi, recovered, or Yodo, earned. Either
answer is written in the Amagiri book. The asking is the drama; the House is
honored either way.

**T0 at rung level (R0→R7):**

- **R0 — The weir.** Pulled out; O-Tose calls him Koma; the word
  *kami-kakushi* enters the game in a believer's kind mouth. Actions: haul
  stone, mend the weir screen. Gate: a day's deeds. No name, no roof.
- **R1 — The gate.** Sōetsu buys his labor by the day; O-Riku counts meals
  against work in exact conditionals. First hidden action: a silted sluice
  the field-work keeps hinting at — the discovery latch taught as *noticing,
  repeated*. Gate: field deeds + one repaired tool. Promotion scene: fed at
  the threshold. Not inside.
- **R2 — The woodshed.** A sleeping place (the home surface arrives because
  the story grants a roof, not because a menu unlocked). Denzō takes him for
  mending; first slip — a name, swallowed. Sent to Gonsuke's stall with the
  House's chit: the market opens as *trust the House can't afford but needs*.
  Gate: deeds + the errand's coin accounted to the mon.
- **R3 — The paths.** Charcoal run with Kihachi; wild dogs; first combat and
  first injury — the mountain is dangerous the way hills are, not the way
  stories are. Kihachi's path-notes seed the field-guide. He points out,
  conversationally, where each vanished person was last seen. Gate: a stance
  learned + the ridge route walked whole.
- **R4 — The recopying.** Sōetsu's eyes fail before the tax season; the old
  day-books must be duplicated. Long, honest copying labor — and inside it,
  earned by tedium, the struck entry: three lines inked out, *settled.* The
  game's thesis rung: the reveal is not adjacent to the grind; it is *in*
  it. Gate: copying deeds + brush-skill raised.
- **R5 — The visit.** Ihei arrives; Yodo carries the books and watches the
  House be humiliated in passive voice. A quest to cover a small arrears by
  extra labor — met with a nod, not thanks. The player learns what standing
  is by watching its absence, and learns the fiction won't pay warmth the
  work hasn't earned twice over. Gate: coin + hauling deeds.
- **R6 — Winter.** O-Tose at the gate in snow, calling Koma in. Walking her
  home, the player hears 1761 told as the mountain's kindness — the lie at
  its most human, treated gently. Across a winter of shared mending — the
  same bench, night on night — Denzō's second slip lands where the player
  can finally hold it: "Sutekichi carried two bales at your age." Gate: the
  winter survived — hearth, recovery, mending deeds.
- **R7 — Written in.** Spring census. Sōetsu enters him in the roll: *Yodo,
  of the weir, under the Amagiri roof.* The name read aloud; O-Riku's
  conditional loses one clause. And pages back in the same register, the
  struck lines — the year now matching O-Tose's story. Ascension is the
  player's opt-in: to be written is to be bound, taxed, and *askable*. He
  signs anyway. That is the tier.

## 5. Fiction↔mechanics causation

The pitch's core mechanical claim: **the discovery latch is the mystery
mechanic.** The inventory already contains an investigation engine — hidden
actions that permanently reveal through repeated attempts, with tightening
hint text. Reframed as *asking again* — at a bench, a stall, a grave, a
register — it models exactly how rural truth surfaces: not by deduction
scenes but by presence, repetition, and standing. Denzō's slips, Gonsuke's
papers, the sluice, the storehouse wall: all latches. The hint-tightening
*is* rumor consolidating.

**Required by this pitch:**

- **Every surface is a period object** (kernel #6): the event log is the
  House day-book, kept in the keeper's voice; deeds are ledger lines; the
  rung ladder is the census/service-grade; the bestiary is Kihachi's
  path-notes; the balance is koku and mon. No surface renames later — they
  are born in fiction.
- **The Missing-book**: one new surface — the investigation ledger — created
  on-screen by the T1 temple-copying beat (the need dramatized, per kernel
  #6). It lists vanishings as entries that fill from rumor toward name; a
  closed entry displays the recovered name permanently.
- **Reveals embedded in labor**: the R4 recopying, T1 register work, and T3
  storehouse stage must be real timed labor actions whose *completion* fires
  the discovery — the grind and the spine mechanically one flesh.
- **Promotion scenes as writing-scenes**: every rung's authored beat centers
  the ledger line being written; R7's census entry is the ascension.
- **Estate stages carry mystery beats**: the T3 storehouse wall is a funded
  construction stage; the build order is authored, not free.

**Suggested, not required:**

- Auto-repeat framed as *habit*: a repeated labor becomes a standing
  day-book line ("Mornings: the weir"), quietly satisfying kernel #3 —
  repetition is what gets recorded.
- Perks as named objects/habits (prose law #3): *Denzō's whetstone*, *a
  straw raincoat*, *the weir ledger* — each granted inside the scene that
  produces it.
- Seasonal testimony: certain latches only advance in-season (winter mending
  with Denzō; fog-season rumor), making the calendar narratively load-bearing.
- Combat kept mundane and *reasoned*: roads are dangerous because the
  vanished walked them; each field-guide entry demystifies ("the thing that
  screams on the ridge" latches open as a red fox).
- Standing (never spent, per constraints) as the *lever* of T5's justice:
  the cost is dramatized in coin, health, and a broken marriage-match —
  never in the score.

## 6. One written sample scene + 5 log lines

**R4 — The recopying.** *(Lamplight, the Amagiri main room, late autumn.)*

The old books smelled of the storehouse — mouse and cold rice-straw. Yodo
copied by lamplight, right to left, page for page, the way Sōetsu had shown
him once and not twice.

Sōetsu sat across the brazier with his eyes shut. He was not asleep. He was
listening to the brush.

Meiwa. Hōreki. The years went backward under Yodo's hand. Rice in, rice
out. A roof re-thatched. A loan taken against the east field, and another,
and the entries growing shorter, as if the ink itself were being rationed.

Then a page where three lines had been struck through. Not blotted —
struck, with a ruler, the old man's hand steady even here. The names
underneath had been fed so much ink they were only shapes. In the margin,
one word.

*Settled.*

Yodo's brush stopped. In the quiet the brazier ticked.

"You have found the flood year," Sōetsu said, eyes still shut. "Copy the
page whole. A book with pages missing is worth nothing."

"And the struck lines?"

"Struck lines are also the book."

Yodo copied them as they were: three shapes drowned in ink, and the one dry
word. His copy of the strike was neater than the original. He noticed that
he had made it neater, and did not know what he had made neater, and copied
on.

At the gate, going out for wood, he passed Denzō coming in with the last of
the netting. The old man looked at the ink on his fingers, mid-thought, as
always.

"—cold for it," Denzō said. "Bank the fire before the last page. Your hand
shakes and you don't feel it. His father was the same, that autumn. Wrote
all night."

"What autumn?"

But Denzō had already gone in, to the warmth, the netting over his
shoulder like a man carrying his own fog.

**Five log lines** *(the House day-book, in channels):*

- *Work* — Hauled stone to the weir. Forty loads. Denzō marked the tally.
- *Market* — Sold two bundles kindling at Gonsuke's stall, 24 mon. He
  counted it twice.
- *Field-guide* — The screaming on the ridge: a red fox, seen at dusk.
  Kihachi's note: they cry worst in mating season. Nothing takes them.
- *House* — O-Riku set a second bowl at the threshold. If the gate hinge is
  mended by dark, it may be a habit.
- *Season* — First fog on the mountain. O-Tose burned pine at her door all
  night. No one spoke of it.

## 7. Kernel self-score

1. **The lie of the vanishing** — the spine's engine: every case closes
   mundane (debt, sale, fever, flight); believers held gently through
   O-Tose; the single ambiguity spent once, at her deathbed, and never
   adjudicated. Shin's refusal makes the mundane cut deeper than the myth.
2. **Nobody twice unnamed** — the arc *is* naming: the protagonist's two
   names, the three children recovered by name in a spoken rite, every T1
   training case closing as a name entered. Strain: a large cast of small
   closed cases demands naming discipline in content authoring.
3. **Worth written by work** — reveals are earned inside deed-banking labor;
   the census line is the tier capstone; praise arrives clause by clause
   (O-Riku). The recovered name grants nothing material — Sutekichi was a
   foundling; blood buys zero.
4. **House before the man** — the ladder is the House's ledger; T3 stakes
   the House's restoration against its silence; the final choice honors the
   House under either name. Strain: T4–T5 pull the man's private truth
   forward hard — the drama is the permitted asking, and it must be built
   to resolve House-ward.
5. **Partial justice** — the receipts climb to a dead man's honors and stop;
   the state concedes a permit; only Tokita answers, through process, at
   cost. Full truth pays out to the last name. The contract is kept in both
   directions.
6. **Form is the fiction** — day-book, census, Missing-book, path-notes,
   koku and mon; the one new surface is dramatized as a copying job the
   priest can't pay for; no grant-lists anywhere in the pitch.
7. **The world is nobody's mirror** — every cast member misreads him a
   different way, and the truest reading (Denzō's) is dismissed as senility;
   Shin declines the reunion the player has crossed the map to earn; O-Riku's
   warming is measured in dropped clauses, never owed. Being misread is the
   weather, and the weather never fully clears.
