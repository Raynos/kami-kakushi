# Kami-kakushi — reinvention pitch: **"The Ledger of the Vanished"**

## 1. The premise

A man walks out of his own life on a famine-eve night in 1780, lets his village believe the kami took him, and washes up — half-drowned, nameless on purpose — at the gate of a house that was itself hollowed out by a vanishing. That is the whole game: *kami-kakushi* is not a mystery the protagonist investigates from the outside. **He is one.** The player knows it from the first scene. The world never does — until he chooses to write it down.

The Kurahashi house (renamed from Kurosawa — the audit is right that it's the loudest real-name echo left in the game; *Kurahashi*, "storehouse-bridge," keeps the syllable the player already lives in, because the game begins and ends in a kura) is a declining *gōshi* house in the Asagiri valley — a name kept, but re-earned: *asagiri*, morning mist, is the hour people vanish in, and the valley is named for the weather that eats its people. Seven years ago the house's elder son rode into a flood night and never came back. The lord stopped writing the ledgers that season. The debt has been compounding ever since — because grief, in a world where worth is written, is what happens when a house stops keeping its own books.

Into this walks a man with no papers, no past he'll admit to, and a labourer's hands. Sōan assumes the blow took his memory. *He lets them assume.* The player spends sixty hours doing what incremental games do — raking, hauling, counting, compounding — inside a fiction where **counting is how lies surface**. Every ledger the player grinds is a ledger with a truth buried in it. The title is earned twice over: the game debunks kami-kakushi with kindness, one vanishing at a time — and it is played from inside the lie the title names.

Why it matters for sixty hours: the ledgers are the same object at every scale. The day-book that first records "one man, name unknown" is the instrument that clears him when he's accused, the evidence that convicts the house of its buried crime, the register that names the drowned, and the page his true name is finally written on. The player's number-go-up **is** the story's truth-go-up.

**Stones taken from the quarry, and the building made of them:** the title-is-a-lie premise (kept, then inverted — the MC now lives inside it); compound interest as antagonist (refit: the debt is grief's arithmetic, and its collateral hides the house's guilt); the Otsuru truth, the best idea in the repo (kept nearly whole, but rewired — she becomes the MC's confessor, the one runaway who can read another); partial justice honored to the end; the load-bearing paper texture — *kyō-masu*, sekisho stamps, registers, banzuke — **promoted from texture to the game's entire investigation mechanic**; Kihei's creed and the shame arc (restored as designed); the weir-jizō ambiguity token (kept, re-aimed at the game's emotional center). The new thing built: the amnesia becomes a lie the player is complicit in; the house gets its own vanished; the mystery is solved with arithmetic instead of adventure; and levelling up becomes self-exposure — every skill the nameless man shows is evidence against the nobody he claims to be.

## 2. The mystery spine

There is no single vanishing. There is a **ladder of vanishings — four human causes for one lie — climbing in moral weight**, and the MC's own is threaded through all of them.

**The vanishings, and what actually happened:**

1. **The MC's own (player-known from T0; world-unknown until he writes it).** A tenant family on the eve of the lean years; the holding fed one mouth fewer than it held; the second son did the cold arithmetic himself and walked into the mist, so that his family could tell the kami story and grieve, instead of naming a deserter and being shamed. The lie of the vanishing as a *gift* — kernel #1 made flesh. His birth name is **Tahei**. He never says it. The player watches him not say it for six tiers.
2. **The drowned stranger (T2, small — the rehearsal).** Ryōa's register holds an unnamed man pulled dead from the weir years ago and buried nameless. Through register work and a pedlar's route-memory the MC recovers the name and pays for the reinscription — his first act of naming, performed for a stranger who got the death everyone assumes *he* had.
3. **Otsuru — "Tama" (T2–T3, guaranteed on the spine).** Kept from the quarry on merit: a girl who ran from a violent stepfather and a near-sale, alive and ashamed a decade down-valley, freed to choose, not obliged to forgive. New wiring: when they meet, she clocks him in minutes — one self-vanished person reading another — and becomes the only living soul he tells before the end.
4. **Naokata, the house's elder son (the crown of the spine, T0→T4).** He didn't drown. On the flood night seven years ago, the grandfather's failing embankment works were **breached deliberately** to send the water onto Kuzuhara hamlet and spare the house's own paddies. Naokata — the son whose beautiful clerk's hand fills the old ledgers — found the proof, confronted his father, and could not live inside the house's lie. He walked. The family told the kami story. He is alive: a temple registrar under another name, a man who spends his exile writing vanished strangers' names into death registers so no one is buried nameless. He is met exactly once. He does not come home. He asks one thing: that the house stop calling it kami-kakushi and write the truth.

**How truth surfaces — what the player DOES:** every reveal is earned by the game's own incremental verbs, staged at the access rung that makes it plausible. T0: the kura key (R4) puts the old ledgers in his hands — the **seven-year gap**, pages cut at the stub; Chiyo's errands (R6) pass the loan papers through his hands unread. T1: the debt-renegotiation grind *requires* reconciling the house's books against Tōkichi's line by line — and the reconciliation coughs up the anomaly: paddies pledged as collateral that the house should not have held, the season after the flood. A trade manifest surfaces a sekisho stamp: Naokata's pass, used on foot, two days *after* the flood. **He lived.** T2: village standing opens Ryōa's register and the inn-books — the Tama case is built with the same clerical verbs; exposing Magobei's doctored *kyō-masu* trains the player's eye for rigged instruments. T3: the Kuzuhara resettlement grind exhumes the works-ledger — and the damning document is a **payroll entry**: three men, one koku of sake, "emergency works," dated the flood night, *in Genemon's young hand* — because the steward was too honest not to record even that. Worth is written; so is guilt. T4: the paper-chase through domain and temple records finds Naokata; one meeting; one letter home in a hand the lord has not seen in seven years.

**Where the full-truth contract pays (kernel #5):** by end of T4 the cause of every vanishing the game has ever raised is fully known — to the player, and written in a register in the world. Justice stays partial: Sadamune is dead and beyond reach; Genemon's sentence is to read his own payroll entry aloud in the temple at the naming of the drowned; Otsuru's stepfather is glimpsed once, protected by distance, and never answers; the lord answers by dying with the truth known and the son unreturned. The one permitted ambiguity is spent at maximum load, and it is not supernatural: in the late game, offerings begin appearing at the weir-jizō where "the river man" was found — a child's straw sandal, the offering left for the spirited-away. Whether word ever travelled up-country — whether his mother knows her son lives — the game never says.

## 3. The core cast

*(Ellipsis-reacts are banned house-wide; each voice gets a sentence SHAPE and, where noted, a physical react-gesture instead. The MC talks plainest of all.)*

- **"Nagare" / Tahei (the MC).** Wants: to be worth the rice he eats — literally; he left home to be one less mouth. Misread as: an amnesiac simpleton, then a schemer, then the son-shaped hole's new tenant. *Shape:* bare declaratives, rarely past eight words, no metaphor ever; a question visibly costs him.
- **Genemon (steward).** Wants: to die with the books balanced — a penance he has never named, because his young hand dug the breach. His folding-in of the stranger is itself a misreading: he needs the nameless man to be redeemable to prove that people are. *Shape:* tallies and conditions — "Two koku short; make it three by frost or we sell the mare." React-gesture: he answers surprise by writing something down.
- **Lord Shigemasa.** Wants: to die before the crime is spoken. Misreads the MC as fate's replacement for the son he drove off — warmth arriving too early and too much, which is its own violence. *Shape:* abandoned periods — "The house has always— well." Sole epigram license in the game, and even his maxims curdle into self-doubt.
- **Naoyuki (the younger son, heir by subtraction).** Wants: to be *chosen* once, not left over. Misreads the MC as the stray his father can love uncomplicatedly — wrong about everything except the one thing: "you are hiding something" is the truest sentence anyone says in T0. *Shape:* over-finished corrective sentences; quotes your words back with the numbers fixed.
- **Naokata (the vanished son).** Present for four tiers as handwriting before he is ever a person — the MC comes to know him by reading seven years of his ledger entries. Wants: not to live inside the lie. The *MC* misreads *him* for tiers — assumes flight was cowardice, his own gentle verdict on himself — and learns it was refusal. *Shape (when finally met):* register-entries — dates, names, causes; the speech of a man who became a ledger.
- **Lady Chiyo.** Wants: solvency as dignity; she runs the arithmetic the lord abandoned. Misreads the MC deliberately — declines to read him at all, on the stated ground that a man's legibility matters less than his output's. Warms last, by one degree. *Shape:* audit conditionals — if/then, debit/credit; praise phrased as a finding: "The count is early this month."
- **Kihei (drillmaster).** Wants: one student who outlives him. Misreads the MC permanently: those porter's muscles read as concealed training, and he drills him brutally to force a confession that never comes — because there is nothing to confess. He goes to his grave half-convinced. The creed — *"Talent is a story the lucky tell. You are not lucky. So you will work"* — is his one speech in general terms, which is why it lands. *Shape:* imperatives, counts, gear- and body-nouns; zero abstractions. React-gesture: surprise becomes an assigned drill.
- **Sōan (physician).** The counter-misreading: the one man who reads the MC *right* — head wounds don't heal like that — and says nothing for years, a confidentiality extended to a lie he can't diagnose the harm of. Wants: to have been wrong. *Shape:* prognoses — "Give it a season; if it still hurts, it isn't the rib." Asks two questions where others ask none.
- **Dowager Toku.** Keeper of the kind version — "his own failure, not anyone's crime" — which she believes. The truth must eventually pass *through* her, and she holds both versions at once: believing as grief-work, honored to the end. *Shape:* completed past tense and reported speech; a present-tense sentence from Toku is an event.
- **Otsuru** (kept), **Sayo**, **Ryōa**, **Tōkichi** anchor the village and the debt as in the quarry — with Tōkichi promoted: the tired moneylender's fair books are half the evidence, so beating the T1 antagonist *is* an investigation beat. Otsuru's *shape:* answers a different question than the one asked — ten years of hiding, audible.

## 4. The arc in circles

**T0 — the kura (detailed below).** House at foreclosure's edge; the social line crossed is *unwritten → written* (day-book to household register). Mystery beat owned: the seven-year ledger gap, and the player's own complicity established — the lie visible at R0, the reason for it legible by R7.

**T1 — the estate whole.** The house digs out from under compound interest; the line crossed is *hand → officer* — a man who signs things. Mystery beat: the debt renegotiation itself is the evidence engine — reconciling the two ledgers surfaces the collateral anomaly and the sekisho stamp. The tier ends with the house solvent and the player holding a fact no one has said aloud in seven years: *he lived.*

**T2 — the valley.** The house's man in other people's streets, misread daily; Sayo names him Tama and he must live inside a misidentification he understands from the inside. The line crossed: the house's word becomes currency beyond its gate. Mystery beats: the drowned stranger named (the rehearsal); the *kyō-masu* skim broken (the rigged-instrument eye trained); the Tama case built from registers and inn-books to a living address down-valley.

**T3 — the region.** The crown. The Kuzuhara resettlement exhumes the payroll page; Genemon confesses; the drowned are named in the temple register with the cause beside them — and *this is what wins the region*: while the rival houses Tomita and Akagi contest money and precedence, the Kurahashi do the one thing no incumbent would — publish their own guilt and pay for it in labour. Honour lands as a computed overtake because the house's Name pillar is fed by the costliest deed on the board. Otsuru found; may not forgive her family; reads the MC in one afternoon and receives the only confession he makes before the end. The line crossed: the house arbitrates others' disputes with its own crime in ink.

**T4 — the castle-town.** The paper-chase finds Naokata; the single meeting; the refusal to return; one letter travels home to a dying lord. Naoyuki inherits *knowing he was second choice*, and builds anyway — worth by work, proven on the heir. The line crossed: the house is recorded by the people who actually rule. Partial justice ratified: the reachable answered, the dead beyond reach, the truth complete.

**T5 — Edo, mediated.** The banzuke climb; the inspector's survey reads the house's record aloud — *including Kuzuhara*, because the house wrote it — and the commendation lands not despite the confession but because the ledger balances. The MC's ceiling holds at the lord's right hand. His own thread closes in three quiet moves: an anonymous remittance routed up-country to a village he never names; his birth name written in small characters beside his earned one in the house register — full truth honored even about himself; and the straw sandal at the weir-jizō, never explained. One authored ending; the register page is the final title card.

**T0 at rung level (R0→R7).** The seven-performance-reviews monoculture is dissolved: each rung gets its own scene *grammar*.

- **R0 · Day-labourer.** The cold open: the weir, Sōan's examination, the assumption he does not correct — staged so the player sees him *practice* not-answering. First verb: rake the spilled rice. The day-book entry is the rung's title card: *one man, name unknown.* No motive narration; the why assembles across the tier.
- **R1 · Kept hand.** Not a summons — an **overheard argument**: Genemon and Chiyo, through a wall, on the real cost of keeping an unregistered man (a nameless body is a legal liability to a *gōshi* house; harbouring him is risk, dramatized, not mood). Genemon answers for him; the day-book gets a nickname to write: *Nagare* — the drift, what you call a thing the river brought.
- **R2 · Trusted hand.** No promotion scene at all — a task simply *not taken back*; trust shown by omission. The woodlot, the forage ring, and the wolf: **survived, not won** (canon repaired) — blood on the grain-store sill, most of it his, the rice untouched, the wolf gone bleeding into the dark. The porter's-knot beat recast: his hands tie it unbidden and he *freezes* — the first time the player sees him afraid of his own competence.
- **R3 · Gate-watch.** The first scene the MC ever initiates: ribs wrapped, he confesses the granary loss and **begs** for drills (shame arc restored). The creed, on-screen, once. Kihei's misreading begins — "You've held a spear." "No." — and the drills double as interrogation.
- **R4 · Kura-warden.** No words at all: the **key left on his sleeping mat**. Shelving the old ledgers, he finds the gap — a season with no entries, page-stubs where leaves were cut. The bestiary arrives as Kihei's need, not a grant: he is sick of burying men who can't tell a *mamushi* from a rat-snake.
- **R5 · House-servant.** The anti-promotion — **the accusation**. Three shu of silver short from the errand-purse; Naoyuki leads; Genemon pledges his own wage; the market and the maids turn measurably colder (distrust as mechanical friction, not narration). The MC clears himself the only way a man with no past can: he re-counts the ledger at the source. (Sample scene, §6.)
- **R6 · Steward's man.** An errand that was secretly a test, visible as one only afterward — Chiyo's ledgers and loan papers pass through his hands; the anomaly is planted in plain sight, unreadable to him *yet* (his letters are still forming — see §5).
- **R7 · Trusted of the house.** The tier's **one** formal audience, earning its ceremony by being the only one. In the shoin at dusk, Shigemasa — warm, tired — calls him, once, by *Naokata's* name. "No, lord." "No. Well." The capstone opens the Phase-2 estate grind; the first ascension's title card is the **census register page** — a name that now exists in law.

## 5. Fiction↔mechanics causation

The thesis: in an incremental game the player's core act is *making numbers legible* — so every mystery is a bookkeeping anomaly, and the genre itself is the detective. Nothing is bolted on: the reveal cadence rides the verbs the player already grinds.

**Required by this pitch:**
- **The log is the day-book.** One surface: event log, perk list, and quest record all render as entries in the house's day-book (kernel #6 taken literally). Every perk names its concrete object as an entry — *"put down: one whetstone, gift of Tōzō"* — killing the chiasmus template structurally, not stylistically.
- **Evidence rides existing activities.** The T1 debt grind gains one activity, *reconcile the books* (same act-shape as haul/farm); reveals key off completion counts the way rung requirements already do. No new system — new meaning for the existing meter.
- **Suspicion as friction (R5).** During the accusation arc: market prices worsen, two indoor activities lock, one log line a day marks the cold. Distrust dramatized in the numbers the player watches anyway (kernel #7's application clause).
- **Decide menus stop being moral thermometers.** No warm/cold scalar; every option moves a *different* relationship or opens a different small door (the r4-generous pattern generalized), and in the suspicion arc there is no right answer at all.
- **Ceremony reskin.** The first-ascension title card is the register page; ascensions thereafter are entries, seals, and stamps — paper all the way up.

**Suggested, not required:**
- **Letters as a skill track.** The MC learns to read and write to do steward's work; document reveals gate on literacy milestones — the investigation stat is a labour stat (kernel #3).
- **Competence-as-exposure beats.** A handful of authored log lines keyed to skill milestones, in which the world *notices* he is too good ("pedlars read a yard fast") — the incremental curve itself generating dramatic pressure.
- **The append-only register as no-prestige.** Nothing rewinds; the fiction says why: what is written stays written. A framing line at each ascension, nothing more.

## 6. One written sample scene + 5 log lines

**R5 · Three shu** *(the accusation)*

&gt; Evening rice is cleared and nobody has gone to bed. The errand-purse lies open on Chiyo's table, its string cut loose, its coin laid out in rows. Three shu of silver short.

**Naoyuki:** "Count it again if you like. I counted twice. The purse went to market on his shoulder and came home light, and we are asked to believe the difference walked off on its own. He came to us with no name and no papers. I am only saying aloud what this house says in its sleep."

**Chiyo:** "If the market count was true, the loss is ours to find in this house. If it was false, the loss was never in the purse. Those are the two columns. I have not decided which this goes in."

**Genemon:** "Then put it in mine. Whatever's short, dock it from my wage. I answered for him at the gate and I'm not done answering."

&gt; He does not look at you while he says it, and not wanting your thanks for it.

**Naoyuki:** "You would pay to go on being wrong about him. That is generosity, steward. It is not proof."

&gt; You have no past to put on the table. You have what the house taught you.

**Decide** — the purse, the rows of coin, the room waiting.

- Take the lamp and walk to Tōkichi's tonight; count the ledger at the source.
- Turn out your sleeping corner in front of them — mat, bowl, and all.
- Ask Naoyuki to say plainly what he thinks you are.

*(the first)*

&gt; Tōkichi's clerk keeps a fair book and a tired eye. You set the house's tally beside his, line against line, and find it in the fourth row: the exchange written at the old rate, the new rate paid. Three shu, sitting in a ledger where no thief could spend them.

&gt; It is past the hour of the rat when you lay both books open on Chiyo's table, the two entries side by side.

**Chiyo:** "The clerk's rate. Docked from nobody." *(She looks at the books longer than she needs to.)* "You went to the numbers before you went to your own defence. I will remember which you trusted."

**Naoyuki:** "The rate. Yes." *(He sets the coin back into its rows, evenly, and stands.)* "I was wrong about the purse. I have not said I was wrong about you."

&gt; Genemon has already taken up the brush. In the day-book, under the day's date: *purse squared — the clerk's rate, found by N.* He writes nothing else about the evening, which is his way of closing it.

**Five log lines** (day-book register):

1. *Day-book, third month, in a hurried hand:* "One man taken on at the weir. Name unknown. G. answers for him."
2. "He stacks the last load square to the wall, then squares it again, though no one is watching that he knows of. Sōan is."
3. "Tokubei's price comes down a copper for him this week. Nobody told the pedlar anything."
4. "Two days now the maids count the linen in front of him. Oai says it is the new custom. It is not old enough to be a custom."
5. "At first light he is at the drill yard, ribs still wrapped. Kihei names the fee: sixty thrusts, and the truth of how he held that pole. He pays the sixty."

## 7. Kernel self-score

1. **The lie of the vanishing** — honored and doubled: every vanishing mundane (arithmetic, flight, exile, error), believers treated as grievers, and the protagonist is the lie's living proof; the single ambiguity is spent on the mother's knowledge, not on the supernatural.
2. **Nobody twice unnamed** — the spine *is* naming: the drowned stranger, Kuzuhara's dead, Otsuru, Naokata, and last of all himself; the day-book's "name unknown" is corrected on the final page.
3. **Worth written by work** — literalized: registers gate reveals, literacy is labour, the creed restored to Kihei's mouth; strain: the MC's secret competence must never read as innate gift — it is priced as prior *work* (a porter's decade), and Kihei's suspicion polices exactly that line.
4. **House before the man** — the house's crime, penance, and restoration are the arc; the Naokata question ("what does the house owe the men inside it?") is asked hard and answered in the house's favor: it survives by writing its own guilt.
5. **Partial justice** — Sadamune dead, the stepfather untouched, Genemon self-sentenced, the lord answered only by knowledge; full truth in ink everywhere. The contract holds in both directions.
6. **Form is the fiction** — the log, perks, quests, and ascension cards are one diegetic object (the day-book/register); every unlock is dramatized as a need; strain: the DEV-facing surfaces must be kept out of the conceit, which the constraint sheet already handles.
7. **The world is nobody's mirror** — misreading is the cast sheet's spine: Genemon's hope, Shigemasa's grief, Naoyuki's envy, Kihei's permanent suspicion, Chiyo's refusal to read at all — and the one accurate reader, Sōan, says nothing. The accusation arc makes the weather cost numbers, not just lines.
