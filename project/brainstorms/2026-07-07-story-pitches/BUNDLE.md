## The capsules

**A · The Hollow Book (blind, mystery-spine).** Three children sold east
in the famine of 1761 and written off as spirited away; the MC is the
third — a foundling with no memory of it, pulled from the weir by the
mother of another. Truth recovered ledger by ledger inside the labor the
House needs; ends with three names read back into the temple register,
and the MC choosing which of his two names is his. *Strengths:* the most
classical mystery build; the recopying scene ("Struck lines are also the
book") is the bundle's best prose; investigation-inside-labor is fully
worked out. *Risks:* "the MC was one of the taken" is exactly the move
the audit flagged as generator-instinct — executed here WITHOUT destiny
(he was sold because nobody owned him), but the human should choose it
knowingly; admits its own #4 strain (T4–T5 pull the man's truth ahead of
the House's).

**B · The Paper House (blind, house-spine).** The heir walked into the
snow a decade ago so his family could eat, lived, and cannot come back;
the vanishing-alms and a broker's gentle blackmail have been bleeding the
house ever since. The unstruck register line at R7 — promotion scene and
mystery-crack on one page — is the bundle's best single capstone.
*Strengths:* tightest kernel fit of the five; the silent kami 紙/神
paper-spirit pun; every promotion an act of writing; "warmth costs, and
no one here can afford it" is the fairness-machine fix as household
economics. *Risks:* the quietest and smallest mystery (one vanishing +
training cases); closest in shape to current canon's emotional register,
so least divergent of the blind three.

**C · The Entered Name (blind, social-spine).** A granary-fraud coverup:
the vanished girl saw the tally sticks being shaved and was walked over
the pass; the kami story is the village's collective alibi. The village
investigates the MC for a full tier; rung titles are what the village
calls him; promotions happen only witnessed; distrust is a market
surcharge. *Strengths:* the best anti-fairness-machine execution —
misreading priced in mon and gates, not narrated; "being loved wrongly is
not a problem the game solves"; recovery-vs-return distinction at its
sharpest. *Risks:* the House recedes — the village is the true
co-protagonist, which bends kernel #4; the final send-word-home DECIDE
gives the MC a fully-known past, the biggest canon change of the blind
set.

**D · The Ledger and the Legend (informed, renovator).** Keeps the
proven architecture knowingly — Otsuru/Tama, compound interest, partial
justice to the terminus, the Edo texture, Shigemasa's R7 nearly verbatim,
the proper nouns — and rebuilds everything the audit condemned: canon
repairs staged whole, the R4 suspicion beat ("The Count" — accusation,
ledger-clearing, no apology), promotions never staged alike, decide menus
as trades. Adds the house's own buried wound (the Kuzuhara drowned) and
keeps the MC's origin optional. *Strengths:* highest craft-per-risk;
preserves the strongest existing material; "truth is rank-gated paper" is
the cleanest premise-mechanics fusion sentence in the bundle. *Risks:*
the least NEW — it is the superseded ladder plan's spirit executed
excellently; if the reboot's point was a genuinely different story, D is
the conservative pole (which is exactly what a renovator is for).

**E · The Ledger of the Vanished (informed, reinventor).** The inversion:
the MC vanished ON PURPOSE — famine arithmetic, one less mouth — and the
player knows from scene one what the world never suspects; the amnesia is
a lie the player is complicit in. The house's own heir also self-vanished
(over the flood-night crime hidden in its collateral), so the MC rises
inside a house grieving the choice he himself made. Keeps the quarry's
best stones (Otsuru as his one confessor; the creed; partial justice; the
paper texture promoted from flavor to THE investigation mechanic).
*Strengths:* the pitch nobody else could write; "levelling up is
self-exposure" — every skill shown is evidence against the nobody he
claims to be — is the single best fiction↔mechanics idea in the bundle;
dramatic irony gives every warm scene teeth. *Risks:* the player holds
the secret, so the game's central mystery (Naokata) must carry the
whodunit load alone; a protagonist who deceives sympathetic people for
sixty hours needs the gift-lie framing to hold; two self-vanishings risk
doubling one trick.

## Convergence — what the mixed fleet proved

Blind/informed convergence is signal, not anchoring (reboot plan §5).
Five signals, in strength order:

1. **The ledger IS the game — 5/5.** Every pitch, blind and informed,
   independently made the written record (day-book / register / ledger)
   the central story surface and promotions acts of writing. This is the
   kernel's own gravity. Adopt as settled, whatever the pick.
2. **Recovery ≠ return — 4/5** (A's Shin, B's Naotarō, C's Osae, E's
   Naokata). The found vanished person declines to come back. Three blind
   agents landed here without seeing each other or the canon. Near-canon.
3. **The MC is himself a kami-kakushi — 5/5 by degree.** Lightest: B
   (plain nameless stranger). Middle: D (optional origin), C (a runaway
   whose village tells the same story). Heaviest: A (he IS one of the
   taken, unknowing), E (he vanished on purpose, knowing). The title plus
   kernel #2 force the MC's own vanishing into the field; the real design
   choice is DEGREE, and the bundle lays out the full dial.
4. **The T0 accusation beat — 4/5** (C's rumor-DECIDE, D's Count, E's
   three shu, A's implicit granary suspicion). The fairness-machine fix
   has a consensus shape: accusation → the record clears him → no
   apology.
5. **The lie protects an economic crime — 3/5** (A: children sold; C:
   granary skim; E: embankment breach). The strongest vanishings are
   cold arithmetic, not passion.

## My verdicts and self-pick

Judged as: kernel fit · distinctiveness (could no other pitch have
written it?) · fiction↔mechanics fusion · buildability · risk.

- **Primary recommendation: E — The Ledger of the Vanished**, for the
  three things no rival offers together: the inversion that makes the
  player complicit in the title, competence-as-exposure (the incremental
  curve itself generating drama — the co-design brief's bullseye), and
  informed keeping of the quarry's proven stones. Its risks are real but
  authored — they're choices, not oversights.
- **Grafts I'd carry into E from the others:** C's witnessed-promotion
  grammar and priced distrust (surcharge; rung titles as what the world
  calls him) — fully compatible with E's frame; D's "no two promotions
  staged alike" rule and its Count-scene discipline (E already shares the
  no-apology instinct); B's physical-wrongness motif style (the sandals
  facing away) for E's household grief-texture.
- **The strong alternative: D**, if continuity with the built game and
  the researched canon should win — it is the lowest-risk path to a
  dramatically better game, and its craft is the equal of any pitch here.
- **B, C, A** each carry at least one element I'd preserve in the bible
  regardless of pick (B's unstruck-line capstone staging; C's stranger
  surcharge; A's investigation-inside-labor thesis).
