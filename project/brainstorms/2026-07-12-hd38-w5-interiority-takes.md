# W5 · the MC's inner line (HD-38 / ADR-185 D3) — the three takes

**Not a live-swappable bundle.** The other HD-38 waves rewrote whole scenes, so their
alternates compile as take-sets the DEV Story switcher can swap. W5 is different: it
**inserts ~8 narration lines across 8 different scenes**, so a "take" is a *set of
insertions*, not a replacement scene. Wiring that as a live swap would mean forking every
host scene. These three files are therefore the **archive**, not a DEV toggle — the human
reviews the shipped lines by playing (they are ordinary narration in their scenes) and
reads the not-picked takes here.

## The pick: take C, "the body knows and the head does not" — with three edits

C is the only take with an **engine**: a man estranged from his own hands *wants* something
without remembering anything. Blind reader: *"A is a man, but a narrow one — ten counts in a
row becomes a tic. B is a device — its through-line is a null act, which no amount of craft
makes gripping. C is a person, and the one I'd follow ten hours."*

C also produced the formulation that should govern all future authoring in this register:

> **The hands may testify; only the dream may translate.**

That is D3's never-memory bound restated so an author can actually apply it. Every C line
hands the hands a capability and then **flatly refuses the account** (*"You did not put it
there. Nobody has shown you where it goes."*) — which **feeds** the dream instead of
pre-spending it.

**The three edits, all from the blind read:**
1. **Cut C's R3 line** — R3 already carries the corpus's model line (the seed-bale). C's own
   author flagged this one for the chop if strict. Two interior lines in one scene is a tic.
2. **Take A's R1 as the opener** — *"Six hands wanted. Five sleep here. You are the sixth,
   and there is no sixth place — the sum was short before you stood in this kitchen."* The
   best opener in the bundle, and it turns "no bed" from a cruelty into an arithmetic he can
   see the bottom of.
3. **Graft B's R4 board line** — *"He had taken the loss off your name. Two words put it
   back. There is no wage for it to come out of, and you said them anyway."* The only line in
   any take where he **acts against his own interest**, and the one thing C's man lacked.

## Why the other two lost

- **A · "the man who counts"** — rigorous, and its silences are the best-argued in the bundle
  (it refuses to double Genemon's ledger). But its register is arithmetic, and the game
  already counts constantly: *"Fifteen and fourteen is twenty-nine; eleven more is forty"* is
  homework, not interiority. Warm only at R7. Its R1 and its nengu redline both survive it.
- **B · "the cost of every question"** — owns the single best line anyone wrote (the R4
  graft), and the best-argued silence in the bundle (*works-intro: the exception IS the
  payoff*). But eight instances of "X costs Y, so you didn't" is a **device**, its
  through-line is a **null act**, and *"the most expensive thing this house has ever asked of
  you"* is a feeling wearing a ledger's coat — it obeys D3's letter and dodges its spirit.

## Bugs these takes found in the shipped corpus (all fixed with the wave)

1. **`ask r7-what-changes` narrated the MC's interior in THIRD person** — *"The question
   costs **him** something to ask"* — a direct §0.5.8 violation, in canon, locked the same
   morning. Now "you".
2. **The Count's only stated price sits behind an OPTIONAL ask** (`r5-accused`) — a player who
   never clicks stands accused all night and is charged nothing. *(Left as a finding: the
   picked take places its R5 line in `count-resolve`, not the main body of `count`. Worth the
   human's call.)*
3. **Pre-R7 scenes keep saying "against your name"** when the house has not written him one —
   the same class as the "Gonbei" defect W2 fixed. *(Finding; not swept.)*
4. **Intro 2's "What name did I give? When they pulled me out."** is the first question he
   ever asks and it is about himself — and it is completely unpriced. *(Finding.)*

# W5 take A — "The man who counts"

Through-line: hired as a number, kept as a number, cleared by a number, named because a
number came cheaper. So he counts back — quietly, twice, always to himself. 10 lines /
10 scenes; adds NOTHING to the four scenes that already carry the register.

## R1 — after "> Nobody stops eating… The bowls go on."
> Six hands wanted. Five sleep here. You are the sixth, and there is no sixth place — the
> sum was short before you stood in this kitchen. Nobody at the table says it aloud.
> Neither do you.

## works-intro — after "> He is holding the page flat with two fingers…"
> He has read you four lines. You count the page: there are more than four, and the ones
> he passed over are the ones where the ink is oldest. He is giving you the small ones
> first.

## sb-racks — after Rokusuke's "…I keep the count."
> Four a night. Twenty-eight the week. You run it while he talks, and it comes out even.
> He has not rounded it in his own favour, and he could have.

## sb-market — after Genemon's "…nobody has told you where coin goes."
> He knows the number in the rag. So do you. You have known it since the day it was tied,
> and you have never once opened it to check.

## sb-sickroom — after Sōan's "…you have a page in it now, the same as the mule."
> Two books, then. The steward's, and this one. Both have had a page for you since the day
> they carried you up from the weir, and neither has a name to put at the head of it.

## count (R5) — after "> Nobody objects… the order the room was already thinking in."
> You go through your corner in your head, the way you would count a store. A mat. A bowl.
> A whetstone. A mended cord. Four things, and not one of them the house's. Nobody in this
> room will take your word for that.

## nengu-autumn-frame — REPLACES the ADR-185-named wall line
CUT: "You are learning the house's true size by what it will let itself be seen without."
> Nobody reads the number aloud, so you count what you can. One screen, still standing in
> its room, already spent. The house does not say what it owes. It shows you, one thing at
> a time, what it is paying with.

## R6 — after Yohei's "Eleven for the thread, then?"
> Fifteen and fourteen is twenty-nine; eleven more is forty. You run it twice. His price
> for the basket is the purse in your hand, to the mon — nothing over, nothing back. At the
> steward's nine, two mon come home with you.
(Canon-exact: r6-put-back prices salt+oil at 29; Genemon counted out 40.)

## sb-dog — after Kihei's "Don't leave it half-decided."
> A handful a morning, off one measure a day. You have the winter's total before he
> finishes the sentence. It is not a large number. It is all off your measure.

## R7 — after "> He writes it small and square… One man, Gonbei, sound."
> Six strokes an entry, saved from tonight on. You have the year's total before he sets the
> brush down: a page of paper, and an hour of his evenings. Nobody in this house has ever
> spent that much on you at once.

## R7 · ask r7-before (the take's only 2nd line; cut first if strict)
> Four names. Four lines ruled through. Two walked out, one died here, one was let go in a
> year this house could not pay. None of them was kept. You do not ask what will be written
> at the foot of the fifth.

## DELIBERATELY EMPTY
cold open + Intro 1 (he is the thing being counted; R1's first line should read as a CHANGE) ·
Intro 2 (already seeds it: "Two books in the room now, and only one of them open") · Intro 3
(he contributes nothing to his own entry — that helplessness IS the beat) · R2 (silent rung) ·
R3 (carries the model seed-bale line — one is the dose) · R4 (already at two; its dialogue IS
the payoff of R3's withheld count) · count-resolve · sb-lease ("worth a number" already) ·
sb-crest (already runs on counting) · sb-bon · sb-grove · sb-dog-coda · works-u1–u4 (Genemon's
counts, aloud — doubling the ledger is what ADR-185 exists to stop) · sb-cook (the ONE scene
where someone gives him something and nobody enters it — counting it would ruin it) ·
r7-dream (bound 1, absolutely) · the five season turns.

# W5 take B — "The cost of every question"

Every line prices a visible act, flatly, in things or counts. Never self-pity. 8 lines.

## R1 — after "> Nobody stops eating… The bowls go on."
> An objection costs the sixth place. It buys back no coin and no bed — the house has
> neither to give. So you do not make one.

## r2-yard-hand (the SILENT rung) — after "> You take the broom. Nobody takes it back."
> To ask whose it is, you would have to stop a man and make him answer a stranger. Taking
> it costs nothing until somebody takes it back.

## R4 (board half) — after "> The pen stops above the line a moment longer than the line needs…"
> He had taken the loss off your name. Two words put it back. There is no wage for it to
> come out of, and you said them anyway.

## count (R5) — after "> Lamps at the board at the wrong hour… Nobody arranged that."
> Nothing is asked of you but to stand in the middle of the floor and be looked at. It is
> the most expensive thing this house has ever asked of you, and it costs the house nothing.

## R6 — after Yohei's "Eleven for the thread, then?"
> The steward said nine. To say so out loud is to call a man wrong at his own boards, in
> front of the woman with the basket, carrying a purse that is not yours. The difference is
> two mon.

## R7 — after "> He writes it small and square… One man, Gonbei, sound."
> Six strokes saved on every entry, and an evening of his year. That is what the name is
> worth to the house. What it costs you is that tomorrow you will answer to it.

## sb-market — after Genemon's "…nobody has told you where coin goes."
> You could have asked, any day of that week. You carried the rag instead, and it was the
> cheaper of the two.

## sb-bon — after O-Hisa's "Not this. If a year from now — well. Not this."
> Three loads went past while you got the question out. It cost you all three, and the
> answer was no. You do not ask what a year would have to make of you.

## DELIBERATELY EMPTY
R3 (carries the model line) · count-resolve (Rokusuke declines the thanks himself) · sb-crest
(already: "Asking costs you something every time") · sb-lease · sb-sickroom · sb-cook (same
withheld-question move as sb-market — two in one bundle is a tic) · sb-racks / sb-grove (those
scenes belong to the other person) · sb-dog (its price is already ENTERED in the book — the
thesis doesn't apply) · nengu-autumn-frame (the MC as furniture is the whole design) · the
five season turns · **works-intro/u1–u4 — the strongest empty**: the thesis is that his costs
never reach a book, and Genemon's page is the ONE ledger that will carry what he puts in
("I enter what is put in, and the name that put it"). The exception IS the payoff. ·
r7-dream (memory's only vehicle).

## FINDINGS (bugs, not lines)
1. `rung-r7` / `ask r7-before` reads "The question costs HIM something to ask" — THIRD person
   in the MC's interior register. §0.5.8 violation, in the corpus today.
2. The Count's only price sits behind an OPTIONAL ask (`r5-accused`) — a player who never
   clicks stands in the middle of the floor all night and is charged nothing.
3. Pre-R7 scenes keep saying "your name" (works-u1, works-intro, sb-market) — the house does
   not write him a name until R7. Same class as the "Gonbei" defect.
4. Intro 2's "What name did I give? When they pulled me out." is the most expensive question
   in the game — the first he ever asks, and it is about himself — and it is unpriced.

# W5 take C — "The body knows and the head does not"

Rule that keeps it canon-safe: **the hands may testify; only the dream may translate.**
8 lines across ~25 scenes. No scene gets two.

## R1 — after "…So I ask you once. Is anything owing on you?"
> You have nothing to give him. The book already carries the one line about you that
> nobody in this house doubts — hands, good — and it was your hands that gave it, not you.

## R3 — after Kihei: "You didn't win. The house lost nothing. Keep the watch."
> The spear sits in your hands the way it sat last night: left hand forward, the butt
> against your hip. You did not put it there. Nobody has shown you where it goes.
(Author's note: R3 already carries the seed-bale line. If strictly one per scene, CUT THIS, not the seed-bale.)

## R4 — after "> He takes the oiled staff from the rack and holds it out…"
> Your hands take the staff and set themselves on it without being told — and then stop.
> They know how to hold it. They know nothing about what to do with it, and Kihei is
> watching the difference.

## count-resolve — after "> He bends back to the row."
> Eleven marks on a board, kept by a man who has never worked nearer to you than the next
> row. It is the fullest account of you that exists, and nobody asked you for any of it.

## R6 — after "> He watches the whole count… He enters something in the day-book, unasked."
> You did not work the number out. It was there before the last coin landed, and you could
> not say how it got there. Whatever he has entered, it is not the count.

## R7 — after "> He writes it small and square… One man, Gonbei, sound."
> The book has your hands in it: the loads, the counts that came out even, the night at the
> sill. It has never had anything else of you. Neither have you.

## sb-grove — after Shinnosuke: "I had a plan…"
> Uphill, on the slow side, not looking straight at it — you did all three before you chose
> any of them. If he asks who taught you that, you have nothing to give him.

## sb-sickroom — after "> He writes. The brush stops, and starts again…"
> Two books in this house keep an account of your body, and you have read neither. Your
> hands told him something in this room once. They have never told you.

## DELIBERATELY EMPTY (the take's real argument)
R2 (the silent rung — "You take the broom. Nobody takes it back." is already the body going
first, in eight words) · `count` (already has "The question costs you something to ask") ·
sb-lease (the device is that nobody asks him) · sb-crest (already runs on interiority) ·
sb-bon (exclusion is the content) · sb-market / sb-cook (teaching scenes — they land BECAUSE
they hit a blank wall) · sb-racks (Rokusuke's scene) · sb-dog (the dog IS the explanation) ·
nengu-autumn-frame (the MC is furniture — that is why the take was picked) · the five season
turns (landscape; the MC isn't in them) · works-intro/u1–u4 (Genemon's confession — one
interior line and the scene is about the wrong man) · **r7-dream** (the memory organ: every
waking line hands the hands a capability and refuses the account; the dream is where the
account finally arrives, with a road under it).
