# Process improvements — 25 generated, top 10 kept

Date: 2026-07-03 · Goal: the second wave after the 496-commit retro's five.
25 candidates were generated and ranked by **human-time leverage ×
compounding value × feasibility × non-overlap with the four in-flight
plans** (mechanical checkpoint · playtest inbox · balance sim · PRD
ripple/D-117). The bottom 15 were discarded (summary at the end). The
human picks 1–2 to build.

Ranking lens: the retro's conclusion — the human's scarce resource is
**taste/verdict time**; the agent's is **verified autonomy**. Everything
below buys one of those.

## The top 10

### 1 · Balance-tuning cockpit (DEV)

Sliders in the DEV panel for the liquid tuning levers the balance-watch
flagged (rice yield, sink prices, koku capstone, rest-vs-eat rates), applied
live to the running game — feel it immediately, no rebuild — with an
**"export diff" button** that emits the exact `balance.ts` change for the
agent to commit. Directly multiplies the open human TODO ("tune by feel"):
a tuning session becomes minutes, and its output is a committable artifact
instead of prose. Synergy: the balance-sim envelopes re-verify each
exported diff. *Build: small (DEV-only UI + the existing single-home
`balance.ts`). Opus.*

### 2 · Guided review tour + variant WIP cap

The un-built survivor of the original five (#3). One `/review-tour` entry
point: a deep-linked DEV walkthrough that strings every open R-item
(R1 pass, R6 home diverge, R7 estate-map V5A–G, R9 remasters) into one
guided in-game pass — each stop pre-loads the right state + variant, shows
the agent's self-review, and captures a keyboard verdict straight into
`review.md`. Plus a WIP cap: max 2 surfaces awaiting taste review (gate,
like the queue gate). Attacks the visible bottleneck: verdicts pile up
because reviewing has setup cost. *Build: medium. Opus, tour content
self-reviewed against taste.md.*

### 3 · GitHub Actions CI

The missing rung several plans already point at ("if CI ever lands,
`verify:balance` is its first tenant"). A workflow that runs full `verify`
on every push, the slow suites nightly (balance-sim matrix, fuzz seeds,
bundle DEV-strip check), and gates gh-pages deploys. Compounds every
existing and future gate; local hooks stop being the only proof; overnight
agents get a remote-red tripwire. *Build: small-medium. Opus.*

### 4 · Night-shift loop + morning report

Formalize the overnight autonomous session as a skill: standing priority
order (drain playtest inbox → owed engineering tail → audits → doc
reconciliation), the safety rails (no canon edits, no deploys, surface
forks as H-items), and a **human-facing morning digest** — what changed by
lane, what needs a verdict, what got parked — distinct from the
agent-facing session brief. You sleep; the repo moves; breakfast is a
5-minute read. *Build: small (skill + digest generator). Opus.*

### 5 · Scenario save library

Named, committed fixture saves (`fresh-R3`, `pre-ascension`, `post-loss`,
`wealthy-idler`, `mid-combat-worn-weapon`) loadable via `__qa`/DEV in one
click. Powers: human playtests that start at the interesting moment, bug
repro, review-tour stops (#2), balance checks (#1), and QA screenshots.
Regenerated via the persona bots when schemas migrate, so they never rot.
*Build: small. Opus. Enabler for three other items on this list.*

### 6 · Real-play telemetry → pacing truth

DEV-only, local-only: the running game timestamps rung-ups, session
lengths, and idle gaps in the human's actual save, and files a tiny timing
report into the playtest inbox at session end. Closes the loop the balance
work needs: sim envelopes say what a bot does; this says what YOU did —
the ~85-minute-climb question gets answered by evidence instead of recall.
*Build: small (state already tracks ticks). Opus.*

### 7 · Taste-bar enforcement hook

Once you and Fable lock `docs/living/taste.md` (the queued ⭐ TODO), wire
it in: the diverge/build skills gain a mandatory self-scorecard step
(each new surface scored against the taste principles, written into the
R-item), and pre-ship gains a checklist pass. Turns the taste standard
from shelf-ware into a ritual that fires on every surface — the
"less feedback needed" promise made mechanical. *Build: small, but GATED
on the human taste re-pass landing first. Fable authors the scorecard
rubric; Opus wires it.*

### 8 · Narrative authoring format

Rung beats, intro scenes, and dialogue trees are hand-coded TypeScript
today. A compact validated data format (structured markdown/YAML →
compiled into the typed registries, with a verify gate on referential
integrity: speakers exist, topics resolve, perks are real) makes T1's much
larger narrative load cheap to author — for you, Fable, or Opus alike.
*Build: medium. Design the format with Fable; Opus builds the compiler.*

### 9 · Worktree isolation for parallel agents

Replace the shared-tree hazards (stage-by-path discipline, another agent's
red WIP blocking your push, sweep accidents the hooks now police) with
per-agent git worktrees + a short merge lane. Kills a whole class of
coordination rules by construction instead of by vigilance. Honest cost:
it changes YOUR workflow too (multiple checkouts/ports), and the current
discipline is working — which is why it ranks 9 and not 3. *Build: medium
(conventions + hook updates more than code). Opus.*

### 10 · `/ship` release skill

One command for the release train: version bump → CHANGELOG section →
verify → build → DEV-strip check → deploy → tag → queue the "what's new"
note. Every piece exists (gh-pages script, changelog gate); the skill
removes the between-steps friction and the forget-a-step class. *Build:
small. Opus.*

## What got cut (the other 15, by category)

Folded into in-flight plans (journal-number collisions → mechanical
checkpoint; crash capture + review-mode overlay → inbox/tour) · overlapping
instrumentation (fun-proxy trend charts, spec↔test traceability matrix,
ADR→file impact linker) · premature until T1 planning starts (T1 content
scaffolder) · low leverage per build-cost (in-game "what's new", playthrough
video reels, multi-slot saves, repo doc-search, skills-drift audit, auto-ADR
drafter, `/onboard` skill — session-brief already covers it, queue-TL;DR
gate, Fable/Opus lane router — superseded by per-plan routing sections +
possibly #9).

## Suggested picks

If choosing two: **#1 (cockpit)** — it converts your single open gameplay
TODO into a 10-minute session with a committable output — and **#3 (CI)** —
it is pure compounding infrastructure that every current and future gate
rides on. #2 (review tour) is the pick instead of #1 if clearing the R-item
backlog feels more urgent than balance feel.
