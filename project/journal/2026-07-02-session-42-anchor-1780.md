# Session 42 — anchor the setting to the year 1780 (D-105)

**Date:** 2026-07-02
**Focus:** a human steer arising from a lore Q&A — anchor the game to a specific
historical year and record + plan the impact.

## What happened

A conversation about what *koku* means and how rice/wages worked in Edo Japan led
the human to ask what year the game is set in. The docs only pinned an *era*
("mid-Edo, ~18th century", `ui-design.md`'s "mid-Edo woodblock"), never a year.
The human decided to **anchor the setting to 1780** and asked to record it + plan
the ripple.

## What I did

- **ADR [D-105](../../docs/living/decisions.md)** — anchor to **1780 (An'ei 9)**,
  the last full year before Tenmei. Key framing: this anchors **TIME only** —
  **real year, fictional geography & politics** — so the D-018 / §6.6 real-name
  denylist is untouched (places/houses stay invented; "the shōgun" stays an
  unnamed office).
- **Plan [`docs/plans/2026-07-02-anchor-1780-setting.md`](../../docs/plans/2026-07-02-anchor-1780-setting.md)**
  — the impact ripple. Core finding: **1780 validates rather than disrupts** the
  existing world (koku/mon economy, fudasashi + Dōjima, sankin-kōtai, sekisho,
  the silk meibutsu, and the debt/Tama timeline are all already period-consistent)
  — so it's a grounding + texture pass with **no code/balance change** under the
  chosen defaults.
- Added the plan to the **reading queue** (`project/todo-human.md`).

## Two forks surfaced for the human (defaults picked)

- **Tenmei famine (1782–88):** default **INSULATE** the fictional valley (cozy
  tone + apolitical fiction win); famine is at most faint off-screen texture.
- **Calendar:** default keep the in-game `year(day)` counter **RELATIVE** (Year
  1, 2… of service), 1780 as opening flavour; do **not** surface the live nengō
  era-year (deferred).

- On confirm: apply the §5 ripple (§1.3 wording, README, ui-design one-liner,
  §5 opening year), verify green, graduate the plan to `project/archive/`.
