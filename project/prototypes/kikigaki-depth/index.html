◈Sōan · physician

He finishes with the arm, then does not leave. From the chest by the door he takes a bound book of blank leaves and sets it on your knee like a dose.

Sōan: “A man who has lost his yesterdays should keep better hold of his todays. When something will not sit still in your head — a thing said, a thing seen — write it here, and it is kept.”

He ties his sleeve and looks at nothing in particular.

Sōan: “If you want your night at the weir, ask the man who keeps the weir. I only received what the water was finished with.”

The book is thin, cheap, and completely blank. It is the first thing in this house that is yours.
